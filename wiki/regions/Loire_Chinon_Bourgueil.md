# Chinon and Bourgueil (with St-Nicolas-de-Bourgueil)

Chinon, Bourgueil, and St-Nicolas-de-Bourgueil are the red-wine glory of Touraine. All three lean on **Cabernet Franc** (the grape they share with Saumur upstream and Bordeaux to the southwest), with growing technique and practices making as much difference as soil types. Their wines run lightest at St-Nicolas through structured Bourgueil to **Chinon**, the most concentrated. Very small amounts of **Chinon Blanc** (Chenin) can be excellent too — a more austere interpretation of Chenin Blanc. In 2016, the boundary of the Chinon appellation was extended southwest along the south bank of the Loire to include eight more communes.

The Bourgueil zone is the relatively level plateau north of the Loire; St-Nicolas-de-Bourgueil overlaps from the west on lighter, sandier soils. Chinon sits on both sides of the Vienne tributary, with the best sites on the south-facing tuffeau slopes around Cravant-les-Coteaux and on the gravel terraces (graviers) closer to the river.

## Geography

- **Bourgueil plateau** — gravel terraces (graviers) closer to the Loire, tuffeau higher up
- **St-Nicolas** — sandier, lighter; northwest end of the Bourgueil plateau
- **Chinon** — south of the Loire on the Vienne; tuffeau cliffs at Cravant-les-Coteaux are the prized red-wine sites
- Climate is intermediate — Atlantic still reaches but moderated; growing season longer than Muscadet
