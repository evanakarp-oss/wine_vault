# Auction Catalog 265DE — Wheelhouse (2026-06)

Screened all **1,453 lots** against Evan's taste filters. This is a
**blue-chip Burgundy + cult auction**: 689 Red Burgundy, 294 White Burgundy
(68% of the catalog is Burgundy), 107 Rhône, 103 Champagne, 93 Red Bordeaux,
101 California (almost entirely the cult/hedonist tier — **skipped**), 32 Italy.
Estimates are the house low–high; per-bottle math shown for multi-bottle lots.

The catalog is dominated by trophy Burgundy (DRC 175 lots, Comte de Vogüé 84,
Rousseau 72) that sits above both the budget and the value-axis of the taste
profile. The wheelhouse is the **grower/terroir spine running underneath it**:
grower Champagne, natural/traditional Northern Rhône, classic Piedmont, white
Burgundy growers, and a few Jura/German sleepers — several of them producers
already in the cellar or vault.

## Tier 3 — Burgundy growers (right taste, trophy prices)

The catalog's grower Burgundy is the correct *style* (terroir-driven,
grower-scale) but priced at the collector top. Watch for the value vintages /
village wines if you want exposure: **Roumier, Mugnier** (you own his NSG Clos
de la Maréchale), **Rousseau** (you own his Clos de la Roche), **Barthod,
Lafarge, d'Angerville, Fourrier, Bizot, D. Bachelet, Trapet, Mugneret-Gibourg,
Cathiard, Hudelot-Noëllat, Chevillon**. On the white side: **Coche-Dury (79
lots), Roulot, Comtes Lafon, Leflaive, Dauvissat, Raveneau, A. Ente, H. Lamy,
PYCM**. These are all genuine wheelhouse producers — the constraint is price,
not taste. Bonnes-Mares / village-level lots and the lesser vintages are where
the value sits.

## Skip (off-taste)

- **All California** (101 lots): Sine Qua Non (52 lots), Screaming Eagle (14),
  Harlan, Colgin, Promontory, The Flight, Opus One, Caymus — the cult-hedonist
  tier Evan explicitly skips. No Dunn/Corison/La Jota/Ridge/Dalla Valle present,
  so the entire CA section is off-axis.
- **Penfolds Grange** (2 lots), **Vega Sicília Único / Pingus** (Spain) —
  outside the stated interest areas.
- **Red Bordeaux** (93 lots): mostly classed-growth trophy (Pétrus, La Fleur
  Pétrus, Mouton). Per the filter, aged classed-growth is fine *for drink-now
  only* — the mature '81/'82 Pomerols (lots 3–6) qualify if you want a
  drink-now Bordeaux, but it's not the value/undercovered profile.

## Bottom line

The bid list is the **grower/terroir spine, not the trophies**: Gonon, Allemand,
Chave (St-Joseph/value Hermitage), Burlotto, Roagna, Valentini in Tier 1 — most
already cellar/vault producers — then grower Champagne (Prévost, Savart, the
cheaper Selosse/Bouchard/Péters entries) and the Jura/Saar sleepers (Ganevat,
Les Jardins Vivants, Egon Müller). The 1,000 lots of trophy Burgundy and the
entire California section are correct to mostly pass on at these estimates.
