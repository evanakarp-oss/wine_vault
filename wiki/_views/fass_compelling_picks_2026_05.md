# FASS Compelling Picks — Signal-Tested (2026-05)

The FASS book is 123 producers wide after the 2026-05 rebuild. Lyle Fass
writes the way Lyle Fass writes ("1 of 124 LIMITED LIST", "the Greek's
Cellar"), but underneath the marketing tone there is real wine. This view
strips the noise and tests each pick against three external signals plus
Evan's CLAUDE.md curation filters.

**Signal layers**

- **WB top 100** — Wine Berserkers "Top 10 in your cellar" megathread
  (1,089 posts, 1,115 unique producers). Community-cellar consensus,
  skews US collector. Only **5 of 123** FASS producers land here.
- **WK posts** — William Kelley's Berserkers participation (4,729 posts
  scanned for topic-title hits). Personal critic conviction. **16 of 123**
  FASS producers got a WK topic title.
- **Curation filter** — Evan's CLAUDE.md taste rules.

These three signals serve different jobs. WB = collector consensus,
WK = critic conviction, curation = portfolio-fit. Where two or three
converge is the strongest signal.

The headline finding: **FASS is not a way to fill the WB-top-100 cellar
gaps** (Bedrock / Rhys / Ridge / Rivers-Marie / Produttori / Bruno
Giacosa / Dujac / Rousseau / Willi Schaefer / Hofgut Falkenstein / DRC
etc. are all unavailable here). What FASS *is* is the deepest US source
for several specialist niches: Northern Rhône Cornas micro-domains,
German biodynamic GG, grower Champagne, Alto Piemonte Nebbiolo, aged-
classic late-release programs, and the natural-Beaujolais Gang-of-Four
tier.

## §4 — FASS's true depth (no signal, but distinctive offer)

These are FASS's structural advantages — clusters and aged-stock that
other US retailers don't carry at this depth.

### Cornas micro-domain cluster

FASS carries 8 distinct Cornas growers, none of them in WB top 100,
several stylistically downstream of Thierry Allemand (who *is* in WB
top 100 but unavailable). If you want exposure to next-generation
Cornas, this is the source.

- [[cuchet_beliando|Cuchet-Beliando]] — 2015 / 2016 / 2017 / 2018 / 2019 / 2020 vertical, $95–$117 each. **The vertical itself is the offer.**
- [[guillaume_gilles|Guillaume Gilles]] — Robert Michel's spiritual heir. Cornas Nouvelle R + lieu-dit cuvées, $65–$90.
- [[domaine_du_tunnel|Domaine du Tunnel]] — Stéphane Robert. Vin Noir $90 is the old-vine flagship.
- [[mickael_bourg|Mickael Bourg]] — Vincent Paris alum (who himself worked with Allemand). Les P'tits Bout $70.
- [[jacques_lemenicier|Jacques Lemenicier]] — Cornas veteran, Père Laurier $102.

### Côte-Rôtie

- [[julien_barge|Julien Barge]] — Coeur de Combard 2015–2019 vertical at $135. Old-school élevage.
- [[jean_michel_stephan|Jean-Michel Stéphan]] — Coteaux du Bassenon / du Tupin $146. Natural Côte-Rôtie reference, vintage-variable. **One of the rare names where natural and grand-cru intersect.**
- [[compagnie_de_lhermitage|Compagnie de l'Hermitage]] — Lyle Fass's own négociant project with Greek-born Georges Lelektsoglou. Côte Rôtie La Brocarde from the Côte Blonde. House-special unfined / unfiltered Syrah.

### Aged-stock specialty (the late-release lens)

- [[wilhelm_weber_osterman|Wilhelm Weber-Osterman]] — Brauneberger Juffer Auslese **1989 / 1993** at $33–$42. Aged Mosel Auslese at sub-$50 is essentially a unicorn.
- [[tenuta_monolo_gilodi|Tenuta Monolo Gilodi]] — Bramaterra Riserva **1989 / 1990 / 2003** all $25. Aged Spanna at giveaway pricing.
- [[pier_paolo_grasso|Pier Paolo Grasso]] — Barbaresco Piccola Emma **1998** $85. Aged Nebbiolo without the auction premium.

### Alto Piemonte sweep (Ghemme / Boca / Bramaterra / Fara / Sizzano / Carema)

FASS has the broadest Alto Piemonte cross-section in the country. None
of these producers carry WB signal — that thread doesn't see this zone —
but the appellations themselves are the real signal.

- [[vigneti_valle_roncati|Vigneti Valle Roncati]] — three DOCG cross-section: Ghemme Leblanque $46, Sizzano Riserva Roano $32, Fara Riserva Ciada $30. **One-stop shop for the volcanic-soil Spanna appellations.**
- [[podere_ai_valloni|Podere ai Valloni]] — Boca Vigna Cristiana $62. Vallana-adjacent porphyry.
- [[sergio_barbaglia|Sergio Barbaglia]] — Boca $42, Croatina $23. Plus a 96-month-aged traditional method Rosé.
- [[vini_chiussima|Vini Chiussima]] — Carema $60. Alpine Nebbiolo on terraced pergolas at the Valle d'Aosta border.

### Graubünden Pinot Noir cluster

FASS has the deepest US book for Bündner Herrschaft Pinot Noir. No WB
signal because the WB top 100 doesn't reach into Swiss wine, but the
quality is genuine.

- [[weingut_hansruedi_adank|Weingut Familie Hansruedi Adank]] — Spondis Pinot Noir $88–$110, Chardonnay Am Berg $155. Fläsch domaine.
- [[patrick_adank|Patrick Adank]] — Chardonnay Am Berg $192, Pinot Noir Herrenacker $192. Top-tier solo project.

## §5 — What to skip (FASS noise that doesn't pass the filter)

Even after the seed pass, parts of the book don't justify allocation.

- **Generic mid-tier Bordeaux** (Chateau Bouillerot, Samion, Bonneau, Vieux St-Andrée) — sub-$40 Bordeaux without the WK-undercovered hook.
- **Pierre Brisset / Maison Brisset** — Burgundy négoce covering the Côte d'Or pyramid. Functional but no edge over Chambers Street's grower book.
- **Most Champagne NV without grower / aged / dosage angle** — Paul Drappier Charles de Gaulle NV ($58) is fine but generic.
- **Late-arriving Franken Cabernet / Merlot** — Richard Östreicher's red Bordeaux blends ($42–$54) are interesting curiosities but Franken is opened for Pinot / Silvaner depth, not Cabernet.
- **Maison Alexandrins** — Nicolas Jaboulet's négoce project. Modern style, fine, but the natural / grower cluster above is the FASS edge.
