# Low-Cost, High-Aging-Potential Producers in Vault

Producers tracked in the vault whose **cheapest genuinely-ageworthy bottle** is inexpensive — the "affordable wines that will age" thesis made concrete. Two price sources are used and labeled per row:

- **Vault** — a current DTE / Raeders portfolio price already in the wiki (Raeders `master_2026-04-25.csv`, DTE `dte_portfolio_2026-03-25`).
- **Market** — open-market US retail as of **July 2026** (wine-searcher averages + named shops), pulled to fill the gap for high-aging producers the vault carries *no* retail price for. These are external, not vault-canonical — treat as ballpark, confirm before buying.

> **Methodology note (why this matters).** `aging_score` is a *producer-level* signal — it reflects the estate's **flagship**. Pairing it with a producer's cheapest bottle is only valid when that cheap bottle is itself ageworthy. A great Barolo house's cheapest wine is often a drink-young Arneis. Every row below names the **actual bottle** and was checked for that; producers whose cheap bottle is *not* their ageworthy wine are quarantined in "Don't be fooled."

## The headline: the whole top of the aging ladder is sub-$30

The vault's **six highest aging-scores** are all Loire Cabernet Franc or Mosel Riesling — and every one is a $15–28 bottle. This is the answer to "cheap but worth cellaring," and vault pricing alone had hidden it (none of the six carried a retailer price in the wiki):

1. **Olga Raffault, Chinon "Les Peuilles" ~$22–26** — score 14. Traditional Chinon that CSW cites at "15–30 years"; among the longest-lived wines in the vault at a mid-$20s price.
2. **Catherine & Pierre Breton, Bourgueil "Trinch!" ~$20–28** — score 14. Natural-wine-pioneer Cab Franc; "Trinch!" is the entry cuvée and still holds a decade.
3. **Domaine Baudry, Chinon "Les Granges" $20** — score 13, 45 CSW articles / 20 ★. The single most-championed value in the vault.
4. **Stéphane Guion, Bourgueil ~$15–20** — score 12. 60 years organic farming; CSW's literal "best values are back" producer. Arguably the cheapest genuinely 15-year wine here.
5. **Domaine de la Chevalerie, Bourgueil ~$17–20** — score 10. Organic Bourgueil, mineral, ageworthy.
6. **Willi Schaefer, Graacher Himmelreich Kabinett ~$24** — score 6. Perennially scarce Mosel Kabinett that ages 10–20 years (ignore wine-searcher's $55 avg — real shop price is ~$24).

Add **Clemens Busch vom Roten Schiefer (~$22–28)** and you have a seven-bottle, all-sub-$30 cellar spanning the two most reliably-aging value categories in wine: Loire Cab Franc and Mosel Riesling.

## Caveats

- **Market prices are July-2026 snapshots**, external to the vault (wine-searcher + named US shops). They're ballpark and vary by vintage/state — confirm before buying. Vault prices are dated per source (Raeders 2026-04-25, DTE 2026-03-25).
- **Coverage is sparse.** Of 829 producers, 121 carry `aging_score`, only 22 score ≥4. Web-pricing closed the biggest gap (the top-of-ladder Loire/Mosel producers), but ageworthy producers outside CSW's scored set still won't appear.
- **Cellar windows** are vendor/critic quotes or inference; actual aging depends on vintage, storage, and taste.
