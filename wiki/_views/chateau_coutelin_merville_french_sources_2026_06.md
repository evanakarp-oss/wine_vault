# Château Coutelin-Merville — French-Source Research (2026-06)

Seed: a bottle of **Coutelin-Merville Saint-Estèphe 2015** (photo, 2026-06-14).
The wine is barely covered stateside — the English record is thin merchant copy —
so this note is built from the French sources. No producer page existed before;
created alongside this view.

## Who they are (sources françaises)

- **Cru Bourgeois of Saint-Estèphe**, on the classification since its **1932**
  creation ([crus-bourgeois.com](http://www.crus-bourgeois.com/chateau-coutelin-merville),
  [Maison du Vin de Saint-Estèphe](https://saint-estephe.fr/fr/chateau/coutelin-merville)).
- **Estager family property for over a century** — the family came from the
  **Corrèze** and has been on the estate **since 1902**
  ([twil.fr](https://www.twil.fr/france/bordeaux/saint-estephe/cru-bourgeois-saint-estephe-wine-2623.html),
  [Maison du Vin de Saint-Estèphe](https://saint-estephe.fr/fr/chateau/coutelin-merville)).
- **1987 succession**: after the death of **Guy Estager**, his children took over —
  **Bernard**, an accountant who left the profession, retrained, and came back to
  run the domaine to "perpétuer la tradition familiale," with brother **François**
  in constant support
  ([twil.fr](https://www.twil.fr/france/bordeaux/saint-estephe/cru-bourgeois-saint-estephe-wine-2623.html)).
  A genuine family hand-down, not a corporate or insurance-group holding — rare
  for the Médoc.

## Terroir & encépagement

- **~30 ha** vineyard in the **north of Saint-Estèphe**, on **argilo-calcaire**
  (clay-limestone) soils; planting density **~8,300 pieds/ha**
  ([chateaunet.com](https://www.chateaunet.com/vins/bordeaux/chateaux/chateau-coutelin-merville),
  [lapassionduvin.com](https://www.lapassionduvin.com/bordeaux/599-chateau-coutelin-merville-saint-estephe)).
- **Encépagement** (the interesting part): **~50% Merlot, 25% Cabernet Sauvignon,
  ~20–22% Cabernet Franc, ~5% Petit Verdot**. The Merlot-led blend with an
  **unusually high Cabernet Franc share** is atypical for the left-bank Médoc and
  is the estate's signature
  ([chateaunet.com](https://www.chateaunet.com/vins/bordeaux/chateaux/chateau-coutelin-merville)).
  (Sources disagree 20 vs 22% on the Cab Franc — both reported.)

## Vinification

- **Vinification traditionnelle**, **élevage 12 mois en barriques** with the barrel
  park renewed **1/5 each year** (so ~20% new oak, gentle)
  ([cavusvinifera tasting fiche, 2015](http://www.cavusvinifera.com/fr/voirdegustpublique.php?iddegustation=126306)).
- No organic / biodynamic / HVE certification surfaced in any French source —
  treat farming as **conventional / unstated**.

## Le millésime 2015

2015 was a strong, ripe Médoc vintage. French notes for this bottle:

- **Robe** grenat-pourpre profonde / "couleur profonde."
- **Nez** expressif de **fruits noirs** — cassis, mûre, cerise noire — avec
  **violette**, **épices douces**, **vanille** et **tabac blond**.
- **Bouche** : beau corps, **tanins fins, ronds et souples**, finale persistante
  sur les **épices douces** et le **moka**; oscille entre fraîcheur et gourmandise,
  "harmonieuse et accessible."
  ([chateaunet.com 2015](https://www.chateaunet.com/chateau-coutelin-merville-1124178-chateau-coutelin-merville-saint-estephe-rouge-2015-075),
  [cavusvinifera](http://www.cavusvinifera.com/fr/voirdegustpublique.php?iddegustation=126306)).
- Style read: a **mid-weight, supple, fruit-forward Saint-Estèphe** — the soft,
  approachable face of the appellation (the high Merlot + Cab Franc), not the
  brooding tannic Montrose/Calon archetype. **Drink-now through ~2027–2030**;
  this is not a long-haul cellar wine.

## Critic record (French)

- **Guide Hachette** lists the property and the wine across vintages (e.g. a 2019
  entry) — coverage confirms it's a recognized Cru Bourgeois, but no headline
  star rating was recoverable from the blocked pages
  ([Hachette producer page](https://www.hachette-vins.com/guide-vins/producteurs/16202/ch-coutelin-merville/)).
- Anglo critics on the **2015** (for cross-reference): the vault's Phélan-Ségur
  page records the neighbourhood at WA 90 / WE 93 for 2015 St-Estèphe value tier;
  Coutelin-Merville sits a notch below that in profile and price.

## Curation verdict (Evan's Bordeaux filter)

Evan's Bordeaux filter: *"WK-style undercovered/value with great farming. Aged
classed-growth for drink-now bottles only. Skip generic mid-tier."*

Coutelin-Merville is a **genuine value / undercovered** Cru Bourgeois with a
**century of unbroken family ownership** and a **distinctive high-Cab-Franc blend**
— all points in its favour, and the kind of affordable Saint-Estèphe William
Kelley champions in spirit. **But**: farming is conventional/unstated and the wine
is a soft, drink-now mid-weight rather than a terroir-tensioned keeper. Net: a
**pleasant value drinker, not a cellar-defining buy** — fine to enjoy, sits right
at the edge of the "skip generic mid-tier" line. Worth a page for the family
story and the unusual encépagement; not worth chasing more vintages.
