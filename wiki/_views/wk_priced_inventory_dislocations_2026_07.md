# WK × Priced Inventory — Live Dislocations (2026-07)

The [[wk_price_quality_dislocations_2026_07|dislocation board]] restricted to
**wines with actual price data in the vault** — the Raeders snapshot
(2026-04-25, bottle-level) and the DTE portfolio (per-producer ranges). 59
producers carry both a WK Berserkers signal and a price; the frontmatter join
plus a manual sweep for WK names lacking the `berserkers_kelley` block
(Mugnier, Bouley, Maltroye, Faiveley, Roulot, de Villaine, Fourrier…).

**On scores:** there are almost none to use. `raw/clippings/wine_advocate/`
is empty and every score column in the Raeders CSV is zero. Concrete numbers
in the corpus: 98 pts on the 2019 Arnoux-Lachaux RSV (his own), and a
third-party report that he "gave many 95pts" across a Lorenzon vertical
(`173448__0024`). So per the brief, **the ranking below runs on his
qualitative WB prose**, weighted:

- **W1 — he bought it himself** (a critic spending his own money is the
  strongest signal he emits)
- **W2 — explicit mispricing statement** ("amazing it isn't more expensive")
- **W3 — emphatic praise** (benchmark comparisons, "not to miss")
- Mere mentions carry no weight and were excluded.

## Tier 3 — price ≫ pleasure on today's sheet (avoid or trade down)

- **Arnoux-Lachaux** (DTE, $299–6,999) — his 98 pts vs his own zip-code
  logic; quality real, price fully fashionable. Skip at retail.
- **Michel Noëllat** (DTE $45) — the one outright pan in the join: "a pity
  they don't do a better job" with Suchots (2017); "some work to do in the
  vineyards" (2022).
- **"Esprit Leflaive" négoce line at Raeders** — Pouilly-Fuissé at
  **$370–470**, St-Romain $430, Savigny 1er $540. No WK pan on record
  (inference, flag it as such): these are Mâconnais/négoce appellations at
  Puligny-domaine prices — the exact inverse of his "value lives in lesser
  appellations" rule. The domaine's Mâcon-Verzé at $90 is the sane entry.
- **Dom Pérignon Rosé "Lady Gaga Edition" 2008** (Raeders $500) — inference,
  same flag: a label premium on an otherwise obtainable wine; Comtes de
  Champagne 2013 sits on the same sheet at $280. His aged-NV Champagne
  value call (`163231`) points the opposite direction entirely.
- **Clos du Mont Olivet** (DTE $69) — dated caveat: "pre-2009… would have
  been a contender" among CdP's best (`124179__0034`); post-2009 less so.
- **Palmer 2019** (Raeders $1,299) — he praises the estate's oak integration
  today, but the same sheet has **1981 Palmer at $400 and 1964 at $500** —
  the aged side is where both WK's palate and Evan's filter point.
