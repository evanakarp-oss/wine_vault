# JP (ex-Babbo) — Barolo Recommendations

JP, the former sommelier at **Babbo** (NYC), passed along six Barolo producers
on 2026-06-04, grouped by style. His overall read: *"not really large wineries
but farms with a master of craft."* That's a clean match for Evan's Piedmont
curation taste — terroir-driven, grower-scale, traditional. Verbatim source:
`raw/recommendations/2026-06-04_jp_babbo_barolo.md`.

## "You should visit"

- [[fratelli_alessandria|Fratelli Alessandria]] — Verduno benchmark. Already in
  the vault (CSW ★, Roscioli profile).
- [[fratelli_barale|Barale]] (Fratelli Barale) — commune of Barolo; Castellero +
  Bussia fruit; resolutely traditional, "vino da meditazione." CSW ★ (2015).
- [[giacomo_fenocchio|Giacomo Fenocchio]] — Monforte / Bussia; organic, long
  ferments, Slavonian oak. CSW ★ (2020).

## "More powerful wines"

- [[silvano_bolmida|Silvano Bolmida]] — Monforte / Bussia; 60–120 day macerations,
  built for power. No CSW coverage yet.
- [[flavio_roddolo|Flavio Roddolo]] — Monforte / Bricco Appiani; copper-and-sulfur
  farming, old-school, age-demanding. CSW ★ ("top tier of traditional Barolo").

## "More elegant but structured"

- [[luigi_vico|Luigi Vico]] — Serralunga d'Alba; ~2.1 ha certified-organic
  micro-grower; Barolo del Comune + Prapò. No CSW coverage yet.

## Follow-ups

- **[[flavio_roddolo|Flavio Roddolo]] is already owned** — 10 bottles across 3
  cuvées (7× Langhe Cabernet 2010, 2× Barbera d'Alba Superiore 2012, 1× Barolo
  Ravera 2013). These cellar entries previously had **no producer page** — JP's
  rec closed that orphan-reference gap.
- **Bolmida & Vico have no retailer footprint in the vault** — neither is at CSW,
  DTE, Raeders or FASS as modeled. US sourcing: Bolmida via Regal Wine Imports;
  Vico via North Berkeley Imports. Worth confirming current availability.
- The other five are acquisition candidates, not yet owned.
