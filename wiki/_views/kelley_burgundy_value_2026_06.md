# Kelley's Burgundy Value Map (2026-06)

A buy-side map distilled from **William Kelley's** Wine Berserkers posts
(`raw/berserkers/William_Kelley/`). These are forum posts, not ingested
Wine Advocate reviews — Kelley writing casually as a participant, dated
2017–2025. Treat as directional, not as scored notes.

The thread that kicked this off: Kelley's NSG/Vosne **"zip code"** critique —
that glamorous 1er crus (esp. Vosne **Les Suchots**) are "**priced more by zip
code than by what's in the bottle**" (`topic 137497`, 2017; e.g. Hudelot-Noëllat
Suchots = same price as the "much superior" Clos Vougeot). What follows is the
inverse: where he says the value actually is.

## The meta-pattern

**Value lives wherever prestige signaling is absent.** Six distinct moves,
each a different lever than "find a cheaper famous name":

1. A red from a *white-famous* commune (Chassagne Rouge)
2. The under-loved *neighbor* of a glamour commune (Savigny vs Volnay)
3. Buy *down within* a top grower's range (the unfashionable cuvée)
4. Village wine from a *Grand-Cru* house (Marsannay from Roty)
5. The quiet classicist nobody bids on
6. The cult producer's "value label"

This is a more durable filter than chasing discounted famous names, and it maps
cleanly to Evan's Burgundy taste (terroir-driven, grower-scale, biodynamic-leaning).

## 1. A whole appellation the market still ignores: Chassagne *Rouge*

Kelley's most emphatic value call — thread titled *"The last great vestige of
value in Red Burgundy"* (`topic 330056`, Jan 2025):

> "The best reds in Chassagne today are from the **Bachelet brothers at Domaine
> J-C Bachelet**, **Thierry Pillot at Domaine Paul Pillot**, and **the Cardeuse
> bottling** (now split between the two **Moreau brothers**)… they all farm
> exceptionally and are working with top terroirs. **Morey-Coffinet** and
> **F & L Pillot** are two addresses to watch."

On the Bachelet bros' farming: "tressage in some sites, cultivation using
chénillard… long élevage in increasingly well-chosen oak… one of the unsung
top estates of the Côte de Beaune."

**Logic:** red from a white-famous commune is structurally underpriced — nobody
chases it.
**Caveat:** J-C Bachelet ≠ Bachelet-Ramonet ≠ [[denis_bachelet|Denis Bachelet]]
(Gevrey). The vault only has the Gevrey one — **gap**.

## 2. The under-loved neighbor of a glamour commune: Savigny-lès-Beaune

Same thread (`post 64`) — drink Savigny instead of Volnay:

> "If you drink old bottles of **Bize, Guillemot or Chandon de Briailles**, to
> say nothing of Leroy Narbantons… there is no doubting one can make exquisite
> wines! I love Savigny because the combination of fine tannins and immediate
> fruit makes for very versatile wines with broad drinking windows."

Both [[domaine_simon_bize|Simon Bize]] and [[chandon_de_briailles|Chandon de
Briailles]] are already in the vault — organic/biodynamic, grower-scale,
dead-center Evan's taste.

## 3. Buy down *within* a top grower's range

[[thomas_et_jean_marc_bouley|Thomas Bouley]], Volnay (`topic 173245`, 2021) —
skip the name-recognition crus:

> "The real values in the range are certainly the **Hautes-Côtes** (a beautiful
> enclave on the plateau above Volnay) and the **Volnay Clos de la Cave**…
> I've tried to highlight how good **Fremiers** is — Rugiens and Caillerets get
> all the love due to name recognition, whereas Fremiers is the better wine:
> deeper, more complete… his Fremiers is 'more Rugiens' than his Rugiens."

Same logic seen earlier in Vosne (`topic 137497`): "I'd rather drink
**Mugneret-Gibourg Vosne village**" than a "facile" Chaumes 1er cru.

## 4. Village wine from a Grand-Cru house: Marsannay from Roty

[[domaine_joseph_roty|Joseph Roty]] (`topic 137528`, 2017):

> "For those interested in value — **the Marsannay line-up of Roty is fabulous**…
> Roty's Mazis and Charmes are a step up over Rousseau's relatively weak
> renditions."

The cheapest label from a domaine that makes Charmes/Mazis-Chambertin.

## 5. The quiet classicist nobody bids on

[[joseph_voillot|Joseph Voillot]], Volnay (`topic 149875`, 2019):

> "A very classic style with supple tannins and lovely purity of fruit, they age
> well and **drink well quite young too — somewhat under the radar.**"

Entry tier: **Thierry Glantenay's Bourgogne** (`topic 139570`, 2018) — "not
well-known and very reasonably priced… delicious." *(Vault has the sibling
`georges_glantenay.md`, not Thierry — gap.)*

## 6. The cult producer's "value label"

Whites — the Mâconnais (`topic 149236`, 2019):

> "**Verget has effectively become the 'value range' from Guffens**, concentrating
> on the Mâconnais… everything under screwcap and prices are very keen indeed.
> The wines can be terrific, with the best cuvées among the best wines in the
> region — but not on the level of Guffens-Heynen."

Cult winemaking at négoce pricing.

## Cross-reference: original NSG/Vosne value picks (the grower-down move)

From the same corpus, before the price ran up:

- **Mugneret-Gibourg NSG 1er Les Chaignots** — "**one of the few remaining great
  values in pedigreed Côte de Nuits red Burgundy**" (`topic 163772`, 2020). The
  1999 "magical." *(Now ~$375 retail — value mostly priced in. No vault page —
  gap; `gerard_mugneret.md` is a different domaine.)*
- **Felettig, Vosne Reignots** — "quite under the radar, think it all goes to
  Asia" (`topic 137497`).
- **Clos Vougeot, broadly** — "quite underrated," and a better buy than
  same-priced Suchots from [[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]].
