# Vosne-Romanée — Adjacent Parcels (North & South)

Study companion to the *World Atlas of Wine*, "Southern Côte de Nuits" spread. Vosne's
heart (Romanée-Conti, La Tâche, La Romanée, Richebourg, Romanée-St-Vivant, La Grande Rue)
is the most expensive dirt in the world; the interesting question for a buyer is what
sits on either shoulder of those grand crus, and which of those parcels deliver Vosne
character at a non-Vosne price.

The Vosne slope is bracketed by two very different neighbors. Walking **south to north**
along the Côte:

```
                Prémeaux  ─►  NSG (south)  ─►  NSG (north, "the Vosne shoulder")
                                                       │
                                                       ▼
                                              VOSNE-ROMANÉE
                                                       │
                                                       ▼
                                  Flagey-Echézeaux  ─►  Vougeot  ─►  Chambolle-Musigny
```

---

## South: Nuits-Saint-Georges (the "Vosne shoulder")

The NSG commune is long. The Atlas: *"There are not many real Premiers Crus around
Nuits-St-Georges that demand long bottle-age."* True for the southern (Prémeaux) end —
**different territory** — but the **northern** end of NSG, immediately below the
Vosne-Romanée boundary, is essentially Vosne's geological continuation. These are the
parcels that share Vosne's slope and Vosne's clay-limestone, but sell under the
Nuits-Saint-Georges name. That's the value structural arbitrage.

**Border climats (north → south, the slice that touches Vosne):**

| Climat | Classification | Character | Vosne-likeness |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aux Boudots** | 1er Cru | Closest to Vosne (touches VR 1er Cru Aux Malconsorts); silky, perfumed | ★★★★★ "the most Vosne-like NSG" |
| **Les Damodes** | 1er Cru | Upper slope, lighter, more aromatic | ★★★★ |
| **Aux Cras** | 1er Cru | Mid-slope, ripe but structured | ★★★★ |
| **Aux Murgers** | 1er Cru | Stony (its name); firmer than Boudots, dark fruit | ★★★ |
| **Aux Thorey** | 1er Cru | Upper slope above Vosne shoulder; aromatic | ★★★ |
| **Aux Chaignots** | 1er Cru | Just south of Murgers; mid-weight | ★★★ |
| **La Richemone** | 1er Cru | Tiny, above-slope | ★★★ |

**Then the NSG town interrupts the slope.** South of the town the character shifts —
firmer, more tannic, more "savoury Pinot" than "silky Vosne":

| Climat | Classification | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Les Vaucrains | 1er Cru | Stony, tannic, long-aging |
| Les Saint-Georges | 1er Cru | The reference NSG — structured, deep, ageworthy |
| Les Cailles | 1er Cru | Floral counterpoint to Saint-Georges |
| Les Pruliers | 1er Cru | Plummy, dark |
| Les Roncières / Les Perrières | 1er Cru | Mid-tier, structured |
| Rue de Chaux | 1er Cru | Plush, approachable |

**Prémeaux-Prissey** (further south, left margin of the Atlas map) is sold under the
NSG name but is a different terroir again — monopole-heavy, often with more weight than
elegance: Clos de la Maréchale (Mugnier), Clos de l'Arlot, Clos des Forêts St-Georges,
Clos des Argillières, Clos des Corvées, Clos des Corvées Pagets.

## North: Flagey-Échézeaux → Vougeot → Chambolle-Musigny

Walking out of Vosne to the north, the slope **never breaks**. The commune lines do,
which is where the bargains hide.

### Flagey-Échézeaux

Technically a separate commune, but its grand crus are sold as Vosne-area:

- **Grands Échézeaux** — 9 ha. Tightly bounded, directly adjoining Clos de Vougeot's
  upper section. The Atlas notes it produces wines *"that mark the very great
  burgundies."* Smaller, more uniform than Échézeaux. The serious one.
- **Échézeaux** — 36 ha. *"Some would say too big"* (Atlas). Wide quality range — the
  upper-slope sub-climats (Les Rouges du Bas, Les Poulaillères, Les Cruots, Les
  Beaumonts) approach Grands Échézeaux; the lower-slope sections are merely good.
  Producer matters enormously here.

The Flagey village itself has **no village-level wine** of its own — village-level
juice from Flagey is sold as **Vosne-Romanée Villages**, which is why Vosne village
production is meaningfully larger than the Vosne commune alone would suggest. The
"Flagey shoulder" 1er crus of Vosne are: **Les Beaux Monts (Beaumonts)**, **Les
Suchots**, **En Orveaux**, **Les Rouges du Dessus**, **Aux Brûlées**, **Les Petits
Monts** — these all sit at the Vosne–Flagey hinge and drink with Échézeaux ambition
at 1er Cru pricing.

### Vougeot

- **Clos de Vougeot** Grand Cru — 50 ha, 80+ owners, the most heterogeneous grand cru
  on the Côte. The classic rule: **upper third** (touching Grands Échézeaux and Musigny
  on the slope) ≈ grand cru quality; **middle** ≈ premier cru; **lower** (near the road,
  poorly drained) ≈ village. Reading the producer's parcel location is mandatory before
  buying.
- **Vougeot 1er Cru** (the village's own 1ers, *outside* the Clos walls) — Les Petits
  Vougeot, Les Cras, Clos Blanc de Vougeot (white). Often overlooked, often the cheapest
  way into the Vougeot–Musigny axis.

### Chambolle-Musigny

- **Musigny** Grand Cru — Atlas: *"The glory of Musigny is that it covers its undoubted
  power with a lovely, haunting delicacy of perfume; a uniquely sensuous savour…
  'peacock's tail' in your mouth."* The honorary Grand Cru of Burgundy by reputation.
- **Bonnes Mares** Grand Cru — *"pale soil in its western half and red in the east"*;
  the masculine counterweight to Musigny at the north end of Chambolle.
- **Les Amoureuses** 1er Cru — effectively grand-cru priced; sits just below Musigny.
- **Les Cras / Les Fuées** 1er Cru — the Atlas highlights these as *"sought-after
  today as a Les Charmes"* — the value-seeking upper-tier Chambolle 1ers as the climate
  warms.

## Best value — NSG-adjacent (Vosne shoulder)

**What the vault currently surfaces** in the NSG climats touching or near the Vosne
border. Producers in **bold** are CSW-championed or DTE-portfolio standouts.

### Tier 1 — explicitly framed as "Vosne value"

- **[[domaine_jean_chauvenet|Domaine Jean Chauvenet]]** — CSW headlined a 2025 dedicated
  article: *"Incredible Value in Vosne-Romanée with Domaine Jean Chauvenet."* The framing
  is literal: Chauvenet sits in NSG but the editorial pitch is "this is your Vosne
  bottle at NSG pricing." Two CSW dedicated articles (2017, 2025). Raeder's carries the
  2019 NSG villages. **This is the headline pick.**
- **[[chicotot|Chicotot]]** — DTE in depth: 20 cuvées spanning Aux Thorey (mid-Vosne-side
  1er), Pruliers, Vaucrains, Les Saint-Georges, Rue de Chaux, plus four village
  parcels (Allots, Saint Juliens, Charmottes, Plantes au Baron) and Côtes de Nuits-Villages
  Creux de Sobron. **Village NSG at $69, top 1er crus at $179.** William Kelley flagged
  the domaine on Berserkers (2021) as quietly serious — permaculture, no/minimal
  hedging, vineyard-driven. The best "buy across the lineup" play in the whole region.
- **[[domaine_forey|Domaine Forey]]** — CSW: *"great vineyards, not done in a popular
  style, ageworthy and well priced for the quality."* Régis Forey works Vosne and NSG
  side by side, including a piece of Clos Vougeot. Two CSW dedicated articles (2007,
  2025). Aging score 1 (the CSW shorthand for serious cellar candidates).

### Tier 2 — Vosne-shoulder NSG at fair (not cheap) prices

- **[[georges_noellat|Georges Noëllat]]** (DTE) — **NSG 1er Cru Boudots 2021 at $199.**
  Boudots is the closest NSG climat to Vosne; Noëllat's style is famously precocious
  (per William Kelley, "wines that will drink much earlier than e.g. Mugneret-Gibourg's,
  irrespective of appellation"). Boudots from a respected domaine under $200 is genuine
  value relative to a $349 VR-Beaumonts from the same producer.
- **[[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]]** (DTE) — **NSG villages Bas de Combe 2023 at
  $109.** Hudelot-Noëllat's village-level entry into the Vosne-shoulder slope.
  Berserkers reports the 2016s as "exquisite … why not."
- **[[gerard_mugneret|Gerard Mugneret]]** — CSW championed twice (2024, 2025).
  Vosne villages Précolombière / Quatrain on DTE at $129–$169. Organic from 2010,
  biodynamic-leaning. Not strictly NSG-adjacent but the cheapest *Vosne* villages on
  the shelf, which is the same arbitrage from the other direction.

### Tier 3 — aged depth on the shelf

- **[[patrice_rion|Patrice Rion]]** (DTE) — **Vougeot 1er Cru Les Cras 2013/2014 at
  $65.** Aged Vougeot 1er Cru under $70 is unusual. Not NSG-adjacent strictly, but
  represents the same "the slope keeps going" arbitrage on the *north* side.
- **[[meo_camuzet|Méo-Camuzet]]** (Raeder's) — **NSG Aux Murgers 2018 at $359.99 / 2020
  at $329.99.** The cheapest entry into Méo's domaine bottlings; Aux Murgers is on the
  Vosne shoulder. The Atlas's "long bottle-age" caveat applies — this needs cellar time.

### Already in the cellar from this arbitrage

- **Maison Pierre Brisset, NSG 1er Cru Aux Thorey 2023 — $94.99 × 2** *(drink 2028–2043).*
  Thorey is on the Vosne shoulder. Currency-good entry-point 1er Cru.
- **Maison Pierre Brisset, Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru Les Rouges du Dessus 2023 — $174.99 × 3**
  *(drink 2029–2046).* Rouges du Dessus sits at the Vosne–Flagey hinge; same idea on
  the north side.
- **Jacques-Frédéric Mugnier, NSG 1er Cru Clos de la Maréchale 2018 — $135 × 4**
  *(drink 2033–2065).* Prémeaux (south end) monopole — a *different* arbitrage: not
  Vosne character, but Mugnier-level winemaking at NSG pricing.

## Best value — North side (Flagey/Vougeot/Chambolle)

- **Vougeot 1er Cru** (not Clos) — **[[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]] Petits Vougeot
  2022/2023 at $159–$169** (DTE); **[[patrice_rion|Patrice Rion]] Les Cras 2013/2014 at
  $65** (DTE, aged). The Petits Vougeot parcel sits right against Musigny's southern
  edge — premium dirt, Vougeot prices.
- **Flagey-shoulder Vosne 1er crus** — **[[georges_noellat|Georges Noëllat]] VR 1er Cru
  Beaumonts 2021 at $349** (DTE); **[[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]] VR 1er Cru
  Suchots / Beaumonts 2023 at $299** (DTE). Beaumonts borders Échézeaux on the slope;
  Suchots borders Romanée-St-Vivant. Pre-grand-cru ambition at 1er Cru pricing.
- **Échézeaux from a serious house** — **[[meo_camuzet|Méo-Camuzet]] Echézeaux "Les
  Rouges du Bas" 2020** (Raeder's, WA 95, price not listed; check current). Upper-slope
  Échézeaux is the value tier within Vosne grand crus.

### Stretch picks (priced but landmark)

- **[[arnoux_lachaux|Arnoux Lachaux]]** — no longer value (DTE $299–$6,999) but
  documented for completeness; the trajectory illustrates what happens when a
  Vosne-shoulder domaine breaks into the cult tier.
- **[[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]] Clos de Vougeot Grand Cru 2022/2023 at $329**
  (DTE). Among the cheapest serious Clos Vougeots on the shelf; Hudelot's parcel is
  upper-slope (the "Musigny side" of the Clos).
