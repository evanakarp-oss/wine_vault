# Vintage Guide — Best Years by Region (Cooler-Classic Lens)

A working vintage chart for the regions in the cellar, tuned to **Evan's palate
preference for the less-ripe end of the spectrum** — tension, lift, transparency,
and lower alcohol over power, opulence, and high ripeness. This is strongest for
**Tuscany** (where Evan explicitly prefers cooler, leaner vintages), but it runs
across the whole vault as an overarching axis: *sense of place + tension* (same
phrase used in the Napa filter in `CLAUDE.md`).

Ratings here are **not** raw quality scores. A "skip-for-now / caution" flag on a
hot vintage doesn't mean the wines are bad — critics often score them highest. It
means *for this palate* you're more likely to find them too big, too alcoholic, or
too fruit-forward relative to a cooler year of the same region.

## Legend

| Tag | Meaning for Evan |
|---|---|
| ❄️ | **Cool / classic** — high acid, lower ripeness, structure-over-flesh. **Evan's sweet spot.** Chase these. |
| ⚖️ | **Balanced** — ripeness in check, freshness retained. Broadly safe; often the smart all-rounder buy. |
| ☀️ | **Warm / ripe** — bigger, richer, more fruit-forward. Buy selectively and by producer; many will read "too big." |
| ⚠️ | **Hot / atypical** — high-alcohol or overripe (or a hazard year: frost dilution, smoke). Generally skip for this palate. |

## Two principles before the charts

1. **Site structure compounds with vintage ripeness.** Evan's own calibration
   point: the **2008 Mugnier Clos de la Maréchale** read "a bit too big." 2008 red
   Burgundy is itself a *cool, high-acid* vintage — so the "big" sensation came from
   the **site**, not the year. Clos de la Maréchale (a NSG-Prémeaux monopole) is one
   of the broader-shouldered, more structured terroirs Mugnier farms. Takeaway: for
   inherently powerful sites (much of **Nuits-Saint-Georges**, **Pommard**,
   **Corton**, **Gevrey**), lean *even harder* toward the coolest vintages and the
   most lifted producers — or favor higher-toned communes (Chambolle, Volnay,
   Marsannay) outright. See [[jacques_frederic_mugnier]] · [[burgundy_communes]].
2. **In Napa and Mendoza, producer beats vintage.** A restrained grower (Dunn,
   Corison, Mayacamas, Montelena; Chacra in Patagonia) makes a leaner wine in a hot
   year than a hedonist producer makes in a cool one. Use the vintage tag to *amplify*
   the restrained house style, not to override producer choice.

## Tuscany — Sangiovese (Brunello, Chianti Classico, Vino Nobile)

> The region where Evan's preference is most explicit: **less ripe over more ripe.**
> Sangiovese keeps its acid spine and savory/sour-cherry transparency in cool years;
> in hot years it goes raisined, jammy, and high-alcohol. Bias hard toward ❄️/⚖️,
> and toward **Chianti Classico** over Brunello in warm years (higher elevation,
> fresher). See [[lowest_alcohol_tuscan_umbrian_veneto_reds_2026_05]].

| Vintage | Tag | Style |
|---|---|---|
| 2010 | ❄️⚖️ | Classic benchmark — structured, fresh, high-acid, long-lived. **Evan top.** |
| 2011 | ☀️ | Hot, ripe, broad. Caution. |
| 2012 | ☀️ | Warm; decent but riper. |
| 2013 | ❄️ | Cool, fine-boned, fresh, classic — especially Chianti Classico. **Evan sweet spot.** |
| 2014 | ❄️ | Cool/wet, lean, light; variable. The *leanest* year — CC outperformed Brunello. Evan-curious. |
| 2015 | ☀️ | Ripe, opulent, concentrated. Bigger — caution. |
| 2016 | ⚖️ | **The both/and vintage** — fresh + structured + balanced. **Evan top** (the safe great one). |
| 2017 | ⚠️ | Hot, drought-stressed, ripe. Generally skip for this palate. |
| 2018 | ❄️ | Cooler, fresher, lighter; classic Chianti profile. **Evan-friendly.** |
| 2019 | ⚖️ | Excellent, balanced, classic structure. **Evan-friendly.** |
| 2020 | ⚖️ | Fresh-ish, earlier-drinking, charming. |
| 2021 | ⚖️☀️ | Structured, ripe-but-balanced; promising, more grip than 2020. |
| 2022 | ☀️ | Hot year, but Chianti Classico shows grip/crunch that belies the heat — buy CC selectively. |

**Chase:** 2010, 2013, 2016, 2018, 2019 (lean toward Chianti Classico + high-elevation Brunello). **Skip-for-palate:** 2011, 2015, 2017.

| 2012 | ⚖️ | Ripe, balanced, charming, generous. |
| 2013 | ☀️ | Ripe, structured, powerful, ageworthy. Big. |

## Bordeaux

> Evan's filter: WK-style undercovered/value with great farming; aged classed-growth
> only for drink-now. The cooler "claret-style" vintages (lower alcohol, medium body)
> fit best — and they're usually the value years.

| Vintage | Tag | Style |
|---|---|---|
| 2010 | ☀️⚖️ | Powerful, tannic, structured, big — superb but built, needs decades. |

| 2015 | ☀️ | Ripe (Right Bank stronger). Bigger. |

| 2017 | ⚖️ | Lighter, fresher, earlier (frost-reduced). Value / drink-now. |

**Chase (value/claret):** 2014, 2016, 2017, 2019, 2021. **Caution:** 2010, 2018.

## Champagne

> Evan's filter: aged vintage cuvées, late-disgorged (P2/RD), or grower. The cool,
> high-acid declared years are exactly the tension-driven ones to cellar.

| Vintage | Tag | Style |
|---|---|---|
| 2002 | ⚖️ | Ripe-but-balanced classic; mature now. |
| 2008 | ❄️ | **Vintage of the century so far** — searing acid, tension, structure, long-lived. **Evan top.** |
| 2012 | ⚖️ | Concentrated + balanced; the next great one after '08. |
| 2013 | ❄️ | Cool, taut, high-tension; likely outlasts 2012. **Evan top.** |
| 2014 | ❄️ | Fresh, precise, traditional/classic style (not warmth-driven). **Evan-friendly.** |
| 2015 | ☀️ | Riper, rounder. Selective. |
| 2016 | ⚖️ | Moderate; uneven. |

**Chase:** 2008, 2013, 2014 (and 2002, plus classic high-acid back-vintages like 1996 for late-disgorged).

## Evan cheat sheet — the cooler-classic years to chase

| Region | Buy-first vintages | Approach with caution |
|---|---|---|
| **Burgundy red** | 2010, 2013, 2014, 2016, 2021 | 2018, 2020, 2015 |
| **Burgundy white** | 2014, 2017, 2021 | 2019 |
| **Tuscany** | 2010, 2013, 2016, 2018, 2019 | 2011, 2015, 2017 |
| **Napa Cab** | 2010, 2016, 2019 (2011 lean) | 2013, 2017, 2020 |
| **Piedmont** | 2010, 2013, 2016, 2018, 2021 | 2011, 2017 |
| **Bordeaux** | 2014, 2016, 2017, 2019, 2021 | 2010, 2018 |
| **Champagne** | 2008, 2013, 2014 (2002) | 2015 |
| **Germany** | 2010, 2013, 2016, 2021, 2023 | 2018 |
| **N. Rhône** | 2013, 2016, 2021 | 2019, 2015 |
| **Loire reds** | 2014, 2016, 2020, 2021 | 2018, 2022 |
| **Argentina** | 2016, 2019, 2021 | 2017, 2020 |

**The repeatable rule:** when in doubt between two vintages of a region, take the
**cooler, higher-acid, lower-alcohol** one — and for structurally big sites/producers,
take the cool vintage *and* a lifted, transparent grower.
