# Auction 261W Week-29 × Fass Aesthetic × Vault Importers (2026-07-13)

Follow-up lens on the [[auction_261W_week29_wheelhouse_2026_07|week-29 screen]]:
which Italy/France lots line up with the Fass Selections sensibility, and what
the vault's importer data says about the catalog.

## The headline: zero direct overlap, total stylistic overlap

**No producer from the ~123-name Fass book appears in the week-29 catalog.**
Fass sells the shadow-benchmark tier (Buffet, Rebourgeon, Defaix, Tribut,
Motalli, the Cornas micro-domains); auctions sell the benchmarks themselves.
The connection is tighter than it looks, though: a grep of the 181 Fass
articles in `raw/fass/markdown/` shows Lyle's own marketing copy constantly
prices his growers *against* names in this catalog — "taste blind against
Ar.Pe.Pe, **Burlotto**, Bartolo **Mascarello**"; "same sentence as **Giuseppe
Rinaldi**, **Cappellano**"; "in the same league as Mascarello, Burlotto,
**Giacosa**"; "think Chambolle-Musigny from a **Bertheau** or Mugnier";
"equal to **d'Angerville**, **Lafarge**". Week 29 is a chance to buy the
reference points at fair money instead of the proxies.

## Bottom line

Fass and this auction are complementary, not overlapping: Fass sells the
proxies at retail, the auction sells the originals at (sometimes) proxy money.
The three purest "Fass wines that Fass doesn't sell": **J. Sunier cru
Beaujolais at $15/btl**, **2003 d'Angerville Champans at $79** (the wine
Buffet is sold as equal to, mature, for Buffet money), and the **1975
Fonroque / Phélan Ségur / 1978 Chevalier claret at $35–50** (the Bel
Air-Marquis d'Aligre move). On the importer map, Kermit Lynch is the deepest
exposure (the new Méo-Camuzet NSG cases at $133–142 are the buy), and the
1974 Biondi Santi Greppo at $80 — surfaced only by this cross-reference —
is the single best new find of the pass.
