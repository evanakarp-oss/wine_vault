# Burgundy — Commune-by-Commune Field Guide

Grand Cru / Premier Cru / commune-level vineyards for every appellation in Burgundy that matters, with a curation-tuned verdict (**🔥 compelling** / **💰 value** / **👑 prestige** / **⏭ skip**) and Kelley-endorsement flags (**[K]** for William Kelley personal recommendation or Wine Advocate review, **[K-adj]** for shared-parcel / direct connection).

Skip ratings are about value-tier focus: a *skip* doesn't mean bad, it means you're paying for the name not the terroir relative to alternatives.

## TL;DR — Most compelling communes for Evan

In rough rank order, factoring curation taste (terroir-driven, biodynamic-leaning, grower-scale) × current pricing × climate trajectory:

| Rank | Commune | Why it's compelling now |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | **Marsannay** (Chenôve / Marsannay-la-Côte / Couchey) | 1er Cru promotion 2025/26 incoming for 12 climats — last pre-reclassification vintage on shelf |
| 2 | **Saint-Aubin** | Decanter calls it "Burgundy's best value village" — En Remilly + Murgers des Dents de Chien overlook Montrachet |
| 3 | **Auxey-Duresses + Saint-Romain** | Cooler-climate winners; CSW [[lafouge]] ★8, Kelley-pick Alain Gras |
| 4 | **Mercurey + Givry** | Côte Chalonnaise reds, real 1er Crus at sub-Côte d'Or pricing |
| 5 | **Chablis 1er Cru** | Montée de Tonnerre is a Grand Cru in everything but name; Vaillons, Fourchaume, Mont de Milieu close behind |
| 6 | **Pernand-Vergelesses + Ladoix** | Corton-Charlemagne adjacent whites without the Corton tax |
| 7 | **Chambolle-Musigny village + Cras/Fuées** | Les Amoureuses is Musigny-priced now, but the northern 1er Crus (Cras, Fuées) still in reach |
| 8 | **Viré-Clessé / Mâcon-Villages** | Lafon, Leflaive, Merlin pushed quality south; ~$30–40 white Burgundy with character |
| 9 | **Maranges** | Kelley's red Côte de Beaune value pick (Bachelet-Monnot) |
| 10 | **Nuits-Saint-Georges (south)** | Les Saint-Georges under Grand Cru evaluation; Chevillon at sub-Vosne prices |

## CÔTE DE NUITS

### Marsannay — see [[marsannay]]

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** none currently — **12 climats pending 1er Cru elevation (2025/26)** incl. Clos du Roy (Chenôve), Les Longeroies, La Charme aux Prêtres, En la Montagne, Es Chezots, Les Grasses Têtes, Au Champ Salomon, Champ-Perdrix, Clos de Jeu, Les Favières, Les Boivin, Les Saint-Jacques
- **Notable lieux-dits now:** Le Chapitre (Chenôve, promoted to Marsannay AOC in 2019; was reputed grand cru level in 16th–17th c.), Les Étales (whites), Les Finottes (Bart monopole)
- **Producers:** [[sylvain_pataille]] **[K]** · [[domaine_bart]] · [[charles_audoin]] **[K]** · [[jean_yves_bizot]] · [[bruno_clair]] **[K]** · [[rene_bouvier]] · [[regis_bouvier]] · [[charlopin_tissier]]
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — pre-promotion window closing

### Fixin

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** Clos de la Perrière, Clos du Chapitre, Les Hervelets, Les Arvelets, Clos Napoleon
- **Producers:** [[berthaut-gerbet]] **[K]** · Pierre Gelin **[K]** · [[amelie_berthaut]]
- **Verdict:** 💰 — true Côte de Nuits at sub-Gevrey prices

### Gevrey-Chambertin

- **Grand Cru:** **Chambertin** · **Chambertin-Clos de Bèze** · Charmes-Chambertin · Mazoyères-Chambertin · Latricières-Chambertin · Mazis-Chambertin · Ruchottes-Chambertin · Chapelle-Chambertin · Griotte-Chambertin
- **Premier Cru (top):** **Clos Saint-Jacques** (frequently above some Grand Crus), Les Cazetiers, Lavaux Saint-Jacques, Les Combottes, Aux Combottes, Estournelles Saint-Jacques, Petite Chapelle, Champonnet
- **Other lieux-dits:** Les Crais, Les Justices, Les Évocelles
- **Producers:** [[sylvie_esmonin]] (Clos Saint-Jacques) · [[domaine_trapet]] · [[domaine_duroche]] · [[domaine_joseph_roty]] · [[jane_et_sylvain]] · Domaine Bachelet · Rousseau · Fourrier · Dugat-Py · Dugat
- **Verdict:** 👑 (Grand Cru / Clos Saint-Jacques) / ⏭ (generic village — overpriced)

### Morey-Saint-Denis

- **Grand Cru:** Clos de la Roche · Clos Saint-Denis · Clos de Tart (Mommessin monopole) · Clos des Lambrays · Bonnes Mares (small Morey portion)
- **Premier Cru:** Les Ruchots, Les Genevrières, Clos des Ormes, Monts Luisants, Les Millandes, Les Sorbès, La Riotte
- **Producers:** [[magnien]] · [[stephane_magnien]] · [[lignier-michelot]] · Hubert Lignier · Ponsot · Dujac · Arlaud
- **Verdict:** 💰 — under-the-radar Morey village is the value play; Grand Crus prestige

### Chambolle-Musigny

- **Grand Cru:** **Musigny** · Bonnes Mares (shared with Morey)
- **Premier Cru (top):** **Les Amoureuses** (regularly priced above Musigny), Les Charmes, **Les Cras**, **Les Fuées**, Les Sentiers, Les Baudes, Les Borniques, Les Combottes, Les Beaux Bruns
- **Producers:** [[domaine_amiot-servelle]] · [[domaine_roumier]] **[K]** · [[ghislaine_barthod]] **[K-adj]** · [[jacques_frederic_mugnier]] **[K]** · [[cecile_tremblay]] · de Vogüé
- **Verdict:** 👑 (Musigny / Amoureuses) / 🔥 (Cras + Fuées — Atlas notes climate warming making these "more sought-after," still affordable)
- **Kelley note:** Roumier / Mugnier / Barthod are Kelley's stated trio for understanding Chambolle; he shares vineyard parcels with Roumier + Barthod

### Vougeot

- **Grand Cru:** **Clos de Vougeot** (50ha — quality varies wildly across producers)
- **Premier Cru:** Le Clos Blanc (rare white), Les Cras, La Vigne Blanche
- **Verdict:** ⏭ — Atlas note: "value varies as much as both style and quality." Buy by producer not appellation

### Vosne-Romanée + Flagey-Échezeaux

- **Grand Cru:** **Romanée-Conti** (DRC monopole, 1.8ha) · **La Tâche** (DRC monopole, ~6ha) · **Richebourg** · **La Romanée** (Comte Liger-Belair monopole, 0.85ha — smallest AOC in France) · Romanée-Saint-Vivant · Grands Échézeaux · Échézeaux
- **Premier Cru (top):** Aux Malconsorts, Les Suchots, Les Beaux Monts, Cros Parantoux (Méo-Camuzet / Henri Jayer historic), Les Brûlées, Aux Brûlées, Aux Reignots, Clos des Réas
- **Producers:** [[domaine_forey]] · [[cecile_tremblay]] · [[gerard_mugneret]] · [[meo_camuzet]] · [[arnoux_lachaux]] · [[georges_noellat]] · DRC · Liger-Belair · Leroy
- **Verdict:** 👑 — the most expensive commune in Burgundy. Skip village wines; if you swim here it's Grand Cru only

### Nuits-Saint-Georges

- **Grand Cru:** none (but **Les Saint-Georges under formal evaluation for Grand Cru elevation**)
- **Premier Cru "elite quartet"** (southern slope): **Les Saint-Georges**, Les Vaucrains, Les Cailles, Les Didiers (Hospices)
- **Premier Cru (next tier):** Les Pruliers, Les Roncières, Les Porets-Saint-Georges, Les Boudots (north), Aux Murgers, Les Damodes
- **Premeaux-Prissey monopoles:** Clos de l'Arlot, Clos de la Maréchale (Mugnier), Clos des Forêts Saint-Georges, Clos Saint-Georges, Clos des Argillières
- **Producers:** [[robert_chevillon]] **[K]** (Les Saint-Georges / Vaucrains / Cailles) · [[domaine_jean_chauvenet]] · [[domaine_lienhardt]] · [[jacques_frederic_mugnier]] **[K]** (Clos de la Maréchale) · Henri Gouges · Arlot · Faiveley
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Chevillon at sub-Vosne prices is one of the great deals in CdN; Les Saint-Georges promotion would reset the market

## CÔTE DE BEAUNE

### Ladoix

- **Grand Cru:** part of Corton (small portions of Corton-Bressandes, Le Rognet et Corton, etc.)
- **Premier Cru:** Le Clou d'Orge, La Corvée, La Micaude, Bois Roussot, Les Joyeuses, Hautes Mourottes, Basses Mourottes
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "refreshingly mineral whites and quite succulent reds." Some Premier Crus (Les Joyeuses, Sous Frétille, Les Gréchons) can be village-or-Premier-Cru declared

### Pernand-Vergelesses

- **Grand Cru:** part of Corton-Charlemagne · part of Corton
- **Premier Cru:** Île des Vergelesses (top), Les Vergelesses, Sous Frétille (whites), En Caradeux, Creux de la Net, Les Fichots
- **Producers:** [[rapet_pere_et_fils]] **[K]** · [[chandon_de_briailles]] · [[domaine_pavelot]]
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Kelley's pick for white; Atlas: "Corton-Charlemagne can occasionally rival Montrachet" — Pernand is the value cellar door

### Aloxe-Corton

- **Grand Cru:** **Corton** (red — huge, fragmented across many parcels: Le Corton, Bressandes, Clos du Roi, Renardes, Pougets, Maréchaudes, Perrières, etc.) · **Corton-Charlemagne** (white)
- **Premier Cru:** Les Maréchaudes, Les Valozières, Les Chaillots, Les Fournières, Clos du Chapitre
- **Verdict:** 👑 (Corton-Charlemagne) / 💰 (red Aloxe-Corton non-Grand Cru — Atlas: "fruity, often tannic… top reds should produce much simpler wine"). Buy by producer

### Chorey-lès-Beaune

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** none (all commune-level)
- **Notable lieux-dits:** Les Beaumonts, Les Crais, Pièce du Chapitre
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "lies on flat ground next to the main road, but is nonetheless a useful source of approachable red burgundy." Cheap entry to real Côte de Beaune fruit

### Savigny-lès-Beaune

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** Les Lavières, Les Dominode (top — east-side), Les Marconnets, Les Peuillets, Aux Vergelesses, Aux Guettes, La Dominaude, Aux Serpentières
- **Producers:** [[domaine_simon_bize]] **[K-adj]** (CSW ★10) · [[chandon_de_briailles]] · [[fanny_sabre]]
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "wines that are up to the highest Beaune standard, now fully reflected in their price"

### Beaune

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru (top):** Les Grèves, Bressandes, Les Fèves, Clos des Mouches (red + white), Vignes Franches, Les Teurons, Les Cent Vignes, Aux Cras, Marconnets, Boucherottes, Champs Pimont, Les Avaux, Les Toussaints
- **Hospices de Beaune** owns parts of many of the best Premier Crus
- **Producers:** [[jean-claude_rateau]] (biodynamic Beaune 1er Crus, CSW ★6) · Tollot-Beaut · Lafarge · Bouchard Père et Fils · Drouhin · Jadot · Louis Latour
- **Verdict:** 💰 (with Rateau, biodynamic at fair prices) / ⏭ (big négoce village-level)

### Pommard

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru (top):** **Les Rugiens-Bas** (often called the closest thing to a Pommard Grand Cru), **Les Épenots** (Petits + Grands), **Clos des Épeneaux** (Comte Armand monopole), Pézerolles, Les Jarollières, Clos de la Commaraine, Clos Blanc, Les Arvelets, Les Charmots
- **Producers:** [[comte_armand]] (Clos des Épeneaux monopole) · [[domaine_de_montille]] · [[de_courcel]] · Jean-Marc Boillot · [[joseph_voillot]]
- **Verdict:** 💰 (Rugiens-Bas + Clos des Épeneaux are real) / ⏭ (generic village Pommard — Atlas: "dark, hearty… different style entirely")

### Volnay

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru (top):** **Caillerets**, **Champans**, **Taillepieds**, **Clos des Chênes**, Santenots (Meursault-Santenots from Meursault side), **Clos des Ducs** (d'Angerville monopole), Bousse d'Or, Pitures, Mitans, Les Angles, Les Fremiets
- **Producers:** [[marquis_d_angerville]] · [[michel_lafarge]] · [[domaine_de_montille]] · Pousse d'Or · Comte Armand · Coche-Dury (Santenots) · [[domaine_des_comtes_lafon]] (Santenots)
- **Verdict:** 🔥 (Lafarge / d'Angerville / Montille at top Premier Crus — "elegant Côte de Beaune at fair-ish prices vs. Chambolle equivalents") / ⏭ (Volnay village)

### Monthélie

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** Sur la Velle, Les Champs Fulliot, Les Duresses, Les Vignes Rondes
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "Volnay's bargain alternative." Behind Auxey-Duresses on most lists but real terroir

### Auxey-Duresses

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** Les Duresses, Le Val, Climat du Val, Les Bretterins (la Chapelle), Clos du Val
- **Producers:** [[lafouge]] **[K]** (CSW ★8, La Chapelle 1er Cru) · [[domaine_chassorney]] (Frédéric Cossard)
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Kelley pick + CSW championed; cooler climate now an asset

### Saint-Romain

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** none (all village + lieux-dits)
- **Notable lieux-dits:** Sous Roche, Sous le Château, Combe Bazin, Le Jarron
- **Producers:** [[alain_gras]] **[K]** · [[domaine_de_chassorney]] (CSW ★5)
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Atlas: "Burgundy that has been experiencing convincingly white wines." Climate winner. Kelley names Alain Gras directly

### Meursault

- **Grand Cru:** none (despite quality reputation — long-standing curiosity)
- **Premier Cru (top):** **Les Perrières**, **Les Genevrières**, **Les Charmes**, Les Porusots, Goutte d'Or, Bouchères, Cras, Santenots (red), Le Poruzot
- **Other top lieux-dits (village but premium):** Les Narvaux, Tessons, Tillets, Rougeots, Casse Têtes
- **Producers:** [[domaine_des_comtes_lafon]] · Coche-Dury · [[robert_ampeau]] (CSW ★1) · Roulot · [[domaine_boisson]] · Pierre-Yves Colin-Morey
- **Verdict:** 👑 (top Premier Crus + Coche/Lafon/Roulot) / 💰 (Tessons / Narvaux from a real grower)

### Puligny-Montrachet

- **Grand Cru:** **Montrachet** (shared with Chassagne) · **Chevalier-Montrachet** · **Bâtard-Montrachet** (shared) · **Bienvenues-Bâtard-Montrachet**
- **Premier Cru (top):** Les Pucelles, Les Caillerets, Les Combettes, Les Folatières, Le Champ Canet, Les Referts, Les Perrières, Champ Gain, Clavoillon, La Truffière
- **Producers:** [[domaine_leflaive]] · Etienne Sauzet · Jean-Marc Boillot · [[pierre-yves_colin-morey]]
- **Verdict:** 👑 — peak white Burgundy pricing; Atlas: "the wines so greatly exceeds supply… reputation was severely tested"

### Chassagne-Montrachet

- **Grand Cru:** Montrachet (shared) · Bâtard-Montrachet (shared) · **Criots-Bâtard-Montrachet**
- **Premier Cru (top):** Les Caillerets, Les Ruchottes, Les Vergers, Morgeot, Les Embrazées, Les Grandes Ruchottes, La Maltroie, Champs Gain, Clos Saint-Jean, La Boudriotte, Les Chaumées, En Cailleret
- **Notable:** Atlas: "fell in love with Chardonnay" only in 20th century — historically red. Some terroirs still red (Morgeot et al)
- **Producers:** [[ramonet]] · [[simon_colin]] · [[pierre-yves_colin-morey]]
- **Verdict:** 👑 (whites) / 💰 (reds — Atlas: "tasting more like a rustic Gevrey")

### Saint-Aubin

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru (top):** **En Remilly** (on Puligny/Chassagne border, similar soils to Montrachet), **Les Murgers des Dents de Chien** (overlooks Le Montrachet directly), Chatenière, Les Frionnes, Sur le Sentier du Clou, Sur Gamay, Le Charmois
- **Producers:** [[domaine_larue]] (Kermit Lynch) · [[pierre_girardin]] · Hubert Lamy · Marc Colin · Jean-Claude Bachelet · [[domaine_de_montille]]
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Decanter: "Is this Burgundy's best value village?" Murgers + En Remilly are Grand-Cru-soils-without-Grand-Cru-prices

### Santenay

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** **Clos de Tavannes**, **La Comme**, **Les Gravières**, Beauregard, Beaurepaire, La Maladière, Clos Rousseau, Grand Clos Rousseau, Passetemps, Clos Faubard
- **Producers:** [[domaine_david_moreau]] (CSW ★4)
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "These red wines are naturally tough, tasting more like a rustic Gevrey"

### Maranges

- **Grand Cru:** none
- **Premier Cru:** Clos des Loyères, Clos des Rois, La Fussière, Le Clos des Rois, Les Clos Roussots
- **Producers:** [[bachelet_monnot]] **[K]** (Kelley pick for red Maranges)
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Kelley's stated value pick for Côte de Beaune reds

## CÔTE CHALONNAISE

### Bouzeron

- **AOC:** village-only, **Aligoté monopoly AOC** (only single-village Aligoté AOC in Burgundy)
- **Producers:** [[de_villaine]] (A. & P. de Villaine — Aubert de Villaine of DRC + wife) · Chanzy
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Atlas: "reward for the perfectionism of Domaine A and P de Villaine, perhaps." Real Aligoté terroir from the DRC co-owner

### Rully

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** ~23 climats — Vauvry, Grésigny, Margotés, Préau, Raclot, Les Cloux, Mont-Palais, La Pucelle, Marissou
- **Producers:** [[dureuil_janthial]] **[K]** (Kelley reviewed his 1985 Puligny 1er Cru as "taut, concentrated, full-bodied with lovely extract")
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "Rully makes more white wine than red, brisk, relatively high in acidity; ideal material for Crémant de Bourgogne, and in the era of warmer summers a source of appetizing white burgundy at exceptional value"

### Mercurey

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** ~32 climats — **Clos du Roi**, Clos des Barraults, Les Crêts, Les Champs Martins, Clos l'Évêque, La Mission, Les Velley, Les Combins, Clos Tonnerre, Les Naugues, Clos des Myglands
- **Producers:** [[bruno_lorenzon]] (Les Champs Martins — 3 btl in cellar) · [[francois_raquillet]] **[K]** · [[michel_juillot]] · [[domaine_faiveley]] · Chanzy · Antonin Guyon
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Kelley pick (Raquillet); Atlas: "important Premier Cru… number in Mercurey jumped in the 1980s to 32"

### Givry

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** ~27 climats — **Clos Salomon** (monopole), Cellier aux Moines, Clos du Cras Long, Clos Jus, Petit Marole, Clos Marceaux, Les Bois Chevaux, Clos de la Servoisine, En Choué
- **Producers:** [[clos_salomon]] · [[domaine_joblot]] · [[francois_lumpp]] · Domaine Ragot · René Bourgeon · Gérard Parize
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Atlas notes Givry's 27 villages "many of them" with strong Premier Crus

### Montagny

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** entire commune used to be 1er Cru–eligible (since 1991 only specific climats)
- **Producers:** [[stephane_aladame]] · Jean Vachet · Cave de Buxy
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "all-white appellation, includes neighbouring Buxy, whose co-operative is probably the most successful in southern Burgundy"

## MÂCONNAIS

### Pouilly-Fuissé

- **Grand Cru:** none · **Premier Cru:** **22 climats elevated to 1er Cru status starting with the 2020 vintage** (first 1er Crus ever in the Mâconnais) — Au Vignerais, Les Brûlés, Les Chevrières, Les Ménétrières, Les Reisses, Le Clos de Solutré, Les Vignes Blanches, etc.
- **Producers:** [[domaine_cheveau]] · Château Fuissé · Domaine Robert-Denogent · Domaine Saumaize-Michelin · Olivier Merlin
- **Verdict:** 💰 — recent 1er Cru reclassification still being priced in

### Pouilly-Vinzelles / Pouilly-Loché

- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "theoretical alternatives to the real thing, but are in short supply"

### Saint-Véran

- **Notable:** straddles Mâconnais / Beaujolais geology
- **Producers:** Domaine de la Croix-Senaillet, Robert-Denogent, Olivier Merlin
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "St-Véran tends to be drier and leaner than Pouilly-Fuissé"

### Viré-Clessé

- **Producers:** [[domaine_guillemot-michel]] (CSW ★7, biodynamic) · Domaine de la Bongran (Thévenet)
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Atlas: "lucky village with the name of the grape" (Chardonnay village just north); Viré-Clessé is the prestige Mâconnais village

### Mâcon-Villages (Lugny, Uchizy, Chardonnay, Loché, etc.)

- **Producers:** [[domaine_des_comtes_lafon]] (Mâcon-Milly-Lamartine, Mâcon-Uchizy) · [[domaine_leflaive]] (Mâcon-Verzé) · [[olivier_merlin]] · Dom Nicolas Mallet · Dom Frantz Chagnoleau · [[guillot_broux]] (biodynamic)
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Atlas explicit: Lafon + Leflaive moved south, "considerable proportion of the white wine made in the Mâconnais is sold, quite legally, as Bourgogne Blanc"

## CHABLIS

### Chablis Grand Cru

- **Climats:** Les Clos · Vaudésir · Valmur · Grenouilles · Les Preuses · Blanchot · Bougros
- **Producers:** Raveneau · Dauvissat · Fèvre · Drouhin Vaudon · [[duplessis_chablis]]
- **Verdict:** 🔥 (Raveneau / Dauvissat at allocation prices) / 👑 (open-market)

### Chablis Premier Cru

- **Top tier:** **Montée de Tonnerre** (regularly Grand Cru quality — "first to be elevated"), Fourchaume, Vaillons, Mont de Milieu, Côte de Léchet, Les Forêts, Beauroy, Vau de Vey, Vaillon (sub-climat of Vaillons)
- **Verdict:** 🔥 — Atlas: "Premier Cru ladder still cheaper than Côte d'Or village whites of similar quality." Montée de Tonnerre is the must-buy

### Petit Chablis / Chablis village

- **Verdict:** 💰 (good grower) / ⏭ (big négoce)

### Saint-Bris

- **Grape:** Sauvignon Blanc (unique to Burgundy)
- **Producers:** [[goisot]] (CSW seed in vault)
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "unusual for this part of France, has its own appellation"

### Irancy

- **Grape:** Pinot Noir + small César allowed
- **Verdict:** 💰 — Atlas: "Pinot Noir to make light red burgundy" — climate winner
