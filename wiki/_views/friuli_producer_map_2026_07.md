Until this pass the vault had **zero Friuli producer pages** despite "Italian
Friuli" being named in the cellar style — the cellar owned 25 Friuli bottles
(Ronchi di Cialla, Le Due Terre, Radikon, Dario Princic, Marco Sara) with no wiki

2025-06 Radikon feature, the 2016 aged-Collio piece) seeds the pages it covers.

## How to read Friuli (one paragraph)

Four terroirs matter: **Collio** (ponca marl hills on the Slovenian border —
structured whites), **Colli Orientali** (same marl, more native reds:
Schioppettino, Pignolo, Refosco), **Isonzo** (gravel plain — dense
single-site whites), **Carso** (karst limestone above Trieste — saline Vitovska,
Terrano). Two stylistic schools cut across them: the **classic school** (unoaked or
subtly oaked varietal whites) and the **Oslavia/Carso maceration school** (orange
wine: Gravner, Radikon, La Castellada, Dario Princic, Damijan, Zidarich). Both can
be great; the skip tier is the flat-land commercial middle.
