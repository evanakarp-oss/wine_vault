# Rugged Cool-Climate Mountain Cabernet — Off-the-Radar Hunt (2026-06)

A discovery pass for genuinely under-the-radar, restrained, farming-driven mountain
Cabernet — the rugged/site-transparent end Evan flipped the Napa filter toward on
[2026-05-29](../log.md). Seeded by the question "what Bedrock uses for their Cabernet
vineyards, like Lazy Dog." The premise needed two corrections (below), but the chase
landed on a much better answer than Lazy Dog: the **Montecillo Vineyard cluster** on
Moon Mountain, plus a tier of obscure mountain estates.

## Premise corrections (verified, multi-source)

1. **Lazy Dog is NOT a mountain vineyard.** It's a cool-climate **valley-floor** site on
   the lower west side of Sonoma Valley (Carriger Rd & Grove St; one source places it
   nearer the Carneros/Sonoma Coast border). Planted 2002, 5 acres Cab (clone 337) +
   2.5 acres Malbec, **drip-irrigated (not dry-farmed)**, farmed organically-then-
   sustainably by Steve Waymire & Kate Farrell. Bedrock does **not** make a standalone
   "Lazy Dog" Cab — it's a *blend component* (the ~6% Malbec + some Cab) in their ~$45
   **Sonoma County Cabernet** (92pt Galloni '23). So Lazy Dog gives Bedrock's entry Cab
   *coolness/freshness*, not *mountain ruggedness*. ([sonomavalleywine.com](https://sonomavalleywine.com/growers/lazy-dog-vineyard/), [bedrockwines.com](https://www.bedrockwines.com/bedrock-cabernet-sauvignon))
2. **The mountain/rugged signal in Bedrock's Cab program is Montecillo and Monte Rosso**
   (Moon Mountain), not Lazy Dog. That's the real thread to pull.
3. Bonus corrections surfaced: **Stellwagen** is a Zinfandel/mixed-blacks site, not Cab;
   **Sandlands** (Tegan Passalacqua) makes **no Cabernet** at all — his Cab work is via
   his day job at **Turley**, which bottles Montecillo; **Diamond Creek "Gravelly Meadow"**
   is dry-farmed and *restrained*, not opulent — it belongs on the buy list, not the skip list.

## Tier 0 — The actual Bedrock-spirit answer: the Montecillo cluster

**Montecillo Vineyard** (Moon Mountain District AVA, Sonoma) is the bullseye. Top of
Nelligan Rd on the ridgeline dividing Napa from the Valley of the Moon; **~1,700–2,000 ft**
(sources vary across the slope), **dry-farmed**, iron-rich red volcanic clay loam, Cabernet
**planted 1964** — among the oldest Cab in California. The remarkable thing: a whole cluster
of the restrained "New California" names all work this single rugged old site, which makes it
a perfect comparative study.

**Monte Rosso** (Moon Mountain, the other historic site): Louis Martini planted Cabernet
here in **1940** on bright red iron-rich volcanic soil, ~690–1,300 ft; those vines still
produce and are among California's oldest Cab. **Scribe** bottles a Monte Rosso Cab; Louis
M. Martini and various négociants too. ([Historic Vineyard Society](https://historicvineyardsociety.org/vineyard/monte-rosso))

**New AVA to know: Pine Mountain–Cloverdale Peak** (Mendocino/Sonoma line, 1,600–2,600 ft,
some blocks to ~3,000 ft) — highest-elevation appellation in the Mayacamas, above the fog,
~80% Cabernet. The literal answer to "highest, coolest, most rugged Cab outside Napa."
