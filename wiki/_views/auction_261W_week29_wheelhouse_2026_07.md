# Auction 261W — Week 29 Wheelhouse (2026-07-13)

One week after the [[auction_261W_week28_wheelhouse_2026_07|Week-28 screen]].
Lot numbers are stable; of the 1,443 distinct lots, **730 are new**, 713 carried
over, and 925 dropped off Week 28. Most of the Week-28 heroes (1996 Jamet, the
Châteauneuf block, the 2005 Gouges/Chevillon consignment, the Montelena vertical)
have sold. The new material is one giant 28xxx consignment block, and it is the
most on-taste single consignment these sales have carried yet.

Four things define this week: (1) a deep **grower Red-Burgundy consignment** —
Rousseau, Dujac, Mugneret-Gibourg, Bertheau, Duroché, H. Lignier, Roty, Grivot,
Cacheux, plus 2018 d'Angerville and 2019 Lafarge; (2) a **2019 Raveneau /
Roulot / Ramonet white-Burgundy block**; (3) a **late-disgorged Champagne
cluster** (DP P2 '96/'00, Krug '04, Cédric Bouchard) — dead-center on the P2/RD
filter; and (4) an **aged old-guard Napa consignment** (Heitz Martha's '69–'77,
BV Private Reserve '69/'74, Mondavi '69/'74, Phelps '74/'76, Inglenook '73,
Monte Bello '74) — exactly the mature pre-cult classics flagged in the
2026-07-06 taste note. Estimates are the house low–high; per-bottle math on the
low. Locations: **DE** (Delaware) or **HK** (Hong Kong — shipping friction).

## Bottom line

The most wheelhouse-dense 261W week yet, on the strength of one consignment.
**Highest-conviction value:** the aged old-guard Napa singles — 1974 Mondavi and
1974 BV Private Reserve at ~$90–120 and 1973 Inglenook at $75 are the taste-note
thesis (mature, structured, undervalued) in its purest form — plus the F. Rinaldi
Brunate/Cannubi cases at $47–53 and 2007 Prüm Spätlese cases at $18–50. **Best
depth plays:** the Cacheux Vosne cases ($63–142), the Roty Charmes TVV mags
($217), and the M. Noellat aged Clos Vougeot cases ($108–150) — terroir Burgundy
with age for less than current-release money. **Dead-center Champagne:** DP P2
1996/2000 and Cédric Bouchard, the P2/grower filter made flesh. Extends vault
producers Roty, Bertheau, Duroché, Bart, Levet, Burlotto, G. Conterno, Niellon,
Ramonet, Chandon de Briailles, Heresztyn, Fonroque, Phélan Ségur, Dönnhoff — and
the cellar's Rousseau Clos de la Roche 2017 gets a 2016 sibling option (28191).
