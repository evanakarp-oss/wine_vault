# Vosne-Styled NSG — Deep Dive on Producers & Parcels

Companion to [[vosne_adjacent_parcels|Vosne-Romanée Adjacent Parcels]]. That
view answers the geography question ("what is on either side of Vosne?").
This view answers the **stylistic** question Kelley framed in 2021:

## The canonical Kelley framework

From [Wine's Naturalistic Fallacy](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/wines-naturalistic-fallacy/168700/60),
post #60, 2021-02-24 (William Kelley, Wine Advocate):

> *"Picking those three producers was slightly tricky of me, because to my
> mind **the Mugneret sisters make Vosne-styled Nuits** and **Mugnier makes
> Chambolle-styled Nuits**, whereas **Chevillon … makes the most obviously
> 'Nuits' Nuits** (after all, they are based there and even buy their
> barrels from Sirugue, a cooper actually based in Nuits). Mugnier's
> Maréchale is, to me, a great example, because it is both obviously
> different from his Chambolle wines and still very obviously a Mugnier
> wine."*

And the parallel post from [the NSG Grand Cru thread](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/is-nuits-les-saint-georges-worthy-of-grand-cru-status/164465/23)
(post #23, 2020-10-04):

> *"I have had bottles of '30s Nuits from Thomas Bassot, 1940s Gouges,
> magical Chevillon old and young, **Lalou's Boudots**, and even, frankly,
> some vintages of the **Mugneret sisters' Chaignots** (try the 1999 blind
> next to DRC's 1999 Echézeaux — not necessarily a foregone conclusion)
> that would call that assertion into question."*

The named Vosne-styled NSG references in Kelley's own writing:

- **Domaine Leroy / Lalou Bize-Leroy — NSG 1er Cru Aux Boudots**
- **Mugneret-Gibourg — NSG 1er Cru Les Chaignots** (and by extension Vignes Rondes)
- ***Implicit anti-reference:*** Mugnier's **Clos de la Maréchale** = Chambolle-styled
  (already in the cellar — 4 × 2018, drink 2033–2065)

## What makes an NSG "Vosne-styled" — two axes

Kelley's framing collapses two things that are often treated separately:

### Axis 1 — site (where on the slope)

Vosne sits between two NSG halves. The **northern** NSG climats (above the
town, against the Vosne border) sit on the *same slope* as Vosne — clay-marl
mid-slope, well-drained limestone above, on a continuous south-east aspect.
These are the **Vosne-shoulder climats**:

| Rank | Climat | Touches |
|---|---|---|
| ★★★★★ | **Aux Boudots** | Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru Aux Malconsorts |
| ★★★★ | **Les Damodes** | Boudots (upper continuation) |
| ★★★★ | **Aux Cras** | Mid-slope above Boudots |
| ★★★ | **Aux Murgers** | "the stony one"; firmer than Boudots |
| ★★★ | **Aux Thorey** | Upper slope above shoulder |
| ★★★ | **Aux Chaignots** | South of Murgers, mid-weight |
| ★★★ | **Les Vignes Rondes** | Adjoins Chaignots (the climat WK cited via Mugneret-Gibourg) |
| ★★ | **La Richemone** | Tiny, above-slope |
| ★★ | **Aux Bousselots** | North-side, slightly heavier |

The **southern** NSG climats (below the town: Vaucrains, Les Saint-Georges,
Cailles, Pruliers, Roncières, Perrières, Rue de Chaux) are a different
terroir — firmer, more iron-rich, structurally tannic. These are what
Kelley calls "'Nuits' Nuits" wines: Chevillon, Gouges, Faiveley's Saint-Georges.

The **Prémeaux** climats (further south of town: Clos de la Maréchale, Clos de
l'Arlot, Clos des Forêts, Clos des Corvées, Clos des Argillières) are a
third terroir again — Kelley's "Chambolle-styled Nuits" reference set.
Mugnier's Maréchale is the textbook example: floral, lifted, lower-toned.

### Axis 2 — winemaking signature (who's making it)

A Vosne-shoulder parcel can be made *not* in the Vosne idiom. Chevillon's
Chaignots is on Vosne-shoulder dirt but his cellar (hot ferments, some stems,
in-house Sirugue barrels) imprints a recognizable Nuits-savoury signature
that Kelley specifically identifies as **not** Vosne-styled. Conversely, an
allocation-tier Vosne house buying NSG fruit and applying the same massale-
selection + clean élevage + perfumed extraction they use on Vosne 1er Cru
will deliver a wine that reads as Vosne even from non-shoulder fruit.

**The Vosne-house signature** ≈ near-total destem, clean ~50% new oak from
the Vosne-favored cooperages (François Frères, Rousseau, Damy), patient
late-malo élevage, no rack-and-return extraction, expression of perfume
over structure. Late releases. Polished but not slick.

**Both axes aligned = textbook Vosne-styled NSG.** This is the target tier.

These three are the **shape** Kelley is pointing at. Buying any of them at
retail in the US is functionally impossible without a relationship; the
practical exercise is to find Tier A wines that replicate the shape.

### Brisset's full Vosne-adjacent range

Beyond the NSG Aux Thorey, Brisset is FASS's most complete coverage of
the Vosne-adjacent geography:

- **NSG Aux Thorey** (Vosne shoulder) — Vosne-styled NSG ✅ owned
- **Vosne 1er Cru Rouges du Dessus** (Vosne / Flagey hinge) ✅ owned
- **Chambolle 1er Cru Les Noirots** (Chambolle adjacent) ✅ owned
- **Échézeaux Grand Cru** (Flagey / Vosne Grand Cru) — not owned, the
  natural next step within the Brisset house signature

If the goal is *"the Brisset Vosne-adjacent set"*, the Échézeaux is the
missing piece — and per Lyle Fass it's the apex of the entire FASS
Burgundy range (*"1 and 1A with Poisot RSV"*). Flagship pricing /
allocation only.

**The structural insight:** at FASS, the Vosne-styled-NSG question
collapses to a single buy — **Brisset Aux Thorey** — and the user has
already executed it. The FASS contribution to the analysis is not "more
Vosne-styled NSG options" but rather:

1. **Validation** that the existing Brisset purchase is the right
   FASS-side answer.
2. **A natural south-side counterpart** in Vincent Ledy Porets-Saint-Georges
   if the user wants to do the three-styles tasting flight entirely
   within FASS allocations. The **Ledy JV at $79.99** makes this flight
   affordable.
3. **A Grand Cru calibration anchor** in Poisot RSV at FASS-direct
   pricing — useful because it sets the "what Vosne-styled actually
   means" reference at a price-point the broader market makes
   inaccessible.
4. **A Brisset Échézeaux target** as the next-tier purchase for someone
   already deep in Brisset's CdN range.

Lyle Fass himself explicitly nests Brisset and Ledy together — in the
Ledy 2021 Porets-Saint-Georges post, he writes: *"Easily the best NSG
I've had at Fass. Easily on the same level as Brisset."* Two different
WK-styles, both at the top of the FASS NSG roster.

**Pedigree signal on Ledy:** per Lyle's 2020-release post, Ledy is now
imported by the same US importer as Domaine Leroy / Lalou Bize-Leroy —
the same importer Kelley flagged for the legendary Lalou Aux Boudots.
That's a meaningful peer-validation signal even though Ledy stylistically
sits in the opposite ("Nuits Nuits") corner of the WK framework.

### Concrete acquisition plan

1. **Anchor buy — [[georges_noellat|Georges Noëllat]] NSG 1er Cru Aux
   Boudots 2021, DTE, $199.** Single best available proxy for "Lalou's
   Boudots." Buy 2–3. Drink 2030+.

2. **Per-dollar play — [[maxime_cheurlin|Maxime Cheurlin]] NSG 1er Cru
   Les Damodes 2020 *or* 2021, DTE, $149.** Same hand as #1, adjacent
   climat, $50 cheaper. Pair vertically with the Boudots from #1 for the
   in-house comparison.

3. **WK's literal climat — [[maxime_cheurlin|Maxime Cheurlin]] NSG 1er
   Cru Les Vignes Rondes 2021, DTE, $149.** Same Vignes Rondes parcel WK
   cited via Mugneret-Gibourg, from a Vosne house, under $150. The
   "buying-by-quote" move.

4. **South-side reference — Chevillon NSG 1er Cru Vaucrains or Les
   Saint-Georges**, *vintage TBD from market query*. Not currently in the
   vault's retailer data but widely available in NYC (Astor, Crush, K&L
   import lists). Aim for $120–180 a bottle. This completes the WK
   triangle: Vosne-styled (#1–3), Chambolle-styled (Mugnier owned),
   Nuits-styled (Chevillon).

5. **Drink-soon tier — [[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]] NSG villages
   Bas de Combes 2023, DTE, $109.** Vosne-house village-level Vosne-
   shoulder. Drinks 2027–2032. The "open this on a Tuesday" tier.

6. **Stretch — [[meo_camuzet|Méo-Camuzet]] NSG 1er Cru Aux Murgers 2020,
   Raeder's, $329.** Only at $329 (not the 2018 at $359). Murgers
   bridges Boudots and Chaignots geographically; Méo is the polish-tier
   modern-Vosne reference. Drink 2032–2045.
