# Côtes du Vivarais & Southern Ardèche — Terroir Neighbors That Haven't Blown Up

**Seed:** [[domaine_gallety|Domaine Gallety]] (added 2026-06-19 from the Kermit
Lynch PDF). Gallety is the benchmark of the **Côtes du Vivarais** — a small, cool,
**limestone** AOC (status only since 1999) on the **west bank of the southern
Rhône**, in the **Ardèche**, geographically between Cornas and Châteauneuf. The
prompt: who else farms this same terroir but is still flying under the radar?

## The terroir thesis

What makes Gallety work is portable to its neighbors:

- **West-bank, higher-altitude limestone** in the southern Rhône → freshness and
  tension that the valley-floor Grenache zones across the river lack.
- **Southern-Rhône grapes in a cool register** — Grenache turns *elegant*, Syrah
  stays *savory/peppery* rather than jammy.
- **Obscurity = value.** "Ardèche" still reads as bulk IGP country to most buyers,
  so quality here sells on the wine, not on hype.

Three concentric circles of neighbors, from same-appellation to same-idea:

1. **Inside Côtes du Vivarais** (literal neighbors, same AOC).
2. **Southern-Ardèche west bank** (Côtes du Rhône / IGP Ardèche, same limestone belt).
3. **Brézème** — the *east*-bank (Drôme) limestone-Syrah outpost that is the
   structural mirror image, already half-validated by [[eric_texier|Eric Texier]].

## The cohort that HAS been discovered (contrast, so we don't double-count)

Southern Ardèche around **Valvignères / Alba-la-Romaine** is the cradle of a famous
**natural-wine** scene. These share the *place* but **not** the "hasn't blown up"
brief — they're allocation-tight cult names:

- **Le Mazel** (Gérald Oustric, Valvignères) — natural-wine landmark.
- **Andrea Calek** (Alba-la-Romaine) — sought-after.
- **Gilles & Antonin Azzoni**, **Ozil / Domaine de la Grange** circle — established natural cred.

Use these as a reference for the *style ceiling* of the zone; skip them for the
"under-the-radar value" mandate.
