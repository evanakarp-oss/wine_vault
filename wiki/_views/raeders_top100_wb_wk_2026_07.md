# Raeders Top 100 — Berserkers × Kelley × Taste (2026-07)

The buy list: every bottle below is **in the Raeders 2026-04-25 snapshot with a
listed price**, its producer carries a **Berserkers community signal** (rank +
mentions in the *Top 10 producers in your cellar* megathread) and/or a
**William Kelley signal** (his WB post count on the producer), and it passes
the curation taste filters — aged classed-growth Bordeaux for drink-now,
vintage/grower Champagne, old-guard/mountain Napa, terroir growers everywhere
else. Vintage-graded against [[vintage_guide|the cooler-classic vintage lens]].

**Legend:** `WB #r·Nmn` = megathread rank · mentions (name variants split
mentions, so treat as a floor). `WK n` = Kelley's Berserkers post count on the
producer. `WA/JS` = retailer-listed critic scores from the snapshot (not
necessarily Kelley's own review). ❄️/⚖️/☀️/⚠️ = vintage tag from
[[vintage_guide]] (❄️ cool-classic chase · ⚖️ balanced · ☀️ ripe, by producer ·
⚠️ hot/hazard). Prices are the 2026-04-25 snapshot — verify stock before
buying; bottles ≥25 years old warrant a fill/provenance check.

## Caveats

- **Snapshot risk**: prices and stock are from 2026-04-25; Raeders churns.
- **WB mentions are a floor**: the megathread splits producers across name
  variants ("Mugnier" / "JF Mugnier" / "Freddy Mugnier"), so cited mentions
  undercount true consensus.
- **WK post count ≠ endorsement**: it measures Kelley's engagement with the
  producer on Berserkers, not a score. Actual Kelley reviews live in
  `raw/clippings/wine_advocate/` (still sparse).
- **Vintage tags are the Evan lens, not quality scores**: a ☀️ kept on a
  producer/site override (Ravera '15, Pajé '17, Batailley '82) is a conscious
  trade, flagged inline. See [[vintage_guide]] for the full doctrine.
- **Old bottles**: everything pre-1990 (esp. #43–45, #48, #51, #52) should get
  a fill-level and label-condition check before purchase.
- **Cellar overlap not filtered**: this is a buy list, not a gap list —
  [[roagna|Roagna]] (#5–7) is already in the cellar and included deliberately
  as a vertical play.
