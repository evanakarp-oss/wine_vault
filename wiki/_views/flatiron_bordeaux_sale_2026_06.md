# Flatiron Wines — Bordeaux Sale Top Picks (2026-06)

Flatiron's 84-bottle Bordeaux Sale, screened through Evan's Bordeaux filter:
**WK-style undercovered/value with great farming; aged classed-growth only for
drink-now; skip generic mid-tier.** The list splits three ways — a cheap
cru-bourgeois Médoc floor (mostly skip), a strong **value/farming seam** in
the $20–45 band, and an **aged classed-growth drink-now** tier from ~$80 up.
Below is what actually fits the brief.

## Tier 1 — Buy (farming-driven value, best fit)

| Wine | Appell. | Vint. | Price | Why it fits |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| [[chateau_la_grolet|Château la Grolet]] | Bourg (Côtes de Bordeaux) | 2022 | $22.99 | **Bullseye.** Hubert family, **certified organic + biodynamic since the 1990s**, CSW-championed (6 articles, cellar pick). Already a tracked producer in the vault. The single most on-taste bottle on the list, and the cheapest of the keepers. *(WK: no mention.)* |
| [[chateau_cantemerle|Château Cantemerle]] | Haut-Médoc | 2019 | $39.99 | **WK-endorsed value.** Homogeneous Mindel-gravel vineyard, high-density planting, gentle winemaking, modest oak — WK: *"When this estate gets everything right it's terrific value, and they are increasingly getting everything right."* 2019 is one of his polished, early-drinking value vintages. Tracked in vault. |
| **Château Pontet-Canet** *(gap — no vault page)* | Pauillac | 2007 | $129.99 | **Biodynamic classed-growth** Pauillac at 19 yrs — aged, drink-now, with the farming story. **Vintage matters here:** WK rates Pontet-Canet *through 2011* and singles out that it "excelled in challenging years such as 2007," but is openly critical of the **2012-onward** biodynamic/amphora era (mildew signatures, drying finishes). The **2007 on sale is on the right side of that line.** |

## Tier 2 — Strong consider (Denis Durantou satellites — WK-style value, GAP)

The late Denis Durantou (Château L'Église-Clinet) made the canonical
"undercovered Right-Bank value" wines. None are in the vault — clean gap
candidates, all in the value band:

| Wine | Appell. | Vint. | Price |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Saintayme, Saint-Émilion Grand Cru | Saint-Émilion GC | 2021 | $26.99 |
| La Chenade, Lalande-de-Pomerol | Lalande-de-Pomerol | 2021 | $34.99 |
| Château Les Cruzelles (Durantou) | Lalande-de-Pomerol | 2022 | $44.99 |

Merlot-led, polished, transparent — Durantou's hallmark. Saintayme at $27 is
the value play; Les Cruzelles is the step-up. (Note: *Le Carillon de Rouget*
on the same list is **not** Durantou — it's Château Rouget's second wine.)
**WK overlay:** he knew Durantou personally, ranks L'Église-Clinet near the top
of Pomerol, and esteems the house — though he hasn't reviewed these satellites
by name on the forum. The house pedigree is the endorsement.

## Tier 3 — Aged classed-growth, drink-now (Evan's "drink-now only" rule)

These pass *only* because they're mature and ready. Skip the young classed
growths on the list (the 2016/2018/2020s) — those don't fit the drink-now rule
and aren't value.

|---|---|---|---:|---|

Tracked in vault. The most WK-aligned bottle in this tier. |
| Château Giscours | Margaux | 2001 | $139.99 | 3rd growth, 25 yrs — squarely drink-now. **WK caveat:** his enthusiasm is for *recent* Giscours ("their best wines since 1982" — the post-revival vintages); 2001 predates that. Fine as a mature drinker, but it's not the vintage WK is excited about. |
| Château du Tertre | Margaux | 2010 | $109.99 | 5th growth; 2010 structured, now drinking (2012 also listed, $79.99, readier). WK positive on du Tertre (a 2019 polished early-drinker; loves the 1979). |
| Château Potensac | Médoc | 2005 | $89.99 | Delon-owned (Léoville-Las-Cases stable — Evan owns the '98 Las Cases). Cru bourgeois, but a 21-yr Delon Médoc is legit aged value. *(WK: no mention.)* |
| [[domaine_de_chevalier|Domaine de Chevalier]] | Pessac-Léognan | 2015 | $109.99 | Classed growth, tracked in vault. **WK caveat:** he's cool on the oaky **mid-2000s** DDC (2004–2010 "leaves lovers of traditional claret cold") but says recent vintages recapture the DNA — 2015 sits on the better side of that shift. Longest-haul of this tier. |

## Skip / caution

- **Generic mid-tier Médoc floor** ($12–25 cru bourgeois: Noaillac, de Panigon,
  Poitevin, Laujac, Roc Taillade, Larrivaux, Bel Orme, etc.) — no farming story,
  exactly the "generic mid-tier" the filter says to skip. **La Grolet is the one
  exception** in this band because of the biodynamics.
- **Cult/trophy tier** — Lafleur 2022 ($1,199), Haut-Brion 2021 ($579),
  Trotanoy 2021 ($399), La Mission 2021 ($329), La Conseillante 2021 ($239),
  Carruades 2017 ($249). Not value, not the brief.
- **Already owned / wrong vintage per WK:** [[chateau_gruaud_larose|Château
  Gruaud-Larose]] 2005 ($159.99) is in the cellar (6 btl) — and note WK's
  enthusiasm is for the **post-2019** organic-conversion Gruaud ("transformed"),
  not the 2005/2016 on sale. [[chateau_leoville_barton|Léoville Barton]] 2009
  ($159.99): WK loves Barton but targets 1985/1988/2000, not the ripe 2009.
  [[chateau_rauzan_segla|Rauzan-Ségla]] 2017 ($119.99): WK's standout is the
  **2020** ("best since the 1950s"), not the 2017. All three: pass.

## One-line answer

If buying one: **Château la Grolet 2022 ($22.99)** — biodynamic, CSW-championed,
in the vault, and dead-center of Evan's taste. **WK's two best-aligned bottles
on this list: Haut-Bailly 2007 ($99.99)** ("buy with confidence") **and
Cantemerle 2019 ($39.99)** (his "terrific value" estate). Best *new* discovery:
the **Durantou trio** (Saintayme / La Chenade / Les Cruzelles). And the
**Pontet-Canet 2007** is the rare bottle where the *specific vintage* on sale is
the one WK still rates — buy that vintage, not the post-2012 ones.
