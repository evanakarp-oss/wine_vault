# WK Price ↔ Quality Dislocation Board (2026-07)

Every wine where **William Kelley's expressed pleasure and the market price
visibly diverge**, ranked by the size of the gap and how explicit his words
are. Grounded strictly in his Berserkers corpus
(`raw/berserkers/William_Kelley/markdown/`, verbatim cites inline) — the Wine
Advocate clipping ingest (`raw/clippings/wine_advocate/`) is still empty, so
this is forum prose, not scored reviews. Directional, but in his own words.

His framing sentence, from the Vosne "zip code" thread that anchors the whole
board: glamour 1ers are "**priced more by zip code than by what's in the
bottle**" (`137497`, 2017; reaffirmed 2024: vineyard-work differences
"the zip code can't efface," `176407__0258`). Dislocation runs both ways.

---

## A. Quality ≫ price — the market underpays (ranked)

### 1. [[domaine_joseph_roty|Joseph Roty]] — Marsannay line-up, and the grand crus vs Rousseau

The single largest gap in the corpus. "For those interested in value — **the
Marsannay line-up of Roty is fabulous**… Roty's Mazis and Charmes are **a step
up over Rousseau's relatively weak renditions**" (`137528`, 2017); "with
bottle age, I would rank the domaine's three grand crus among the best wines
produced in Gevrey" (2017-11). Rousseau's Charmes/Mazis trade at many
multiples of Roty's. Entry point: **$49 at DTE** for the one listed cuvée.

### 2. Chassagne-Montrachet **Rouge** — a whole appellation mispriced

His most emphatic category call — thread titled *"The last great vestige of
value in Red Burgundy"* (`330056`, Jan 2025): **J-C Bachelet**, **Paul Pillot
(Thierry)**, the **Cardeuse** bottling (Moreau brothers); Morey-Coffinet and
F & L Pillot to watch. Plus [[ramonet|Ramonet]]: "their reds can still [be]
amazing values and I look out for old vintages" (`160002__0029`), and "**crazy
how undervalued the Ramonet 1er Crus were** in the market until a year or two
ago" (`146614__0008`). Anchors in the vault: Ramonet Chassagne Rouge 2021
**$120 at Raeders**; Ch. de la Maltroye Chassagne 1er Clos St-Jean Rouge 2013
**$70 aged** — 🍷 *owned* ([[2013_chateau_de_la_maltroye_chassagne_montrachet_1er_cru_clos_st_jean_rouge|cellar]]).

### 3. [[jacques_frederic_mugnier|J-F Mugnier]] — NSG 1er Clos de la Maréchale

The most explicit single-wine statement in the corpus: "This cuvée is really
good, and actually quite readily available. **It's amazing that it isn't more
expensive.** I bought 2015 in magnums" (`153663__0006`, 2019). A
Musigny-grower's 1er at a fraction of the Chambolle range. **$230 at Raeders
(2022)**; 🍷 *owned: 2018*.

### 4. [[charles_audoin|Charles / Cyril Audoin]] — Marsannay

2019 Marsannays "**no less fresh and dynamic than Jeremy Seysses' wines at
Domaine Dujac**" (2020-12) — a Dujac comparison at **$45–65 (DTE, 19
cuvées)**, roughly a fifth of Dujac village pricing. His standing Marsannay
recommendation (2019, 2022 Q&A). *Zero bottles owned — the clearest actionable gap.*

### 5. [[thomas_et_jean_marc_bouley|Bouley]] — Pommard 1er Fremiers

Name-recognition mispricing inside one range: "Rugiens and Caillerets get all
the love due to name recognition, whereas **Fremiers is the better wine**…
his Fremiers is 'more Rugiens' than his Rugiens" (`173245`, 2021). Fremiers
made his hypothetical "dozen wines for life" (2022). DTE carries the range
$55–249.

### 6. Dureuil-Janthial — Rully 1er Meix Cadot

"**If this came from Puligny-Montrachet, imagine the price tag**"
(`157011__0019`) — the whole dislocation thesis in one sentence. He ranks
Dureuil-Janthial with Lorenzon atop the Chalonnaise. *No producer page — gap.*

### 7. Richoux — Irancy

"**Cheaper than most Bourgogne Rouge from serious domaines on the Côte d'Or
and better than most of them**" (`157011__0019`). *No producer page — gap.*

### 8. [[jean_javillier|Jean Javillier]] — Meursault ($26–79 DTE)

"Now that is a producer I **badly wanted to keep to myself**!" (2021) —
old-school Meursault at Bourgogne-blanc money. Highest QPR-per-enthusiasm in
the DTE eval.

### 9. [[georges_glantenay|Thierry Glantenay]] — Volnay (from $33 DTE)

"Rising star… wines not to miss — Caillerets, Clos des Chênes, Santenots…
**not well-known and very reasonably priced**" (2017). Top climats at
village-adjacent prices.

### 10. One-line dislocations from his own drinking lists (`157011__0019`, `163231`)

- **Pepière, Muscadet Briords** — "reliably one of the best values in French white wine."
- **Château des Tours Côtes du Rhône** (cool vintages) — "Reynaud magic for a modest tariff, but how long will that last?"
- **Chermette Beaujolais-Villages Cœur de Vendanges** — "routinely great value, **better than a lot of cru Beaujolais**."
- **Kalin Cellars Sonoma Pinot (2000, their last)** — "tastes like serious Burgundy and still can be had for a two-figure sum."
- **Aged NV Champagne** — a case of 1970s Delamotte NV "more exciting than plenty of much more expensive vintage Champagnes" (`163231__0022`). Matches the aged/late-disgorged Champagne taste filter.

### 11. Savigny-lès-Beaune vs Volnay — the neighbor discount

"If you drink old bottles of **Bize, Guillemot or Chandon de Briailles**…
there is no doubting one can make exquisite wines" (`330056` post 64). Both
[[domaine_simon_bize|Bize]] and [[chandon_de_briailles|Chandon de Briailles]]
are in the vault, biodynamic, dead-center Evan's taste.

### 12. Blanket region calls (`117826__0034`)

Cru Beaujolais (**Bouland**, [[clos_de_la_roilette|Clos de la Roilette]]),
[[domaine_levet|Levet]] Côte-Rôtie, Muscadet, "pretty much everyone" in the
Loire except [[clos_rougeard|Clos Rougeard]]/Joly, topped-up Jura Chardonnay
([[jean_francois_ganevat|Ganevat]], [[stephane_tissot|Tissot]] — "still a lot
cheaper than Côte d'Or alternatives"), Raveneau/Dauvissat at allocation,
**Pepe and Valentini** in Abruzzo, and "**pretty much any top German wine**."

### 13. Contrarian Bordeaux — off-vintages from great terroirs

Against the crowd calling them overpriced: "**2011 VCC (half the price of the
2015… a genuinely great wine)** and some **2011 Château Latour (cheaper than
2013, 2004, 2007 etc Latour and better)**" (`174071__0009`). Also defends
well-stored 1982s — disappointing bottles "have travelled too much"
(`161887__0050`); his cold-cellar 1982 Ducru "a decade from full maturity."

## B. Price ≫ quality — the market overpays

1. **Glamour Vosne 1ers, esp. Les Suchots** — "priced more by zip code than by
   what's in the bottle"; [[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]] Suchots at the
   same price as the "much superior" Clos Vougeot (`137497`). Worst case:
   [[michel_noellat|Michel Noëllat]], a largest Suchots holder — "a pity they
   don't do a better job" (2017), "some work to do in the vineyards" (2022).
2. **Rousseau's Mazis & Charmes** — "relatively weak renditions" (`137528`) at
   trophy prices; the flip side of the Roty call above.
3. **[[arnoux_lachaux|Arnoux-Lachaux]]** — WK rates the wines (98 pts on the
   2019 RSV) but at $299–6,999 his own zip-code logic argues against buying.
   Quality real; price fully fashionable.

## C. Dislocations that already closed (buy the lesson, not the wine)

- **Mugneret-Gibourg NSG Chaignots** — "one of the few remaining great values
  in pedigreed Côte de Nuits red Burgundy" (`163772__0008`, 2020) — now ~$375;
  mostly priced in.
- **Jean-Marc Vincent (Santenay)** — "the wines were underpriced for many
  years… now valorized at their proper level" (`176513__0061`,
  `308324__0091`). WK himself says the gap closed.
- **Ramonet 1er Cru whites** — undervalued "until a year or two ago"
  (`146614__0008`); the *reds* remain the live play (§A.2).
- His macro warning (`166582__0104/0114`): un-globalized Burgundy names —
  especially **top red Côte de Beaune** — will re-rate as new markets arrive.
  That is exactly the window for Bouley / Glantenay / Audoin / Chassagne Rouge.
