# Burgundy Gap Analysis — What Importers Are We Still Missing?

After cross-checking 10 importer portfolios against Raeder's, **~81 of the 198 Burgundy/Chablis/Beaujolais bottles at Raeder's remain unattributed** — i.e., the producer doesn't appear on any of the pasted portfolios. Grouping the unattributed producers by their most-likely US importer surfaces **5 importers worth pasting next** to close the gap.

## Priority pastes (by bottle count + value)

### 1. Maisons Marques & Domaines (MMD) — Roederer Group  →  ~18 bottles

MMD is the US arm of the Roederer family group and handles their Burgundy network: Drouhin, Faiveley, Laroche, William Fèvre, Henriot. Many of Raeder's highest-end Burgundies pass through here.

- **Joseph Drouhin** *(3 bottles incl. Chambolle Amoureuses 2022 $1,199.99, Chassagne Morgeot Laguiche 2022 $269.99, Echezeaux GC 2022 $599.99, Corton GC 2022 $339.99, Puligny Folatières/Pucelles 2022)*
- **Domaine Faiveley + Faiveley négoce** *(5 bottles incl. Gevrey VV 2023 $199.99, Chambolle 1er Aux Beaux Bruns 2022 $229.99, Meursault 2022 $229.99)*
- **A.F. Gros + Michel Gros** *(6 bottles incl. Echezeaux GC 2022 $699.99, Aloxe-Corton 1er Valozières, Vosne-Romanée 1er Clos des Réas 2022)*
- **Domaine Laroche** *(3 Chablis bottles)*
- **William Fèvre** *(Chablis Bougros 2006 $199.99)*

### 2. Kobrand  →  ~14 bottles

Kobrand handles the two large Burgundy houses Louis Jadot and Louis Latour, plus parts of Champagne (Taittinger).

- **Louis Jadot** *(8 bottles — Beaujolais-Villages NV through 1er Cru up to $250)*
- **Louis Latour** *(6 bottles — Chassagne 2024 through Premier/Grand Crus up to $105)*

### 3. Vineyard Brands  →  ~11 bottles (highest single bottle in the entire unidentified set)

The big one. Ramonet is the single largest gap.

- **Ramonet** *(9 bottles — **Bâtard-Montrachet Grand Cru 2020 $1,299.99**, plus Bourgogne Aligoté and other village/1er Cru wines)*
- **Henri Boillot** *(Meursault Les Genevrières 1er Cru 2023 $319.99 — distinct from Lucien Boillot who is on KL)*
- **Camille Giroud** *(Morey-St-Denis 1er Cru Clos des Godelles 2019 $99.99)*

### 4. Becky Wasserman / Le Serbet  →  ~9 bottles

Specialty Burgundy importer, longtime advocate of small grower-domaines.

- **Domaine Dugat-Py** *(3 bottles — Gevrey VV 2022, Hautes-Côtes Blanc 2022 $99.99, up to $210)*
- **Geantet-Pansiot** *(3 bottles — Chambolle 1er Les Baudes 2022 $199.99, Feusselotte 2022 $219.99, Gevrey VV 2023)*
- **Michel Niellon** *(Chassagne Les Chaumées 2022 $239.99)*
- **Bruno Lorenzon** *(Mercurey Le Chapitre 2022 $79.99; Montagny 1er Cru Choix du Roi Blanc 2022 $99.99)*

### 5. Wilson Daniels  →  ~3 bottles (but cult-tier)

Wilson Daniels traditionally handles Burgundy royalty.

- **Domaine Comte Georges de Vogüé** *(Bonnes-Mares Grand Cru 2016 — $799.99)*
- **Ponsot** *(Morey-St-Denis Cuvée des Alouettes 2016 — $279.99)*
- **Dujac** *(Chambolle-Musigny 2023 — $259.99)*

Pasting Wilson Daniels would likely also resolve the **Méo-Camuzet** conflict (currently KL + Wildman flagged) and the **Roulot** conflict (currently KL + GCS flagged) — Wilson Daniels has historically handled both.
