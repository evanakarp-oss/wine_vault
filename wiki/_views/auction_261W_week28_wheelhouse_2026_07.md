# Auction 261W — Week 28 Wheelhouse (2026-07-06)

A fresh catalog two weeks after the [[auction_261W_week26_wheelhouse_2026_06|Week-26 screen]].
Lot numbers are **stable** (same number = same physical wine); the catalog
**turned over ~64%** — of the 1,638 distinct lots, **1,050 are new** to Week 28,
588 carried over, and 1,181 dropped off Week 26. Most of the Week-26 hero lots
(Jamet '95/'96/'98, the 2007 Prüm/Zilliken/Schaefer/Dönnhoff cases, Ridge Monte
Bello '92, Dunn '09, La Jota '92) have **sold**, so this is a fresh screen with
**NEW** marking lots absent from the prior file.

What's distinctive this week: three deep, fresh consignments land squarely in
the wheelhouse — a **Châteauneuf vertical block** (Beaucastel + P. Usseglio Mon
Aieul + Vieux Télégraphe La Crau), a **2005/2007 German Riesling block** (Dönnhoff,
F. Haag, J.J. Prüm, Christoffel — mature, $16–50/btl), and a **2005 mature-Burgundy
grower consignment** (H. Gouges, R. Chevillon, Comte Lafon, Fèvre Chablis). Plus
the on-taste reference California returns as a **Montelena Cabernet vertical**
(not the cult tier). Estimates are the house low–high; per-bottle math shown.
Locations: **DE** (Delaware) or **HK** (Hong Kong — adds shipping/consolidation
friction).

## Skip (off-taste)

  (Sine Qua Non — 18 lots at $150–733/btl; Harlan, Hundred Acre, Quintessa, Opus,
  Peter Michael, Hourglass, Rudd) plus SQN-adjacent Rhône projects (Next of Kyn 44
  lots, Pax 32, The Third Twin 25). Off the site-transparent axis. On-taste here is
  the reference set + aged old-guard classics in Tier 2 (Montelena/Dunn/Dominus/
  Diamond Creek/Ridge/Mayacamas/Togni/Spottswoode + mature Beringer PR/Mondavi
  Reserve/Sterling/Flora Springs).
- **Trophy Red Burgundy** (424 lots): Dujac Bonnes-Mares ($8K–11K), Roumier Amoureuses,
  Fourrier VV — great estates but priced as trophies, not value. Fourrier village-level
  ('06 Champeaux/Cherbaudes 26425/27, $220–300) is the only near-approachable tranche.
- **Red Bordeaux** (205 lots): classed-growth trophy (Haut-Brion 1952 $1.4K–1.75K/btl);
  aged drink-now bottles only per the filter, not the WK-value profile.
- **Australia / Spain trophies, Sine Qua Non** — outside stated interests.

## Bottom line

A wheelhouse-heavy catalog, like Week 26 but with fresh consignments. **Highest-conviction
value:** the 1996 Jamet Côte-Rôtie at $40–55, the 1996 S. Grasso Barolo at $37–50, the
2005/2007 Dönnhoff/Prüm/F. Haag Mosel-Nahe cases at $16–50/btl, and the P. Usseglio /
Vieux Télégraphe / R. Usseglio Châteauneuf block at $25–100/btl. **Best depth play:**
the 2005 mature-Burgundy grower consignment (H. Gouges, R. Chevillon, Comte Lafon
Santenots at $75–167/btl) — terroir Burgundy at 20 years for less than current-release
village pricing. **Rarest on-taste opportunity:** the Montelena Cabernet vertical and
2005 Dunn — the restrained, site-transparent California that these sales almost never
carry, standing in for the cult tier you skip. Extends producers you already own
(Ganevat, Burlotto) or hold pages for (Jamet, Allemand, Dönnhoff, Clos Rougeard,
Pierre Péters, Agrapart, Leflaive).
