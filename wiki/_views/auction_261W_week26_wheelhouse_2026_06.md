# Auction Catalog 261W (Week 26) — Wheelhouse (2026-06)

Screened all **2,233 lots** (1,955 Delaware / 278 Hong Kong) against Evan's
taste filters. Unlike the 265DE trophy-Burgundy catalog, this one is **deep in
the wheelhouse**: a serious German Riesling block (52 lots), grower/natural
Northern Rhône, classic Piedmont with real value, a Loire/Jura natural-wine
trove (France Misc. 65 lots), and — rare for these sales — the **on-taste
reference-set California** (Dunn, La Jota, Ridge Monte Bello, Dalla Valle)
rather than only the cult tier. Many picks are producers already in the
cellar or vault. Per-bottle math shown; HK lots add shipping/consolidation
friction.

## Tier 3 — grower Champagne + white Burgundy growers

**Grower Champagne** (dead-center filter): Jérôme Prévost La Closerie (23727/28
$320–400; 25328/26180 Esperluette $190–240), Cédric Bouchard Roses de Jeanne
(25543/26062–66, $180–300), Ulysse Collin Les Maillons (25812/26074, $300–400),
Dhondt-Grellet Le Bateau VV (25811, $150–200), Chartogne-Taillet Les Barres
(25977, $85–110), Claude Cazals Clos Cazals BdB (25810, $75–100), and aged
single-site **Philipponnat Clos des Goisses 2011** (24057–62, $300–400 — six
singles). Discovery names from the vinolist grower-Champagne vein.

**White Burgundy growers** (right taste, trophy-to-fair pricing): Roulot,
Raveneau, Comtes Lafon, Leflaive, Dauvissat, H. Lamy, PYCM, Coche-Dury,
Guffens-Heynen, V. Dancer, Bernard-Bonin. Chase the village/Bourgogne-level
lots and Chablis for value.

## Skip (off-taste)

- **Most of California** (619 lots across Cab/Red/White): the bulk is mid-tier
  and cult/hedonist (Screaming Eagle, Opus, Caymus, Hall, Silver Oak, Jones
  Family, Promontory-style). Only the reference set above (Dunn, La Jota, Ridge,
  Dalla Valle) is on-taste. **Dalla Valle Maya** (25636, $750–1000) is the
  opulent cuvée — skip vs. the estate Cab.
- **Washington/Oregon** (mostly off-axis), **Penfolds Grange**, **BK Wines
  (Austria)**, Spanish trophies — outside stated interests. *Exception:* Cayuse
  Syrah (WA) is biodynamic and arguably on-taste if you want to stretch.
- **Red Bordeaux** (245 lots): classed-growth trophy; aged drink-now bottles
  only per the filter — not the value/undercovered profile.

## Bottom line

This catalog is the inverse of 265DE — the wheelhouse is broad and well-priced,
not just a thin grower spine. **Highest-conviction value:** Jamet mature
Côte-Rôtie at $40–66, the 2007 J.J. Prüm / Zilliken / Schaefer / Dönnhoff Mosel
cases at $10–40/btl, F. Rinaldi & Conti Costanti Piedmont at <$60/btl, and the
Gonon/Allemand/Roagna/Burlotto/Ganevat lots that extend producers you already
own. **Rarest opportunity:** the on-taste reference-set California (1992 Ridge
Monte Bello, 2009 Dunn, 1992 La Jota) — these almost never show up, and the cult
tier you skip is what usually fills the CA section.
