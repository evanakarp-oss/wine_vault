# Auction 261W — Week 30 Wheelhouse (2026-07-21)

One week after the [[auction_261W_week29_wheelhouse_2026_07|Week-29 screen]]. Of
1,734 distinct lots, **1,078 are new**, 656 carried over, and 787 dropped off
Week 29 — a ~62% refresh. Most of the Week-29 heroes (the Rousseau/Dujac/Cacheux
Burgundy block, the DP P2 '96/'00 cluster, the Heitz/BV/Mondavi old-guard Napa
consignment) have sold; the new material comes in as several large single-owner
consignments, and collectively it is even more on-taste than Week 29.

**Seven consignments define the week:** (1) a deep **grower-Champagne block** —
Cédric Bouchard, Jacques + Guillaume Selosse, Jérôme Prévost, Ulysse Collin,
David Leclapart, Pierre Péters, Tarlant, plus the new-wave farmer-fizz
(Gaspard/Jules Brochet, Chaillon, Nakada-Park) and aged late-disgorged Charles
Heidsieck / Deutz / DP P2 — dead-center on the P2-RD-grower filter; (2) a
**grower-Loire block** — [[boulay|G. Boulay]], Huet, Dagueneau Silex, Vatan,
Richard Leroy, Bernaudeau, Ch. des Tours; (3) **traditional-Piedmont depth** —
[[cavallotto|Cavallotto]], G. Mascarello Monprivato, [[giacomo_conterno|G.
Conterno]], [[burlotto|Burlotto]], [[roagna|Roagna]], B. Giacosa, F./G. Rinaldi;
(4) **aged Dunn Howell Mountain verticals** + pre-cult Napa classics (Inglenook
'60/'67, Stag's Leap SLV '75, Phelps Insignia, [[philip_togni|Philip Togni]]);
(5) an **Anthill Farms + Matthiasson California** consignment — cool-climate
Sonoma Pinot by the case and farming-first Napa field blends; (6) a **terroir
red-Burgundy grower run** — R. Chevillon, S. Bize, [[meo_camuzet|J. Grivot]]; and
(7) **aged drink-now Bordeaux** — [[chateau_fonroque|Fonroque]] '75,
[[chateau_phelan_segur|Phélan]] '75, Monbrison '76, and a first-growth 1982/'86
vertical wall. Estimates are the house low–high; per-bottle math on the low.
Locations: **DE** (Delaware), **HK** (Hong Kong — shipping friction), **LN**
(London).

## Bottom line

The most on-taste 261W week to date, and unusually **case-deep**. Highest-conviction
value: the **2020 Cavallotto Bricco Boschis OCB at $50/btl** (traditional Barolo,
whole case), the **aged Dunn Cabernets at $100–180** (the mountain-Cab thesis at
30 yrs), **G. Boulay Sancerre at $21–30**, and the **2007 Prüm WS Spätlese case at
$26**. Two **exact cellar siblings** to grab: **Domaine Garon Côte-Rôtie Les
Triotes 2016** (you own the '20, $60) and **Guastaferro Taurasi Primum 2019** (you
