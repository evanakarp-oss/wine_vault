# William Kelley — What He Says He Likes

WK = **William Kelley**, Deputy Editor at *The Wine Advocate* (reviews Burgundy,
Bordeaux, Champagne, Madeira, English sparkling). The vault has no WA score
clippings — but it does have **4,727 of his own WineBerserkers posts**, where he
argues his palate at length and in his own words. This page distills them.

## His palate in one paragraph

Classically balanced wines built on **tension, freshness, finesse and real
structure**. He prizes acidity, low pH, "chiseled" precision and chalky
grip/dry extract; he's cool on soft, "consensual," overripe, oxidative, pruney
or high-alcohol wines (he treats excess alcohol as a *defect*, even in famous
bottles). **Farming is his signature obsession** — viticulture is "the missing
link" between terroir and winemaking; he credits low yields, canopy work, cover
crops and precise picking for quality, and judges organic/biodynamic by results,
not certificates. He rejects the lazy "modern vs. traditional" binary: he
welcomes modern technique (better oak integration, less-oxidative élevage,
elimination of brett) *in service of* a classical aesthetic. He's patient and
maturity-loving ("I'd rather open a ready lesser wine than an unready great
one"), grounds vintage judgment in hard chemistry (pH, potential alcohol, dry
extract), and backs his palate by buying what he praises.

## Aesthetic — what he values

- **Tension / freshness / dry extract over ripeness & oak.** *"while I love the
  racy acid-driven tension and aromatic range of cooler Burgundy vintages … I
  cut my teeth on very classic stuff: old Bordeaux and old Mosel … [a few years
  of] 'cult' California and the contemporary southern Rhône … led me back to
  where I had started, which was very classically balanced, mature wines."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/william-kelley/162610/204))
- **"Minerality" = high dry extract / skin structure**, not a vague descriptor.
  *"the rare counterexamples where the wines contain appreciable extract from the
  skins, lending them real physical structure and chalky grip … what is often
  described as 'minerality' … is actually frequently what we perceive when
  tasting a wine with high levels of extract."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/its-critic-bingo-energetic-vertical-explosive-beams-of-tannin/132031/1337))
- **Low pH must come from farming, not just early picking.** *"The keys to
  maintaining low pHs are in farming techniques on the one hand, and pressing on
  the other, not simply by picking early."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/tns-mostly-white-burgundies/299867/9))
- **Overripeness/oxidation = flaw**, even at the top. *"I concluded the wine
  [1990 La Chapelle] was overripe and getting more pruney and oxidative … the
  last great La Chapelle is actually the '88."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/topic/108169/59))
- **Farming is the missing link.** *"a proper emphasis on viticulture is the
  missing link … between talk about 'terroir' and the details of winemaking.
  Looking at a cadastral map … all one would learn is that Darviot-Perrin is
  'next to Coche'. But evidently there is more to it than that."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/topic/161110/69))
- **Organic/biodynamic: judged by results, not certificates.** *"there are very
  good producers who are not certified organic … just as there are some pretty
  bad farmers who are nonetheless certified organic."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/bettane-on-burgundy-from-1970-to-today/325343/3))
- **"Modern vs traditional" is a false binary.** Modern methods (oak
  integration, less-oxidative élevage, brett elimination) are welcome *in service
  of* a classical profile.
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/oh-the-irony-i-just-subscribed-to-wine-advocate-and-heres-why/179185/107))
- **Maturity over hierarchy.** *"I would rather open e.g. a 2000 Beaux Bruns
  today than a 2010 Cras, even if the Cras is the 'better' wine."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/ranking-barthod/170830/7))
- **Puts his money where his mouth is.** *"I buy, within the limits of my means,
  the wines I praise … 'I own 10 cases from this producer' is a much warmer
  endorsement than any score."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/william-kelley/162610/204))

**Models he holds up** (terroir-driven, low-yield growers): Leroy/d'Auvenay,
Dugat-Py, Claude Dugat, Barthod, Thomas Bouley, the Bachelet brothers, Guffens,
Vincent Dancer, Bizot. **Cool on:** Lamarche ("attenuated, diffuse and drying"),
Le Pin (lacks Pomerol "tension, precision and slight austerity"), and the
chunky/over-extracted early-2000s "old-fashioned" style as a mild pejorative.

## Bordeaux worldview

- **Big thesis — "contemporary classicism":** today's Bordeaux is the **best
  since the early 1960s**, after the post-1956-frost replanting, 1970s nadir, and
  the over-ripe/over-oaked "garage" peak of ~2000–2009; course-correction from
  ~2010 (canopy work, more Cabernet, parcel picking, gentler extraction, less
  oxidative élevage, brett elimination).
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/figeac-vertical-and-wondering-about-bordeaux-critics/299653/29))
- **The point of his coverage = value beyond the famous labels.** Publicly: he
  hopes to "interest readers who have given up on Bordeaux … the region's
  excitement isn't limited to a few dozen famous labels … critics ought to …
  identify unheralded and underappreciated producers."
  ([Wine Journal](https://winejournal.robertparker.com/reviewer-favorites-2022-william-kelley-france-bordeaux))
- **"Back to their DNA" estates he rates as overdelivering now:** Canon 2020
  ("best since the early 1960s"), **Rauzan-Ségla 2020** ("best since the 1950s"),
  **Giscours** ("best since 1982"), Troplong Mondot ("180-degree course
  correction"), **Gruaud-Larose** (post-2019 organic + agroforestry, "just as
  radical").
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/new-william-kelley-mega-article-on-bordeaux-today/298476/56))
- **Well-farmed cru bourgeois = real value** "when they get everything right"
  (Cantemerle, Sociando-Mallet, Les Ormes-de-Pez).
- **Vintages:** 2014 strong N. Médoc / weak Right Bank; 2019 high quality with
  polished tannins; 2021 a low-alcohol "classicist's" vintage he's excited about;
  2005 "hard to say it wasn't overrated." Loves old value years: 1985, 1988, 1981.
- **On being banned (Pontet-Canet won't be reviewed by him):** *"for anyone who
  takes the profession of critic seriously in Bordeaux, being banned by one or
  two wineries is a very good sign."*
  ([url](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/bordeaux-2022/299143/328))

## Net takeaway for Evan's cellar

WK's filter and Evan's curation taste (CLAUDE.md) are **closely aligned** — both
prize farming-driven, terroir-transparent wines with tension over ripe/oaky
"opulence." Where they differ: WK is more bullish on *contemporary* Bordeaux
(the post-2010 classicism revival) than the "aged classed-growth drink-now only"
rule implies — so his enthusiasm often points at *recent* vintages of revived
estates (Giscours, Gruaud-Larose, Rauzan-Ségla 2019/2020+), not the older
bottles. See [[flatiron_bordeaux_sale_2026_06]] for the WK overlay applied to a
live sale list.
