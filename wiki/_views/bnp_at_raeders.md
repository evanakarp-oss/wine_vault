## Conflicts and resolutions

- **Biondi-Santi**: BNP lists Biondi-Santi (1 bottle in inventory), and Wildman's cross-check earlier counted Biondi-Santi bottles via the Santi → Biondi-Santi token chain (which I'd already marked as a false positive). Biondi-Santi's US importer is more likely BNP (or another house) than Wildman's "Santi" Valpolicella producer. The Biondi-Santi bottles at Raeder's (incl. Brunello Riserva 2016 $899.99) are likely from BNP or another channel.
- The Bordeaux first-growths are now clearly anchored to BNP — they didn't appear on any of the previous 9 importer pastes (KL, Rosenthal, Skurnik, GCS, Polaner, Bowler, Wildman, Wine Source, Banville).

## False positives caught and excluded

- *Château Redon* (BNP Bordeaux) ≠ Château Mont-Redon (Rhône — already tracked under Wildman)
- *Château La Croix* (BNP, generic) — multiple unrelated La Croix châteaux at Raeder's
- *Domaine Marc Antonin Blain* → Antonin Guyon (Burgundy — Wildman) — generic "antonin" token
- *Domaine Dubois / R. Dubois & Fils / Raphael Dubois* → Anne Sophie Dubois (Fleurie — Bowler / Grand Cru) — different producer
- *The Terraces of Saint Christophe* → Domaine Georges & Christophe Roumier (Burgundy — Grand Cru) — generic "christophe" token
- *Don Tomasi* → Don Guillermo / Don Melchor / Don Sebastiani — generic "don" token
- *Viña Seña* → Almaviva / Vina La Vina — generic "vina" token

## Methodology

Source name normalization handled the machine-translated names ("Castle" → "Château", "Pope Clement's" → "Pape Clément", "Castle The Gospel" → "L'Évangile", "Little Horse" → "Petit Cheval"). Trailing SKU counts `(N)` stripped at parse time.

Bordeaux first-pass match rate is exceptionally high (~35% of BNP producers found at Raeder's) because most BNP châteaux have exact-name matches at Raeder's collector inventory.
