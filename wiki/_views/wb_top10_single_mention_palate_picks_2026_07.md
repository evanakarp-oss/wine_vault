# WB "Top 10 in your cellar" — single-mention palate picks (2026-07)

**The question.** In the [[wb_top10_in_cellar_top_100|Top-10-producers thread]] most of the
signal is in the names that recur. But the long tail — 1,320 producers named by
exactly **one** collector — is where the idiosyncratic, off-the-beaten-path
cellars show themselves. Screened for *your* palate and for *novelty* (nothing
that already has a vault page), that tail hides a genuinely strong list.

**How this was filtered.**
- Started from the 1,320 single-mention producers.
- Dropped the parse noise the tally carries at the tail (forum chatter like
  "You?", "answer above", region headers, CellarTracker table rows).
- Removed the 96 that already have a `wiki/producers/` page — you know those.
  (Cut here on that basis: **[[cavallotto|Cavallotto]]**, **[[brovia|Brovia]]**,
  **[[wasenhaus|Wasenhaus]]**, **[[domaine_pierre_gonon|Pierre Gonon]]**,
  **[[boulay|Gérard Boulay]]**, **[[domaine_marnes_blanches|Marnes Blanches]]** —
  all single-mention, all on-taste, all already yours.)
- Ran the rest through the CLAUDE.md filters: terroir-driven, farming-first,
  grower/artisan-scale, biodynamic-leaning, classicist over cult-hedonist.
  Every survivor was web-verified (July 2026).

Everything below is **new to the vault** and squarely on-taste.

## ★ Top tier — buy-a-bottle-to-learn, high conviction

| Producer | Region | Why it's you |
|---|---|---|
| **Edmond Vatan** | Sancerre (Chavignol) | The Clos la Néore benchmark — pre-modern, low-yield, oxidative-edged Sauvignon that ages like white Burgundy. The un-generic Sancerre; deeply undercovered relative to its stature. |
| **Domaine du Collier** | Saumur / Saumur-Champigny | Antoine Foucault (of the Clos Rougeard family) — chenin + cabernet franc, old vines, long élevage, biodynamic-leaning. Loire terroir at the highest level, still a whisper. |
| **Thibaud Boudignon** | Anjou / Savennières | The reference grower for modern dry Chenin — organic, precise, tension-driven. Exactly the "sense of place + tension" axis. |
| **Domaine des Cavarodes** (Étienne Thiébaud) | Jura (Cramans) | 60–120-yr-old vines, horse-ploughed, organic→biodynamic; luminous, terroir-first Jura that's nearly impossible to get. Cult without the hedonism. |
| **Flavio Roddolo** | Barolo (Monforte) | Old-school, extended-maceration, large-cask traditionalist. Undervalued Barolo of place, no makeup. |
| **Antonio Vallana** | Alto Piemonte (Boca/Colline Novaresi) | Historic old-vine Nebbiolo (Spanna) that ages for decades; the aged-classic, undervalued vein you like in Napa, transposed to Piedmont. |
| **Le Ragnaie** | Brunello di Montalcino | Highest-elevation estate in Montalcino, organic, restrained and cool-toned — the antithesis of blockbuster Brunello. |
| **Il Marroneto** | Brunello di Montalcino | Traditional, ethereal, Madonna delle Grazie site — a purist's Brunello reference. |
| **Valentini** | Abruzzo (Loreto Aprutino) | The mythic Trebbiano/Montepulciano house — biodynamic-in-spirit, radically old-school, ages forever. Peak "farming + place." |
| **Julian Haart** | Mosel | The young Mosel grower everyone chased this decade — filigree Riesling off Wintricher/Piesporter slate, tiny production. |
| **Roses de Jeanne / Cédric Bouchard** | Champagne (Aube) | Single-parcel, single-vintage, single-variety grower Champagne — the philosophical opposite of NV blends, exactly your Champagne filter. |
| **Jérôme Prévost — La Closerie** | Champagne (Gueux) | Solo-parcel Pinot Meunier, Selosse-mentored, oxidative and vinous. Grower Champagne at its most singular. |

## ★★ Strong tier — same wheelhouse, slightly more findable

**Loire**
- **Olga Raffault** — Chinon; library-aged Cabernet Franc (Les Picasses), classic and savory.
- **Ferme de la Sansonnière** (Mark Angeli) — Anjou biodynamic pioneer; chenin of great purity.
- **Domaine de l'Écu** (formerly Guy Bossard) — biodynamic Muscadet, granite/gneiss/orthogneiss cuvées that age.
- **Catherine & Pierre Breton** — Bourgueil/Chinon; benchmark organic cabernet franc.

**Northern Rhône (traditional Syrah)**
- **Noël Verset** — the retired Cornas legend; ferrous, old-vine, bottles still surface at auction.
- **Marcel Juge** — Cornas classicist, tiny, ages beautifully.
- **Hervé Souhaut / Romaneaux-Destezet** — Ardèche gamay + syrah, whole-cluster, low-intervention.

**Piedmont / Alto Piemonte**
- **Oddero** — traditional Barolo estate (La Morra), classic cru range, fair prices.
- **Colombera & Garella** — Cristiano Garella's Lessona/Bramaterra; native varieties, light-handed, the Alto Piemonte revival's flag-bearer.
- **Nervi-Conterno** — Gattinara, now Roberto Conterno-owned; high-tension northern Nebbiolo.
- **Calabretta** — Etna, old-vine Nerello, long-aged and traditional (not the polished Etna style).

**Tuscany / Central Italy**
- **Paolo Bea** — Montefalco Sagrantino/Rosso; macerated, natural, ageworthy — a landmark estate.
- **San Giusto a Rentennano** — Chianti Classico (Gaiole); Percarlo, organic, structured and pure.
- **Chiara Condello** — Predappio (Romagna) Sangiovese in the Giulio Gambelli lineage; organic, large neutral casks, cherry-blossom-and-incense spungone terroir. A rising star.
- **Elena Fucci** — Aglianico del Vulture, single-cru Titolo off volcanic soils; concentrated but savory.

**Germany / Austria / Baden (terroir Riesling & beyond)**
- **Ziereisen** — Baden (Markgräflerland); Gutedel, Pinot & Syrah farmed for site, no makeup.
- **Lichtenberger González** — Leithaberg (Burgenland) organic/natural Blaufränkisch + Neuburger on limestone-slate; long-aged, native varieties only.
- **Heymann-Löwenstein** — Terraced Lower Mosel (Winningen) Riesling, dry and slate-driven.

**Champagne (grower)**
- **La Rogerie** — Justine Boxler (of Alsace's Boxler) + François Petit; 2.3 ha organic Grand Cru Blanc de Blancs (Avize/Cramant/Oger), parcel-by-parcel, no additives. New-wave grower cult.
- **Varnier-Fannière** — Avize Grand Cru grower, chalky Blanc de Blancs, still a value.
- **Marie-Noëlle Ledru** — Ambonnay grower, Pinot-driven, low-dosage classic.

**Iberia / Portugal (terroir, old-vine)**
- **Dominio del Águila** — Ribera del Duero; old-vine, high-elevation, classically-styled (a Rioja-clásico sensibility in Ribera).
- **Nit de Nin** (Familia Nin-Ortiz) — Priorat, biodynamic, old-vine Garnacha/Carignan on llicorella; more finesse than power.
- **Luís Pato** — Bairrada Baga, structured and ageworthy; Portugal's answer to Nebbiolo.

**Australia (the undervalued, ageworthy end)**
- **Wendouree** — Clare Valley; century-old vines, iron-fisted, glacially-aging reds. One of the great undervalued cellar wines on earth.
- **Bass Phillip** (older, pre-Jean-Marie Fourrier era) — Gippsland Pinot; the burgundian benchmark of Australia.
- **Sami-Odi** — Barossa multi-vintage Syrah, whole-bunch, low-intervention; the anti-blockbuster Barossa.

## Notes & caveats

- **"Single mention" ≠ obscure wine, just an idiosyncratic cellar.** Vatan,
  Valentini, Wendouree and Bea are famous *among the terroir crowd*; they show
  up once here only because few Berserkers built a top-10 around them. That's a
  feature — it's precisely the "sense of place + tension" cohort under-owned by
  the CA-Cab/Burgundy-trophy majority of that thread.
- **Verification.** Every pick above was web-checked (July 2026) for farming,
  scale and appellation. Corrections the check caught: La Rogerie is
  **Boxler/Petit**, not a Dom Pérignon alumnus as sometimes cited; Cavarodes'
  vines run 60–120 yrs, horse-ploughed.
- **Deliberately excluded** as off-your-axis even though single-mention:
  L'Aventure, Booker, McPrice Myers, Villa Creek, Epoch, Austin Hope (Paso
  opulence); Mollydooker, Elderton, Clarendon Hills, d'Arenberg (big Aussie);
  Pahlmeyer, Merus, Maybach, Ovid, Arkenstone, Kapcsándy, Darioush, Buccella,
  Carter (Napa cult-hedonist). All fail the "site-transparent, not opulent"
  filter.
- **Next step if you want to onboard any:** these are single-source (WB) names,
  so per CLAUDE.md they need a per-producer LLM pass (verify farming + critic
  coverage) before a `wiki/producers/` page — not a bulk create. The richest
  veins to start with: grower Champagne (Roses de Jeanne, Prévost, La Rogerie)
  and Alto Piemonte (Vallana, Colombera & Garella, Nervi-Conterno).
