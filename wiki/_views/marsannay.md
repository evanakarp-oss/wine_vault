# Marsannay — Northern Côte de Nuits

Marsannay AOC covers three communes at the northern tip of the Côte de Nuits: **Chenôve**, **Marsannay-la-Côte**, and **Couchey** — running south from Dijon's suburbs. Only municipal AOC in Burgundy to produce all three colors (red, white, rosé); ~240 ha claimed (171 red / 36.5 white / 31.5 rosé as of 2011).

Reputation lagged: an AOC only since 1987 because the 1936 founders missed the train, leaving the appellation under-priced relative to similar terroir further south. That is now ending.

## Headline: 1er Cru promotion incoming (2025/2026)

In 2024 INAO confirmed **12 climats** to be elevated from village-level Marsannay to **Premier Cru**, with implementation hoped for the **2025 or 2026 vintage**. Among them: **Clos du Roy**, **Les Longeroies**, **La Charme aux Prêtres**, **En la Montagne**, **Es Chezots**, **Les Grasses Têtes**, **Au Champ Salomon**, **Champ-Perdrix**, **Clos de Jeu**, **Les Favières**, **Les Boivin**, **Aux Genelières**, **Les Saint-Jacques**, **Le Clos**.

Practical consequence: pre-promotion pricing is still live on 2022/2023 bottlings at retail. Reclassification will reprice the top single-vineyard cuvées up — especially Clos du Roy, Longeroies, La Charme aux Prêtres.

## Commune anatomy

### Chenôve (northernmost — Dijon edge)

Two flagship lieux-dits:

- **Clos du Roy** — northernmost named site in Marsannay, 270m, thin stony soils over white limestone above / limestone marl below. Originally **Clos des Ducs**, possession of the Dukes of Burgundy until 1477; renamed Clos du Roy when Charles the Bold's defeat at Nancy passed it to the French king. Per CSW: "the most structured and elegant of the Bart Marsannays" alongside Au Champ Salomon. Hold 3–5y from release.
- **Le Chapitre** — 5.25ha vineyard once owned by the Chapter of Autun Collegiate Church. In the 16th–17th c. reputed **on par with the grand crus**. Lavalle 1855: "second vintage." Long classified as plain Bourgogne with a place-name appendage; **promoted to village-level Marsannay AOC starting with the 2019 vintage**. Red, white, and a small amount of rosé (Pinot Gris–based).

Other Chenôve parcels visible on Jasper Morris / Atlas maps: **En Montagne** (high slope), **Clos de la Chapelle**, **Bas / Dessus des Longeroies** (extends north from Marsannay-la-Côte).

### Marsannay-la-Côte (middle)

Largest concentration of named climats: **Les Longeroies** (subdivided Dessus / Bas), **Es Chezots**, **La Montagne**, **La Charme aux Prêtres**, **Les Grasses Têtes**, **Les Favières**, **Au Champ Salomon**, **Les Étales** (whites), **Les Finottes** (Domaine Bart monopole), **Les Ouzeloy**.

### Couchey (southernmost)

**La Varangée**, **Les Vaudenelles**, **En Auvone** (= Aux Avoines / Les Auvonnes depending on cadastre).
