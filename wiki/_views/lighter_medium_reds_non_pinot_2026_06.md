# Lighter-to-Medium Reds, Beyond Pinot — Collecting Targets

*Filed from `/ask-cellar`, 2026-06-01. Curation filters from CLAUDE.md applied:
terroir-driven, biodynamic-leaning, grower-scale, sense of place + tension.*

## The exposure picture (427 red bottles)

The cellar's red spine is **powerful**: Cabernet Sauvignon (82), Syrah (51),
red Bordeaux blend (36) — well over half the reds are full-bodied. The
lighter-to-medium tier is real but **lopsided toward Pinot**:

| Light-to-medium reds | Bottles |
|---|---:|
| **Pinot family** (Pinot Noir 86 + Spätburgunder 10 + Pinot Nero 1 + Pinot Blend 2) | **~99** |
| Gamay (cru Beaujolais) | 19 |
| Friuli natives (Refosco 6 / Schioppettino 3 / Pignolo 3) | 12 |
| Cabernet Franc | 5 |
| Sangiovese | 5 |
| Dolcetto | 4 |
| Barbera | 3 |
| Blauer Zweigelt | 2 |
| Frappato | 1 |

Nebbiolo (39 incl. blends) is medium-bodied but tannin-heavy — counted
separately. **Takeaway:** outside Pinot, the light/medium bench is ~50 bottles
against ~230 full-bodied reds. Your instinct is right — collect *non-Pinot*
light-to-medium with intent.

Five buckets, all inside your taste, ranked by how cleanly they fill the gap.

---

## 1. Cru Beaujolais (Gamay) — extend a strength you already love

You own Lapierre, [[2018_jean_foillard_morgon_eponym|Foillard]], Bouland,
Chapel, Diochon — but none of the **vault-tracked, CSW-championed** Beaujolais
houses are in the cellar yet. These are the obvious next buys:

- [[desvignes|Louis-Claude Desvignes]] — Morgon Côte du Py / Javernières, the
  granite benchmark (13 CSW articles).
- [[clos_de_la_roilette|Clos de la Roilette]] — Fleurie on Morgon-leaning
  clay-schist; ages like a baby Burgundy.
- [[anthony_thevenet|Anthony Thevenet]] — old-vine Morgon, Lapierre lineage.
- [[julien_sunier|Julien Sunier]] — perfumed Régnié / Fleurie / Morgon, low-sulfur.

## 2. Jura reds — Poulsard & Trousseau — the biggest clean gap

The lightest *serious* reds in France: pale, savory, high-acid, biodynamic,
grower-scale — squarely your taste. You track **15 Jura producers and own zero
Jura reds.** Start here:

- [[overnoy-crinquand|Overnoy-Crinquand]] — Pupillin Poulsard, the reference.
- [[jacques_puffeney|Jacques Puffeney]] — "the Pope of Arbois," Trousseau (library bottles only now, retired 2014).
- [[stephane_tissot|Stéphane Tissot]] — single-vineyard Trousseau (Singulier, En Barberon), biodynamic.
- [[michel_gahier|Michel Gahier]] — Trousseau from Montigny-lès-Arsures.
- [[domaine_de_la_tournelle|Domaine de la Tournelle]] / [[domaine_ratte|Domaine Ratte]] — value Poulsard/Trousseau.

## 3. Loire Cabernet Franc — medium, structured, ageable

Chinon / Bourgueil / Saumur-Champigny: cool-climate Cab Franc, terroir-transparent,
built to age. You own only a bulk De Negoce bottle — a genuine hole given how
heavily CSW champions these:

- [[domaine_baudry|Domaine Bernard Baudry]] — Chinon
  across limestone/gravel cuvées (Les Grézeaux, La Croix Boissée); 45 CSW articles.
- [[domaine_de_la_chevalerie|Domaine de la Chevalerie]] & [[stephane_guion|Stéphane Guion]] — old-vine Bourgueil, organic.
- [[clos_rougeard|Clos Rougeard]] — Saumur-Champigny's grail (Le Bourg, Les Poyeux); buy on sight if priced sanely.

## 4. Alpine / Alto-Piemonte Nebbiolo — Nebbiolo without the Barolo weight

You're deep in Barolo/Barbaresco. The lighter, higher-acid Nebbiolo zones give
the variety's perfume at medium body. You already own
[[2006_ar_pe_pe_valtellina_superiore_grumello_rocca_de_piro_riserva|AR.PE.PE Valtellina]]
and [[2010_antonio_vallana_e_figlio_spanna_cuvee_bernardo_vallana|Vallana Spanna]] —
both single bottles worth deepening. Vault-tracked targets:

- [[le_piane|Le Piane]] — Boca, sandy volcanic Alto Piemonte (6 DTE cuvées).
- [[pianelle|Pianelle]] — Bramaterra/Boca, lithe northern Nebbiolo.
- And re-buy AR.PE.PE (Valtellina) — Carema / Gattinara are the obvious next region pages to seed.

## 5. Sicily — Etna Rosso (Nerello Mascalese) & Frappato — volcanic tension

Light-to-medium, high-acid, mineral — the "sense of place + tension" axis in red
form. You have a base ([[arianna_occhipinti|Occhipinti]] 5 btl,
[[frank_cornelissen|Cornelissen]] 3 btl) — extend it:

- [[bonavita|Bonavita]] — Faro, ethereal Nerello Mascalese/Cappuccio.
- [[masseria_del_pino|Masseria del Pino]] — northern-slope Etna Rosso.
- [[gulfi|Gulfi]] — Vittoria Frappato / Cerasuolo for the lightest, juiciest tier.

## Footnotes / adjacent

- **Friuli natives** (Refosco, Schioppettino, Pignolo): you already have the
  Ronchi di Cialla line — a coherent place to keep adding light-medium Italian reds.
- **Austrian Blaufränkisch / Zweigelt** (Burgenland): medium-bodied, peppery,
  terroir-driven — only 2 bottles now; a natural extension if you want to widen
  past France/Italy.
- **Deliberately excluded** to honor the no-more-Pinot brief: Oregon/Sonoma
  Pinot, German/Swiss Spätburgunder.
