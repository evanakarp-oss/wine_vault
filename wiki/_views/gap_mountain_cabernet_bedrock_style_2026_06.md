# Cool-Climate Mountain Cabernet — Bedrock-Style Discovery (2026-06)

**Why this page exists.** The vault's [[California_Producers|California rollup]] is
stocked almost entirely with the cult-hedonist tier — [[colgin|Colgin]],
[[schrader_cellars|Schrader]], [[screaming_eagle|Screaming Eagle]],
[[hundred_acre|Hundred Acre]], [[bryant_family_vineyard|Bryant]],
[[sine_qua_non|Sine Qua Non]]. That's exactly the style Evan's **updated** taste
filter (flipped 2026-05-29) says to *skip* — too opulent, not site-transparent. So
this whole "rugged dry-farmed mountain Cab" axis is a **real gap**: nothing in-vault
serves it today. None of the producers below have wiki pages yet — they're discovery
candidates.

## The template: what Bedrock actually sources

The anchor is **Montecillo Vineyard** (top of Nelligan Road, northern edge of the
**Moon Mountain District AVA**, Sonoma side of the Mayacamas). Owned by Karin Lee
(widow of Kenwood founder Mike Lee). The original block was planted in the **late
1960s**, **dry-farmed** on widely-spaced "California Sprawl," sitting ~**1,400–2,200'**.
Among the oldest remaining Cabernet in the state. Bedrock's bottling is explicitly
pitched at "those of us who love old-school California Mountain Cabernet" — dark fruit,
gravel, tobacco, dried herbs, serious aging curve. **This is the reference template**:
high-elevation Mayacamas/Moon Mountain, dry-farmed, old vines, restrained.

> **"Lazy Dog" note.** Lazy Dog Vineyard is a real Sonoma Valley site (Carriger Rd
> × Grove St) — but it's younger (planted 2002, Cab clone 337, ~7.5 acres), a *cooler*
> valley-floor-adjacent site, bottled by Lake Sonoma Winery and the Atmosphere label.
> It's the cool-climate angle, not the rugged-old-vine-mountain angle. The producers
> below lean harder into the Montecillo end of the spectrum, which is where the
> off-radar gold is.

## Tier 1 — buy/chase first (squarely Bedrock + Evan taste, genuinely off-radar)

| Producer / Vineyard | Where | Why it fits |
|---|---|---|
| **Far Mountain** (`Fission` Cab) | Moon Mountain District, Sonoma | Rodrigo Soto + Mai Errázuriz (est. 2018). Sources **The Nunnery** (end of Nunn's Canyon, Sonoma-Mayacamas) — Phil Coturri-farmed, **dry-farmed/organic**, plus terraced 1970s blocks ~1,600'. Literally the same mountain corridor as Montecillo. The closest spiritual sibling to the Bedrock Cab. |
| **Montecillo Vineyard — other bottlings** | Moon Mountain, Sonoma | The source itself. Bedrock isn't the only one with fruit; the vineyard is bottled under several labels. Buy the vineyard, not just the brand. |
| **Mount Eden Vineyards** (Estate Cab) | Santa Cruz Mountains | ~2,000', **dry-farmed, no irrigation**, austere Franciscan shale. ~40-yr-old vines tracing to Emmett Rixford's La Questa. Founded 1945 (Paul Masson → Martin Ray lineage). Power + finesse, mineral, age-worthy. A neighbor in spirit to Evan's existing reference pick **Ridge Monte Bello**. |
| **Renaissance Vineyard & Winery** | North Yuba, Sierra Foothills | The deep cut. Terraced **1,700–2,300'** decomposed-granite + schist slopes above the fog line, 40°F diurnal swings. **Austere, leathery, stony, traditional-European** Cab built for the cellar. About as off-the-radar as serious CA Cab gets. Older library vintages still findable cheap. |

## Tier 2 — same ethos, slightly more known or adjacent

| Producer | Where | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Lasseter Family Winery** | Moon Mountain District | Phil Coturri-farmed volcanic sites (Justi Creek, Trinity Ridge). Rugged Moon Mountain Cab/blends. |
| **Coturri Winery** | Sonoma Mountain | Est. 1979 NorCal natural-wine pioneer; organic/biodynamic, dry-farmed, zero-additive. Rugged and divisive — the antithesis of polished Napa. |
| **Monte Rosso Vineyard bottlings** | Moon Mountain, Sonoma | Planted 1886; 1940s Cab blocks still in production. Red volcanic loam, elevation, acid + mineral streak. The historic benchmark for the corridor. |
| **Smith-Madrone** | Spring Mountain, Napa | Dry-farmed mountain estate, restrained old-school Napa Cab — a cousin to Evan's Corison/Dunn reference set. |
| **Robert Craig** | Mt. Veeder / Howell Mountain | Mountain-Cab specialist, value-leaning, site-driven — bridges toward the Dunn/La Jota end of the cellar's reference set. |

## How this connects to the existing cellar reference set

Evan's stated CA-Cab north stars are **Dunn (Howell Mtn)**, **Corison (St. Helena)**,
**La Jota (Howell Mtn)**, **Dalla Valle (Oakville)**, **Ridge Monte Bello (Santa Cruz
Mtns)** — "sense of place + tension." The picks above extend that set *off the Napa
floor and onto the Mayacamas/Moon Mountain + Sierra granite frontier*:

- **Mount Eden** ↔ Ridge Monte Bello (Santa Cruz Mtns, dry-farmed, austere shale)
- **Far Mountain / Lasseter / Monte Rosso** ↔ the Mayacamas spine Dunn & La Jota sit on, just the Sonoma side
- **Renaissance** ↔ no current analog — a genuinely new terroir frontier (Sierra granite, North Yuba)
