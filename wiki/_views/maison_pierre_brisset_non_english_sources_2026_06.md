# Maison Pierre Brisset — Non-English Source Research (2026-06)

Evan owns **7 cuvée-vintages** (2021 Pommard 1er, 2022 Gabrius, 2023 Cassaneas /
NSG Aux Thorey / Chambolle Les Noirots / Chassagne Abbaye de Morgeot /
Vosne Les Rouges du Dessus) — all Fass Selections buys — but the vault has
**no producer page**. The English-language record is essentially Fass hype
copy; this note is what the French and Japanese record adds.

## Who he is (French sources)

- **Micro-négociant, founded 2014** (first vintage 2014), based in the
  **Château de Bligny, Bligny-lès-Beaune**, just south of Beaune. The BIVB
  directory lists him as *Négociant Viticulteur*
  ([vins-bourgogne.fr](https://www.vins-bourgogne.fr/vignerons-savoir-faire/femmes-et-hommes-passionnes/brisset-pierre-bligny-les-beaune-21200,2397,9211.html?args=Y29tcF9pZD0xNDA2JmFjdGlvbj12aWV3RmljaGUmaWQ9VklOQk9VMDAwVjUwRDBaSCZ8),
  [pierrebrisset.com/maison](https://www.pierrebrisset.com/maison/)).
- **Second-career vigneron**: studied finance, co-founded and ran the Paris
  travel site **VoyagerMoinsCher.com**; per the Japanese importer Firadis he
  **sold the business to Rakuten in 2010** and pivoted to wine
  ([pierrebrisset.com bio](https://www.pierrebrisset.com/maison/pierre-brisset/),
  [Firadis profile](https://firadis.co.jp/product/producer-info/?producerid=3636)).
- **2013: bought 0.5 ha of Chassagne-Montrachet 1er Cru (Abbaye de) Morgeot**
  (Chardonnay) — his **only estate parcel**; everything else is purchased
  fruit. The négoce range is deliberately premium-skewed: almost exclusively
  1er + Grand Cru from Côte de Nuits and Côte de Beaune (Meursault,
  Puligny, Chassagne, Vosne, Chambolle, Gevrey, Échezeaux,
  Corton-Charlemagne…) ([Guide Hachette producer page](https://www.hachette-vins.com/guide-vins/producteurs/21148/pierre-brisset/),
  [Firadis](https://firadis.co.jp/product/producer-info/?producerid=3636)).
- Official/BIVB copy claims production "deliberately limited to **4,000–5,000
  bottles/year**" — treat with caution; given the breadth of the current
  range (and Fass allocation volume) this is likely stale or per-launch.

## The Wine Studio (the structurally interesting bit)

Brisset has **no winery of his own**. He vinifies at the **"Wine Studio"** in
the 14th-c. cellars of Château de Bligny — Burgundy's only California-style
**custom-crush facility**, running since **2015** around **Dominique Lafon**
(Comtes Lafon) and **Pierre Meurgey** (ex-Champy). Capacity: ~750 barrels,
40 vats, two sorting tables, two pneumatic presses, a bottling line; tenants
rent at ~€400/barrel. It celebrated its 10th anniversary in 2025
([France Bleu](https://www.francebleu.fr/emissions/plus-belle-la-vigne-en-bourgogne/a-bligny-les-beaune-le-wine-studio-fete-son-10eme-anniversaire-9881807),
[Presse Agence](https://presseagence.fr/beaune-10-ans-du-wine-studio-au-chateau-de-bligny/),
[BIVB press release](https://www.vins-bourgogne.fr/presse/detail-d-un-communique-de-presse,2309,9200.html?args=Y29tcF9pZD0xMzkwJmFjdGlvbj12aWV3RGV0YWlsQ29tbXVuaXF1ZSZpZD0yNjkzJnw%3D),
[Réussir Vigne](https://www.reussir.fr/vigne/le-studio-de-vinification-un-lieu-dechange),
[Decanter/Anson in English](https://www.decanter.com/wine-news/opinion/news-blogs-anson/anson-on-thursday-a-game-changer-for-burgundy-winemaking-280193/)).

Two implications for the taste filter:

1. **Mentorship halo is real**: he shares a cellar (and exchange-of-ideas
   culture) with Dominique Lafon — consistent with the polished, Côte de
   Beaune-classicist style Fass describes.
2. **No farming claim**: Réussir Vigne notes explicitly that **no Wine
   Studio tenant claims organic/domaine status** — purchased fruit, shared
   facility. Brisset shows **no bio/HVE certification** anywhere in the
   French record. He is *not* a biodynamic-grower archetype; he's a
   quality-obsessed custom-crush négociant. Worth knowing given the vault's
   biodynamic-leaning Burgundy filter.

## Winemaking (Firadis, JP — most detailed source found)

Minimal-intervention cellar aimed at terroir transparency: gentle pneumatic
pressing; **whites barrel-fermented and raised in barrique**; **reds
fermented in stainless, raised in barrique**; **bottled unfiltered**
([Firadis](https://firadis.co.jp/product/producer-info/?producerid=3636)).
Firadis also relays critic praise (Allen Meadows on texture; Jancis Robinson
tasting every release since the debut vintage) — Japan gets a formal import
channel (Firadis → retailers like
[Wine Library](https://wine-library.net/collections/brisset)).

## Cuvée name decoder (relevant to two cellar bottles)

- **Cuvée Gabrius** (Bourgogne Pinot Noir) — *Gabrius* is the ancient name
  of **Gevrey**; fruit from vines in Gevrey-Chambertin just outside the AOC
  ([pierrebrisset.com](https://www.pierrebrisset.com/vins/bourgogne-pinot-noir-cuvee-gabrius-2021/)).
- **Cuvée Cassaneas** (Bourgogne Chardonnay/Côte d'Or) — *Cassaneas* = old
  name of **Chassagne**; vines in Chassagne, Meursault and Volnay outside
  the AOC; soft press, **14 months in barrel, ~900 bottles**
  ([pierrebrisset.com](https://www.pierrebrisset.com/vins/bourgogne-cote-dor-chardonnay-cuvee-cassaneas-2023/),
  [Vinothèque Charrière, CH](https://www.vinotheque-charriere.ch/fr/Vins/BOURGOGNE-Pierre-Brisset-Cuvee-Cassaneas--2022.html)).
  This is the declassified-1er-cru story Fass sells — the French record
  backs it up.

## Bottom line for the vault

The French/Japanese record confirms Brisset is a **legitimate, critically
recognized micro-négociant** (RVF 18/20, Hachette stars, B&D, JP import
channel) and not just a Fass house brand — but he is a **custom-crush
négociant with one 0.5 ha parcel and no farming certification**, so he sits
in the cellar on the *terroir-transparency + Lafon-adjacent polish* axis,
not the biodynamic-grower axis. Strong case to promote this note into
`wiki/producers/maison_pierre_brisset.md` (7 cuvée-vintages owned is the
largest producer holding without a page after Catena).
