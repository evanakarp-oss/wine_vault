# Definitive Cabernet Target List (2026-06)

Applies the two Cabernet-relevant taste filters: **California** — rugged,
farming-driven, terroir-distinct, NOT the cult-hedonist tier (reference set:
Dunn, Corison, La Jota, Dalla Valle, Ridge Monte Bello). **Bordeaux** —
WK-style undercovered/value with great farming; aged classed-growth for
drink-now bottles only. Overarching axis: sense of place + tension.

What the cellar already proves: the Cabernet holdings lean hard into the
**Santa Cruz Mountains** (Sandar & Hem Bates Ranch ×3 vintages, Santa Cruz
Mountain Vineyard ×5 bottlings, Ceritas Peter Martin Ray ×2, Ridge Three
Valleys) plus restrained Napa (MacDonald 2014, Beta ×6). The buy list below
extends that axis rather than fighting it.

## §1 — The core gap: reference-set producers with zero bottles owned

The five producers named in the taste filter itself. None has a producer
page; none is in the cellar. These are the definitive buys.

| Producer | Site | Why | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dunn Vineyards** | Howell Mountain, Napa | The benchmark for structured, low-alcohol mountain Cab; decades-long ager | WB top-100 #57 (16 mentions) |
| **Corison** | St. Helena benchland, Napa | Restrained, balanced Napa Cab; Kronos Vineyard old-vine bottling is the flagship | Filter reference |
| **Ridge Monte Bello** | Santa Cruz Mountains | THE Santa Cruz Mountains Cab; cellar already owns Three Valleys 2021 but not the flagship | WB top-100 **#3** (80 mentions) — highest-ranked unowned Cab producer |
| **La Jota** | Howell Mountain, Napa | Mountain-tannin, site-transparent | Filter reference |
| **Dalla Valle** | Oakville hillside, Napa | Maya (Cab Franc blend) + Cabernet; hillside structure over opulence | Filter reference |

Priority order: **Ridge Monte Bello first** (deepest community signal +
extends the existing SCM spine), then **Dunn**, then **Corison Kronos**.
