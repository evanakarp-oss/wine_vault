# WK × Down to Earth — Top Picks, Value-First (2026-06)

What **William Kelley** actually endorses across the whole [[Down_to_Earth_Wines_Panzer|Down to Earth]]
book — Burgundy, Champagne, and the Rhône — sorted into a value core (most
bottles under $80) plus a short trophy list. Signal is from Kelley's
Berserkers posts (forum, directional — not scored Wine Advocate reviews;

## Caveats & skips

- [[marguet\|Marguet]] (Champagne, $69–159) — "I like the wines a lot, but
  sometimes the levels of volatile acidity can be quite elevated (something
  Benoît accepts, even embraces)." A like with an asterisk — taste first.
- [[agrapart\|Agrapart]] ($59–79) — only factual mentions in the corpus (the
  Pascal/Ambroise succession), no enthusiasm on record. Pedigree is real;
  this scrape just doesn't speak to it.
- [[arnoux_lachaux\|Arnoux-Lachaux]] ($299–6,999) — WK rates the wines (98 pts
  on the 2019 RSV) but his own "priced by zip code, not by what's in the
  bottle" critique argues *against* buying at these levels. Skip on price.
- [[michel_noellat\|Michel Noëllat]] ($45) — the one DTE name WK has knocked:
  "a pity they don't do a better job… some work to do in the vineyards."

## Bottom line

DTE's book is unusually Kelley-aligned. The five highest-conviction
under-$80 buys, in order: **Charles/Cyril Audoin** (Marsannay, the clearest
gap), **Thierry Glantenay** (Volnay climats from $33), **Jean Javillier**
(Meursault), **Lassaigne + Petit Clergeot** (chiseled Champagne pairing), and
**deepen Lorenzon**. For the trophy shelf: **Bouley** Volnay/Pommard 1ers and
**Marc Sorrel** white Hermitage.
