## 1. Traditional vs. Modern Barolo / Barbaresco — *style map* {#trad-modern-barolo}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/traditional-vs-modern-barolo-barbaresco/99925)

The canonical WB sorting of Nebbiolo producers by cellar philosophy — long
maceration + large old botti (*tradition*) vs. short maceration, rotofermenters,
new French barriques (*modern* / the "Barolo Boys" of the late 1980s–90s).

- **Traditional / classicist:** Giacomo Conterno, Bartolo Mascarello, Giuseppe
  Rinaldi, Cappellano (the "last of the Mohicans" trio), Giuseppe Mascarello
  (Monprivato), Bruno Giacosa, Cavallotto, Francesco Rinaldi, Brovia, Burlotto,
  Marcarini, Elvio Cogno, Poderi Colla, Giacomo Borgogno, Oddero, Massolino,
  Luigi Pira; Barbaresco side: Produttori del Barbaresco, Roagna, Albino Rocca,
  Rizzi, Cascina delle Rose, Giuseppe Cortese, La Ca' Növa, Paitin, Serafino
  Rivella, Lorenzo Accomasso.
- **Modern / Barolo Boys:** Elio Altare (the originator), Paolo Scavino, Domenico
  Clerico, Roberto Voerzio, Luciano Sandrone, Conterno Fantino.
- **Live nuance from the thread:** the divide isn't binary — Clerico (and others)
  swung *back* toward big old casks; participants argued Nebbiolo "can't stand up
  to" heavy new oak, so even modernists moderated over time. There's also a social
  edge: ultra-traditionalists treated any non-traditional cuvée as leaving "the club."

**Cellar take:** straight down Evan's Piedmont lane (terroir-driven classicists).
The traditional column is the shopping list; the cellar already leans here via
Produttori (a top-10 mention producer) and Giacosa. Cross-ref [[../producers/produttori_del_barbaresco|Produttori del Barbaresco]]
and the cellar's existing Piedmont holdings; gap-check the classicist names not yet
papered (Cavallotto, Brovia, Burlotto, Cogno, Oddero).

## 2. Traditional vs. Modern Bordeaux? — *style map* {#trad-modern-bordeaux}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/traditional-vs-modern-bordeaux/85688)

The Bordeaux sibling, explicitly modeled on the Barolo thread: which châteaux still
make *classically styled* claret — not "parkerized / Rolland-ized" (over-extracted,
high-new-oak, riper picking). Built as a buying aid for wines you can't pre-taste.

- **Classically styled names surfaced (left bank value end):** Cantemerle &
  Sociando-Mallet (Haut-Médoc), Brane-Cantenac, d'Issan, Rauzan-Ségla (Margaux),
  Potensac (Médoc), Chasse-Spleen (Moulis), Batailley & Clerc-Milon (Pauillac);
  the perennial traditionalist references Léoville-Barton (St-Julien) and Montrose
  (St-Estèphe) recur in the discussion as the benchmarks of restraint + ageability.

**Cellar take:** this *is* Evan's Bordeaux filter — "WK-style undercovered / value
with great farming," classed-growth aged for drink-now only, skip generic mid-tier.
Sociando-Mallet, Potensac, Chasse-Spleen, Batailley, Léoville-Barton are textbook
"value with great farming." Use as a buy-screen against retailer offers and auction
catalogs (cross-ref the `auction_261w_*` views).

## 3. Northern Rhône Tour: Clusel-Roch · Jamet · Perret · Gonon · Sorrel · Cave de Tain · Clape · Allemand — *producer tour* {#northern-rhone-tour}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/northern-rhone-tour-clusel-roch-jamet-perret-gonon-sorrel-cave-de-tain-clape-allemand/120248)

A week-long N. Rhône cellar tour (orig. post 2016-04-21) by a group of California
Rhône-style winemakers, organized by **Paul Gordon of Halcón Vineyards** after a
conversation with Rhône authority **John Livingston-Learmonth** — explicitly to
get the California and N. Rhône communities to know each other. Visits span the
appellation's reference addresses:

- **Côte-Rôtie:** Clusel-Roch, Jamet · **Condrieu/St-Joseph whites:** André Perret
  · **St-Joseph:** Gonon (the benchmark) · **Hermitage:** Sorrel, plus the co-op
  Cave de Tain · **Cornas:** Auguste Clape, Thierry Allemand.
- A **follow-up blind tasting** (Paul & Jackie Gordon) pitted most of the visited
  N. Rhône domaines against the California Rhône producers on the trip — a rare
  apples-to-apples Old-World/New-World syrah read.

**Cellar take:** the spine of serious N. Rhône syrah, and dead-center for Evan
(Cornas/Côte-Rôtie/St-Joseph). Note the strong overlap with the [[lpv_signal_map_french_regions_2026_06|LPV signal map]]:
Clape, Jamet, Allemand, Gonon all have deep dedicated *fils* on LPV too — i.e.
**double community consensus**. Treat Gonon (St-Joseph) and Clape/Allemand (Cornas)
as priority targets; cross-ref any of these already in the vault.

## 5. Thoughts on Buying and Consuming BURGUNDY — *strategy* {#buying-consuming-burgundy}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/thoughts-on-buying-and-consuming-burgundy/146528)

Less a list than a *philosophy* thread: how to build and drink a Burgundy cellar
without going broke or chasing unobtainium. OP frames Burgundy as "the most
intellectually stimulating region," name-checking the apex (DRC, Roumier, Dujac,
Leflaive, Raveneau) but the useful through-line is the **"good, actually available,
not insanely expensive" bracket** and the observation that Burgundy's "peak" drinking
window varies more by *taster* than Nebbiolo does — so trust your own palate on hold
times rather than a published window.

**Cellar take:** the strategic complement to thread #4's name-list — buy at the
village/1er level from honest growers, drink on your own curve, don't over-cellar.
File alongside [[drink_window_due|drink-window]] and the Burgundy value views.

## 6. California's great old-vine Zinfandel vineyards — *vineyard atlas* {#old-vine-zin}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/forum/viewtopic.php?t=18190)

A site-by-site atlas of California's historic (often pre-Prohibition, head-trained,
mixed-black field-blend) Zinfandel vineyards — the *vineyards*, not the labels,
which is exactly how the Bedrock/Carlisle/Ridge world thinks. Sites documented:

- **Russian River / Sonoma:** Mueller (block planted 1915), Croix Estate (1904, 39
  acres co-planted Zin/Carignan/Alicante/Petite Sirah/Mataro), Sanchietti (100+ yo).
- **Contra Costa:** Evangelho (~120 yo, sandy; source for Precedent, Bedrock, Carlisle).
- **Lodi:** Soucie (1916), Mettler (1907).
- (thread breadth extends across Amador, Sonoma, Napa, Contra Costa, Lodi.)

**Cellar take:** direct adjacency to the rugged, farming-driven California already
favored (Bedrock, Carlisle, Ridge are top-mention producers in `top10_in_cellar`).
This is the field-blend / old-vine map underneath those labels — use it to read a
Bedrock/Carlisle vineyard-designate offer and know *what* you're buying. Cross-ref
[[gap_mountain_cabernet_bedrock_style_2026_06|Bedrock-style gap view]].

## 8. The 16-bottle Chardonnay Blowout Challenge! — *aging experiment / humor* {#chardonnay-blowout}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/the-16-bottle-chardonnay-blowout-challenge/101084)

Beloved stunt thread: a member found a shop dumping 10–14-year-old Chardonnay at $2
a bottle, walked out with ~12 for **$26.16 total**, and serially opened them to see
how cheap, forgotten, well-stored Chardonnay had aged. The durable lesson under the
comedy: **cellar conditions + screwcap/cork luck matter more than price**, and aged
inexpensive Chardonnay can over-deliver (or oxidize) unpredictably.

**Cellar take:** not a buy list — an aging-curve gut-check. Reinforces that white-Burgundy
premox anxiety is partly a storage story, and that cheap aged whites are a low-stakes
way to calibrate your own palate on tertiary development.

## 9. Another Shitty '07 Oregon Pinot Noir — *vintage rehabilitation* {#shitty-07-oregon}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/forum/viewtopic.php?t=45228)

A long-running, tongue-in-cheek serial tasting (~100 wines from ~60 producers) of
the 2007 Oregon Pinot vintage — a cool, wet, rain-at-harvest year the critics
*panned* on release. The thread's arc is the payoff: held a decade, '07 turned into
a **graceful, classically structured, lower-alcohol** vintage that aged far better
than the riper years critics preferred — a documented case of a "written-off"
vintage rehabilitating.

**Cellar take:** the lesson, not the bottles — restraint/structure outlasts ripeness,
and contrarian buying of critically-dismissed cool vintages is a real edge (squarely
the "tension / sense of place" axis). Goodfellow and Patricia Green (top-mention
Oregon producers in the cellar tally) are central to that '07 Willamette story.

## 10. Premier Cru Master Complaint Thread (MERGED) — *cautionary / fraud* {#premier-cru}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/premier-cru-master-complaint-thread-merged/56697)

The community's real-time documentation of the **Premier Cru** (Berkeley retailer)
collapse — a pre-arrival / futures Ponzi where customers paid for wine that was never
delivered, ending in 2016 bankruptcy and the owner's federal fraud conviction. The
thread also surfaces the bankruptcy-trustee **clawback** mechanics (wine delivered in
lieu of owed wine within 90 days treated as improper preference; the window extends
for fraudulently-run businesses).

**Cellar take:** a procurement-risk checklist, not a wine list — **be wary of
deep-discount pre-arrival/futures from a single retailer**; take delivery promptly;
diversify sources; a price too good to be true on en-primeur is a red flag. Relevant
discipline given how much of the vault sources from retailer offers and auctions.

## 11. Building a subterranean wine cellar / root cellar — *cellar build* {#subterranean-cellar}
[thread →](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/building-a-subterranean-wine-cellar-root-cellar/170960)

A DIY **passive** (no active cooling) cellar build log + community engineering help.
The OP had a slope, an excavator, and basic skills; the plan: cinder-block walls,
concrete floor, steel ceiling with concrete poured over, fully sealed and then buried,
~12'×12' with an 8' ceiling. Practical advice from the thread: cut the **access into
the low side of the slope** with a floor drain "to daylight," set the door header at
ceiling height with the steps inside; sealing against moisture is the hard part. Other
members' passive cellars hold a steady **58–60°F** year-round once earth-coupled.

**Cellar take:** the reference build if Evan ever goes passive — earth coupling can
hold ideal 55–60°F storage with zero running cost, but moisture sealing and drainage
are make-or-break. Keep for the build-vs-buy-a-cooling-unit decision.
