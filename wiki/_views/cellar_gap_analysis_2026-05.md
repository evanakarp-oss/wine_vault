## What NOT to expand

- **Napa cult Cab**: Stated bar is Harlan / Hundred Acre / Bond / Colgin / Schrader /
  SQN / Ridge Monte Bello tier. One Hundred Acre Kayli Morgan + Renaissance already
  in cellar. Buying more in this category is expensive and breaks the
  terroir/grower thread defining the rest of the cellar. Hold.
- **Bordeaux mid-tier**: 25 bottles across 8 classed-growth châteaux (Pichon
  Comtesse, Cos, Léoville Las Cases, Gruaud, Prieuré-Lichine) is exactly the
  "aged classed-growth for drink-now" footprint stated. Don't add WK-style
  mid-tier — stated taste explicitly says "skip generic mid-tier."
- **Generic California Chardonnay**: 23 bottles of Ceritas is already heavy.
  Don't broaden into Aubert, Kistler, Peter Michael tier — that's exactly the
  technique-over-place style stated taste avoids.

## Suggested execution order

1. **Immediate (low-allocation, high CSW coverage)**: Domaine Baudry,
   Hofgut Falkenstein, Willi Schaefer, Stéphane Tissot. All have ≥9 CSW articles,
   ≥3 dedicated ★, pages already built, no allocation barrier at most retailers.
2. **Second wave (medium effort)**: Chartogne-Taillet, Bérêche, Foillard,
   Castro Ventosa / Raúl Pérez. Grower-Champagne and Bierzo broadening; some
   require allocation but not gatekept.
3. **Third wave (allocation / hard-to-find)**: Egly-Ouriet, Ulysse Collin,
   Overnoy-Houillon, Yvon Métras, Egon Müller. Cellar-defining but require
   relationships / waitlist / lottery.
