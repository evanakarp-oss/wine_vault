# WK × Down to Earth — Burgundy Portfolio Eval (2026-06)

Every Burgundy producer with `dte.in_portfolio: true` (35 after excluding
[[loersch|Loersch]], a Mosel estate mis-tagged Burgundy — see housekeeping),
scored against what **William Kelley actually wrote** in the Berserkers
corpus. Forum posts, not Wine Advocate reviews — directional, not scored.
Companion to [[kelley_burgundy_value_2026_06|Kelley's Burgundy Value Map]],
which derives his value logic; this page applies it to one retailer's book.

**Headline: DTE's Burgundy book is unusually WK-aligned.** 22 of 35
producers appear in his corpus, and the overlaps aren't incidental — several
of his most emphatic endorsements (Lorenzon, Bouley, Audoin, Glantenay,
Henri Magnien, Javillier) are DTE producers at sane prices.

## Tier 3 — liked, with nuance

- [[hudelot_noellat|Hudelot-Noëllat]] (12 cuvées, $49–1,199) — deep respect, detailed parcel knowledge, "Richebourg has the edge" there; tone technical rather than effusive.
- [[denis_bachelet|Denis Bachelet]] (1 cuvée, $45) — owns and trades for the wines (the covid facemask story); quality reference point. At $45 for one cuvée, an easy yes.
- [[roty|Joseph Roty]] (1 cuvée, $49) — "with bottle age, I would rank the domaine's three grand crus among the best wines produced in Gevrey" (2017-11); toasty oak caveat, but old vines carry it.
- [[jean_collet|Jean Collet]] (3 cuvées, $25–39) — "Romain has taken over… doing great work. The 1ers are great QPR" (2018-02).
- [[guillot_broux|Guillot-Broux]] (2 cuvées, $42–49) — "serious wines that need a bit of age," owned back to the 1980s.
- [[barraud|Barraud]] (5 cuvées, $26–55) — quality acknowledged but "comparatively rich interpretations"; he steers chiseled-wine drinkers to Saint-Véran En Crèches and En Buland specifically. 🍷 *owned: 7 entries incl. En Crèches*
- [[michel_mallard|Michel Mallard]] (24 cuvées, $33–329) — flagged "under-the-radar" in his 2020/2019 report rollout (2022-01). Watchlist.
- [[chicotot|Chicotot]] (20 cuvées, $45–179) — "the quality of the raw materials is evident, so the only way is up" (2021-09). Emerging; buy before the report catches up.
- [[gerard_julien|Gérard Julien]] (5 cuvées, $45–299) — "destemmed, somewhat richer, oakier style that's very well done" (2022-04). Style fit depends on taste.
- [[sigaut|Sigaut]] (9 cuvées, $69–135) — one isolated rave ("2016 Sentiers… a terrific wine").
- [[georges_noellat|Georges Noëllat]] / [[maxime_cheurlin|Maxime Cheurlin]] (13–18 cuvées, $35–899) — described factually as a *precocious* style (2013s, 2015s drink early); no strong personal enthusiasm on record.

## Skeptical

- [[michel_noellat|Michel Noëllat]] (1 cuvée, $45) — "one of the largest holders [of Suchots], it's a pity they don't do a better job" (2017-11); "some work to do in the vineyards" (2022-01). The only DTE Burgundy name WK has knocked.

## Action shortlist (WK signal × Evan's taste × price)

1. **[[charles_audoin|Charles Audoin]]** — WK's Marsannay benchmark, 19 cuvées at $45–65, *zero bottles owned*. The single clearest gap.
2. **[[georges_glantenay|Thierry Glantenay]]** — top-tier Volnay climats (Caillerets, Clos des Chênes) at village-adjacent prices from $33.
3. **[[jean_javillier|Jean Javillier]]** — the producer WK wanted to keep secret, $26–79 Meursault.
4. **[[thomas_et_jean_marc_bouley|Bouley]]** — desert-island-list endorsement; Volnay 1ers $55+.
5. **Deepen [[bruno_lorenzon|Lorenzon]]** — already owned, and "peak of his career" says buy more; Champs Martin in either color.
6. **One-bottle flyers**: [[denis_bachelet|Bachelet]] $45, [[roty|Roty]] $49, [[amelie_berthaut|Berthaut]] $55 — all single-cuvée listings, all WK-endorsed.
7. **Skip**: Michel Noëllat (his only pan); Arnoux-Lachaux on price (his own zip-code logic).
