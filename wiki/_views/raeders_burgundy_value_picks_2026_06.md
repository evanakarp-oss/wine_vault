# Raeders Burgundy Value Picks — Top 15 Under $400 (2026-06, v2)

Re-ranked **strictly off what William Kelley actually said on Wine Berserkers**
(`raw/berserkers/William_Kelley/markdown/`), not general reputation. Every pick
below is anchored to a verbatim quote with a file citation. Producers with **no
substantive WB comment were demoted or dropped** even where reputation is strong
(see "Dropped for lack of evidence" — this is where v1 over-rated).

Screened from the **128 Burgundy bottles under $400** in the Raeders snapshot
(`raw/raeders/master_2026-04-25.csv`). No critic scores in the scrape
(`score_wa = 0` throughout), so the ranking is Kelley's *prose*, weighted by:
**(a) wines he says he personally buys, (b) explicit value calls, (c) general
esteem.** Caveat: these are casual forum posts (2016–2023), not WA reviews.

## Kelley's stylistic lens (his words) — the filter behind the ranking

- **Farming above all:** Dugat-Py "have among the best, if not the best, farming
  in the Côte d'Or" (`163471__0013`); his admiration for Ch. de la Maltroye is a
  farming note (`308120__0004`).
- **Value lives in lesser appellations + village/regional tier:** "first rate
  winemaking, serious vineyards and great farming, even if the appellations don't
  have the most name recognition" (`160244__0016`); "older village wines can make
  very smart purchases" (`139791__0018`).
- **Agnostic on whole-cluster** — defends fully-destemmed Mugnier as not lacking
  freshness or perfume (`167959__0026`).
- **Defends modern gentle-extraction Faiveley** (post-2007): "more supple… fruit
  purer… distinctions of site articulated better… aging just fine" (`148077__0005`).
- **Classically-structured, age-worthy whites** are his benchmark (`176078__0091`).
