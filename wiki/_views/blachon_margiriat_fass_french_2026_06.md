# Sébastien Blachon Saint-Joseph "Margiriat" — Fass verdict + French sources (2026-06)

## TL;DR

- **Does Fass like it? Yes — unambiguously.** Lyle Fass names **"Blachon Margariat"**
  one of his **top two Saint-Josephs of the vintage**, alongside the Domaine des
  Pierres Sèches "Revirand" he rates 9.8/Delectable. He also lists Blachon's white
  ("Islaline") among his elite Saint-Joseph blancs. Note Fass's spelling
  ("Margariat") is a typo — the producer's own spelling is **"Margiriat"** (it's the
  street the cave sits on).
- **French sources are thin on *scores*.** No Guide Hachette / RVF rating turns up for
  the *Sébastien Blachon* Margiriat itself; the substantive French material is the
  producer's own dégustation note + the cuvée's technical sheet. Vivino FR shows a
  producer-level ~4.0 but only ~3 reviews on the Margiriat page specifically.
- **Watch the name collision:** there are **two Blachons** in Saint-Joseph (see below).
  The Guide Hachette 1★ entries belong to the *other* one.

## Vault status

- **No producer page yet.** Blachon is a **Tier 1 "onboard now"** candidate in
  [[fass_unmatched_triage_2026_05|the FASS unmatched triage]] (`domaine_blachon`
  slug, cuvées "Margariat / Isaline", $45–$66). Fits Evan's Rhône taste
  (terroir-driven, grower-scale, granite Saint-Joseph).
- **Not in the cellar**, **not in Raeders** — Fass is the only source in the vault
  that touches it.

## What Fass actually said

From [[FASS_Selections|Fass Selections]] (Lyle Fass), in the Pierres Sèches "Revirand" offer:

> "This and **Blachon Margariat** are my top two St. Joseph's of the vintage."

And in "The Birth of Athena" offer, his shortlist of elite Saint-Joseph **blancs** includes:

> "Sebastien Blachon St. Joseph Blanc **'Islaline'**" (alongside Gonon Les Oliviers,
> Gripa Le Berceau, Darnaud, and the Pierres Sèches Cuvée 1930).

So Fass rates Blachon in the same breath as the St-Épine reference set (Gonon, Chave,
Gripa) he constantly benchmarks against — strong implicit endorsement, even though the
offers themselves were built around Pierres Sèches rather than Blachon.

## ⚠️ Two different "Blachon" estates — don't conflate

| | **Cave Sébastien Blachon** *(the Margiriat one)* | **Domaine Blachon** *(EARL)* |
|---|---|---|
| Where | 16 Chemin de Margiriat, **Saint-Jean-de-Muzols** | **Mauves** ("cradle of Saint-Joseph") |
| Who | Sébastien Blachon, est. **2010**, young vigneron | Roger Blachon family; bottling since 1984 |
| Cuvées | **Margiriat**, Islaline/Isaline (blanc), Alban, Pépé | Hommage Roger, Prestige, Tradition |
| Critic trace | producer note only (no Hachette/RVF found) | **Guide Hachette 1★** (Hommage Roger 2011/2012) |

They're almost certainly related (father's domaine in Mauves / son's cave in
Saint-Jean-de-Muzols), but they bottle under separate labels. The Fass triage lumps
"Cave Sebastien Blachon" into the `domaine_blachon` slug — **when the page is created,
keep these as the Sébastien/Cave entity and don't pull in the Mauves Hommage-Roger
Hachette score by mistake.**

## What French-language sources say about the Margiriat

**Producer (caveblachon.fr) — the cuvée, in French:**
- 100% **Syrah**, coteaux de Saint-Jean-de-Muzols; vignes de **15 à 90 ans** (mélange de
  syrah des années **1930** en bas de coteau + plantation **2004** plus haut).
- Rendement ~**30 hl/ha**, **100% grappe entière** (vendange entière), **élevage 12 mois**
  en barriques. C'est la cuvée haut-de-gamme du lieu-dit.
- **Dégustation (note maison/cavistes):** *« Nez très expressif, intense, floral — violette
  et litchi, puis des notes plus fraîches d'eucalyptus. Attaque ronde, tanins bien
  intégrés avec de la gourmandise, bouche suave et d'un beau volume. »*
- Service **16–18 °C**, carafer ~**1 h**.
- Maison décrite comme jeune vigneron travaillant **dans le respect du terroir et de
  l'environnement**, vinification traditionnelle (pas de certification bio/biodynamie
  trouvée dans les sources — "respect de l'environnement" seulement).

**Aggregators / guides:**
- **Vivino FR** — note producteur ~**4,0** (~898 avis tous vins confondus); la page
  *Margiriat* elle-même ne porte qu'**~3 avis** → échantillon trop faible pour en tirer
  une note fiable.
- **Guide Hachette** — entrées trouvées (1★) concernent **Domaine Blachon "Hommage Roger"**
  (Mauves), **pas** la cuvée Margiriat de Sébastien Blachon.
- **RVF** — aucune note spécifique trouvée pour le Margiriat.

## Bottom line for Evan

Fass loves it and it slots cleanly into your Saint-Joseph / granite-Syrah lane, but the
**French press hasn't really covered this specific cuvée** — it's a small, young grower
bottling, so the best "French source" is the vigneron's own sheet (whole-cluster, old
granite vines, floral/eucalyptus St-Épine-leaning profile). If you want a French *critic*
benchmark, the nearest scored Blachon is the other estate (Domaine Blachon, Mauves) in
Guide Hachette — not the same wine. Good candidate to finally turn into a producer page.
