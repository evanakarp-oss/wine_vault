# Di Costanzo

Two-person family project of site-specific, single-vineyard Cabernet Sauvignon,
founded with the **2010 vintage** by owner-winemaker **Massimo Di Costanzo** (with
wife Erin). Massimo grew up in an Italian household in Berkeley, studied at UC Davis,
and apprenticed at Tignanello (Tuscany), Adi Badenhorst (South Africa), and Clos de
los Siete (Argentina) before serving ~2.5 years as assistant winemaker to Andy
Erickson at **Screaming Eagle** — but his own label is the deliberate inverse of that
opulent house style. He describes the goal as "a little more finesse and natural
acidity": picking at reasonable brix for all-natural acidity, organic/native yeast,
low sulfur, little-to-no fining or filtration, and no technological manipulation.
Named a Wine Enthusiast *40 Under 40* in 2018.

This is squarely in the **sense-of-place + tension** Napa/Sonoma bucket
(see [the restrained-Cab canon](../_views/restrained_napa_sonoma_cab_canon_2026_06.md)) — a
farming-driven, cool-site, lower-alcohol counterpoint to the cult-hedonist tier.
**A MUST per Evan.**

## Vineyards

- **Farella Vineyard (Coombsville)** — one of Coombsville's older vineyards,
  holding the **oldest Cabernet vines in the appellation** (planted late 1970s by
  the Farella family). Napa's coolest AVA; a source of proven longevity and purity.
  The flagship bottling.
- **Caldwell Vineyard (Coombsville)** — a special Cabernet block that vintner John
  Caldwell offered Massimo in August 2019, **previously farmed by Randy Dunn** (of
  Dunn Vineyards) before Dunn bought Eagle Summit on Howell Mountain. Debut vintage
  released with the 2018 fall release.
- **Montecillo Vineyard (Moon Mountain District, Sonoma)** — west-facing, windswept
  site high above Sonoma Valley on the ridgeline dividing Napa and Sonoma; old vines,
  elevation, volcanic soils, **dry/organically farmed**. The same distinctive mountain
  site that anchors the restrained-Cab canon (also sourced by Bedrock and
  [[arnot-roberts|Arnot-Roberts]]).

## Notes

- **Style axis:** finesse, reasonable brix, all-natural acidity, no fining/filtering,
  minimal SO2 — site-transparent rather than amped. Fits Evan's flipped (2026-05-29)
  Napa taste filter: rugged, farming-driven, terroir-distinct; the Dunn / Corison /
  Ridge Monte Bello reference set, *not* Harlan/Schrader/SQN/Colgin.
