# Domaine Gallety

The reference estate of the **Côtes du Vivarais**, a small, cool, limestone
appellation on the **western bank of the southern Rhône** in the **Ardèche** —
geographically between Cornas to the north and Châteauneuf-du-Pape to the south,
just south of Montélimar and west of the river. Founded in **1974** by **Alain
Gallety** (who began farming with his father that year) and now run with his son
**David-Alexandre**, the domaine works **~15 hectares** of clay-limestone
vineyards in the commune of **Saint-Montan**. Farmed **organically since 1979** —
decades ahead of the appellation — and made with a deliberately **non-interventionist**
hand in the cellar. Côtes du Vivarais only earned AOC status in **1999**; Gallety
is the producer who proved the zone could make serious, age-worthy wine, and it
is the bottle that put the appellation on the map.

This is squarely in Evan's wheelhouse: **terroir-driven, organic, grower-scale,
value, and genuinely undercovered** — a cooler-climate, higher-altitude
expression of southern-Rhône grapes that drinks well above its price and its
AOC's reputation. Imported to the US by **Kermit Lynch**.

## Terroir

- **Location:** Saint-Montan, southern Ardèche (postal 07220), western Rhône
  Valley. The cellar is built **into the hillside below the vineyards**.
- **Altitude:** high-sited plateau vineyards — meaningfully **cooler** than the
  southern-Rhône valley floor across the river, which is what gives the wines
  their freshness and an unusually **elegant, lifted expression of Grenache**.
- **Soils:** predominantly **clay-limestone** (argilo-calcaire) — rolled pebbles,
  stony clay, and ancient alluvial deposits over limestone. Vine age **~40–45
  years**.
- **Why it matters:** limestone + altitude on the *west* bank is the structural
  reason the reds carry tension and the whites carry minerality. It is the same
  "limestone outpost of the southern Rhône" logic that makes Eric Texier's
  **Brézème** (east bank, Drôme) compelling — see the terroir-neighbors view below.

## Wines

- **Côtes du Vivarais Rouge** — the flagship. Roughly **50% Syrah / 50% Grenache**.
  Syrah brings dense structure, pepper, and savory aromatics; Grenache brings
  stoniness and ripe juiciness but in an unusually **fine-boned** register thanks
  to the cool site. Aged in **Burgundian barrels** (largely neutral wood) for
  finesse; racked **by gravity**. Built to age.
- **Côtes du Vivarais "Fûts de Chêne"** — an oak-aged *cuvée* (reds, and in some
  vintages a barrel-fermented white) representing a more ambitious *élevage*.
- **Côtes du Vivarais Blanc** — **~50% Grenache Blanc / 30% Marsanne / 20%
  Roussanne**. Fermented in stainless with **malolactic blocked**, giving a
  clean, taut, mineral, precise white. (A "Fûts de Chêne" barrel-aged white
  appears in some vintages.)

## Notes

- **Style axis:** sense of place + tension. Cool-site southern-Rhône varieties on
  west-bank limestone — fresher, finer, more savory than the AOC's modest
  reputation implies. Organic since 1979, gravity-racked, neutral-leaning *élevage*.
- **Why it hasn't "blown up":** the appellation is tiny and obscure (AOC only since
  1999), the Ardèche reads as bulk-country IGP to most buyers, and Gallety is the
  lone serious name — so the wine still sells on quality rather than hype. A
  drink-now-and-cellar value.
