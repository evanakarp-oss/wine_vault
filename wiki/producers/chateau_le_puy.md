## Notes

**Château Le Puy** is one of Bordeaux's most distinctive biodynamic estates, operating within the smaller Right Bank appellation of Francs-Côtes de Bordeaux and known for producing elegant, food-focused Merlot-based reds that explicitly reject the region's typical weight and extraction. CSW describes the estate as "often referred to as the Burgundy of Bordeaux" — positioning it squarely within a philosophy emphasizing terroir refinement, acidity preservation, and age-worthiness over the power-focused approaches dominant in modern Bordeaux. The estate exemplifies biodynamic Bordeaux farming (still a minority position in the region) and represents Evan's specific Bordeaux taste filter: "WK-style undercovered/value with great farming."

**Producer Philosophy & Biodynamic Commitment.** Le Puy operates under biodynamic certification and practices, making the estate one of a small minority of Bordeaux producers pursuing this comprehensive farming philosophy. CSW notes position the estate as part of their active support for "estates in Bordeaux that practice organic and biodynamic farming, still unfortunately a very small minority in the region" — framing biodynamic Bordeaux as genuinely countercultural relative to conventional Pauillac/Pomerol industry practice. This positioning aligns perfectly with Evan's stated preference for "great farming" and biodynamic approaches. The farming philosophy directly influences winemaking: lower yields from biodynamically-managed vines produce concentrated, complex fruit with natural structure rather than requiring extraction-based concentration.

**Terroir & Appellation Position.** Le Puy operates in Francs-Côtes de Bordeaux, a smaller Right Bank denomination known for overlooked, terroir-driven estates. Merlot, Cabernet Franc, and Malbec are the core varieties. The appellation's soils are clayey-limestone blends that naturally produce wines with freshness, acidity, and mid-weight structure — the antithesis of ultra-ripe, extracted modern Pomerol. CSW's positioning of Le Puy as "Burgundy of Bordeaux" reflects the estate's commitment to expressing specific terroir character (fresh acidity, mineral precision, food-pairing elegance) rather than pursuing internationally-styled power. The appellation is significantly undervalued relative to nearby Pomerol/Saint-Émilion, presenting exceptional value positioning for collectors seeking authentic Bordeaux at reasonable prices.

**Winemaking & Signature Expressions.** Le Puy's approach emphasizes: **natural fermentation**, **minimal new-oak influence** (traditional use of older barrels), **traditional fining and bottling practices**, **no over-extraction**. The resulting wines show Merlot's natural character — plum, red cherry, earth, leather — without the oak or extraction that dominates modern Pomerol. The flagship "Emilien" bottling represents the estate's core quality statement, produced from vineyard parcels selected for quality and terroir expression. Alcohol levels are moderate (typically 12.5-13.5%) relative to modern Right Bank practice, reflecting the philosophy of balanced, food-focused wine rather than hedonistic power.

**Critical Recognition & CSW Positioning.** Le Puy commands CSW championed status (3 articles, 1 dedicated) despite minimal Raeders presence, indicating positioning within specialty retailer networks and terroir-focused venues rather than mainstream fine-wine channels. CSW's "Burgundy of Bordeaux" framing and library offer (offering aged Château Le Puy Emilien bottles at retail) confirm sustained vendor enthusiasm and collector interest. The absence of Raeders tracking suggests limited secondary-market demand — consistent with undiscovered value positioning rather than cult collector focus. CSW's active support for the estate reflects philosophical alignment with natural wine/biodynamic farming values.

**US Market & Availability.** Imported by Neal Rosenthal Selections (a prestigious importer known for terroir-focused European producers), Le Puy has specialty retailer presence aligned with natural wine and biodynamic farming communities. Neal Rosenthal's reputation as a quality-focused, philosophically-driven importer (rather than volume-driven commercial distributor) positions Le Puy within serious collector networks. CSW's library offers of aged bottles confirm retail access and mature-bottle availability at reasonable pricing — suggesting strong value retention relative to cult Pomerol/Saint-Émilion comparable pricing.

**Aging Potential & Cellar Strategy.** Le Puy's Merlot-based reds show solid aging potential: minimum 8-10 years cellaring before peak drinking; optimal window 15-20 years from vintage; extended aging to 25+ years in top vintages. The Burgundy-like freshness and acidity (relative to modern Pomerol power) actually enhance aging — high acidity preserves wines and allows development of secondary complexity. CSW's library offering of aged Emilien bottles confirms successful historical aging. Current releases are well-positioned for immediate enjoyment (food-pairing elegance) while also supporting extended cellaring projects.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Château Le Puy exemplifies core taste filters: (1) **biodynamic farming** — certified, documented commitment; (2) **WK-style undercovered/value** — genuinely overlooked appellation (Francs-Côtes vs. prestige Pomerol), exceptional value-for-quality positioning; (3) **great farming approach** — biodynamic methods applied to Bordeaux context; (4) **food-focused, age-worthy structure** — the opposite of opulent, extraction-driven modern Bordeaux; (5) **Neal Rosenthal representation** — a philosophically-aligned importer choice; (6) **CSW champion status** — extraordinarily championed despite remaining undervalued. Le Puy should be a regular acquisition for Evan's Bordeaux cellaring strategy — both for current food-pairing exploration and for serious aging projects seeking authentic, biodynamically-farmed Right Bank Bordeaux at exceptional value positioning.
