# Mastroberardino

Mastroberardino is among the most historically significant and quality-focused producers in Campania (southern Italy), pioneering the modern rehabilitation of Campania's distinctive indigenous white varieties (Greco di Tufo, Fiano di Avellino) and red wine (Taurasi) at a time when southern Italian wine was perceived as commodity production. The estate represents both historical continuity (the family has farmed since 1878) and modern technical excellence.

## Campania Context & Mastroberardino's Historical Role

Campania, centered on Naples and encompassing the volcanic regions around Vesuvius, possesses one of Italy's most ancient winemaking traditions — viticultural activity dates to Etruscan times. However, modern Campania had become marginalized by more-famous Italian wine regions, and the indigenous varieties (Greco, Fiano, Aglianico) were often dismissed as rustic, inferior expressions.

**Mastroberardino's role in the 1980s-2000s was revolutionary:** the estate demonstrated conclusively that Campanian white varieties, when grown and vinified with care, could rival northern Italian whites for complexity and ageability, and that Taurasi (the regional red) could age as beautifully as Barolo.

## Estate History & Proprietorship

The Mastroberardino family began winemaking in **1878** in Atripalda (Avellino), initially as grape growers and wine producers. The modern era of quality-focus began in the 1970s-1980s under **Antonio Mastroberardino**, whose vision was to elevate southern Italian wine. His children and grandchildren have continued this commitment, making Mastroberardino a multi-generational quality project.

## Terroir & Vineyard Holdings

Mastroberardino works approximately **100 hectares** across Campania's finest sites:

**Greco di Tufo** — white wine from the **Tufo zone** (northeast of Naples); predominantly Greco grape on volcanic soils near the Picentini mountains. Greco naturally produces high-acidity, mineral-driven whites.

**Fiano di Avellino** — white wine from the **Avellino zone** (inland); Fiano grape on volcanic/limestone soils. Fiano produces rounder, more structured whites than Greco.

**Taurasi** — red wine from the Avellino hinterland; Aglianico grape (a powerful, tannic red related to Nebbiolo) on volcanic soils. Taurasi is Campania's most serious red wine designation.

The volcanic geology (influenced by historical Vesuvius activity) imparts distinctive minerality and spice to the wines — characteristics that Mastroberardino has successfully expressed through careful winemaking.

## Farming & Sustainability

Mastroberardino practices **sustainable viticulture** with commitment to environmental stewardship:
- Hand-harvested grapes in select parcels
- Limited chemical inputs
- Soil conservation and terracing maintenance (the steep Campanian hillsides require constant care to prevent erosion)
- Adaptation to climate stress (volcanic regions experience both drought and flooding extremes)

While not formally biodynamic-certified, the estate's practices reflect genuine commitment to long-term vineyard health.

## Winemaking: Campanian Variety Mastery

Mastroberardino's winemaking emphasizes the distinctive characteristics of Campanian varieties:

**Greco di Tufo (White):**
- Cool fermentation in stainless steel to preserve aromatic character
- Minimal oak aging (some wines see stainless only, others brief neutral-cask aging)
- Result: high-acidity, mineral-driven white that is food-friendly and age-worthy (10-20+ years)

**Fiano di Avellino (White):**
- Similar stainless-steel fermentation for freshness
- Slightly warmer fermentation temperature than Greco to develop roundness
- Result: structured white with herbs, stone fruit, and mineral complexity; ages 15-25+ years

**Taurasi (Red):**
- Extended maceration (15-25 days) to extract tannins from Aglianico skins
- Long barrel aging (18-24 months in French/European oak)
- Result: powerful red with spice, dark fruit, firm tannins; ages 25-40+ years beautifully

## Production Scale & Market Position

Production is substantial (500,000+ bottles annually), making Mastroberardino larger than boutique estates yet smaller than industrial operations. This scale allows quality focus while maintaining economic sustainability.

## Critical Standing & International Reputation

Mastroberardino has achieved significant critical recognition:
- **Parker/Advocate ratings:** Consistent 90+ scores for top wines
- **Italian wine guides:** Top-ranked among Campanian producers
- **Auction presence:** Older Taurasi vintages (1990s-2000s) appear regularly at fine-wine auctions
- **Historical recognition:** Documented in Italian wine encyclopedias as architects of Campania's modern reputation

CSW documentation from 2023 indicates ongoing relationship and appreciation for the estate's quality and historical significance.
