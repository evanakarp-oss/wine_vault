# Ockfenholz

Ockfenholz (Weingut Ockfenholz) represents the extreme end of Mosel artisan winemaking: a micro-production estate (fewer than 500 cases annually) in the village of Ockfen, working old vineyard parcels with organic farming and minimal-intervention winemaking philosophy, producing dry Rieslings of extraordinary purity and complexity that exemplify the Lower-Middle Mosel's potential.

## Ockfen Context: Overlooked Excellence

Ockfen sits in the Lower-Middle Mosel, a zone historically overlooked in favor of the more prestigious Middle Mosel villages (Zeltingen, Urzig, Bernkastel). However, Ockfen possesses excellent south-facing vineyard sites with slate soils that, in the hands of quality-focused winemakers, yield remarkable wines. The village's relative obscurity has allowed serious producers like Ockfenholz to pursue quality without the prestige-marketing burden that affects more-famous neighbors.

## Vineyard Sites & Old-Vine Focus

Ockfenholz works primarily the **Ockfener Bockstein** vineyard, a south-facing slate slope with old-vine plantings (many vines 50-70+ years old). Old vines naturally yield lower quantities but concentrate flavor into exceptional complexity and mineral expression — a signature of serious Mosel producers.

The slate geology produces wines of extraordinary minerality: the weathered slate imparts "pencil shaving," "flint," and "slate dust" aromatics that are the hallmarks of exceptional Lower-Middle Mosel Riesling.

## Organic Farming & Minimal Intervention

Ockfenholz practices **organic viticulture** with biodynamic-aligned practices (not formally Demeter-certified, but philosophically aligned):
- Hand-harvested grapes
- No synthetic pesticides or herbicides
- Composting and soil-building practices
- Biodynamic preparations used in vineyard management
- Yields kept deliberately low to concentrate fruit

## Winemaking: Dry Riesling & Natural Wine Methods

Ockfenholz's winemaking exemplifies natural-wine philosophy applied to Mosel Riesling:

**Fermentation & Cellar:**
- **Wild yeast fermentation** — no commercial inoculants; fermentation proceeds from ambient microbes
- **Extended lees contact** — wines remain on gross lees for 6-12 months, developing autolytic complexity
- **Fully dry fermentation** — fermentation continues to dryness, typically < 2 g/L residual sugar
- **Minimal sulfites** — only trace amounts at bottling
- **Unfined & unfiltered** — wines retain natural cloudiness from fine lees; clarity develops gradually with bottle age
- **Old-oak aging** — uses traditional large casks (500-1,200 liters), neutral in flavor impact but allowing controlled oxygen exchange

**Style:** Ockfenholz's Rieslings are bone-dry, intensely mineral, and food-friendly. They emphasize slate minerality, white flowers, and subtle citrus balanced by impressive acidity. Early on, they can show some reductive character (struck flint) that resolves with decanting or aeration. These are wines for serious collectors and restaurateurs seeking dry-wine expressions with remarkable aging potential.

## Production Scale & Philosophy

Production is truly micro (250-400 bottles annually) — a scale comparable to garage wines in Bordeaux. This tiny production reflects philosophical commitment: the winemaker is willing to accept severe economic constraints in pursuit of uncompromising quality and minimal intervention.

## Regional Position & Market Presence

Ockfenholz has achieved significant reputation among natural-wine enthusiasts and serious Mosel collectors:
- Strong presence in European natural-wine markets and specialist retailers
- Presence at German natural-wine fairs and events
- Cult following among sommeliers and wine collectors seeking dry, natural Mosel Riesling
- Very limited US distribution (likely through specialty natural-wine importers or direct relationships)

The winery has not pursued commercial distribution; instead, reputation has developed through word-of-mouth and natural-wine community recognition.

## US Importation & Availability

Ockfenholz has minimal documented US distribution. The micro-production scale and natural-wine philosophy suggest limited availability, likely through specialized natural-wine importers or direct-to-consumer relationships. Wider retail discovery may be limited.

## Cellar Fit & Taste Alignment

**Excellent fit.** Ockfenholz exemplifies:
- **Terroir-driven:** Ockfener Bockstein slate soils are expressed through minimal-intervention, dry-wine production
- **Grower-scale:** Micro-production (250-400 bottles annually) on old-vine parcels
- **Organic-aligned:** Organic farming with biodynamic practices
- **Natural-wine methods:** Wild fermentation, minimal sulfites, unfined/unfiltered
- **Dry-wine discipline:** Complete fermentation to dryness emphasizes mineral purity
- **Ageability:** Old-vine Rieslings demonstrate 20-30+ year aging potential
