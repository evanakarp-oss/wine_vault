# Vincent Ledy

**Alain Michelot's nephew** (Alain Michelot is the historic Nuits-Saint-
Georges domaine; the lineage is direct). Based in the Hautes-Côtes de
Nuits, but the marquee bottling is the NSG 1er Cru Les Porets-Saint-Georges
Vieilles Vignes that came to him in 2013. Lyle Fass: *"the Modern and the
Traditional — a modern notion of how to work the land to create the best
grapes, a traditional approach to winemaking."* Holds wines back from
release until he thinks they're ready (the 2018s shipped at the same time
as most producers' 2019s).

## Style — important for the Vosne-styled NSG question

Lyle Fass: *"I once compared Ledy's style to the great Henri Gouges and
this is the wine that made me think Gouges as soon as I smelled it. Like
red cherries in pastille clay."*

**This locates Ledy explicitly in the WK "Nuits Nuits" tier**, not the
Vosne-styled tier. The Porets-Saint-Georges parcel is also on the
**south side** of NSG (in the Porets/Pruliers/Saint-Georges complex),
not the Vosne shoulder.

Tasting comp Lyle places Ledy in: *"a lineup of the top NSG —
Mugneret-Gibourg NSG Chaignots, Gouges Vaucrains/Les St. Georges,
Chevillon Vaucrains/Les St. Georges."* Two of those (Gouges, Chevillon)
are the WK "Nuits Nuits" anchors; the Mugneret-Gibourg is the Vosne-
styled outlier. Lyle places Ledy nearer the Gouges/Chevillon end.

**Implication for the framework:** Ledy is FASS's *south-side / "Nuits
Nuits"* reference, not its Vosne-styled NSG. The user already owns the
Vosne-styled FASS NSG ([[maison_pierre_brisset|Brisset]] Aux Thorey);
Ledy Porets-Saint-Georges would complete the FASS "Nuits Nuits" leg of
the three-styles tasting triangle.

## Holdings

- **NSG 1er Cru Les Porets-Saint-Georges Vieilles Vignes** — flagship.
  85+ year old vines. **24 hl/ha yields** (vs. ~37 avg for NSG 1ers).
  No new oak. Came to him in 2013; split into VV and Jeunes Vignes
  cuvées.
- **Bourgogne Hautes-Côtes de Nuits "La Vacherotte"** — Fass: *"drinks
  like a brilliant NSG village wine."* Sometimes split VV / JV.
- **Savigny-lès-Beaune**
- **Chorey-lès-Beaune "Les Beaumonts"** — Lyle's notes flag a client
  blind-tasting result where the 2015 finished ahead of two Corton Grand
  Crus and a Beaune 1er Cru.
