# Cascina delle Rose

## Notes

**Cascina delle Rose** is a small, organic Barbaresco producer in Neive (Piedmont) run by **Giovanna Rizzolio**, who pioneered organic farming in Barbaresco during the 1980s-90s when women rarely farmed grapes and organic viticulture was virtually unknown in Italy—the estate has become a benchmark reference for the tiny, elite "Rio Sordo" and "Tre Stelle" crus of Neive, producing what CSW describes as "ethereal" Barbaresco. The Rizzolio family has owned Cascina delle Rose since the late 1940s, cultivating modest vineyard holdings (3.6 hectares total, ~20,000 bottles annual production) on the limestone-rich soils characteristic of Barbaresco's finest sites. Organic viticulture emphasizes natural pest management (copper and sulfur only when necessary), no synthetic chemicals, hand harvesting with selective sorting, and modest yields to concentrate phenolic ripeness and aromatic complexity in the small Nebbiolo berries. Winemaking follows a traditional Barbaresco approach: native yeast fermentation in temperature-controlled conditions (not manipulation, but natural Piedmont cellar management), extended maceration on skins for tannin development, and aging in a mix of barrels (primarily neutral or older barrels, not excessive new oak) for 18–30 months before release. The flagship expressions include **Barbaresco Tre Stelle** (Evan owns the 2019 vintage at $75.99, with a drinking window of 2025–2034; typical current-release pricing $60–90) and **Barbaresco Rio Sordo** (similar pricing, both built for 15–25+ year cellaring)—Wine Advocate scored the 2021 vintage at 92 points, reflecting quality consistency. CSW championed Cascina delle Rose with a dedicated article (star) describing Rizzolio as a pioneering force in Piedmont organic farming and her Barbaresco expressions as distinctive, terroir-driven benchmarks. Roscioli's profile page (with winemaker video, established 2022) confirms international collector interest and import-network representation. The estate's organic-farming commitment (pioneered during an era when organic Barbaresco was rare), small-scale production, terroir focus on elite Neive crus, and Rizzolio family legacy align directly with Evan's documented Piedmont taste filter (terroir-driven, biodynamic-leaning, grower-scale producers).
