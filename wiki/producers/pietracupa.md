# Pietracupa

Founded in 1990 and situated in Montefredane, near Avellino in mountainous Campania, Pietracupa gained a reputation within Italy and abroad for some of the finest Greco and Fiano wines around. Winemaker Sabino "Sammy Bruno" Loffredo built this reputation through careful vineyard work and respectful winemaking. The vineyards are situated at altitudes of 300–500 meters above sea level in the Irpinia area, with soil composition including volcanic deposits that provide ideal conditions for Aglianico ripening and white grape freshness. The Pietracupa Taurasi, the winery's flagship red, spends two years in large oak barrels followed by three years in bottle before release—a patient schedule befitting wines designed to age 15+ years. The resulting wine erupts with notes of blackberry, dark cherries, wild violets, heather, dried sage, leather, and earth—the terroir of Irpinia expressed through Aglianico.

## Notes

**Greco and Fiano mastery.** Pietracupa built its reputation through white-wine excellence, challenging the assumption that Taurasi reds dominate the region. The facility with Greco di Tufo and Fiano di Avellino suggests Loffredo understands Campanian viticulture at a fundamental level.

**Irpinia elevation strategy.** The 300–500 meter elevation range in Montefredane creates the temperature and acidity conditions that define modern Campania. Higher elevations deliver fresher, more mineral-driven whites; volcanic soils provide structure for reds.

**Patient Taurasi aging.** The 2-year large-oak + 3-year bottle regimen is deeply traditional—a schedule that trusts the wine to develop rather than rushing to market. This is the opposite of modern short-barrel-aging trends. Wines designed for 15+ years suggest Loffredo believes in Taurasi's longevity.

**Terroir through tasting notes.** The descriptor list—blackberry, dark cherries, violets, heather, sage, leather, earth—reads like a geological profile of Irpinia. This suggests Loffredo's winemaking philosophy prioritizes terroir expression over fruit concentration or oak influence.

**"Barolo of the South" credentials.** Taurasi's nickname—the Barolo of the South—applies squarely to Pietracupa's approach: structured, age-worthy reds from a specific mountain region, farmed with respect, and vinified traditionally. This is not trend-chasing but deep regional identity.

**Cellar fit.** Pietracupa is essential for any serious Taurasi exploration. The combination of red-wine focus, patient oak aging, and established reputation make this a reference-point producer. The dual white-wine strength (Greco, Fiano) adds versatility. Likely available through specialty importers despite minimal CSW presence. Top-tier Campania selection.
