# The Eyrie Vineyards

_The founding estate of Oregon Pinot Noir. David Lett planted the first Willamette Valley Pinot Noir and Pinot Gris in 1965; son Jason Lett now farms the original dry-farmed, own-rooted vineyards. Restrained, cool-toned, remarkably age-worthy Pinot and pioneering Pinot Gris in a deliberately un-showy register._

## Notes

Historically and stylistically the anti-cult Oregon estate: dry-farmed, low-alcohol, patient, built to age (old Eyrie Pinot is legendary). The Pinot Noir '16 ($42) and Chardonnay/Pinot Gris in the [[auction_261W_week30_wheelhouse_2026_07|Week-30 261W sale]] are the value curiosities; the estate is a natural onboarding for the cellar's cool-climate axis.
