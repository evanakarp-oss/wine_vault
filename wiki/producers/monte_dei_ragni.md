# Monte dei Ragni

Small artisan winery in Fumane (Verona province) run by Zeno Zignoli, representing five generations of Ragno family winemaking tradition. Name derives from 19th-century land register reference to "spider hill" (ragni = spiders) in Fumane location. Just 8 hectares total land (2 planted vines); rest cultivated with olive trees, cereals, fruit trees, vegetables creating biodiversity. Produces ~6,000 bottles annually: Valpolicella, Amarone, Ripasso (occasional), Garganega white. Chemical-free viticulture using traditional techniques; often employs horses and animals in vineyard work.

## Notes

**Heritage scale:** Five-generation family operation; 2-hectare vineyard constraint creates scarcity premium. Cult status reflects quality-over-volume commitment. Polyculture approach (olives, cereals, fruit) reflects integrated farmland philosophy. Animal-powered cultivation honors traditional methods. Zeno's stewardship balances heritage preservation with contemporary natural viticulture.
