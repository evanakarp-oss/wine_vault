# Igni

A sommelier-driven project sourcing premium Aglianico del Vulture from the Picini family, one of the region's finest growers. Igni is a collaborative venture between Jeff Porter and Shelley Lindgren, two of America's most Italian-focused sommeliers. Their mission centers on highlighting the unique volcanic soils and mineral expression of Italy's most compelling wine terroirs. Working with the Picini family—established growers on the slopes of Monte Vulture—Igni produces a small-lot Aglianico that champions the wine's elegance and food-friendly structure while fully expressing the Vulture denomination's distinctive mineral grip and aging potential.

## Notes

The Igni Aglianico is sourced from two premium vineyards in the communes of Forenza and Maschito, plantings worked by the Picini family on the mineral-rich volcanic slopes of Monte Vulture. Grapes are hand-harvested in late September and early October; fermentation occurs in steel tanks with indigenous yeast at approximately 28°C, with a ten-day maceration period. The wine then ages for twelve months in used barriques and tonneaux, followed by an additional eighteen months in bottle before release. Annual production is approximately 6,500 bottles. The resulting wine shows layered cherry and strawberry, hints of violets and grilled herbs, a medium body, and a fresh palate with bright red fruits and distinctive mineral notes characteristic of the Vulture terroir. Igni's collaborative approach—pairing American sommelier expertise with multigenerational Italian vineyard knowledge—exemplifies the emerging wave of quality-focused, terroir-transparent producers bringing Basilicata's volcanic Aglianico to international recognition.
