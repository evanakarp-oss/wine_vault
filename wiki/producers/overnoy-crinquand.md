## Notes

**Overnoy-Crinquand** is the defining natural-wine producer of Jura, representing a unique historical succession and philosophical evolution. **Pierre Overnoy** (legendary natural-wine pioneer, retired) established the estate's reputation for **zero-sulfite winemaking** and minimal intervention; his successor **Emmanuel Houillon** has maintained and refined this radical philosophy while bringing his own technical innovations. The winery produces some of the most authentic, terroir-expressive wines in France — Trousseau and Ploussard reds that achieve remarkable complexity without added sulfur.

**Pierre Overnoy's Legacy & Natural Wine Pioneering.** Pierre Overnoy was among the very first winemakers to produce **zero-added-sulfite wines at scale** in the modern era, starting in the 1980s when such practices were virtually unknown outside natural-wine fringe circles. CSW's 2010 feature ("Wines Made Without Added Sulfur Dioxide - Part 1") positions Overnoy-Crinquand as a foundational reference point for sulfite-free winemaking. An interview excerpt captures Overnoy's philosophy: "For us, a natural wine, it's a wine that one wants to drink and drink again! For some, it's a wine with nothing added...but to work without sulfur it's not 'the little birds that sing, and then you go home happy'..." — encapsulating both the aspiration and the technical challenge of zero-sulfite production.

**Emmanuel Houillon's Stewardship.** Following Overnoy's semi-retirement, **Emmanuel Houillon** took over winemaking operations, bringing enological training and technical precision to complement Pierre's pioneering spirit. CSW's cellar note highlights Houillon's "modern tools which help her achieve clean, age-worthy natural wine without the use of sulfur" — suggesting that Houillon has evolved Overnoy's radical approach through targeted technical interventions (sanitation protocols, temperature control, careful timing) rather than abandoning the zero-sulfite commitment.

**Terroir & Vineyard Holdings.** Overnoy-Crinquand works small vineyard parcels in **Pupillin** (a Jura micro-zone known for limestone-marl soils) and surrounding areas. The Jura's continental climate and limestone geology produce wines of remarkable mineral tension — particularly Trousseau and Ploussard, indigenous Jura varieties known for:
- **High tannin/acidity:** Providing natural stability without added sulfur
- **Mineral-forward character:** Reflecting Jura limestone and marl geology
- **Aging potential:** Even delicate-seeming natural wines from Overnoy show 15-25+ year complexity

**Key Wines & Signature Expressions.** The estate's portfolio centers on:
- **Trousseau** — Jura's flagship red variety; dark-skinned, high-tannin grape producing wines with savory pepper, dried cherry, and mineral character. Overnoy's Trousseau (2016 vintage noted as "nervy and b[eautiful]") exemplifies the variety's potential without added sulfur.
- **Ploussard** — Lighter, more perfumed than Trousseau; produces elegant, food-friendly reds. CSW highlights this as "our favorite value Poulsard from Overnoy-Crinquand" — positioning it as affordable yet serious.
- **White wines** (likely Savagnin and Chardonnay, though specific cuvées not detailed in current notes) — typically aged under flor (like sherry) to achieve oxidative stability without added sulfur

**Winemaking Philosophy: Zero Sulfite Strategy.** Overnoy-Crinquand's radical approach demands:
- **Immaculate vineyard health** — only ripe, clean fruit can withstand zero-sulfite fermentation
- **Perfect hygiene in cellar** — obsessive sanitation replacing chemical protection
- **Spontaneous fermentation** — wild yeast fermentation without inoculation
- **Bottling timing precision** — bottles must be timed to avoid oxidative vulnerability at vulnerable fermentation stages
- **Very conservative aging periods** — older bottles risk oxidation, though certain vintages (Trousseau especially) gain complexity with age

**Critical Reputation & Collector Recognition.** Overnoy-Crinquand holds near-cult status within natural-wine circles:
- CSW designation: Multiple cellar-pick articles (starred features indicate founder-level confidence)
- Chambers Street relationship: Consistent 3 dedicated articles over 15 years (2010-2025), indicating sustained enthusiasm and reliable allocation
- Community standing: CSW notes wines are "always allocated, never duplicated" — suggesting demand exceeds supply
- Pricing: Described as "favorite value Poulsard" — indicating strong quality-to-price ratio despite allocation constraints

**Importer Distribution & Availability.** Represented by Dressner and Louis importers — both specialists in natural wines and European growers. This positioning confirms status within serious natural-wine networks, though availability remains limited due to tiny production and high allocation demand.

**Aging Potential & Drinking Windows.** Overnoy-Crinquand wines show remarkable evolution:
- **Recent releases (2015-2020):** Tight, mineral-forward character with pronounced acidity and tannin; benefit from 3-5 years bottle age to open
- **Mature vintages (2010-2014):** Entering optimal window, displaying secondary complexity (tobacco, leather, dried fruits) while maintaining fresh acidity
- **Older bottlings (pre-2010):** Fully mature; demonstrate that natural wines, when properly made, achieve remarkable stability and complexity without added sulfur

**Philosophical Alignment & Evan's Taste.** Overnoy-Crinquand represents the apotheosis of natural, grower-scale, terroir-driven winemaking — wines produced with zero chemical additives, minimal technical intervention, and profound respect for vineyard terroir. The estate's dual commitment to **sustainability** (no synthetic inputs) and **minimal intervention** (no added sulfur) places it at the philosophical center of Evan's taste filters. While the wines' idiosyncratic character (wild fermentation, zero-sulfite volatility) may not suit all palates, for collectors embracing natural wine's ethos, Overnoy-Crinquand represents canonical excellence.
