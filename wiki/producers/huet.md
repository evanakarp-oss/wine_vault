## Notes

Domaine Huet is the benchmark estate of Vouvray and one of the world's great Chenin Blanc addresses, operating as a masterclass in how a single grape and region can express terroir across an entire spectrum of sweetness levels and aging trajectories. The domaine comprises three monopole-scale vineyard sites (Le Haut-Lieu, Le Mont, Clos du Bourg) — each farmed biodynamically since 1990 (Demeter-certified since 1999) and each bottled across four sweetness levels: Sec (dry), Demi-Sec (off-dry), Moelleux (sweet), and Cuvée Constance (rare late-harvest, ultra-sweet). The operation is relatively small (12–15 hectares total across the three sites) but produces 40–50 different cuvées per vintage, each representing a distinct terroir-sweetness combination. Huet is owned by the Puech family (Noël Puech is the proprietor), and the wines are internationally renowned and actively traded at auction.

**Vineyard Holdings & Terroir Philosophy.** Huet's three vineyards are distinct terroir expressions within Vouvray:

- **Le Haut-Lieu:** The largest holding (6–7 hectares), on the north bank of the Loire with limestone-rich, clay-based soils. Produces wines with brightness, acidity, and floral aromatics.
- **Le Mont:** A south-facing slope (3–4 hectares) with limestone-dominated soils, producing fuller-bodied, richer Chenins.
- **Clos du Bourg:** A small (2–3 hectare) enclosed vineyard near the village, with mixed limestone and alluvial soils, producing elegant, complex wines.

The biodynamic farming practices include lunar-phase attention, cover crops, minimal sulfur, hand harvesting at optimal ripeness (variable ripeness levels for different sweetness targets), and meticulous canopy management. The philosophy is that the vineyard expression and vintage character should dominate, with winery work kept minimal.

**Winemaking Style & Sweetness Spectrum.** Huet's signature is not a single wine but a system: the same vineyard, same vintage, bottled at four different sweetness levels to showcase how Chenin Blanc can express site across the spectrum. The sweetness levels are achieved through harvest timing (earlier picks for Sec, later for Moelleux, latest for Constance) and fermentation management (some wines fermented dry, others with residual sugar preserved via yeasts stopping naturally or via gentle chilling).

- **Sec (dry):** Typically 1–3 g/L residual sugar, showing citrus, green apple, white flowers, and mineral salinity. Ages 8–15 years, developing complexity.
- **Demi-Sec (off-dry):** Typically 10–20 g/L residual sugar, showing ripe white fruit, honey, and mineral balance. Ages 10–20 years.
- **Moelleux (sweet):** Typically 60–100 g/L residual sugar, showing candied citrus, apricot, honey, and intense botrytis character (in affected vintages). Ages 15–30+ years, developing tertiary complexity.
- **Cuvée Constance (ultra-sweet):** Typically 150+ g/L residual sugar, from the latest-harvested (and sometimes botrytized) lots. The rarest bottling, aged 20–40+ years, developing profound complexity.

**Reputation & Critical Standing.** Domaine Huet is universally recognized as the greatest Vouvray producer and one of the world's most important Chenin Blanc addresses. The wines command high critical scores (Parker scores 90–96 for various cuvées) and active secondary-market trading (auction presence is strong). The Cuvée Constance is iconic — a bottle of 1993 or 1996 Constance is a sought-after collector wine, priced $200–400+. The domaine is allocated in the US through select importers and appears on serious restaurant wine lists.

**Aging Potential — The Defining Feature.** Huet's wines age like no others. Even the Sec bottlings develop complexity after 8–10 years in bottle, showing petrol, honey, and mineral notes. The Demi-Sec is stunning at 10–20 years, reaching silky mid-palate and pronounced honeyed complexity. The Moelleux is a 15–40 year wine, developing layer upon layer of botrytis-derived complexity (candied citrus, dried apricot, acacia honey, tobacco). The Cuvée Constance is a 30–50+ year wine, gaining richness and complexity for decades. A bottle of Cuvée Constance 1959 or 1996 is still drinking beautifully, improving with every year.

**Key Cuvées (Terroir-Sweetness Matrix):**
- **Le Haut-Lieu Sec:** Classic dry Vouvray, citrus and mineral-forward; ages 8–15 years.
- **Le Haut-Lieu Demi-Sec:** Off-dry, balanced, showing honey and white flowers; ages 10–20 years.
- **Le Mont Moelleux:** Fuller-bodied sweet, showing ripe fruit and honey; ages 15–30 years.
- **Clos du Bourg Moelleux:** Elegant, complex sweet, showing botrytis and mineral balance; ages 15–30 years.
- **Cuvée Constance:** The flagship ultra-sweet, from whichever site had the best late-harvest fruit. Legendary ager; 30–50+ year aging curve.

**Sourcing & Allocation.** Huet produces approximately 100,000–150,000 bottles annually across all cuvées, making it relatively available compared to boutique producers. However, allocation is managed (select importers and retailers) and pricing escalates significantly for older vintages and Cuvée Constance. Recent releases (2020–2023 Sec/Demi-Sec) are $20–40; Moelleux are $25–50; Cuvée Constance ($60–80 on release) commands $200–400+ at auction.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Domaine Huet is a canonical reference for multiple reasons: it's Demeter-certified biodynamic (check), grower-scale and terroir-obsessed (check), and produces wines of stunning aging potential. The Loire white focus aligns with Evan's stated interest in "terroir-driven, biodynamic-leaning" producers. The Vouvray Sec and Demi-Sec offer excellent value (fresh aromatic whites at $20–35, ages 8–15 years), while the Moelleux and Constance are cornerstones of a serious white wine cellar. The Cuvée Constance 1993 ($72/bottle in the 261W sale) represents the rare opportunity to buy legendary sweet wine at fair pricing. Huet is not just a producer to track — it's a institution, and allocation should be prioritized.
