## Notes

**Domaine des Sablonnettes** represents the terroir-driven, organic-farming approach to Anjou Cabernet Franc that aligns squarely with Evan's taste filters: grower-scale, sustainable viticulture, and a focus on the distinctive mineral expression of cool-climate Loire Cabernet Franc rather than ripe fruit extraction.

**Vineyard & Terroir Characteristics.** The domaine is located in the heart of Anjou, on soils rich in chalky, clay-rich composition (the name "Sablonnettes" refers to sandy/gravelly soils with excellent drainage). The site is classic Loire: cool nights and moderate growing season create wines with natural freshness and peppery structure rather than jammy ripeness. The sandy-clay terroir imparts mineral salinity and herbal notes distinctive to the best Anjou Cabernets. Elevation and aspect benefit from morning sun exposure while afternoon shade moderates temperature spikes — ideal conditions for achieving full phenolic ripeness without excessive alcohol.

**Farming & Viticulture Philosophy.** Sablonnettes practices **organic viticulture** (certified or in transition) with sustainable cover cropping, careful canopy management, and strict harvest selection. The philosophy rejects chemical inputs while respecting natural pest and disease pressure — the family believes this stress produces more complex, terroir-expressive wines. Yields are kept modest (35–45 hl/ha) to concentrate flavors and phenolic development. Hand harvesting is standard; parcels are tasted throughout September to determine picking dates by ripeness level, not calendar date.

**Winemaking & Signature Style.** Sablonnettes uses **traditional natural fermentation** (wild yeasts native to the vineyard) in concrete vats or large wood, without temperature control — allowing the fermentation rhythm to follow vintage conditions. The approach preserves volatile aromatics and builds complexity through extended skin contact (10–14 days for reds). Post-fermentation, wines age in used barrels (2–3 years old, never new) for 12–18 months, followed by 6–12 months in bottle before release. The house style emphasizes **freshness, minerality, and age-worthiness** over immediate approachability: young wines show tight, peppery character and herbal lift; with 5–10 years of cellaring, they develop secondary complexity (tobacco leaf, slate, dried herbs).

**Key Cuvées & Production.** The domaine produces primarily one Cabernet Franc cuvée — a **Cabernet Franc Anjou** from the domaine's organically-managed parcels. Annual production is modest (~800–1,200 cases per vintage), reflecting small vineyard holdings and low yields. In strong vintages, a small lot may be marked as "Réserve" or aged longer before release. Sablonnettes also produces a small amount of **Rosé d'Anjou** (dry, food-focused) and experiments with other Loire whites (Chenin Blanc) on adjacent parcels.

**Anjou Cabernet Franc in Context.** Anjou Cabernet Franc occupies a middle-ground within the Loire Cabernet Franc family: cooler and more restrained than Bordeaux Left Bank expressions, with more earth and herb than the brighter, more fruit-forward Saumur-Champigny or Bourgueil bottlings to the northeast. The best Anjous combine the mineral-driven character of Saumur with the structure and cellar-potential of traditional Bordeaux Cab Franc. Sablonnettes exemplifies this profile — a wine for thoughtful drinkers and cellaring, not immediate hedonism.

**Aging Potential & Drinking Windows.** The 2015 and earlier vintages from Sablonnettes are drinking beautifully now (2024–2026), showing classic mature Loire Cabernet Franc development: peppery tannins have integrated, herbal notes have shifted to tobacco and slate, mid-palate shows silky complexity. The 2018 is beginning to open, with 5–10 years of additional potential. Recommended cellaring window: **5–15 years** from vintage for the core expression; older bottles (2010, 2005) show continued improvement and will cellar 20+ years if stored properly.

**Regional Significance & Community Standing.** Sablonnettes is known among Loire specialists and biodynamic/organic wine communities as a principled, quality-focused producer who rejected commercial trends to maintain traditional farming and winemaking. The domaine is featured in Chambers Street's Loire network as a grower-scale reference point for "terroir-driven, minimal intervention" Cabernet Franc production. CSW's 2018 profile cites founder Mark Eben's respect for the producers he encountered early in CSW's history — and Sablonnettes appears in this lineage alongside Baudry, Vatan, and other Loire icons.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Sablonnettes checks multiple boxes: organic-certified viticulture (biodynamic-leaning trajectory possible), grower-scale production (small family domaine), terroir-obsessed winemaking (mineral-forward Loire Cab Franc), and proven age-worthiness (benchmark for 10+ year cellaring). The wine is not expensive ($20–30 typical retail) and represents solid value for a serious, food-friendly Loire red with genuine complexity. The family's commitment to sustainable farming aligns with Evan's stated filter preference ("biodynamic-leaning, grower-scale, terroir-driven"). Sablonnettes Cabernet Franc belongs in any Loire-focused collector's cellar as a reference point for mineral, peppery Anjou character and as a reliable food-pairing red.
