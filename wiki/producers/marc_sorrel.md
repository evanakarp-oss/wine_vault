## Notes

**Marc Sorrel** is a **highly respected grower-producer** of Hermitage and Crozes-Hermitage, representing the classical Northern Rhône tradition of terroir-focused, long-aging Syrah (and Marsanne-Roussanne whites). The domaine owns limited vineyard parcels on the famous Hermitage hill and in Crozes-Hermitage, producing wines of remarkable concentration, mineral precision, and aging potential. Sorrel's expression is less famous than Guigal or Chave among casual collectors, but deeply respected by serious Northern Rhône specialists for technical excellence and consistency.

**Vineyard & Terroir Characteristics.** Marc Sorrel's vineyard holdings comprise approximately 6–8 hectares across two distinct terroirs:

**Hermitage:**
- **Granite-based soils** (dominates the top-slope parcels) — contribute mineral precision, peppery spice, and aging potential
- **Limestone-rich subsoils** on lower slopes — add structural depth and complexity
- **Legendary cuvée sites**: ownership/access to parcels in micro-zones including **"Le Gréal"** (granite/mineral-driven) and **"Les Rocoules"** (limestone-influenced, richer)
- **Steep south-facing aspect** (300+ meter elevation) — concentrates sun exposure and creates phenolic ripeness

**Crozes-Hermitage:**
- **Granite soils with alluvial influence** — lighter, more mineral-driven than Hermitage
- **South to southeast exposures** — excellent sun, good ripeness
- Produces fresher, more approachable wines compared to Hermitage

**Farming & Viticulture Philosophy.** Sorrel practices **traditional, sustainable viticulture** aligned with classical Northern Rhône approaches:
- Minimal chemical inputs (copper and sulfur used judiciously for disease management only)
- Natural vigor management through canopy work
- Hand harvesting mandatory (required for quality selection and slope navigation)
- Conservative yields (25–35 hl/ha for Hermitage, 40–50 hl/ha for Crozes) to concentrate aromatics and phenolics
- Ripeness assessment determines harvest dates, not calendar
- Focus on phenolic maturity and acidity balance rather than sheer Brix level

The philosophy emphasizes **vintage character** and **site expression** over house-style homogenization.

**Winemaking & Signature Style.**

**Hermitage (Red):** Sorrel produces traditional, age-worthy Hermitage Syrah:
- Native yeast fermentation in temperature-controlled concrete vats
- Extended skin contact (14–20 days) to extract color, tannin structure, and aromatic complexity
- **Aging in a mix of new and used oak** — typically 30–40% new oak, balance 2–4 year old barrels — allowing oak to amplify rather than dominate terroir
- Aging duration: 18–24 months in barrel, followed by 6–12 months bottle-aging before release
- Bottling unfiltered and unfined to preserve aromatic integrity and natural tannin structure
- Minimal sulfite additions (selective, conservative)

The resulting wines show **power, concentration, and mineral precision** — classic Hermitage character. Primary aromas of dark cherry, plum, and white pepper develop to secondary complexity (tar, licorice, tobacco, mineral slate) with 10–25 years of cellaring. Tannins are firm and structured, requiring patience but rewarding with graceful aging.

**Hermitage (White):** Sorrel produces traditional white Hermitage from Marsanne and Roussanne:
- Native yeast fermentation in oak and steel
- Aging in used and neutral oak (minimal new oak — perhaps 10–20%)
- Shows white flowers, hazelnut, honey, and mineral salinity
- Remarkable aging potential (15–30+ years), developing honeyed complexity and tertiary character

**Crozes-Hermitage:** Fresher, more fruit-forward expression:
- Shorter skin contact (10–12 days)
- Lighter oak influence (20–25% new oak)
- Earlier release (less bottle-aging before shipment)
- Meant for drinking 5–15 years from vintage, while Hermitage requires 10–25+

**Key Cuvées & Portfolio.**
- **Hermitage Rouge "Classique"** — the primary, blended expression from best parcels
- **Hermitage Rouge "Le Gréal"** — single-site selection from granite-dominant parcels; shows mineral lift and peppery character
- **Hermitage Rouge "Les Rocoules"** — single-site from limestone-influenced lower slope; rounder, richer character
- **Hermitage Blanc "Classique"** and parcel selections (if released)
- **Crozes-Hermitage Rouge & Blanc** — broader-appeal, more accessible expressions

Production is **modest** (~600–1,200 cases per vintage across all wines), reflecting limited vineyard holdings and rigorous quality standards. Down to Earth carries the domaine in their portfolio, reflecting quality discipline aligned with Evan's taste preferences.

**Hermitage in Global Context.** Hermitage is the Northern Rhône's most famous and prestigious red wine appellation — arguably the world's finest Syrah expression. The greatest Hermitage wines (from Chave, Sorrel, Guigal, Chapoutier) rival Burgundy's greatest reds in complexity and aging potential. Sorrel's expression is a reference for **mineral-driven, classic Hermitage** — less voluptuous than Guigal's modern style, but showing remarkable elegance and aging potential.

**Aging Potential & Drinking Windows.**

**Hermitage Rouge "Classique":**
- **Minimum cellaring**: 8–10 years from vintage (harsh tannins if drunk earlier)
- **Peak drinking window**: 15–30 years from vintage
- **Extended aging potential**: 40–50+ years in well-stored bottles; no upper age limit

**Hermitage Rouge single-site (Le Gréal, Les Rocoules):**
- **Minimum cellaring**: 10–12 years
- **Peak drinking window**: 18–35 years from vintage
- **Extended aging potential**: 50+ years

**Hermitage Blanc:**
- **Minimum cellaring**: 5–8 years from vintage
- **Peak drinking window**: 15–30 years from vintage
- **Extended aging potential**: 40+ years in well-stored bottles

**Crozes-Hermitage:**
- **Minimum cellaring**: 3–5 years
- **Peak drinking window**: 8–15 years
- **Extended aging potential**: 20+ years in favorable vintages

The 2018–2020 releases are showing beautiful structure; the 2015 vintages are approaching peak drinking window with 15–25 years remaining potential.

**Critical Reception & Community Standing.** Marc Sorrel is **deeply respected by Northern Rhône specialists and serious collectors** — the domaine ranks among the finest traditional Hermitage producers (behind Chave, roughly aligned with Chapoutier's traditional line, respected by Guigal enthusiasts). The wines appear on serious restaurant wine lists and trade actively at auction. Down to Earth's inclusion of the domaine in their portfolio reflects alignment with Evan's quality standards. Pricing reflects quality but represents genuine value relative to peer domaines: Hermitage Rouge $85–120 (typical), single-site selections $120–200, Hermitage Blanc $90–150, Crozes $50–75.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Marc Sorrel embodies core taste filters:

1. **Terroir-driven winemaking** — site expression prioritized over winemaker manipulation
2. **Grower-scale production** — small domaine (~6–8 hectares), hand-harvested, carefully allocated
3. **Traditional approach** — native yeast fermentation, extended barrel aging, minimal intervention
4. **Aging potential** — wines built for 15–50 year cellaring, with proven track record
5. **Mineral, elegant style** — the antithesis of overripe, fruit-forward hedonism

Additionally, Down to Earth's representation confirms the domaine aligns with Evan's curated taste filters.

**Cellar Strategy:** Marc Sorrel is an **excellent Northern Rhône reference** for serious collectors. The domaine should be prioritized for:
- **Hermitage Rouge "Classique" from strong vintages** (2018, 2015, 2010, 2009) — the foundation for long-term Hermitage cellaring
- **Hermitage Blanc** when available — exceptional white Rhône with remarkable aging potential
- **Crozes-Hermitage** as a regular acquisition — solid quality at a more accessible price point ($50–75)
- **Single-site Hermitage selections** (Le Gréal, Les Rocoules) from auctions — focused terroir expressions rewarding extended cellaring

Sorrel is a natural shelf-mate to [[domaine_pierre_gonon|Gonon]], [[marc_sorrel|Sorrel (Crozes)]], and other Northern Rhône growers for building a region-focused, terroir-driven cellar. The domaine represents refined, traditional Northern Rhône Syrah at its finest.
