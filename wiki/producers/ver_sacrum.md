## Notes

**Ver Sacrum** is a pioneer of Rhône-variety winemaking in Argentina, founded by **Eduardo Soler** in 2010 and based in Mendoza. The producer represents a distinctive segment of Argentina's natural-wine movement: recovering undervalued, traditional Rhône varieties (Grenache, Mourvèdre, Cinsault) and pre-phylloxera Argentine varieties (Criolla) through minimal-intervention, low-alcohol winemaking. **Key expressions** include **Grenache**, **Mourvèdre**, and **Cinsault** — all typically produced with whole-cluster fermentation, minimal sulfite additions, and sometimes amphora aging, emphasizing aromatic purity and textural delicacy. The winemaking philosophy echoes Rhône tradition (and French natural-wine practice): preservation of acidity, spice character, and site expression over extraction or new-oak influence. Soler's work demonstrates that Argentina's altitude, continental climate, and old-vineyard parcels can produce elegant, mineral-driven Rhône expressions rivaling southern France in quality — a bold statement that positions Ver Sacrum as a discovery within Argentina's broader shift toward terroir-driven, natural winemaking. According to curator Paz Levinson's Argentina Reloaded (a comprehensive survey of Argentina's emerging natural-wine producers), Ver Sacrum "adds another dimension" to the country's portfolio. US distribution through Skurnik reflects alignment with serious, boutique-focused importer networks. Production remains modest, making allocation-based availability typical for this emerging producer.
