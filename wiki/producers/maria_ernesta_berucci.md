# Maria Ernesta Berucci

Maria Ernesta Berucci comes from a multi-generational wine family rooted in Piglio. Her father Manfredi Berucci was a pioneer of modern Cesanese del Piglio production and heir to the historic Massimi Berucci Company. After a period away, Maria Ernesta decided in 2009 to reclaim the old vineyards and revive Cesanese tradition, partnering with her brother Francesco to vinify grapes from the ancient Colli Santi vineyard under the "L'Onda" label. In 2015, Maria Ernesta and Geminiano merged their vineyards between Piglio and Paliano, establishing a modern winery that transcends conventional organic farming through "holo-homeopathy"—a cousin to biodynamics—combined with spontaneous fermentation and zero additives.

## Notes

**Holo-homeopathy winemaking.** Maria Ernesta practices an innovative farming philosophy that aligns with biodynamic principles but takes a distinct path: holo-homeopathic treatments in the vineyard that work with natural rhythms and plant vitality. This approach goes beyond organic certification to address soil and vine health at a systemic level.

**Indigenous varietal focus.** The winery works exclusively with indigenous Lazio grapes: Cesanese del Piglio (the estate's red centerpiece) and Passerina del Frusinate (white). All fermentations are spontaneous with absolutely no additives—a radical commitment to terroir purity.

**Family heritage and innovation.** The Berucci name carries historical weight in Piglio's wine story, yet Maria Ernesta's approach is thoroughly modern, blending family tradition with cutting-edge natural-wine philosophy.

**Cellar fit.** Strong prospect for Evan's Lazio deep-dive; represents the emerging generation of producers reviving indigenous varieties through biodynamic/homeopathic methods. Cesanese's minerality and age-worthiness deserve exploration.
