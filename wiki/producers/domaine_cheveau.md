# Domaine Cheveau

## Notes

**Domaine Cheveau** is a small Pouilly-Fuissé producer in the southern Burgundy Mâconnais region, recently featured in CSW's articles as a producer of "fantastic White Burgundy" from an appellation historically undervalued by collectors focused on Côte-d'Or sites. The estate cultivates Chardonnay on the limestone (Jurassic) soils of Pouilly-Fuissé, terroirs known for producing mineral, elegant white Burgundy with natural acidity and aging potential that rivals more famous northern sites. The domaine practices sustainable viticulture with careful canopy management and hand harvesting, concentrating yields to favor complexity and natural phenolic development in the grapes. Winemaking follows a traditional Burgundy model with native yeast fermentation and careful oak aging (using primarily used and older barrels rather than new oak) to build complexity while preserving the wine's bright, mineral character—an approach that contrasts sharply with over-oaked, butter-forward California Chardonnay. Key wines include the **Aux Bouthières** 1er Cru (recently celebrated by CSW as "a great Cabernet Franc" in a featured article; current release $30–50 range) and village-level expressions that offer excellent value for age-worthy white Burgundy. Pouilly-Fuissé wines from serious producers like Cheveau typically reward 8–15 years of cellaring, developing secondary honey, almond, and mineral notes while maintaining freshness and complexity. CSW's championing (dedicated article, one star) suggests this producer is gaining recognition as a quality-focused, undervalued source for Pouilly-Fuissé whites—precisely the grower-scale, biodynamic-leaning, terroir-driven profile that aligns with Evan's Loire/Burgundy taste filters.
