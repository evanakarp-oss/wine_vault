# Schiopetto

Iconic modernist producer and pioneer of contemporary white-wine expression in Collio (Friuli), founded 1965 by Mario Schiopetto. 30 hectares producing ~170,000 bottles/year. Three-generation family heritage. Recently acquired by Rotolo family (Volpe Parisini). Known as "master of white wine" in Italy; revolutionary figure who redefined Italian white-wine quality and technique.

## History & Philosophy

Mario Schiopetto founded the winery in 1965, when he rented a property in the hills of Capriva del Friuli. In that founding year, he made history by **barreling his first Tocai Friulano**—a pivotal moment marking the birth of the modern white-wine era in Friuli. This single decision fundamentally transformed the conception of Italian white wine internationally.

Mario Schiopetto is renowned as the **"master of white wine"** in Italy, a visionary who **revolutionized Italian white-wine production in the 1960s** by introducing modern techniques that enhanced the aromatic and structural qualities of grapes. He elevated white wine from a regional commodity to an expression of terroir and technique, establishing Collio as the epicenter of Italian white-wine innovation.

**Legacy**: Three generations of the Schiopetto family have been active in Collio. Today, the winery operates at scale (30 hectares, ~170,000 bottles/year) while maintaining the quality focus and modernist ethos Mario established.

**Recent Ownership**: As of 2026, Schiopetto has been acquired by the Rotolo family (through Volpe Parisini). The transaction details remain confidential, but the acquisition signals continued investment in this historic estate.

## Vineyard & Terroir

The estate spans 30 hectares in Capriva del Friuli, within the Collio Goriziano DOC—one of Friuli's premier white-wine territories. The vineyards benefit from the region's distinct terroir and are farmed biodynamically.

## Key Wines & Philosophy

Schiopetto's portfolio centers on **aromatic white varieties** and expresses the modernist principle that technique and timing are as crucial as the vineyard. The estate's iconic cuvées include:

- **Friulano** (traditionally Tocai) — the flagship; elegant, mineral-driven
- **Sauvignon Blanc** — refined and structured
- Additional varietals leveraging Collio's terroir

## Farming & Biodynamic Practice

The estate practices **biodynamic viticulture**, aligning with Mario's original commitment to enhancing grape quality through innovative, respectful farming.
