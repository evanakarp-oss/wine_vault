# Tenute Dettori

Pioneering biodynamic estate in Oristano, Sardinia, established in the late 20th century by the Dettori family. The winery operates on approximately 60 hectares of vineyard, all certified Demeter biodynamic since the mid-1990s. Tenute Dettori is one of Sardinia's most committed biodynamic practitioners, emphasizing low-intervention winemaking and terroir expression through native yeasts and minimal sulfites. Their flagship wines are built around Cannonau (Grenache) for reds and Vermentino for whites, showcasing the island's volcanic and granitic mineral character.

## Notes

Tenute Dettori exemplifies Sardinia's emerging quality-oriented movement. Their Cannonau wines display elegant structure with wild red fruit, mineral tension, and silky tannins—distinctly different from the riper, more rustic Cannonau tradition. Vermentino bottlings show crisp minerality and herbal complexity. The estate's commitment to biodynamic viticulture and natural winemaking aligns with grower-scale Mediterranean producers; they're a reference point for terroir-focused Sardinian wines.
