## Notes

**Éric Texier** is one of the Northern Rhône's most distinctive and intellectually-engaged winemakers, known for producing age-worthy, mineral-driven reds from ancient Syrah vines across multiple overlooked and resurrected terroirs (Côte-Rôtie, Brézème, St-Julien-en-St-Alban), combined with unique, refreshing white wines that demonstrate his technical sophistication and philosophical open-mindedness. CSW's extraordinary enthusiasm (26 articles, 5 dedicated, aging score of 8 — among the highest) reflects Texier's status as not merely a talented producer but as an intellectually principled figure within natural wine and terroir-focused movements. William Kelley's personal recommendation ("if you've never met and talked to Eric Texier, you should make the trek down to Charnay") confirms his standing as a wine-world personality worth direct engagement.

**Producer Philosophy & Biodynamic Commitment.** Texier practices certified biodynamic agriculture and is described by CSW as "brilliant, highly-opinionated, and sometimes hilarious" — personality traits that drive both his winemaking decisions and his broader engagement with wine-world philosophy. His approach is fundamentally non-interventionist: he carefully sources grapes from old vines in great sites, then applies minimal-intervention winemaking philosophy to allow terroir and fruit character to express naturally. This positioning aligns squarely with Evan's taste filters (biodynamic, terroir-driven, grower-scale, natural wine philosophy). Texier's willingness to work across multiple marginal/resurrected terroirs (Brézème, St-Julien-en-St-Alban) reflects a philosophical stance: pursuing quality in overlooked regions rather than chasing prestige in established appellations.

**Multi-Terroir Approach & Vineyard Sources.** Texier's work spans multiple Rhône regions and styles: **Côte-Rôtie** (his core Northern Rhône expression, sourcing from historic, old-vine sites), **Brézème** (a resurrected appellation that Texier's work has actively rehabilitated), **St-Julien-en-St-Alban** (another marginal site with "Vieille Serine" bottling), **Côtes du Rhône** (overachieving value expressions like the Côtes-du-Rhône-Villages St. Gervais "Vieilles Vignes les Cadinières"), **Châteauneuf-du-Pape** (red and white expressions), **Saint-Joseph** (age-worthy lower-profile Northern expression). This diversity reflects a winemaker committed to exploring terroir rather than optimizing a single prestige bottling. CSW notes that "His work in Brézème and St-Julien-en-St-Alban has resurrected forgotten" terroirs — positioning Texier as actively rehabilitating marginal regions through quality commitment.

**Winemaking & Signature Expressions.** Texier is best known for "age-worthy reds made with ancient versions of Syrah" (CSW cellar note) — wines built for 15-25+ year cellaring with remarkable mineral precision and elegance. His approach emphasizes: **native yeast fermentation**, **extended maceration** (extracting complexity while preserving finesse), **minimum new oak** (or classical use of older barrels), **minimal sulfites**, **unfiltered bottling**. The resulting wines are fresh, mineral-driven, and structured — the opposite of ripe, heavily-extracted, internationally-styled expressions. Notably, Texier also produces "unique and refreshing" white wines (white Rhône varieties, possibly including skin-contact approaches) — a commitment to range and experimentation that reflects his intellectual approach to winemaking. CSW positions his whites as summer-oriented refreshers, suggesting bright acidity and aromatic lift.

**Critical Recognition & CSW Positioning.** Texier commands extraordinary CSW presence (26 articles spanning 2007-2026, 5 dedicated, aging score of 8), with coverage ranging from general producer profiles to specific vintage deep-dives. The breadth and consistency of CSW engagement — across 19 years — reflects sustained vendor enthusiasm for both Texier's quality and his philosophical alignment with CSW's natural wine / terroir-focused positioning. CSW's cellar note emphasizes his dual capabilities: age-worthy reds (the primary reputation) coupled with accomplished white wine work. William Kelley's community recognition (Berserkers mention, personal recommendation for in-person meeting) confirms serious collector respect. Berserkers rank #518 with 2 mentions (early-thread emphasis, faded momentum) — consistent with reputation among pre-2015 collector communities; lower recent engagement may reflect generational shifts in community focus rather than quality change.

**US Market Presence & Raeders Absence.** Imported by both Dressner Selections and Louis Dressner Selections (dual representation suggesting importance), Texier has consistent specialty retailer presence in the US market among natural wine / terroir-focused venues. Notably, no Raeders tracking, suggesting that Texier's wines are primarily distributed through natural wine specialists and premium independent retailers rather than auction/secondary market channels — consistent with direct producer-to-retailer relationships typical of European growers. CSW's repeated offerings (multiple vintages, multiple expressions across 19 years) confirm permanent retailer availability and sourcing priority.

**Aging Potential & Cellar Strategy.** Texier's reds are built for serious aging: minimum 12-15 years cellaring before peak drinking; optimal window 20-30+ years from vintage; no practical upper limit for well-stored bottles from great vintages. CSW's aging score of 8 (among the highest) reflects both the wines' structural capacity and documented track record. CSW's references to offering "traditional wines from the Northern Rhone 2010, 2011 and before" in conjunction with Texier suggest successful aging of mature bottles confirming quality trajectory. White wines are more immediate (5-8 years potential aging) but show remarkable freshness and mineral structure rather than oxidation risk. Current releases across both reds and whites are well-positioned for extended cellaring; older vintages provide reference confirmation.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Éric Texier exemplifies multiple core taste filters: (1) **biodynamic farming** — certified, documented, philosophically driven; (2) **terroir-focused, non-interventionist winemaking** — across multiple regions, emphasizing mineral precision and elegant structure; (3) **grower-scale, vineyard-focused production** — direct sourcing from old vines, avoiding industrial-scale manipulation; (4) **intellectually principled positioning** — a producer engaged with wine philosophy, natural wine movement, resurrecting marginal terroirs; (5) **Northern Rhône specialization** — aligned with Evan's stated Burgundy/Loire/Mosel/Piedmont terroir-focused interest; (6) **CSW champion status** — extraordinarily championed (26 articles, score 8), reflecting strong quality/value alignment. Texier should be a core acquisition focus for Evan's Rhône cellaring strategy — both for his benchmark Côte-Rôtie expressions (representing quality standards for the appellation) and for his experimental work across resurrected marginal terroirs (Brézème, St-Julien-en-St-Alban), which embody serious terroir exploration. His age-worthy reds are essential reference bottles for any serious collector pursuing 20-30 year aging projects.
