# Catherine & Pierre Breton

Pioneering biodynamic Bourgueil and Chinon producer. Since 1985, the Bretons have championed terroir-driven winemaking, organic farming (1990), and biodynamic viticulture. They organized "La Dive Bouteille" (1999), one of the first group tastings celebrating the natural wine movement, with 40 winemakers. CSW describes their wines as both "elegant and ageworthy" and delightfully unpretentious "vins de soif."

## Timeline & Milestones

- **1985** — First vintage; Pierre and Catherine founded the estate
- **1990** — Organic farming certification
- **1992** — First "sans-soufre" cuvée: Bourgueil "Nuits d'Ivresse" (zero-sulfur wine, pioneering for the era)
- **1999** — Organized "La Dive Bouteille," one of the first natural wine movement group tastings (40 winemakers from across France; has grown into an international event)

## Key Wines

- **Bourgueil "Les Perrières"** — flagship, age-worthy cuvée; aged 70-year-old vines on south-facing slope with sand/clay-limestone soils; hand-harvested, wild yeast fermentation, minimal sulfites; 20+ year cellaring
- **Chinon "Saint-Louans"** — elegant, structured Chinon bottling
- **Bourgueil "Nuits d'Ivresse"** — zero-sulfur cuvée (pioneering since 1992); vibrant, fruit-forward style
- "Vins de soif" — lighter, unpretentious daily-drinking expressions alongside the structured, ageworthy bottlings

## Terroir & Winemaking

**Bourgueil/Chinon, Restigné area** — clay-limestone slopes producing structured Cabernet Francs. South-facing aspect, sandy soils over limestone clay base.

**Method:** Hand-harvested, wild-yeast only fermentation, unfined/unfiltered, minimal sulfite additions (pioneering low-sulfur practices since 1992). Long elevage with complexity-building emphasis over fruit-forward profiles.

**Biodynamic Conviction:** The Bretons' 1990 certification preceded the broader biodynamic movement in Loire by years and helped inspire peers across France to adopt biodynamic / organic methods.
