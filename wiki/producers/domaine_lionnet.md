## Notes

**Domaine Lionnet** is one of Cornas's most respected traditional producers, known for producing powerful, structured, age-worthy Syrah from the appellation's most demanding terroirs, using organic farming practices and classical Northern Rhône winemaking methods. CSW's enthusiastic championing (7 articles, 2 dedicated, aging score 5 — among the highest) reflects the estate's status as a reference-level Cornas producer. CSW positioning emphasizes "traditional vineyard work and vinifications producing superb, age-worthy wines of terroir" that "reward drinkers over the next couple of decades" — directly aligned with Evan's taste filters for terroir-focused, age-worthy wines built on organic farming and classical structure.

**Producer Philosophy & Organic Commitment.** Lionnet practices organic certification and employs traditional, conservative Northern Rhône winemaking emphasizing terroir expression over extraction or new-oak manipulation. The estate's approach reflects a philosophical commitment to letting Cornas's steep-slope terroir speak through minimal intervention and careful site selection. CSW notes position Lionnet as part of "a small group of producers in Cornas practicing organic farming" — confirming biodynamic/organic positioning as genuinely countercultural within the appellation. This approach aligns squarely with Evan's stated taste filters: organic farming, grower-scale production, terroir-focused, age-worthy structure.

**Cornas Terroir & Vineyard Character.** Cornas is the Northern Rhône's most serious, age-demanding Syrah appellation, characterized by steep granite slopes, thin soils, and cool microclimate that forces late ripening and acidity preservation. Lionnet's vineyard parcels occupy prime Cornas sites, exposing vines to mineral-driven granite expression, morning shade (preserving acidity), and stress conditions that concentrate fruit character. These terroir factors, combined with organic farming (low yields, stressed vines), produce wines naturally inclined toward 20-30+ year aging. The appellation's challenge — late-ripening, structurally demanding wines — is precisely what Lionnet has mastered through traditional techniques.

**Winemaking & Signature Style.** Lionnet employs classical Cornas winemaking: **whole-cluster fermentation** (preserving fresh aromatics while building structure), **extended maceration** (15-25 days, extracting tannin and phenolic complexity), **aging in older French barrels** (24+ months, not new oak), **minimal sulfites**, **unfined/unfiltered bottling**. The resulting wines show Syrah's characteristic profile —peppery spice, dark berries, herbs, minerals — with a distinctive Cornas signature: tight, structured, mineral-forward character requiring 10-15 years minimum cellaring before opening. CSW describes these wines as "elegant, natural and terroir-expressive" — suggesting brightness and freshness despite structural intensity.

**Critical Recognition & Collector Standing.** Lionnet commands exceptional CSW positioning (7 articles spanning 2014-2025, aging score 5, cellar pick status) reflecting sustained vendor enthusiasm and serious collector respect. CSW regularly compares Lionnet to other Cornas greats (Allemand, Balthazar, Thierry Allemand reference) in vintage reviews, positioning the estate as a reference point for traditional Cornas quality. The complete absence of Raeders tracking (no auction data) reflects positioning within classic wine collector networks and specialty retailers rather than commercial fine-wine circuits — consistent with authentic terroir-focused producer status. CSW's archive of aged-vintage coverage (2010, 2011, 2012 reviews) confirms long-term collector engagement and proven aging track record.

**US Market & Availability.** Imported by Neal Rosenthal Selections (a prestigious importer known for serious, terroir-focused European producers), Lionnet has specialty retailer presence aligned with classic wine collector networks. Neal Rosenthal's reputation for philosophical alignment with natural/organic/traditional winemaking makes Lionnet an obvious fit. CSW's repeated offerings across 12-year coverage period (2014-2025) confirm permanent availability status and vendor priority positioning.

**Aging Potential & Cellaring Strategy.** Lionnet's Cornas shows remarkable aging architecture: minimum 12-15 years cellaring before peak drinking; optimal window 20-35 years from vintage; extended aging potential to 40+ years in top vintages. CSW cellar notes emphasize wines "rewarding drinkers over the next couple of decades at least" — confirming collector expectation of 20-30 year + drinking windows. Documented CSW coverage of 2010, 2011, 2012 vintages (now 15-16 years old) suggests current drinking readiness for older vintages while new releases position for extended aging projects. This combination of proven track record (documented aging reviews) and current quality confirms Lionnet as an essential aging reference.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Domaine Lionnet exemplifies core taste filters: (1) **organic farming** — certified, documented commitment; (2) **traditional, terroir-focused winemaking** — classical Northern Rhône methods emphasizing mineral expression; (3) **age-worthy, structured Cornas** — built for 20-30+ year cellaring; (4) **CSW champion status** — extraordinarily championed (7 articles, score 5), confirming quality-for-value alignment; (5) **Neal Rosenthal representation** — philosophically-aligned importer; (6) **Northern Rhône specialization** — aligned with Evan's stated Burgundy/Loire/Mosel terroir-focused interests. Lionnet should be a cornerstone acquisition for Evan's Cornas cellaring strategy — both as a reference for traditional Cornas quality standards and as a serious long-term aging vehicle. Current vintages (2021+) are positioned for 20-30 year projects; older available vintages (2010s, 2012) provide documented aging confirmation.
