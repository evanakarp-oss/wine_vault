# Duplessis Chablis

## Notes

**Duplessis Chablis** is a father-and-son organic and biodynamic estate in the cool-climate Chablis region producing mineral, age-worthy Chardonnay under deliberately challenging conditions—CSW describes their approach as "doing it the hard way," combining organic/biodynamic farming with Chablis' marginal climate to produce "powerful wines that are built for aging" rather than immediate gratification. The domaine's vineyards sit on the famous Kimmeridgian limestone-clay soils of Chablis, terroirs that naturally produce Chardonnay with mineral salinity, bright acidity, and genuine aging potential when managed with restraint and natural methods. Organic and biodynamic farming in Chablis' cool, disease-prone climate is deliberately challenging (frequent fungal pressure, late budbreak, harvest timing risks); the family's commitment to these methods prioritizes soil health and terroir expression over ease of production—yields are restrained and sulfur inputs are carefully managed to let natural yeast fermentation and oak aging (in older, neutral barrels) showcase the wine's mineral core. The resulting Chablis expressions show bright stone-fruit aromatics, citrus acidity, and textural complexity that reward 8–15 years of cellaring; the CSW cellar note emphasizes these are "powerful wines" with clear aging intent, not refreshing young Chablis for early drinking. Current-release pricing is estimated $25–45 for village-level to 1er Cru expressions—excellent value for organic, biodynamic Chablis of this quality and potential. CSW championed Duplessis with two dedicated articles (stars), highlighting the family's philosophy and commitment to terroir-driven, organically-farmed Chardonnay in a region where many producers abandoned biodynamics for easier farming. The estate's commitment to organic/biodynamic farming, mineral-focused Chablis, and the deliberate difficulty of quality-first viticulture aligns squarely with Evan's documented Burgundy taste filters (terroir-driven, biodynamic-leaning, grower-scale producers).
