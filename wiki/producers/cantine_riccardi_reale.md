# Cantine Riccardi Reale

Piero Riccardi and Lorella Reale, who met while working in television, left the screen in 2010 to establish Cantine Riccardi Reale in Bellegra, southeast of Rome. Their mission: to preserve and elevate Cesanese, one of Lazio's most important indigenous red grapes, now largely forgotten. The seven-hectare estate spans the volcanic and Cretaceous sandstone soils of the Monte Celeste chain between Bellegra and Olevano Romano. Certified organic by Suolo e Salute and farmed biodynamically with hand harvesting, the winery has become a reference point in Italy's natural-wine movement, proving that Cesanese can rival other Italian noble varieties.

## Notes

**The Cesanese mission.** Riccardi Reale exists to save Cesanese del Piglio from obscurity. The biodynamic farming and volcanic terroir produce wines with both complexity and freshness, challenging the notion that only northern Italian reds deserve serious attention.

**Terroir expression.** Dual soil types—volcanic and Cretaceous sandstone—create the duality that defines these wines. The hand-harvested, naturally fermented approach reveals the grape's true character.

**Cellar fit.** Strong candidate for Evan's Lazio exploration; demonstrates how biodynamic farming and indigenous varieties create distinctive, age-worthy reds outside the mainstream canon. Cesanese's rusticity and mineral tension align with the vault's terroir-first ethos.
