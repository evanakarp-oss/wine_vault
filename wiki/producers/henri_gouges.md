# Henri Gouges

One of the **founding domaines of the modern AOC system**. Henri Gouges was
a leader of the 1930s estate-bottling movement and co-architect of the
Burgundy appellation hierarchy. Now run by cousins Grégory and Antoine
Gouges. ~15 ha, all in Nuits-Saint-Georges. Organic since 2008.

## Holdings

Concentrated almost entirely on the structured (southern) side of NSG, plus
the village proper:

- **1er Cru** Les Saint-Georges, Les Vaucrains, Les Pruliers, Les Chênes
  Carteaux, Clos des Porrets-Saint-Georges (monopole), Chaignots
- **Village** NSG (red and white)

## Pinot Gouges

The domaine is the source of a famous **white mutation of Pinot Noir** — a
spontaneous chimera discovered in Clos des Porrets in 1936 and propagated
by Gouges. Sold as NSG Blanc 1er Cru "La Perrière," it is one of the few
white Nuits wines on the market and a curiosity worth tracking. Massale
cuttings have been shared with other producers (notably Bruno Clair, JL
Trapet for their white experiments).

## Style

Old-school: long cuvaison, modest new oak (~20%), patient élevage, late
release. The wines are firm, dark, and reductive in youth — the **textbook
"NSG needs cellaring" experience.** The Atlas's caveat about southern NSG
demanding bottle-age is calibrated against producers like Gouges.

## Why this domaine matters

For the Vosne-adjacent question, Gouges is the **historical anchor** on the
southern (structured) end of the commune — the reference against which
softer, more Vosne-like northern NSG climats (Boudots, Damodes, etc.) are
implicitly contrasted. Owning Gouges Clos des Porrets and a Boudots from
the same vintage is the cleanest possible "two sides of NSG" comparison.
