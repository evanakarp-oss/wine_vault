# Far Mountain

High-elevation Sonoma project founded in 2018 by Chilean winemaker **Rodrigo Soto**
and his wife **Mai Errázuriz**, built on the conviction that the remote, rugged
pockets of the Sonoma-side Mayacamas — the **Moon Mountain District AVA**, due west
of Mount Veeder — can make some of California's most distinctive Cabernet. Red
volcanic, rocky soils; dry-farming and organic/biodynamic practice throughout. This
is the closest spiritual sibling in the vault to the heritage-mountain Cabernet that
[[Bordeaux_Producers|Bedrock]]-style sourcing chases (cf. Montecillo): old vines,
no irrigation, site-transparent rather than opulent.

## Wines

- **`Fission` Cabernet Sauvignon** — mostly **Alta Vista Gold Ranch**, southern Moon
  Mountain District, ~300 m (~1,000') elevation. **~50-year-old vines, never
  irrigated.** The name nods to the textural, mineral imprint of the volcanic soil.
- **The Nunnery Cabernet** — end of Nunn's Canyon (Sonoma-Mayacamas), a terraced
  1970s site at ~1,600' designed and planted by **Phil Coturri**, organic from
  inception, dry-farmed. Spice, red fruit, chalky tannins.
- **`Myrna` Chardonnay** — high-elevation, dry-farmed 1970s plantings.

## Notes

Soto's background spans biodynamic and terroir-driven work across the Americas; Far
Mountain reads as a deliberate counter to the cult-hedonist Napa style — concentration
from elevation, old vines, and farming rather than extraction and oak. Fits Evan's
post-2026-05-29 California-Cab axis (sense of place + tension) cleanly.

US importer: **Vintus**. UK: Liberty Wines. Allocation/direct + fine-wine retail
(e.g. Flatiron, Wine.com).
