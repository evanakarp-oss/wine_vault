# Marco Tinessa

Marco Tinessa (born 1975 in Montesarchio del Sannio, province of Benevento) pursued a career in finance at Bocconi University and worked as a broker in Milan, yet carried a burning passion for wine nurtured since age eighteen. His obsession with natural wine and love of Taurasi led him to acquire a single vineyard of Aglianico near Montemarano, launching Ognostro in 2007. Tinessa's early wines were vinified in Sicily under the mentorship of Frank Cornelissen, whose winery became a standard-bearer for Italian natural wines, until 2017. Now operating a facility on Milan's outskirts where same-day-harvested Campanian grapes are processed with minimal intervention, Tinessa creates compelling interpretations of Campania's terroir using indigenous varieties (Aglianico, Fiano) without chemicals.

## Notes

**Frank Cornelissen connection.** Tinessa's early trajectory mirrors many serious natural winemakers: mentorship under a recognized master. Cornelissen's Sicily operation became Tinessa's winemaking university until 2017, establishing principles that continue to guide his Campanian work.

**Minimal-intervention philosophy.** No chemicals, same-day transport from vineyard to winery, minimal intervention throughout—Tinessa's approach prioritizes grape and terroir expression over winemaker intervention. This requires meticulous vineyard work to justify the rest.

**Dual regional focus.** Ognostro produces both Aglianico (Taurasi territory) and Fiano (Avellino white), capturing two of Campania's noble varieties. The ability to work both genres suggests versatility and deep regional understanding.

**Milan-based production.** Operating a winery on Milan's periphery while sourcing Campanian grapes is unconventional but allows Tinessa creative freedom and access to resources. Same-day harvesting to city vinification is logistically demanding but worthwhile for minimal-intervention philosophy.

**US Market Portfolio & House Style.** Via Sager and Wine and Grand Cru Selections, Marco Tinessa's Ognostro project offers two key expressions: Ognostro Rosso (Aglianico) and Ognostro Bianco (Fiano)—capturing both sides of Campania's noble-varietal palette. The Ognostro Rosso, sourced from a single vineyard near Montemarano, is described as deep, intense, and full-bodied, with notes of dark berries, spices, cinchona, Mediterranean herbs, and undergrowth complexity. On the palate, it balances elegant power with remarkable freshness and perfectly integrated tannins. Tinessa grows Aglianico at astonishingly low yields and often harvests well into November to achieve phenolic ripeness without compromising Aglianico's high natural acidity. Production utilizes clay amphorae or high-density polyethylene tubs—unconventional vessels chosen specifically to prevent any influence on wine and terroir expression. The Milan-based winery (on the city's outskirts) sources same-day-harvested Campanian grapes, a logistically demanding commitment to the minimal-intervention philosophy he learned from Frank Cornelissen until 2017. **Cellar fit.** Marco Tinessa represents the emerging generation of Taurasi naturalists—serious, well-trained, connected to mentors like Cornelissen.
