# Azienda Agricola Valentini

Legendary Abruzzo estate, producer of Italy's mythic Trebbiano d'Abruzzo. Family grape-growers in Abruzzo since 1650 (Spanish origin); modern bottling operation founded in 1956 by Edoardo Valentini (1927–2006), whose uncompromising commitment to terroir and aging potential transformed regional understanding of the Trebbiano and Montepulciano d'Abruzzo appellations.

## Vineyard & Production

170 acres (~69 hectares) of vineyards in the Loreto Aprutino hills, high in Abruzzo's interior. Remarkable selectivity: Valentini produces only when conditions align perfectly. The estate makes approximately three wines — Trebbiano d'Abruzzo (white), Montepulciano d'Abruzzo (red), and Cerasuolo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo (dry rosé) — but releases them infrequently. The white appears in roughly 7 of 10 vintages; the red even less. Only about 5% of the annual harvest meets Valentini's standards for bottling.

## Winemaking Philosophy

Traditional farming with minimal intervention; organic and biodynamic practices. Extended aging in large wood and concrete vessels. No filtration. The resulting wines possess remarkable intensity, complexity, and aging potential that have become legendary in wine circles. Valentini wines can age for decades, often improving significantly over 20–30 years in cellar.

## Legend: Edoardo Valentini

Edoardo Valentini (1927–2006) set out to prove that Abruzzo's native varieties could produce wines rivaling northern Europe's greatest expressions. He crafted ultra-traditional wines in an era when few believed in the region's potential. His Trebbiano became iconic — sought after by collectors and described as having unprecedented depth and longevity. Since Edoardo's death, his son Francesco continues his father's exacting standards without compromise.

## Accessibility & Mystique

The winery offers no visits, tastings, or direct sales, adding to its mystique. Wines are released through select import channels. Allocation scarcity and the producer's refusal of commercial pressure have made Valentini bottles objects of veneration in serious cellars worldwide.

## Notes

Valentini occupies a unique position in Italian wine: a producer of such renown and selectivity that bottles trade on the secondary market with significant premiums. The legendary status of the Trebbiano d'Abruzzo, combined with the estate's refusal to compromise on quality or expand production, makes Valentini a benchmark for understanding Abruzzo's capacity for ageworthy, terroir-driven wine. A bottle of aged Valentini Trebbiano is among the greatest expressions of white wine produced anywhere.
