## Notes

**G.B. Burlotto** is a historically significant Barolo producer in Verduno (a historic Barolo sub-zone) specializing in classical, whole-cluster fermentation and extended skin-maceration techniques that preserve traditional winemaking methods and yield intensely structured, age-worthy expressions. Run by the Alessandria family (maestro Fabio Alessandria notably featured in CSW tasting visits), Burlotto represents a philosophical commitment to **pre-1960s Barolo methodology** (whole-cluster, foot-pressing, long maceration on stems) that William Kelley has specifically documented as restoration of historical practice. The producer has achieved remarkable recent momentum in the Berserkers community: rank 73 (18 total mentions with 13 mentions just in 2023-2026, representing a 13.0× acceleration spike), underscoring a dramatic rediscovery by serious collectors seeking traditional, structure-driven Barolo.

**Terroir & Vineyard Philosophy.** Burlotto operates in Verduno, one of Barolo's historic but less-famous villages, with flagship holdings in **Monvigliero** (whose 2019 vintage scored a perfect 100 points in auction catalogs) and **Acclivi** (2019 vintage: 97 points). Verduno's terroir emphasizes mineral precision and structural complexity rather than immediate opulence. CSW notes the estate practices **organic farming (uncertified)** and has resisted commercial trend-chasing over recent decades, preferring to "nurture Verduno's uniqueness" through consistent quality commitment rather than marketing noise.

**Winemaking Philosophy.** Burlotto's signature is **classical whole-cluster fermentation** with extended skin maceration (15-40+ days, a practice abandoned industry-wide in the 1960s but preserved here), foot-pressing of grapes, and minimal modern intervention. William Kelley specifically notes: "The grapes are pressed by foot and — crucially — it undergoes an extremely long maceration on the skins," emphasizing how this technique distinguishes Burlotto from modern destemmed, shorter-maceration competitors. The result is wines of extraordinary structure, white-pepper spice, herbal complexity, and mineral precision—characteristics of historical Barolo that predate the modern extraction-focused era.

**Key Wines & Critic Recognition.** The flagship **Barolo Monvigliero** is the house star, scored 100 points in auction catalogs for the 2019 vintage with tasting notes emphasizing "wild strawberry, aromatic herb, white pepper, orange peel, hazelnut, truffle essence." The 2019 **Barolo Acclivi** scored 97 points, noted for "elegant floral intensity" and "lifted delicacy." These scores represent extraordinary recognition for a producer that eschews modern marketing, underscoring genuine quality alignment with collectors who value transparency and classical technique.

**Collector Momentum & Rising Status.** Burlotto has experienced a remarkable collector rediscovery in 2023-2026 (13× momentum acceleration), suggesting serious wine professionals and collectors are recognizing the value and philosophical alignment of whole-cluster, classical Barolo in an era of increasing interest in traditional methods. The combination of CSW Cellar Pick designation, William Kelley documentation, and Berserkers momentum indicates Burlotto has transitioned from "overlooked insider pick" to "rising-star status" within serious collector networks.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Burlotto exemplifies core Evan Barolo filters: classical/traditional/old-guard methodology (whole-cluster fermentation, extended maceration), mineral-driven site transparency, structural complexity, and long aging potential (20-40 years). The Verduno positioning (historically significant but less commodified than Serralunga or Monforte) and organic farming commitment make Burlotto an essential discovery for collectors building serious, philosophy-aligned Barolo cellars.
