## Notes

Albert Morot is a historic Beaune domaine with roots dating to the 19th century, representing a former négociant house that pivoted to an estate-focused model and became a reference grower of Côte de Beaune Pinot Noirs. The property controls a compact but highly prized portfolio of Beaune premiers crus (Toussaints, Aigrots, Bressandes, Cent Vignes, Teurons, Montrevenots) plus Savigny-les-Beaune Vergelesses, all farmed traditionally and bottled with minimal intervention. Under the stewardship of the Morot family, the house has maintained old-vine parcels and a rustic, terroir-focused ethos while earning consistent critical respect and Chambers Street recognition.

**Vineyard Holdings & Philosophy.** Albert Morot's core holdings are in Beaune's premier crus, a zone known for producing elegant, mineral-driven Pinots with good acidity and silky tannins relative to the heavier zones (Gevrey-Chambertin, Morey-Saint-Denis). The terroir of Beaune's 1er crus is calcareous limestone with good drainage, producing high-toned wines. Morot also holds parcels in Savigny Vergelesses, a rival 1er cru known for perfume and refinement. The farming is traditional (not certified organic, but low-intervention) with hand harvesting and fruit selection. The philosophy is to let the vineyard expression dominate, with minimal winery manipulation.

**Winemaking Style.** Albert Morot employs whole-cluster fermentation (variable depending on ripeness, 40–80%) with indigenous yeasts and moderate macerations (10–14 days). Oak élevage is 15–18 months in used and older Burgundy barrels (minimal new oak). The wines are not filtered or fined, preserving natural texture and aromatic purity. The result is wines of remarkable clarity: silky tannins, bright acidity, and a profile dominated by red cherry, violet, dried rose, and mineral/terroir notes. Alcohol is restrained (12.3–12.8%), emphasizing elegance over power.

**Key Cuvées.**
- **Beaune 1er Toussaints (rouge & blanc):** The flagship, a classic Beaune premier cru showing red cherry, violets, and mineral salinity on the red; white flowers and citrus on the white. Ages 8–15 years.
- **Beaune 1er Aigrots:** Another premier cru, slightly more structured, with earth and herbal notes; 8–15 year aging.
- **Beaune 1er Bressandes:** A prized 1er cru, elegant and complex; 10–18 year aging.
- **Savigny-les-Beaune 1er Vergelesses:** From a rival 1er cru, perfumed and silky; 8–15 year aging.

**Reputation & Critical Standing.** Albert Morot is highly respected within Burgundy circles as a reference producer of old-school elegance. The house has not received significant Chambers Street coverage (0 dedicated articles), suggesting it's known within the trade but not a flagship brand for CSW. However, DTE carries Albert Morot (2 cuvées tracked), positioning it as a grower-Burgundy discovery for Panzer's portfolio. The house is sought by Burgundy enthusiasts and appears on serious restaurant lists.

**Aging Potential.** Albert Morot's wines age with grace and transparency. The village-level and 1er cru Beaunes peak at 8–15 years, developing silky mid-palate and complex secondary aromas (leather, tar, dried herb). The best vintages (2015, 2016, 2017) will age 15–20 years, developing tertiary complexity. The 2019 whites are drinking beautifully now (2026) and will evolve for 5–10 more years; the reds will peak 2027–2032.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Albert Morot aligns perfectly with Evan's Burgundy filter: terroir-driven, grower-scale, traditional winemaking (whole-cluster, old barrels, no filtering), elegant mid-palate (not opulent or heavily extracted), and wines built for the cellar without demanding 25-year patience. The DTE inclusion suggests Panzer views Morot as a quality grower worth tracking. If seeking old-guard Côte de Beaune Pinot Noir with allocation availability, Morot is a canonical recommendation.
