# Flavio Roddolo

A "grand elder of the Langa" (Slow Wine) farming at Bricco Appiani in Monforte
d'Alba. Roddolo works without any products other than copper and sulfur, in an
uncompromisingly old-school style; the wines are not flashy or ingratiating and
the Nebbiolos in particular need a lot of time. Chambers Street rates him among
the top tier of traditional Barolo producers — under-the-radar largely because
he works with the reticent importer Jan D'Amore rather than a big name. JP cited
him for "more powerful wines": clean but earthy, with a rustic edge, demanding
patience.
