# Azienda Agricola Musto Carmelitano

A third-generation organic estate in Maschito on the eastern slopes of Monte Vulture, Musto Carmelitano produces classically structured, mineral-driven Aglianico wines from vineyards planted on volcanic pyroclastic tuff. The family has farmed these vineyards for generations; the estate was formally established in 1985, though many of the current vines date back much earlier. Siblings Elisabetta and Luigi Carmelitano have managed the property since 2005, transitioning to full organic farming and natural winemaking principles—spontaneous fermentation with indigenous yeasts only, minimal processing, no fining or stabilizing agents. Their philosophy centers on allowing the volcanic terroir and aged vines to express their full potential in the bottle.

## Notes

The estate works approximately four hectares of low-yielding Aglianico vines planted on the eastern Vulture slopes in Maschito, a hill village at the heart of the denomination. Siblings Elisabetta and Luigi Carmelitano have managed the property since 2005; many of the estate's oldest vines were planted by their great-grandfather over 100 years ago, lending depth and complexity to bottlings from these heritage parcels. In the US market via North Berkeley Imports, the producer offers a focused portfolio: Pian del Moro DOC, Serra del Prete DOC, Maschitano Rosso (standard Aglianico), Maschitano Rosato, and Maschitano Bianco, plus a special no-added-sulfites Aglianico bottled only in exceptional vintages from the estate's oldest 100+ year-old vines. Total annual production averages 20,000 bottles. The volcanic substrate—pyroclastic tuff layered with ash and lapilli—creates the signature mineral grip and aging potential the region is known for. The Carmelitanos learned winemaking from family elders rather than formal training, bringing pragmatic, multi-generational knowledge to their natural-winemaking approach and organic viticulture. This hands-on, family-centered philosophy has positioned Musto Carmelitano among the new generation of terroir-focused producers revitalizing Basilicata's reputation for world-class Aglianico.
