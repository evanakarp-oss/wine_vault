# Hudelot Noellat

## Notes

**Hudelot-Noëllat** is a prestigious Vosne-Romanée producer currently run by winemaker **Charles van Canneyt**, producing some of Burgundy's finest and most sought-after Grand Cru and 1er Cru Pinot Noirs from legendary vineyard parcels in the Côte de Nuits. The domaine controls key parcels across multiple appellations including the extraordinary **Romanée-Saint-Vivant** (Grand Cru), **Clos de Vougeot** (Grand Cru), **Chambolle-Musigny 1er Cru**, and multiple **Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru** sites (Beaux Monts, Suchots)—each vineyard contributing distinct terroir characteristics while maintaining the house's signature elegance and age-worthiness. Farming practices emphasize organic viticulture with modest, natural yields to concentrate phenolic ripeness; some parcels are managed with massale selections of Pinot planted in the 1950s, contributing old-vine complexity and structure. Winemaking follows traditional Burgundy methods: native yeast fermentation, extended maceration to extract structured tannins while preserving aromatic complexity, and aging in French oak that allows the wine's mineral and red-fruit character to emerge clearly. Current-release pricing ranges significantly by cuvée ($49 for village-level reds to $1,199 for Romanée-Saint-Vivant; DTE tracks 12 expressions from 2022–2023 vintages), reflecting the dramatic quality hierarchy from village to Grand Cru sites. Berserkers rank shows Hudelot-Noëllat at #39 among Top 10 cellar producers with strong rising momentum (1.88× post-2023 / 26 total mentions), indicating strong collector demand and enthusiasm. William Kelley has praised recent vintages (particularly 2016, 2017, 2018) as "exquisite" and worthy of serious cellaring; older bottles from the 1980s and 1990s are highly sought at auction. Henderson Selections' representation in the US confirms the domaine's import priority status among serious Burgundy specialists. The estate's reputation as a Grand Cru master, consistent quality, and organic-farming commitment represent the apex of Burgundy production and directly align with Evan's documented Burgundy taste filter (terroir-driven, age-worthy, grower-scale quality).
