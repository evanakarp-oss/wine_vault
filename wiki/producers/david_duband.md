## Notes

David Duband is a leading voice in organic viticulture across Burgundy's Côte de Nuits, farming his estate biologically since the late 1980s (officially certified organic since 1997). His vineyard parcels are concentrated in Gevrey-Chambertin, where he holds old-vine holdings in several premier cru sites, plus smaller parcel stakes in Chambolle-Musigny and Vosne-Romanée. The philosophy is radical transparency: minimal new oak (often using 2–4 year old barrels or none), natural ferments with indigenous yeasts, no filtering, and no fining agents. Duband believes the vineyard work — biodynamic compost, hand-harvesting, and green harvesting for balance — should do the heavy lifting; the winery's job is to get out of the way.

**Farming & Terroir.** The vineyard sites he works are glacial-moraine soils of the Côte de Nuits, high in clay and limestone. His Gevrey parcels span multiple crus: Estournelles (1er cru, structured, savory), Les Evocelles (village-level, silky), and blended cuvées from various sites. All farmed with cover crops, minimal sulfur additions, and meticulous canopy management. The sandy, well-draining parcels of Chambolle-Musigny (Aux Echanges cru) produce perfumed, ethereal wines; his Vosne holdings are more structured.

**Winemaking Style.** Whole-bunch fermentation is common (50–80% depending on ripeness), with natural ferments at cool temperatures (14–16°C initially, allowing slow aromatic extraction). Maceration is typically 12–16 days; aging is 12–16 months in used or very old barrels. The result is archetypal grower Burgundy: silky tannins, mineral precision, aromas of red cherry, forest floor, and often white pepper or graphite depending on the site. Alcohol hovers around 12.5–13%, and the wines age beautifully for 10–20 years in bottle, developing tertiary spice and earthy complexity.

**Reputation & Allocation.** Duband's reputation inside Burgundy circles is stellar — he's considered one of the quality standard-setters for organic growers, respected by sommeliers and collectors for his integrity and consistency. Production is small (roughly 8,000–10,000 bottles annually across all cuvées), so allocation is tight in the US. The wines are sought after by Pinot Noir purists and natural-wine advocates who respect the minimal-intervention philosophy.

**Aging Potential.** The village-level Gevrey and Chambolle can be approachable at 3–5 years but peak at 8–15 years. The premier crus (Estournelles, Aux Echanges) and special cuvées (Vosne) benefit from 5–10 years of bottle age, developing silky mid-palate and persistent finishes. The 2015s, 2016s, and 2017s are at their best now (2026); the 2018s will be beautiful from 2028 onward.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Duband sits at the intersection of Evan's filters: terroir-driven, organic-leaning, grower-scale, biodynamic-inspired (not certified, but farming principles are aligned), and emphasizing elegance and minerality over extraction or ripeness. The wines pair perfectly with the Loire growers and other Burgundy organic producers in the cellar. If exploring Burgundy at the quality bar without the Côte d'Or price premium, Duband is the canonical grower recommendation.
