## Notes

**Daniel Vollenweider** is one of the Mosel's finest Riesling specialists, based in the Wolf/Wolfen region and recognized for **Prädikat-level** (botrytis and late-harvest) Riesling production alongside dry expressions. The producer has been featured at Chambers Street Wines consistently since 2015, with CSW championing him as a "Mosel Master" — placed in the company of celebrated producers like Busch, Haart, Willi Schäfer, and Stein. **Key expressions** emphasize **Riesling with residual sugar** — from elegant **Kabinett** through **Auslese** and beyond — produced from Wolf's limestone-rich vineyard sites, which impart distinctive mineral character and aromatic complexity. Vollenweider's flagship vineyard is **Goldgrube** (meaning "gold-mine"), a south-facing slope known for producing wines of remarkable finesse, minerality, and aging potential. The producer exemplifies classical Mosel tradition: cool fermentation preserving aromatic compounds, natural residual sugar from incomplete fermentation (not chaptalization), and aging in traditional tall, narrow Mosel bottles. While sweet Riesling is sometimes dismissed by dry-wine advocates, Vollenweider's expressions demonstrate that properly-made Prädikat wines offer remarkable complexity, food-compatibility (especially with Asian cuisine), and 20–40 year cellaring potential. CSW notes indicate Vollenweider's wines are worthy of serious collectors' attention for their balance of sweetness, acidity, minerality, and structural integrity. US distribution through Skurnik and Terry Theise reflects alignment with serious German-wine importers.
