## Notes

**Domaine Sigaut** represents a distinctive profile within Burgundy's contemporary producer landscape: a boutique grower focused exclusively on the Côte de Nuits' two most elegant appellations (Chambolle-Musigny and Morey-Saint-Denis), producing wines that prioritize minerality, precision, and age-worthiness over the power or extraction that characterizes many modern Burgundies. The producer operates at deliberate, estate-controlled scale, with carefully curated premier cru holdings that allow for focused terroir expression.

**Vineyard Holdings & Premier Cru Focus.** Sigaut's portfolio centers on multiple premier cru lieux-dits within Chambolle-Musigny and Morey-Saint-Denis:
- **Chambolle-Musigny 1er Cru Les Fuées** — a limestone-rich parcel known for elegance and aromatic complexity
- **Chambolle-Musigny 1er Cru Les Noirots** — another premier cru expression, likely more structured
- **Chambolle-Musigny 1er Cru Les Sentiers Vieilles Vignes** — old-vine designation, suggesting concentration and depth
- **Morey-Saint-Denis 1er Cru Les Charrières & Les Millandes** — the eastern terroir expression

This holdings structure allows Sigaut to explore terroir diversity within a focused geographical range, rather than spreading across multiple regions.

**House Style & Minerality Focus.** The DTE pricing ($69–$135) suggests a producer that is quality-serious but not prestige-driven — wines that offer complexity without commanding trophy-pricing. The emphasis on premier cru (vs. grand cru or village-level) reflects a philosophical stance: these are wines that require study and cellaring, suited to collectors who appreciate Burgundy's terroir subtlety rather than seeking iconic status symbols.

**Chambolle-Musigny & Morey-Saint-Denis Character.** These two appellations share a reputation for elegance within the Côte de Nuits' power hierarchy: wines with silky tannins, aromatic complexity, and excellent aging potential despite their apparent approachability when young. Chambolle-Musigny (the more northerly, slightly warmer zone) produces more perfumed, floral expressions, while Morey-Saint-Denis (slightly cooler) offers more structure and mineral precision. Sigaut's positioning across both appellations suggests a producer who understands these terroir distinctions.

**DTE Relationship & US Market Position.** The exclusive DTE relationship ($69–$135 pricing range, 9 cuvée/vintage entries tracked) indicates a curated, direct-import arrangement typical of Panzer's approach: small quantities, careful producer selection, and pricing that reflects actual quality rather than market inflation. The DTE presence suggests Sigaut appeals to wine professionals and serious collectors who trust Panzer's curation rather than seeking label status.

**Contemporary Burgundy Positioning.** In an era when some Burgundy producers have succumbed to international market pressures (higher alcohol, heavier extraction, new-oak influence), Sigaut's focus on elegant minerality positions them within the "classical Burgundy" school — producers who prioritize terroir expression and food-friendliness over critical scores or collector cachet. **Emerging Reputation & Collector Discovery.** The absence of CSW coverage (unusual for quality Burgundy producers) suggests Sigaut has built its reputation through direct relationships, importer recommendations, and collector word-of-mouth rather than through public marketing.
