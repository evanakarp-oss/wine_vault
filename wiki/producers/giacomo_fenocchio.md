# Giacomo Fenocchio

Historic Monforte d'Alba estate based in the Bussia cru, run by Claudio
Fenocchio, who took over from his father Giacomo in 1989 and resisted the
modernist tide of short fermentations and new barriques. Resolutely
traditional: organic farming, long fermentations, and aging in large Slavonian
oak casks. Crus include Bussia, Villero, Cannubi and Castellero, plus the
famed "Bussia 90 Dì" bottling (90-day maceration). A grower-scale farm of the
kind JP flagged — a "master of craft," not a large winery.
