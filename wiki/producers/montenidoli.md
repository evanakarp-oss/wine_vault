# Montenidoli

## Notes

**Montenidoli** is a small, multi-varietal Tuscan estate in San Gimignano known primarily for producing exceptional **Vernaccia di San Gimignano** white wines that have elevated the appellation's reputation and quality ceiling—CSW describes Montenidoli as one of "Chambers longest adored wines" and credits the estate with transforming Vernaccia from "pretty dull stuff" to a serious, age-worthy white capable of genuine complexity and regional distinction. The domaine cultivates vineyards on the volcanic, limestone-clay soils of San Gimignano, terroirs naturally suited to producing Vernaccia with bright acidity, mineral salinity, and aging potential when managed with organic practices and restrained yields. Farming practices emphasize natural pest management, hand harvesting with selective sorting, and modest yields to concentrate aromatic complexity and phenolic development in the grapes; while formal certifications are not specified in current records, the estate's practice aligns with organic viticulture principles. Winemaking emphasizes Vernaccia's natural character through native yeast fermentation, careful oak aging (primarily neutral or older vessels to preserve the variety's mineral core), and minimal sulfite additions; the resulting wines are bottled unfiltered and unfined to maintain aromatic purity. Signature expressions include **Vernaccia di San Gimignano Tradizionale** (classic bottling; DTE: 2023 at $25), **Vernaccia di San Gimignano Fiore** (2023 at $32 via DTE), and **Vernaccia di San Gimignano Carato** (premium aged expression; 2021 at $45 via DTE—likely reflects extended aging before release). Montenidoli also produces small amounts of **Canaiuolo** (red variant, 2024 rosé at $25, 2021 at $26) and IGT whites ($26–29). Polaner's representation confirms importer priority; Berserkers rank shows Montenidoli at #376 among Top 10 cellar producers with steady momentum. CSW championed the estate with two dedicated articles (stars), crediting the family's commitment to elevating Vernaccia quality and regional prestige. DTE portfolio tracks 7 cuvée/vintage entries ($25–45 range), indicating consistent availability and collector interest. The estate's commitment to Vernaccia excellence, Tuscan terroir focus, and modest scale position it as a core reference for Evan's documented Tuscan taste filter (terroir-driven, grower-scale, organic-farming philosophy).
