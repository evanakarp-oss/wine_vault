# Rémi Poisot

**Micro-production Vosne-Romanée artisan**, FASS direct-allocation.
Lyle Fass's "original golden artisan." Inherited small plots of
top-tier Vosne and Corton terroir from his family (the broader Poisot
family is well-known in Burgundy; Rémi chose to stay small and focused
on quality over scale). **2022 was his first complete harvest since 1985.**

## Holdings

A tiny but extraordinary portfolio centered on **Romanée-Saint-Vivant**:

- **Romanée-Saint-Vivant Grand Cru** — the flagship. Burghound: 92–95
  (2015), 94 (2014). **Horse-plowed since 2015.** Lyle gave the 2022 a
  perfect 10/10 ("PERFECT 2022 Romanee St. Vivant"). Released around
  **$449.99** (FASS direct) — *"by far the cheapest 2021/2020/2022 RSV
  on the market for almost 9+ years running"* (compare: Hudelot-Noëllat
  RSV at $1,029–$2,000, Cathiard at $2,900–$4,800, JJ Confuron — the
  cheapest 3-tier 2021 RSV — at $717).
- **Corton-Bressandes Grand Cru** — *"arguably the greatest Grand Cru
  Burgundy value on earth"* per Fass on the 2022 vintage.
- **Corton-Charlemagne Grand Cru** — micro-production white grand cru,
  also a Fass perfect-10 vintage in 2022.
- **Pernand-Vergelesses 1er Cru "En Carradeaux"** — opposite side of
  the hill of Corton. Burghound 88–92 / 89–91 across recent vintages;
  Lyle calls it 9.6/10 in 2022. **~$56–58/btl on a 3-pack.**
- **Pernand-Vergelesses Blanc** — 100% from the En Carradeaux 1er Cru
  parcel (declassified for bureaucratic reasons). **~$43–45/btl on a
  4-pack.** Lyle: *"a baby Corton-Charlemagne."*

## Relevance to the Vosne-styled NSG question

Poisot is **not** an NSG producer. He is included here because:

1. **He is the FASS Vosne-Romanée Grand Cru reference.** His RSV is the
   apex Vosne-styled wine at FASS — the producer's hand and the site
   together define what FASS clients understand "Vosne style" to mean.
   Per Fass's own framing, *"Brisset Echezeaux and Poisot RSV are 1 and
   1A for Grand Cru Red Burgundy"* — so a tasting calibration on
   Poisot's RSV defines the Vosne-style yardstick the user can then
   apply when drinking the [[maison_pierre_brisset|Brisset NSG Aux
   Thorey]] (the Vosne-styled NSG bottling from the same FASS shelf).

2. **Pricing context.** Poisot RSV at ~$450 demonstrates that "Vosne-
   styled" doesn't strictly require Vosne pricing — and equally that
   Vosne Grand Cru pricing through three-tier is brutally inflated.
   FASS-direct relationships unlock the Vosne style at fair prices in
   ways DTE / Raeder's / CSW generally can't match at the Grand Cru
   tier.

## Style

Fass framing: *"Supple, silky, sweet and very transparent but have the
ridiculous fruit quality of 1999 or 2019."* *"Refined like an 800-thread-
count sheet. So pure. The purest."*

This is the **modern, plush Vosne idiom** — closer to Méo-Camuzet,
Hudelot-Noëllat, or Anne Gros than to the more savoury / structured
Mugneret-Gibourg signature. For the user's framework, Poisot is the
"polished modern Vosne" stylistic anchor at FASS, just as
[[anne_gros|Anne Gros]] / [[meo_camuzet|Méo-Camuzet]] are at other
retailers.
