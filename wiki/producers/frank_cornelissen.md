## Notes

**Frank Cornelissen** is one of Mount Etna's most distinctive and pioneering natural winemakers, recognized as a cornerstone figure in the Etna wine renaissance and a personal icon of the international natural wine movement. Originally Belgian-born, Cornelissen settled on Mount Etna in the late 1990s and has spent 25+ years developing a distinctive, minimalist approach to winemaking that emphasizes volcanic terroir expression, natural fermentation, and non-interventionist philosophy. CSW's long-standing enthusiasm (a staple since ~2006, 6 articles, 2 dedicated profiles, personal dinners with Cornelissen) reflects his status as a mentor figure in natural wine and as one of the most important voices in contemporary Italian winemaking.

**Background & Philosophy.** Cornelissen arrived in Sicily as a European outsider in the late 1990s at the height of Etna's obscurity — when the appellation was largely dismissed as a historical relic with no modern relevance. His philosophical commitment to natural winemaking, volcanic terroir expression, and minimal intervention positioned him as a pioneer figure who effectively launched Etna's modern reputation alongside contemporaries like COS and others. CSW notes that "Frank's wines have been a staple at Chambers Street since about 2006," indicating nearly 20 years of consistent retailer presence and community recognition. His philosophy — described as "mare e terra in vino" (sea and earth in wine) — reflects a commitment to expressing Mount Etna's specific volcanic terroir through purist natural wine methods.

**Natural Farming & Viticulture.** Cornelissen practices fully natural agriculture (not certified but practiced), employing no chemical inputs, no sulfite additions (or extremely minimal), and strict non-interventionist cellar practices. His vineyard work emphasizes soil health, native biodiversity, and minimal human manipulation. This approach aligns directly with Evan's taste filters for biodynamic/natural farming, terroir-driven, grower-scale production. The volcanic soils of Mount Etna naturally present challenges that force minimalist approaches — phylloxera pressure, volcanic ash, mineral saturation — and Cornelissen has embraced these challenges as opportunities for authentic expression.

**Terroir & Vineyard Sources.** Cornelissen works with vineyard parcels across Mount Etna's volcanic zones, including his signature "Susucaru" vineyard (a high-altitude, traditionally-cultivated parcel known for distinctive mineral expression) and "Munjebel" parcels (producing both red and white expressions). Etna's terroir is characterized by volcanic mineral-rich soils, high altitude (400-800m), and dramatic diurnal temperature swings that preserve acidity and create aromatic complexity. The volcanic geology naturally produces wines with mineral precision, chalky texture, and distinctive volcanic ash/gunflint/iodine characteristics. Cornelissen's vineyard holdings are modest (true grower-scale), reflecting philosophical commitment to small production and direct terroir expression.

**Winemaking & Signature Expressions.** Cornelissen employs radical natural wine methods: **native yeast fermentation**, **no temperature control**, **minimal or no sulfites**, **unfiltered and unrefined bottling**, **no wood aging** (or minimal neutral cooperage). The resulting wines show remarkable aromatic purity and mineral precision — the opposite of oaked, manipulated, extracted profiles. Key expressions include: **Susucaru Rosato** (a bone-dry, mineral-driven pink wine with cult following; famously featured in an entertainment video with Action Bronson); **Munjebel Bianco** (white expression showing citrus, herbs, volcanic minerals); **Munjebel Rosso** (red expression emphasizing fresh acidity and food-pairing elegance); **Susucaru Patos Bianco** (another white designation). CSW describes the wines as having "allure and excitement" — suggesting aromatic complexity and personality that transcends simple varietal character.

**Critical Recognition & Natural Wine Positioning.** Cornelissen commands exceptional standing within natural wine communities and among serious collectors pursuing non-interventionist approaches. CSW's long-term engagement (6 articles spanning 2014-2025, personal visit/tasting events) confirms sustained retailer enthusiasm. The complete absence of Raeders tracking (no auction data) reflects Cornelissen's positioning outside commercial fine-wine circuits — his wines are primarily available through natural wine specialists and direct producer relationships rather than secondary markets. This positioning actually confirms the authenticity of his natural wine approach: no commercial manipulation, minimal distribution focus, direct producer-collector relationship.

**US Market & Availability.** Imported by both Dressner Selections and Louis Dressner Selections (dual representation emphasizing natural wine positioning), Cornelissen has consistent availability through specialty natural wine retailers and CSW. No mainstream retail penetration, by design — the wines are "allocated" through personal relationships and natural wine networks rather than through standard distribution channels. CSW's permanent stock and personal event hosting reflects Cornelissen's priority status at the retailer. Evan's cellar holdings (3 bottles MunJebel Bianco 2023) confirm active acquisition and personal enthusiasm.

**Aging Potential & Drinking Strategy.** Cornelissen's natural wines show modest aging potential compared to wood-aged, sulfite-preserving expressions: Rosatos and fresh whites are typically best drunk within 3-5 years of vintage for maximum aromatics and freshness; red expressions show slightly more aging potential (5-10 years) but are primarily designed for immediate, food-focused enjoyment. The lack of preservative stabilization means bottles vary naturally (minimal sulfites allow microbial activity and oxidation) — a feature of authentic natural wine rather than a defect. Current vintages (2023-2025) are positioned for immediate enjoyment and exploration rather than extended cellaring.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Frank Cornelissen exemplifies multiple core taste filters: (1) **natural/minimal-intervention farming and winemaking** — no chemicals, no sulfites, radical non-interventionist cellar approach; (2) **volcanic terroir expression** — Mount Etna's distinctive mineral character expressed through authentic natural methods; (3) **grower-scale, personal production** — small vineyard holdings, direct producer relationships, avoiding industrial scale; (4) **community positioning** — recognized as a mentor figure in natural wine, connected to like-minded Sicily producers (COS, Arianna Occhipinti, Mortellito); (5) **CSW champion status** — extraordinarily championed despite not fitting mainstream fine-wine circuits; (6) **personal cellar ownership** — Evan already owns Cornelissen bottles, indicating personal enthusiasm and acquisition history. Cornelissen should be a regular exploration focus for Evan's natural wine and Sicily interests — both for current immediate enjoyment (fresh, aromatic expressions) and for community/philosophical alignment with natural winemaking philosophy.
