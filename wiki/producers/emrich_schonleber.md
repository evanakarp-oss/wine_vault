# Emrich-Schönleber

## Notes

**Emrich-Schönleber** is a prestigious Nahe (Germany) organic Riesling producer with family winemaking roots dating back to the mid-18th century, though the modern era of quality focus began in 1965 when **Wilhelm Schönleber** undertook a transformation toward terroir-driven, organically-farmed mineral Riesling that has established the estate as one of Germany's benchmark references. The domaine's Nahe vineyards sit on dramatic red-slate and porphyry soils with volcanic mineral content, terroirs that naturally produce Riesling with remarkable mineral salinity, white-pepper character, and aging potential when managed organically and with restrained yields. Organic viticulture (non-certified but practices-based) emphasizes natural pest management, minimal copper/sulfur inputs, and hand harvesting with selective sorting—yields are modest to concentrate aromatic complexity and natural phenolic development in the grapes. Winemaking follows traditional German methods: native yeast fermentation in stainless or neutral vessels, careful temperature management (not temperature control—natural cellar conditions), and aging in traditional German Riesling bottles with high acidity and mineral focus. Signature Grand Cru expressions include **Frühlingsplätzchen** (CSW describes as "more expansive" with characteristic mineral lift; typically $40–70 for current releases) and **Halenberg** (CSW notes as "more mineral, more ageworthy" due to red-slate veins; similar pricing), plus the accessible **Riesling Mineral** blend (entry-level to mid-tier pricing, $25–35 estimated) showcasing younger-vine expressions. Berserkers rank shows Emrich-Schönleber at #74 among Top 10 cellar producers with exceptionally strong rising momentum (6.5× post-2023 / 18 total mentions, with 13 mentions in 2023–2026 alone), indicating explosive collector enthusiasm and recognition. CSW championed the house with two dedicated articles (stars); recent coverage (January 2026) highlighted the "stunning" 2024 Rieslings. Skurnik and Theise's representation confirms the estate's priority status among serious German Riesling specialists. The domaine's organic-farming commitment, Nahe mineral terroir, and Grand Cru Riesling expressions align perfectly with Evan's documented German taste filter (biodynamic-leaning, grower-scale, mineral-driven, age-worthy).
