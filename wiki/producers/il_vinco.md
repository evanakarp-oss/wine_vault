# Il Vinco

Founded in 2014 by three agricultural friends—Daniele Manoni and Nicola Brenciaglia (olive-oil producers) and Marco Fucini (cattle breeder)—Il Vinco took its name from the pliable willow branch traditionally used to tie vines to stakes. Based in Montefiascone, in the province of Viterbo, the winery sits on the southern slopes of the volcanic crater holding Lake Bolsena, between 250 and 450 meters elevation on volcanic soils. The five-hectare estate, split evenly between old and newly planted vines, champions Canaiolo Nero, a rare indigenous Lazio variety. Farmed biodynamically since 2017 using minimal copper and sulfur, Il Vinco represents a new generation of natural winemakers revitalizing Lazio's terroir.

## Notes

**The Canaiolo Nero revival.** Il Vinco's core mission is to recover Canaiolo Nero, a native Lazio variety nearly extinct. The flagship Canajo monovarietal showcases this grape's potential when farmed biodynamically and vinified with minimal intervention.

**Terroir focus.** The volcanic slopes overlooking Lake Bolsena create distinctive minerality and tension. Biodynamic farming since 2017 emphasizes natural balance and soil health, with minimal chemical inputs (small amounts of copper and sulfur only).

**Wine portfolio.** The estate produces Canajo (monovarietal Canaiolo Nero), Mistione blends, and Capannacce reds and whites. Varieties include Canaiolo Nero, Grechetto Rosso, Procanico, Rossetto, and Malvasia.

**Cellar fit.** Emerging biodynamic producer focused on native varietals and terroir expression. The Canaiolo Nero specialty represents exactly the kind of indigenous-variety deep-dive that appeals to serious collectors exploring regional diversity.
