# Sergio Mottura

Sergio Mottura's 321-acre estate lies in Civitella d'Agliano, in Lazio's Viterbo province, on the slopes above Orvieto. After a five-year transition period, the estate received formal organic certification in 1996, becoming one of Italy's pioneers in organic viticulture. Mottura chose the porcupine as his company symbol: an animal that can only live in uncontaminated environments where natural balance is respected. The winemaker is the unquestioned master of Grechetto, Lazio's signature white variety; his Grechetto "Latour a Civitella" was the first white wine from Lazio to earn the prestigious three-bicchieri award, and he was named Gambero Rosso's grower of the year in 2012.

## Notes

**Grechetto mastery.** Sergio Mottura has defined what Grechetto can be. His whites combine full body, minerality, and aging potential—demonstrating that Lazio's signature white rivals northern Italian classics when farmed organically and vinified with care. Latour a Civitella remains the reference standard.

**Organic pioneer.** Certification in 1996 places Mottura among Italy's earliest organic adopters. The large estate (321 acres) proves organic farming is viable at scale without sacrificing quality.

**Varietal palette.** Beyond Grechetto, Mottura works with Procanico, Verdello, and Rupeccio—a curated selection of indigenous Lazio and Umbrian varieties suited to the terroir.

**Cellar fit.** Essential Lazio producer for any serious collector. Grechetto "Latour a Civitella" is a must-try; the combination of organic farming, three-bicchieri recognition, and white-wine focus makes Mottura an obvious addition to the vault. His organic credentials and 30-year track record are unassailable.
