# Gravner

The defining amphora winemaker of Italy. Josko Gravner (Oslavia, on the Slovenian
border) abandoned high-tech winemaking after a 2000 trip to Georgia and now ferments
everything in buried qvevri with months of skin contact, releasing only after ~7 years.
He ripped out his international varieties (Chardonnay, Sauvignon, Pinot Grigio — dates
posted on his own website) to concentrate on indigenous **Ribolla Gialla** and the
**Breg** blend (discontinued after 2012), plus tiny amounts of Rosso Breg (Pignolo) and
Rujno. Surfaced for the vault via the 2026-07 Friuli enoteca list
([[../_views/friuli_producer_map_2026_07|view]]), where he anchors the top of the list.

## Notes

**On the 2026-07 Friuli list:** Breg 2003 magnum €360, Ribolla Gialla 2003 magnum €320,
Ribolla 2013 €180, Pinot Grigio 2001 magnum / 2009 €360, Rosso 2007 €300 — a serious
back-vintage cellar of him.

**Cellar fit.** Dead-center on the sense-of-place + tension axis: biodynamic-leaning
farming (uncertified; own livestock, reforestation), native grapes, radical patience.
The cellar already holds the adjacent [[radikon|Radikon]] 2008 Ribolla — Gravner is the
other half of that Oslavia conversation. Aged releases (2013 Ribolla is current-ish) are
the buy; they need and reward decades.
