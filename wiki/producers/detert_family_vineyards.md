# Detert Family Vineyards

A quiet family grower sitting on one of Napa's most coveted addresses: a ~50-acre
parcel **inside the historic To Kalon Vineyard** in Oakville, bought by the Detert
family in **1954**. The 7-acre **East Block** holds what are thought to be the
**oldest Cabernet Franc vines in Napa Valley** — first planted **1949** (believed to
be the first Cabernet Franc in the valley), replanted in 1979 from the original
cuttings, and propagated as the proprietary **"Detert clone."** Robert Mondavi prized
this fruit so highly in the 1960s that he reportedly paid Cabernet Sauvignon prices
to keep the Cabernet Franc clusters for his iconic blends.

For decades the family simply sold the fruit; since the **2000** commercial debut the
great-grandsons of founder Hedwig Detert (stewards Tom Garrett, his brother John, and
cousin Bob) have kept back a tiny amount for their own label — **limited-production
Cabernet Franc and Cabernet Sauvignon**. The appeal for Evan is exactly that it's the
**understated, family-farmed, old-vine** counterpoint to its blue-chip To Kalon
neighbors: heritage site, not hype.
