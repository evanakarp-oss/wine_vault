# Hyde de Villaine (HdV)

A cross-Atlantic family partnership founded in **2000** in the cool **Los Carneros**
corner of Napa: California viticulturist **Larry Hyde** (of the benchmark Hyde
Vineyard) and **Aubert de Villaine**, co-director of **Domaine de la Romanée-Conti**.
The two families are joined by marriage — de Villaine's wife Pamela is Hyde's
*"belle cousine."* HdV draws from the pick of Hyde Vineyard's 156 acres, and Aubert
drives a deliberately **Burgundian, minimal-intervention** philosophy: let the
variety, the vineyard, and the vintage speak.

This is the restrained/balance end of Napa rather than the mountain-Cab spine — but
it sits squarely on Evan's **sense of place + tension** axis (cool Carneros,
lower-alcohol, site-transparent, ageworthy), which is why it surfaced as the
strongest new fit on the Vinous Napa list. The flagship is **Chardonnay** (three
bottlings, incl. the Hyde Vineyard cuvée); the Bordeaux blend **"Belle Cousine"**
(Cabernet/Merlot) is the red of interest, alongside the **"Californio" Syrah** and
the elusive **"Ygnacia" Pinot Noir**.

## Notes

- **Why it fits:** cool-site Carneros, Burgundian restraint, minimal-intervention
  winemaking under a DRC hand — the antithesis of the opulent Napa cult tier. The
  "Belle Cousine" Bordeaux blend is the restrained red; the Chardonnays are the
  estate's calling card.
- **Pricing (2026-06):** "Belle Cousine" Bordeaux blend ~$96; the Chardonnays
  ~$91–92; "Californio" Syrah ~$95. Allocation-leaning but recent vintages turn up
  at retail (K&L, Total Wine, Brix26).
