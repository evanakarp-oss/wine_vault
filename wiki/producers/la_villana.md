# La Villana

Joy Kull, an American from Connecticut, established La Villana in 2016 as a small natural-wine project just outside Rome near Lake Bolsena. Operating just 2.5 hectares spread across six parcels, La Villana exemplifies minimal-intervention winemaking with no added sulfites, no thermal manipulation, and complete fermentation in neutral tanks. The wines—including the fun and accessible Fuorliluogo and Rosso Puro alongside more serious bottlings like Bianco Uovo—represent pure expressions of the grapes. Kull's unconventional approach includes fermenting wines in concrete eggs, rare in the Lazio region.

## Notes

**Winemaking philosophy.** La Villana's mission is radical simplicity: no manipulation, no interference, no additives. The wines are complete, pure expressions of grapes and terroir. Fermentation occurs in tanks that impart no thermal energy and allow natural processes to unfold.

**Cellar fit.** Emerging natural-wine producer with terroir-driven whites and rosés. Accessible price point and minimal-intervention approach align well with Evan's taste for authentic, unfussy wines that let place speak.
