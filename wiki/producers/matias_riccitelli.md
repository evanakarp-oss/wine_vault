Winemaker: **Matías Riccitelli**. Patagonia / Río Negro (Patagonia). Category: **Low Intervention**.

## Notes

**Matías Riccitelli** stands as one of Argentina's most respected contemporary winemakers, pioneering the use of old vines and minimal-intervention techniques in Patagonia's Río Negro region. The producer has gained international recognition for elegant, low-alcohol expressions of Pinot Noir, Trousseau, and Malbec that prioritize freshness, minerality, and terroir expression over the alcohol and richness that characterize mainstream Argentine wine production.

**Patagonian Positioning & Old-Vine Focus.** Based in Patagonia (specifically Río Negro, Argentina's southernmost wine region), Riccitelli has become synonymous with the appellation's old-vine treasures. The strategy centers on identifying aged vineyard parcels (50+ years old), understanding their terroir potential, and producing wines that honor that history through minimal intervention. The "Old Vines from Patagonia" line is his flagship — a direct statement about methodology and philosophy. Paz Levinson, curator of the Argentina Reloaded selection, identifies Riccitelli as "one of the most significant winemakers in Argentina today," validating his position within Argentina's new-wave hierarchy.

**Winemaking Approach & Low-Alcohol Philosophy.** Riccitelli's hallmark is low-alcohol production in a country known for high-alcohol wines. His Pinots and other expressions typically achieve 11.5–12.5% ABV — values that would be considered thin in warm-climate viticulture but that demonstrate restraint, acidity retention, and terroir expression in Patagonia's cool climate. The use of whole-cluster fermentation and minimal sulfites furthers the low-intervention approach, allowing wines to express vintage variation and site character.

**Key Wines & Varietal Portfolio.** The producer's signature offerings include:
- **Old Vines from Patagonia Pinot Noir** — the flagship expression, demonstrating Patagonia's Pinot potential
- **Riccitelli Trousseau** — an uncommon varietal in Argentina, showing the producer's experimental approach
- **Malbec** expressions — showing how old vines and minimal intervention transform Argentina's signature varietal

This portfolio span demonstrates a producer unafraid to experiment while maintaining philosophical consistency.

**US Market Position & Collector Recognition.** Imported through Skurnik (a progressive, quality-focused importer), Riccitelli has built a dedicated collector base among natural-wine enthusiasts and Patagonia specialists. The producer's inclusion in Argentina Reloaded events (Rio 2024, Buenos Aires 2025) positions him as central to the curator's vision for Argentina's future. The aging score of 0 and lack of CSW coverage might suggest limited conventional retail presence, but this positioning instead reflects a producer who has built reputation through specialty channels rather than mainstream distribution.

**Argentina's Patagonian Frontier.** Riccitelli exemplifies a broader Patagonian narrative: a region that was long considered marginal for quality wine production is now emerging as Argentina's most intellectually interesting terroir. Cool climate, old vines, minimal intervention, and distinctive varietals create wines that compete on complexity and food-friendliness rather than power. Riccitelli's influence on this conversation has been substantial, validating Patagonia as a serious alternative to Mendoza's commercial model.
