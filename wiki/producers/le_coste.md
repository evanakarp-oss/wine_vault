# Le Coste

Gianmarco Antonuzzi and Clémentine Bouveron established Le Coste in 2004, inspired by their experience with renowned French vignerons like Bruno Schueller and Dard et Ribo. Overlooking Lago di Bolsena in Gradoli, they found untapped potential in the region's unique volcanic soils and native varieties. What began with three hectares of abandoned vineyards has grown into a 30-hectare estate encompassing 13 hectares of vines, 5 hectares of olive groves, and diverse farmland. Farmed biodynamically from the outset, the vineyards nestle among chestnut and oak trees on iron-rich, well-draining volcanic soil. Winemaking is fully natural: no added sulfur, no filtration, with aging in vats, amphorae, and neutral wood only.

## Notes

**Biodynamic from inception.** Le Coste has been farmed biodynamically since founding, a philosophical commitment reflected in every bottle. Soil health and biodiversity are central to the winemaking vision.

**Volcanic terroir.** The iron-rich, well-draining volcanic soils of the Bolsena crater create distinctive minerality and tension. The mix of chestnut and oak trees supports the vineyard ecosystem naturally.

**Natural winemaking rigor.** No added sulfur, no filtration, aging in neutral vessels only—Le Coste's approach is radical in its restraint. The choice to work with indigenous varieties (Procanico, Malvasia, Roscetto, Verdello, Petino, Moscato) plus selective international additions (Syrah, Merlot) shows curated balance.

**Vineyard scale and ambition.** The 30-hectare estate—substantial by natural-wine standards—proves that biodynamic farming can scale without compromising philosophy. The integrated olive groves and farmland create a living, breathing agricultural system.

**Cellar fit.** Top Lazio producer for Evan's vault. The biodynamic credentials, volcanic terroir, natural-wine rigor, and founder background in French natural wines position Le Coste as a reference point for the region. Essential for any serious Lazio exploration.
