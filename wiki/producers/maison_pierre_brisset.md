# Maison Pierre Brisset

**Lyle Fass's flagship Burgundy micro-négociant.** Pierre Brisset works with
purchased fruit from elite parcels across the Côte d'Or, applying a single,
recognizable house signature — what Fass repeatedly calls *"elite elegance,
restraint and a silken texture."* In FASS's own framing Brisset is the
benchmark: every comparison ("if Brisset and Buffet had a love child …",
"these Perseval-Farge wines are the closest in Champagne to Brisset", "this
nebbiolo is like if Brisset made nebbiolo") is anchored on his house style.

For the Vosne-styled NSG question, **Brisset is the answer at FASS** — see
`Why this producer matters` below.

## Why this producer matters

For the Vosne-styled NSG framework ([[vosne_styled_nsg|see deep analysis]]):

- **The PARCEL is right.** NSG 1er Cru Aux Thorey sits on the Vosne
  shoulder — the upper slope above Boudots / Damodes, geologically
  continuous with Vosne.
- **The WINEMAKING is right.** Brisset's house signature — restraint,
  silken texture, lifted fruit, perfumed nose, polished tannins — is the
  *exact* shape of Kelley's "Vosne-styled Nuits." It is the opposite of
  Chevillon's hot ferments + stems + Sirugue oak ("Nuits Nuits") signature.
- **It's already in the cellar** (2 × 2023, drink 2028–2043) — meaning the
  user has been buying Vosne-styled FASS NSG without explicitly framing it
  that way. This page formalizes the observation.

In the WK three-styles taxonomy, **Brisset's NSG Aux Thorey is FASS's
Vosne-styled Nuits** — the same role Georges Noëllat Boudots / Maxime
Cheurlin Damodes play in the DTE portfolio, and the same role
Mugneret-Gibourg Chaignots plays at allocation-only retail.
