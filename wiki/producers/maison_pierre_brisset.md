# Maison Pierre Brisset

Micro-négociant working across the Côte de Beaune and Côte de Nuits, and one of Lyle Fass's reference points — Fass repeatedly grades other wines *against* Brisset ("if Brisset made Nebbiolo," "the closest wines I've had in Champagne to Brisset"). The house style is elegance and finesse over power: lithe, mineral, terroir-transparent wines at prices well below their quality tier. Fass calls 2018 the best white vintage and 2019 (closely followed by 2021) the best reds, with 2022 a further "leap" in class across both colours.

Since the 2021 grape-price spike for micro-négociants (which cost him his Chenevottes and Grand Montagne fruit), the white range is three cuvées: Chassagne-Montrachet 1er Cru "Abbaye de Morgeot," St-Aubin 1er Cru "Sur Roche Dumay," and the entry Bourgogne Blanc "Cuvée Cassaneas" (an old word for Chassagne) — the last of which Fass rates a 9.6 and describes as tasting "exactly like a 1er Cru Chassagne." The reds span Bourgogne up through village and 1er Cru in Pommard, Nuits-Saint-Georges, Chambolle-Musigny and Vosne-Romanée. Squarely within the cellar's terroir-driven Burgundy focus.
