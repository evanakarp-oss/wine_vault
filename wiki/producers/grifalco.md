# Grifalco

Grifalco is a prominent natural-winemaking estate on the volcanic slopes of Monte Vulture in Basilicata, producing elegant, mineral-driven Aglianico del Vulture wines. Founded in 2004 by Fabrizio and Cecilia Piccin, who brought twenty years of Tuscan winemaking experience to the region, the estate is now managed by brothers Andrea and Lorenzo Piccin. The winery spans sixteen hectares at 400–500 meters elevation on the mineral-rich pyroclastic tuff that defines the Vulture terroir, with all vineyards farmed organically. Their minimal-intervention cellar philosophy emphasizes spontaneous fermentation, minimal oak use, and a focus on expressing individual vineyard character rather than winemaker intervention.

## Notes

Grifalco produces four main wines: Gricos (entry-level, showcasing fresh red fruit and minerality), Grifalco (the flagship bottling with greater depth and complexity), and two single-vineyard designations, DaMaschito and DaGinestra. All wines are produced from low-yield, organically farmed Aglianico vines planted on the extinct volcano's mineral-laden soils. The brothers' commitment to natural winemaking and terroir expression has made Grifalco a rising reference point in the emerging wave of quality-focused Basilicata producers. The volcanic substrate of Monte Vulture—primarily pyroclastic tuff threaded with ash and lapilli—imparts the distinctive minerality and structure that characterizes excellent Aglianico del Vulture.
