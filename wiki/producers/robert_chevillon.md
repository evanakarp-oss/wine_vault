# Robert Chevillon

The reference family domaine of Nuits-Saint-Georges. ~13 ha, run by brothers
Bertrand and Denis Chevillon (now joined by the next generation). Old vines —
many parcels planted 1930s–1950s — destemmed, traditional élevage in around
30% new oak. Wines that demand patience: structured, transparent to site,
built for the cellar.

## Holdings

Eight 1er Cru parcels covering the canonical NSG climats on **both** sides of
the town:

- **South of town (the structured tier):** Les Saint-Georges, Les Vaucrains,
  Les Cailles, Les Roncières, Les Perrières, Les Pruliers
- **North of town (the Vosne shoulder):** Les Chaignots, Les Bousselots

Plus NSG village from old-vine parcels (Les Vieilles Vignes), and a small
amount of NSG Blanc.

## Why this domaine matters

For the *Vosne-adjacent value* question — Chevillon is the producer that
answers "what does the southern, structured side of NSG actually taste like
when it's done at the highest level." His Vaucrains and Les Saint-Georges are
the textbook Nuits reference wines: stony, savoury, demanding 10–15+ years.
His Chaignots and Bousselots give the same domaine's read on the *Vosne
shoulder* — softer, more aromatic, lifted — making this one of the best
single-producer flights for understanding the commune's geological split.

## Notes

Discussed on Berserkers in the "JF Mugnier vs Chevillon Styles" thread
(2019, William Kelley) — Chevillon ferments quite hot (up to 35°C) with some
stems, contrasted with Mugnier's near-total destem and cooler ferments. The
result: Chevillon is the **stricter, more savoury** of the two NSG references.
