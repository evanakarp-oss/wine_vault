# Ronco Severo

Uncompromising biodynamic producer in Prepotto, Colli Orientali del Friuli, managed by Stefano Novello (enologist, 1988). Six hectares at 150m elevation on ponca terroir, planted with native Friuli whites (Chardonnay, Ribolla Gialla, Friulano, Picolit, Pinot Grigio) and distinctive reds (Schioppettino, Refosco dal Peduncolo Rosso, Merlot). Minimal-intervention philosophy: spontaneous fermentation, minimal sulfite, no additives, hand harvest, lunar bottling. Distributed in USA by Beaune Imports.

## History & Philosophy

Ronco Severo was established in 1968 when Stefano Novello's father decided to restore the ancient vineyards of the counts of Rieppi, eventually founding the winery. Stefano obtained his **enology degree in 1988** and immediately rejected conventional winemaking in favor of **biodynamic viticulture and minimal-intervention winemaking**.

**Core Philosophy**: Stefano uses **no chemical products in the vineyards** and follows a **non-interventionalist approach** in the cellar. Wines are **macerated on their skins for extended periods**, remain **unfiltered and unfined**, and undergo minimal sulfite additions before bottling. The result is a portfolio of wines that express place and vintage with remarkable immediacy and honesty.

## Vineyard & Terroir

Located in Prepotto (province of Udine), the six-hectare vineyard sits at approximately **150 metres above sea level**, with terroir shaped by **ponca soils** (sandstone marls in local dialect). This distinctive geology imparts siliceous minerality and structural tension to the wines.

## Notes

**Ronco Severo** represents the apex of northeastern Italian minimal-intervention winemaking — a **biodynamic-certified micro-estate** in Prepotto (Colli Orientali del Friuli) managed by **Stefano Novello**, an enologist (trained 1988) who rejected conventional winemaking in favor of radical minimal-intervention philosophy. The estate exemplifies uncompromising biodynamic viticulture and natural fermentation, producing wines of remarkable authenticity, terroir purity, and unpredictability.

**Stefano Novello & Enological Philosophy.** Novello's formal enology training (1988) positioned him to work in conventional winemaking, yet he deliberately chose the opposite path — rejecting all chemical inputs, commercial yeasts, and interventionist cellar techniques. This contrarian stance reflects a philosophical commitment to **natural winemaking as terroir expression** rather than winemaker ego or technical mastery. His approach prioritizes:
- **Vineyard work** — biodynamic practices that enhance soil health, root penetration, and natural vine vigor
- **Spontaneous fermentation** — reliance on wild vineyard yeasts without commercial inoculation
- **Minimal sulfite** — additions only at critical points (pre-fermentation stabilization, bottling if necessary)
- **No additives** — no cultured bacteria, no commercial enzymes, no fining agents

**Vineyard Terroir & Historical Context.** The six-hectare Ronco Severo vineyard was established in 1968 when Stefano's father began restoration of ancient vineyards previously owned by the counts of Rieppi. The site sits at 150m elevation on **ponca soils** (sandstone-marl geology distinctive to Colli Orientali), characterized by:
- **High mineral content** — siliceous sandstone imparts distinctive salty, chalky minerality
- **Good drainage** — promotes healthy root systems and natural vigor management
- **Thermal mass** — moderates temperature swings, allowing both white and red varieties to ripen with freshness and acidity

**Grape Portfolio & Varietal Specialization.** Ronco Severo works a diverse portfolio reflecting Friuli's traditional and contemporary varieties:

**White Varieties:**

- Refosco dal Peduncolo Rosso (Friuli red variety; structured, mineral-forward)

**Winemaking Practice & Cellar Protocols.** Ronco Severo's radical minimal-intervention approach encompasses:
- **Spontaneous fermentation** using wild vineyard yeasts (not commercial inocula)
- **Minimal sulfite dosage** — added only when absolutely necessary for stabilization/preservation
- **No additives of any kind** — no cultured bacteria, no commercial enzymes, no fining/clarification agents
- **Hand-picking at harvest** — careful fruit selection replacing mechanical sorting
- **Long aging in large oak** — primarily Slavonian oak barrels (23-35 months depending on wine type), chosen for neutrality rather than flavor imposition
- **Bottling under a waning moon** — biodynamic practice reflecting attention to natural rhythms
- **Unfiltered and unfined** — preserving complete natural stability

**Schioppettino Mastery.** Ronco Severo has emerged as a recognized master of **Schioppettino**, Friuli's most distinctive and geographically-specific red grape. This position reflects:
- Terroir alignment: Ponca soils perfectly suited to Schioppettino's high-tannin structure
- Minimal-intervention fit: Schioppettino's natural power requires no extraction or concentration tricks
