# Mugneret-Gibourg

The reference Vosne house run by the **Mugneret sisters**, Marie-Christine
Teillaud-Mugneret and Marie-Andrée Nauleau-Mugneret, daughters of the late
Dr. Georges Mugneret (a Dijon ophthalmologist and vigneron whose
restorations of Échézeaux and Ruchottes elevated the domaine to its modern
stature). ~9 ha. Hand-harvested, near-total destem, long élevage in
~50% new oak, late release. Strictly allocated.

## Holdings

The lineup is famously logical — one wine per terroir, no village blends:

- **Vosne-Romanée** village (no 1er Cru historically; recently added small
  parcels but not a marquee bottling)
- **Nuits-Saint-Georges 1er Cru** Les Chaignots, Les Vignes Rondes
- **Chambolle-Musigny** Villages
- **Échézeaux** Grand Cru (the historical pivot — Mugneret's Échézeaux is
  a reference for the appellation)
- **Clos de Vougeot** Grand Cru
- **Ruchottes-Chambertin** Grand Cru
- Bourgogne and Aligoté

## Style and reception

William Kelley (Wine Advocate) on Berserkers (2018, 2020) repeatedly flags
Mugneret-Gibourg as **structurally ageworthy** — explicitly contrasted with
faster-maturing peers like Georges Noëllat: *"the Georges Noëllat 2015s
will drink much earlier than e.g. Mugneret-Gibourg's, irrespective of
appellation."* The signature is **savoury, salty, mineral** Vosne — far
from the modern plush idiom.

## Why this domaine matters

For the study view: Mugneret-Gibourg holds **NSG Chaignots and Vignes
Rondes** (the Vosne-shoulder NSG climats), an Échézeaux, and a Clos
Vougeot — i.e. they cover the entire northern arc out of Vosne in one
domaine's lineup, with no village-level dilution. If allocations are
attainable, this is one of the cleanest single-producer ways to taste the
Vosne-adjacent geography. (In practice, allocations are not attainable
without years of waitlist patience.)
