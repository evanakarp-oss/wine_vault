# Dard & Ribo

## Notes

**Dard & Ribo** is a small natural-wine producer in the Northern Rhône (Crozes-Hermitage and Saint-Joseph) whose reputation rests on producing what CSW describes as "some of the most pure and delicious wines of the Northern Rhone, much sought after by lovers of natural wines"—the house's commitment to minimal intervention viticulture and winemaking has evolved the estate into a benchmark reference for elegant, terroir-driven Northern Rhône Syrah. The domaine's vineyards sit on the granite and schist soils of Crozes-Hermitage and the steep slopes of Saint-Joseph, terroirs naturally suited to producing wines with mineral precision and fresh acidity when managed with restraint and natural methods rather than heavy extraction or new oak. Natural farming emphasizes minimal chemical inputs (copper and sulfur only when necessary), native microflora development, and hand harvesting with selective sorting to concentrate quality; yields are modest to favor complexity over volume. Winemaking follows a natural-wine philosophy: minimal sulfite additions at crushing and bottling, native yeast fermentation in concrete or stainless vessels, no temperature control or manipulation, and bottling unfiltered and unfined to preserve the wine's living character and aromatic purity. Signature expressions include **C'est le Vin** (a distinctive low-intervention bottling featuring minimal sulfite additions and bright, unoaked red-fruit character; described by CSW as "delicious" and available in tiny quantities) and Saint-Joseph expressions (typically $30–55 estimated retail for current releases). Production is deliberately limited—CSW notes "quantities are tiny"—making Dard & Ribo wines highly sought among natural-wine specialists and serious Northern Rhône collectors. Dressner and Louis' representation confirms the estate's status as a priority import for natural, minimal-intervention expressions. The domaine's natural-farming commitment, focus on purity over extraction, and Saint-Joseph/Crozes-Hermitage expressions align with Evan's documented Rhône taste filters (terroir-driven, minimal intervention, grower-scale producers).
