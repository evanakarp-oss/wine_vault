# Dunn Vineyards

_Randy Dunn's Howell Mountain Cabernet — the mountain-Cab benchmark. Deliberately picked at modest ripeness (and alcohol-adjusted down), built on iron tannin and acid, and made to drink at 20–40 years rather than on release. The Howell Mountain and Napa Valley bottlings are among the most reliably age-worthy Cabernets in California._

## Notes

Evan's reference point for the whole California-Cab category (CLAUDE.md curation taste, Track 1 — old-guard / restrained / site-transparent). The antithesis of the cult-hedonist style: low alcohol, dusty structure, no new-oak gloss, a wine that is closed for a decade and rewards cellaring. Now run by Mike Dunn. Estate fruit off Howell Mountain volcanic soils; a separate declassified Napa Valley Cab from purchased + estate fruit drinks earlier. Regularly undervalued at auction relative to its longevity — the aged verticals ('94–'97 Napa/Howell Mtn, plus 2005/2008/2016) in the [[auction_261W_week30_wheelhouse_2026_07|Week-30 261W sale]] at $100–200/btl are exactly the buy: mature mountain Cabernet at a fraction of cult pricing.
