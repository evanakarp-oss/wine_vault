## Notes

**Domaine de Beaurenard** is one of Châteauneuf-du-Pape's most established and quality-focused estates, with a long family tradition of excellence and recent decades marked by a strategic commitment to biodynamic farming and refined, structured expressions of Grenache-based Châteauneuf. The estate is recognized as a cornerstone producer within the appellation, managing substantial vineyard holdings across multiple terroir parcels that yield distinct wine expressions, from entry-level bottlings to aged-parcel reserves (most notably, the "Boisrenard" series).

**Producer History & Biodynamic Commitment.** The estate's family heritage extends back generations; modern era (1990s-present) has been marked by a deliberate transition to biodynamic farming and viticulture practices — a commitment that reflects the estate's philosophy of terroir expression through natural farming methods. Biodynamics focus on soil health, biodiversity, and natural pest/disease management, with the goal of producing wines that reflect the specific character of Beaurenard's vineyard parcels. This approach positions the estate squarely within Evan's taste filters: biodynamic-farming, terroir-driven, artisan-scale orientation aligned with French grower philosophy.

**Terroir & Vineyard Architecture.** Beaurenard's vineyard holdings encompass multiple significant parcels across Châteauneuf-du-Pape's complex soil landscape — including distinctive "Boisrenard" (wooded slopes) parcels and multiple sub-regions of the appellation. These parcels are characterized by diverse soil compositions (gravels, sandy loams, clay) that naturally produce wines with layered complexity and aging potential. The estate's multi-parcel structure allows for distinctive cuvée expressions: the main "Châteauneuf-du-Pape" bottling is a blended expression across parcels; the "Boisrenard" is a selection from specific old-vines parcels known for concentrated fruit and refined structure.

**Winemaking Philosophy & Key Expressions.** Beaurenard's approach emphasizes traditional Grenache-forward blending (Grenache typically 65-80%, with Syrah, Mourvèdre, and white Rhône varieties in supporting roles). The estate produces three primary expressions: (1) **Main Châteauneuf-du-Pape** — entry-level blended expression showing freshness and accessibility (2020: WE 95, notes of mulled plum, blueberry, dark cherries, wild thyme); (2) **Boisrenard Rouge** — reserve selection from old-vines parcels showing greater concentration, structure, and aging potential (2020 1.5L magnum: JS 95, WE 96; 2017: WA 96); (3) **Boisrenard Blanc** — white Châteauneuf from old-vines field blend of multiple white varieties, showing beautiful smoothness and salinity (2019: WA 93, D 96).

**Critical Recognition & Performance Profile.** Beaurenard has attracted consistent recognition from major critics: the 2020 Boisrenard (1.5L) achieved WE 96 and JS 95; the 2017 Boisrenard achieved WA 96 — among the highest-rated Châteauneufs in those vintages. More recent releases (2021 Boisrenard: WA 94, JS 94) confirm sustained quality. The white Boisrenard Blanc (2019: WA 93, D 96) demonstrates the estate's depth across both red and white expressions. These consistent scores position Beaurenard as a top-tier Châteauneuf producer commanding serious collector attention.

**US Market Presence & Pricing Strategy.** Imported by Wilson Daniels (a major U.S. distributor of fine Burgundy, Rhône, and Alsace), Beaurenard commands strong presence at fine-wine retailers. Raeders tracks 11 cuvée-vintage entries ranging $69.99 (entry-level 2021 bottling) to $249.99 (2020 Boisrenard 1.5L magnum). Notably, mature vintages (2005 Boisrenard: $82.99) remain available at reasonable prices, reflecting both production scale and market recognition. The broad price range and multiple expressions make Beaurenard accessible across collector budgets — from entry-level exploration ($69.99) through premium aged-parcel reserve bottles ($150-250).

**Aging Potential & Cellar Strategy.** Beaurenard's Grenache-based reds show remarkable aging potential: the main-line Châteauneuf typically benefits from 8-12 years cellaring, with peak drinking 12-20+ years from vintage; the Boisrenard reserve bottlings (2005, 2017, 2020) are built for longer aging, with minimum 12-15 years cellaring and peak drinking 20-30+ years from vintage. The white Boisrenard Blanc, unusually for Châteauneuf blanc, shows significant aging potential (5-12 years) due to its natural acidity and reserve-wine production standards. Current vintages (2020, 2021 reds; 2020 blanc) are well-positioned for extended cellaring projects.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Domaine de Beaurenard exemplifies multiple core taste filters: (1) **biodynamic farming** — a stated commitment reflected in the estate's documented transition and practice; (2) **grower-scale production** — family-owned, vineyard-focused, artisan-scale approach (not industrialized); (3) **terroir expression** — multi-parcel structure and distinctive expressions (Boisrenard vs. main-line) reflect specific vineyard characters; (4) **proven aging track record** — mature vintages (2005, 2017) available for reference; (5) **reasonable pricing** for quality delivered. The estate represents an exceptional value proposition for serious Grenache/Châteauneuf collectors seeking authentic, biodynamically-farmed, age-worthy expressions. Beaurenard should be a regular acquisition target for both immediate enjoyment (entry-level bottlings) and extended cellaring (Boisrenard reserve cuvées).
