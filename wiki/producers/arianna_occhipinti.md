## Notes

**Arianna Occhipinti** is a pioneering Sicilian winemaker who fundamentally elevated the international reputation of Sicily's indigenous grape varieties through organic/biodynamic farming, minimal-intervention winemaking, and a philosophical commitment to "soulful and balanced" expressions of Nero d'Avola, Frappato, and other native cultivars.

**Background & Impact.** Occhipinti began making wine in the early 2000s and released her first vintage in 2006 at age 24 — a remarkable achievement that marked the beginning of a sustained campaign to redefine Sicilian wine identity. Her early releases of single-varietal Frappato and Nero d'Avola wines shocked the wine world, which had largely dismissed Sicily as a source of "commercial, over-cropped" commodity wines. Her success forced a reappraisal of the region's potential and inspired a new generation of serious Sicilian producers.

**Farming & Viticulture.** Occhipinti practices organic and biodynamic agriculture, rejecting chemical inputs and embracing soil-building practices. Her vineyard work reflects deep commitment to terroir expression — preserving native grape varieties, managing vine age (vines on her estate typically average 50 years), and working with low yields.

**Winemaking Philosophy.** Her approach combines traditional Sicilian practices with modern natural-wine aesthetics:
- **Concrete fermentation** — uses concrete vessels extensively (rather than wood or steel) for both fermentation and aging, allowing for oxygen exchange and mineral expression
- **Extended maceration** — on white wines, skin-contact fermentation (maceration) for 2+ weeks, building texture and complexity
- **Minimal intervention** — unfiltered, natural sulfites, hands-off cellar approach
- **Cooperative work** — active in promoting other Sicilian winemakers and sharing knowledge

**Key Wines & Parcels.**
- **SP68 Bianco** — Zibibbo and Albanello from red sand and limestone soils; concrete fermentation with 2-week skin maceration; 8 months concrete aging
- **SP68 Rosso** — Frappato and Nero d'Avola blend from four vineyard sites; fresh, mineral-driven, lightly tannic; flagship entry wine
- **Il Frappato** — single-varietal Frappato (pure varietal expression); estate bottling that "redefined our entire understanding" of Frappato's potential; 50+ year old vines; 30-day maceration; complex aging (8 months Slovenian oak + 8 months concrete); requires 5-10 years cellaring
- **Siccagno** — pure Nero d'Avola; elegant, refined expression of the primary Sicilian grape; "bright dark cherry, herbs, chalk" complexity

**Regional Position & Reputation.** Occhipinti is among the most important figures in Sicilian wine's reputation rehabilitation. She operates within the DOCG Cerasuolo di Vittoria appellation (southeastern Sicily) but often works across IGT designations for freedom of expression. Her relationship with CSW spans years (described as "old friends"), and she has become a reference point for sommeliers and serious wine retailers seeking authentic Sicilian natural wine.

**Personal Connection.** Occhipinti is also connected to the COS winery (her uncle is a partner) and has cultivated a devoted following among wine professionals and natural-wine enthusiasts. Her presence at CSW tastings and personal relationships with ownership reflect her status as a wine-world figure rather than merely a producer-for-hire.
