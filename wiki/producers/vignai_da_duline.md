# Vignai da Duline

Organic-from-founding (1997) artisan producer in Colli Orientali del Friuli, managed by Lorenzo Mocchiutti and Federica Magrini. Seven hectares farmed using natural-farming principles derived from Masanobu Fukuoka's philosophy, with erba medica (alfalfa) self-seeding the vineyard rows. Historic vineyards—some 100 years old—sourced at low yields to concentrate mineral essences from Friuli's ponca soils. Distributed in the USA by Kermit Lynch Wine Merchant (Berkeley, CA).

## History & Philosophy

Vignai da Duline was founded in 1997 when Lorenzo Mocchiutti inherited a few hectares of vines from his grandfather. The small estate, located in Villanova / San Giovanni al Natisone within Colli Orientali del Friuli, has been **organic from its inception**. Lorenzo manages the vineyard alongside Federica Magrini, implementing a **holistic, chemical-free approach** inspired by Japanese agriculturist Masanobu Fukuoka's principles of natural farming.

**Fukuoka Philosophy**: Rather than tilling or importing fertilizers, the vineyard has been colonized by **erba medica** (a clone of alfalfa), which aerates the soil while adding organic matter. The erba medica self-seeds annually, meaning the soil maintains itself through natural cycles—a radical rejection of industrial viticulture.

**Core Philosophy**: The estate embodies **biodiversity, minimal intervention, and respect for the natural balance of the vine**. This approach yields wines of remarkable mineral intensity, sourced from historic vineyards—some approaching 100 years old—harvested at low yields to concentrate the essence of place.

## Vineyard & Terroir

The vineyards occupy seven hectares in the Colli Orientali, with plots ranging across two appellations (Colli Orientali del Friuli and Friuli Grave). The terroir is anchored in **ponca soils** (sandstone and marl), which impart pronounced minerality and siliceous tension to the wines. The practice of low-yield harvesting emphasizes qualitative depth over quantity.

## Key Wines

- **Friulano / La Duline** (white) — the flagship; low-yield, mineral-driven expression
- **Chardonnay / Ronco Pitotti** (white) — from historic old-vine plots
- **Refosco** (red) — including the notable "Morus Nigra" cuvée
- Various small-lot bottlings from historic vineyard parcels

## Winemaking Approach

- **No added yeasts, nutrients, or manipulations**
- Spontaneous fermentation using native vineyard microflora
- Minimal sulfite intervention
- Unfiltered and unfined
- Low production (artisan scale)

## US Importer

**Kermit Lynch Wine Merchant** (Berkeley, California) imports and distributes Vignai da Duline wines in the USA. Kermit Lynch is renowned for curating low-intervention, terroir-focused producers.
