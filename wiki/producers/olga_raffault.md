# Domaine Olga Raffault

Iconic Chinon estate with a 30+ year CSW relationship dating to ~1990. Founded by Olga with legendary winemaker Ernest Zeinninger (a former German POW), Raffault is celebrated for traditional vinifications, minimal intervention, and exceptional age-worthiness. Now run by Sylvie Raffault and Eric de la Vigerie; being converted to organic certification.

## Terroir & Winemaking

**Savigny-en-Véron, Chinon** — clay-limestone terroir producing structured, mineral-driven Cabernet Francs built for 15–30 year aging. CSW 1990 tasting notes: "earthy complexity" and "limestone/clay character" even from difficult vintages.

**Traditional Methods:** Minimal intervention, long elevage in large barrels and vats (4 years for the flagship "Les Picasses"), natural release timing ("waiting until the wine is ready before marketing").

**Winemaking Signature** (Ernest Zeinninger era): Wild yeast fermentation, no fining/filtration where possible, extended barrel age emphasizing primary and secondary complexity over fruit-forward profiles.

## Key Wines

- **Les Picasses** — flagship 1er Cru Chinon, 4-year aging in large barrel, earthy/mineral profile, 20–30 year cellaring capacity
- **Les Barnabés** — softer, more forward style (CSW notes: "for those who enjoy a softer, more forward style of red Chinon")
- **La Singulière** — 2015 vintage featured as limited release (CSW 2024: "quantities are very limited")
- **Blanc** & **Rosé** — white and rosé cuvées also in rotation

## CSW Cellar Note

> These are among the very finest wines of the Loire Valley, full of life and amazing limestone/clay character. The wines show remarkable complexity and delicacy even 15–30 years of age.

## Ownership & Current Status

**Founder:** Olga (with winemaker Ernest Zeinninger, former German POW)  
**Current:** Sylvie Raffault & Eric de la Vigerie  
**Farming:** Being converted to organic certification (as of 2017 CSW note)
