## Notes

**Anthony Thevenet** is a contemporary Beaujolais producer representing the modern grower movement in the region — pursuing light, fruit-driven, terroir-focused Gamay expressions after apprenticeships with two of Beaujolais's most respected winemakers.

**Training & Mentorship.** Thevenet's winemaking foundation comes from extensive apprenticeships:
- **7 years with Georges Descombes** — one of Beaujolais's most celebrated growers, known for elegance and aging potential
- **5 years with Jean Foillard** — another reference producer for natural, minimalist Beaujolais

These mentorships with two of the region's finest establish Thevenet's commitment to quality and traditional grower values rather than commercial/industrial production.

**Philosophy.** Thevenet's stated goal is to "create light, fruit-driven wines that capture the essence of the terroir and offer pure enjoyment." This philosophy distinguishes his approach:
- **Light-bodied** — rejecting the heavier, more extracted Beaujolais styles (especially modern Beaujolais Nouveau)
- **Fruit-driven** — emphasizing primary fruit expression over oak or oxidative complexity
- **Terroir-focused** — wines that speak to their specific vineyard sites and vintage character
- **Drinkable** — wines designed for food pairing and social enjoyment rather than collection prestige

**Vineyard Sites & Production.** Thevenet works small parcels across Beaujolais:
- **Lancié parcels** — 3 small parcels producing Beaujolais-Villages; medium-bodied, bright, berry-driven expression
- **Morgon** — more structured expression from the region's most ageworthy terroir

**Key Wines.** The portfolio emphasizes approachability:
- **2023 Beaujolais-Villages** — medium-bodied, bright acidity, lovely berry fruits; designed for near-term drinking
- **2022 Morgon** — $26 retail (Down to Earth); representing good value in the appellation

**Vintage Context.** Per CSW (2025 coverage), the 2024 vintage was "difficult" in Beaujolais; those favoring "old-fashioned lighter style" will find it rewarding (comparison: 2014 vintage). This positions Thevenet as focused on terroir resilience and vintage expression rather than forced extraction in challenging years.

**Regional Position.** Thevenet exemplifies Beaujolais's grower revolution: learning from established quality producers, pursuing light/elegant expressions rather than blockbuster styles, and working tiny parcels for transparency. His mentorship lineage (Descombes → Foillard → Thevenet) shows the region's knowledge transmission across generations.

**Community Positioning.** CSW coverage (2025 Beaujolais feature) positions Thevenet alongside Daniel Bouland, Christian/Thibaut Ducroux, and Domaine Chignard — a network of quality-focused Beaujolais producers. Down to Earth carries his wines at ~$26 — accessible entry-point pricing that aligns with Thevenet's philosophy of drinkable, food-friendly wines.
