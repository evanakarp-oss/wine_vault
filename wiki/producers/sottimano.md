# Sottimano

## Notes

**Sottimano** is a prestigious Piedmont Barbaresco producer known for elegant, age-worthy Nebbiolo-based reds that exemplify the traditional Barbaresco style—a benchmark estate producing some of Piedmont's most serious, terroir-driven expressions from the region's finest vineyard sites. The domaine cultivates vineyards across multiple prestigious Barbaresco crus and surrounding Piedmont appellations, with particular focus on parcels that yield wines of structure, complexity, and genuine aging potential. The estate's winemaking approach emphasizes Barbaresco's classic character: native yeast fermentation, extended maceration for tannin development, and aging in French oak (primarily older, neutral barrels rather than new wood) for 18–30 months, allowing the wine's mineral and red-fruit core to emerge cleanly without domination by oak spice. DTE tracks a comprehensive portfolio of Sottimano's core expressions: **Barbaresco Cotta** (recent: 2018 at $65; older: 2008 at $49—reflecting aging potential and mature-bottle availability), **Barbaresco Curra** (2008 at $55), **Barbaresco Fausoni** (recent: 2018 at $65; older: 2009 at $49), and **Barbaresco Pajore** (2009 at $55; magnum 2014 at $115), plus secondary expressions including **Barbera d'Alba Pairolero** (2019 at $24, 2009 at $25), **Dolcetto d'Alba Bric del Salto** (2010 at $14), and **Langhe Nebbiolo** (2020 at $24, 2009 at $20). The comprehensive range ($14–$115) and deep back-vintage presence (2008–2010 older bottlings available) indicate mature-bottle trading and collector confidence in Sottimano's aging pedigree. Skurnik's representation confirms the estate's import priority status among serious Piedmont specialists.
