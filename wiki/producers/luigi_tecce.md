# Luigi Tecce

Luigi Tecce represents the fourth generation of his family to work the vineyards of Paternopoli, a mountainous corner of Taurasi in Campania's Irpinia region. In 1997, Luigi took over and began farming the estate without chemicals, a rejection of the previous generation's practices. The four-hectare vineyard sits 550 meters high on calcareous rocky soils overlain with volcanic dust, creating ideal conditions for Aglianico. Luigi farms his oldest vines—some 80 years old from Paternopoli—as Taurasi DOCG, while younger vines from nearby Castelfranci produce the earlier-drinking Campitaurasini DOC. All wines ferment with native yeasts; nothing is added to or subtracted. Vinification occurs in large open chestnut vats for Taurasi and stainless steel for Campitaurasini; aging follows in large Slavonian botte for 24 months. The result is wines of what Chambers Street Wines aptly calls "Power and Elegance."

## Notes

**Power and Elegance.** This phrase, coined by Chambers Street, perfectly captures Tecce's Taurasi: structured power from 80-year-old vines and calcareous-volcanic soils, yet elegant restraint through natural fermentation and minimal intervention. This is not fruit-forward hedonism but terroir-driven complexity.

**Chestnut vats signature.** The use of large open chestnut vats for Taurasi is a regional tradition worth reviving. Chestnut's neutral character, unlike oak's influence, allows the Aglianico to develop complexity without wood dominance. This is a philosophical choice in favor of varietal expression.

**Generational continuity and change.** Luigi inherited vineyards farmed conventionally and transformed them to natural farming—a personal choice, not a trendy pivot. This generational shift (1997) shows deep conviction about terroir and sustainability.

**Dual vineyard philosophy.** The split between 80-year-old Paternopoli vines (Taurasi) and younger Castelfranci fruit (Campitaurasini) is strategic: older vines for serious bottles, younger for earlier-drinking expressions. This allows flexibility in vintage quality without compromising reputation.

**US Market Portfolio & Production Scale.** Via Regal Wine Imports, Luigi Tecce offers a laser-focused portfolio centered on the estate's oldest parcels. The signature releases are Taurasi Riserva 'Poliphemo' (from 80+ year-old vines in Paternopoli, only produced in strong vintages) and Taurasi Riserva 'Puro Sangue', both demonstrating the "Power and Elegance" that Chambers Street identified as the estate's house style. The five-hectare vineyard across seven plots in the high-altitude Sud-Alta Valle subzone (soils layered with sand, limestone, and Vesuvius pumice) produces limited volumes. Luigi ferments entirely with native yeasts, adding nothing and subtracting nothing—he works without an enologist or agronomist, in deliberate solitude, allowing Aglianico's full potential to emerge from the calcareous-volcanic terroir. The chestnut-vat aging for Taurasi and Slavonian botte aging for 24 months represent a regional tradition and philosophical choice favoring varietal expression over wood influence.
