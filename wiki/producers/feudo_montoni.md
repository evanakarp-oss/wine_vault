# Feudo Montoni

## Notes

**Feudo Montoni** is a prestigious Sicilian organic wine producer based in Pachino (southeastern Sicily), known for distinctive, terroir-driven red wines from the **Nero d'Avola** grape, Sicily's most important red variety—the estate exemplifies the emerging quality revolution in Sicilian wine that has earned international critical recognition and collector interest. The Savino family owns Feudo Montoni, cultivating vineyards on limestone-clay soils near the Vendicari lagoon in Pachino, a region famous for producing Nero d'Avola with distinctive mineral character, red-fruit purity, and natural structure when managed organically and with restrained yields. Organic viticulture emphasizes natural pest management (minimal copper/sulfur inputs), hand harvesting with selective sorting, and modest yields to concentrate phenolic complexity and aromatic development in the small Nero d'Avola berries. Winemaking follows a traditional approach emphasizing the variety's natural soul rather than heavy new oak or extraction: native yeast fermentation, extended skin contact for tannin development, and aging in a mix of barrels (primarily neutral or older wood) for 18–30 months, allowing the wine's distinctive mineral and red-fruit character to emerge clearly. Key cuvées include **Nero d'Avola Vrucara** (signature bottling; 2018 scored 93 points from Wine Advocate and 92 from Wine Enthusiast; 2015 scored 94 from WA, 92 from WE; typical current-release pricing $55–75), plus **Grillo della Timpa** (white, 2022: 92 WE / 90 WS), **Lagnusa Nero d'Avola** (2020: 90 WA, 91 WE, 91 WS), and entry-level Catarratto and Nerello Mascalese expressions ($20–40 estimated). Wilson Daniels' representation confirms the estate's priority status in the US; Raeders tracks 7 cuvée/vintage entries ($21.99–$69.99), highlighting availability of mature and current-release bottles. CSW has championed Feudo Montoni with multiple dedicated articles (stars, 5 total), describing Nero d'Avola's natural character and the estate's commitment to terroir expression in a region historically overlooked by collectors. The estate's organic-farming commitment, Nero d'Avola focus, and Sicilian terroir position align with Evan's documented Southern Italy taste filter (organic, terroir-driven, grower-scale producers).
