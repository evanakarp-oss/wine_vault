# Auguste Clape

The standard-bearer of **Cornas** — the benchmark for old-school, whole-cluster,
no-new-oak Syrah from the appellation. Founded by the late Auguste Clape (former mayor
of Cornas, d. 2018), now run by son Pierre-Marie and grandson Olivier. Imported to the
US by Kermit Lynch. Surfaced for the vault via the Berserkers [Northern Rhône Tour](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/northern-rhone-tour-clusel-roch-jamet-perret-gonon-sorrel-cave-de-tain-clape-allemand/120248)
deep dive ([[../_views/wb_northern_rhone_tour_2026_06|view]]).

## Notes

**Winemaking.** Defining old-style Cornas: **no destemming (100% whole cluster)**,
native-yeast fermentation in concrete vats with no temperature control, then ~22 months
in **old foudres and demi-muids — no new oak**, bottled unfined and unfiltered. When
casks need replacing the family famously sends new foudres to **Domaine Ott in Bandol**
to be "broken in" first, so no fresh-oak flavour ever reaches the wine. Stem tannin is
treated as the structural backbone behind the wines' decades-long longevity.

**Vineyards & cuvées.** The flagship Cornas is a **blend of the domaine's terroirs**
(old-vine parcels including La Côte and Reynard) — Clape is regarded as a master of
assemblage despite working a single grape. **"Renaissance"** is the younger-vine Cornas
(earlier-drinking, more accessible entry to the house style). Also small amounts of
Côtes du Rhône (a genuine value bottling) and white Saint-Péray.

**Berserkers Northern Rhône Tour (#120248, 2016).** A 3pm Cornas appointment; one of the
two Cornas anchors of the trip alongside Allemand. In the follow-up **CA-vs-Rhône blind
tasting** (sequel #132977) the **2013 Clape Cornas placed 11th of 16 and "was guessed
incorrectly by most of the group"** — a textbook illustration that the greatest
traditional, whole-cluster Cornas shows closed and idiosyncratic at age 3–4 and needs
20+ years. Don't read the low blind placement as quality; read it as a drinking-window
signal.

**Cellar fit.** Dead-center for Evan (Cornas / traditional / terroir-driven), and a
natural shelf-mate to [[allemand|Allemand]] already in the cellar. Double community
consensus — deep dedicated LPV *fil* too (see
[[../_views/lpv_signal_map_french_regions_2026_06|LPV signal map]]). "Renaissance" is
the value/earlier-drinking entry; the flagship Cornas is a long-haul cellar bottle.
