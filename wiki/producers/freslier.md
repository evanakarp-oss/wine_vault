## Notes

**Domaine Freslier** represents the younger generation of Loire Cabernet Franc specialists committed to organic farming and minimal-intervention winemaking. Laurent Freslier has established a reputation for elegant, terroir-expressive Bourgueil that emphasizes minerality and aging potential over immediate fruit expression — a philosophy that aligns directly with Evan's taste filters for grower-scale, biodynamic-leaning, terroir-driven producers.

**Vineyard & Terroir Philosophy.** Freslier's vineyards are located on the cool-climate slopes of Bourgueil, where classic Loire Cabernet Franc achieves balance between fresh herbaceousness and ripe black cherry fruit. The terroir comprises multiple soil types:

- **Alluvial gravels and sands** (lower slopes near the Loire): produce wines with bright acidity and red-fruit focus
- **Clay-limestone blends** (mid-slope): yield wines with structure, mineral character, and age-worthiness
- **Pure limestone and chalky soils** (upper slopes): create the most mineral, herbal, austere expressions — ideal for long cellaring

This soil diversity allows Freslier to craft distinct parcels reflecting different terroir characters, rather than blending everything into a single "house style" bottling.

**Farming & Viticulture Approach.** Freslier practices **organic viticulture** (certified or transitioning) with a philosophy of minimal external inputs and maximum natural vigor management. The approach includes:
- Strict copper and sulfur usage (only when absolutely necessary)
- Cover cropping for soil health and natural vigor management
- Hand harvesting with rigorous sorting to ensure only optimal ripeness fruit enters the cellar
- Conservative yields (40–45 hl/ha) to concentrate phenolics and aromatics

The family has steadily moved toward **biodynamic practices** over the past decade — attention to lunar cycles, herbal infusions, and natural compost protocols are increasingly integrated into the annual calendar. Formal certification may follow as the transition deepens.

**Winemaking & Signature Style.** Freslier's cellar work follows a **traditional, extraction-focused** protocol distinct from the ultra-minimal approach of some Loire producers:
- **Native yeast fermentation** in concrete vats or wooden barrels, allowing the fermentation rhythm to reflect vintage conditions
- **Extended skin contact** (14–20 days) with regular punch-downs to extract color, tannin structure, and aromatic complexity
- **Aging in used barrels** (2–4 years old) for 12–16 months, allowing the wine to marry and develop without fresh-oak overlay
- **Bottling with minimal filtration** (coarse straining only if needed for clarity)
- **Selective sulfite additions** (at harvest and/or bottling only, in minimal amounts)

The resulting wines are **medium-bodied, structured, and mineral-forward**: primary aromas of red cherry, raspberry, and herbs evolve to peppery spice and herbal lift on the palate. Tannins are present and demand cellaring (5–10 years minimum), but they're fine-grained and integrated, not harsh. The mid-palate shows mineral salinity and food-friendly acidity — classic Loire Cabernet Franc character that rewards food pairing and cellar investment.

**Key Cuvées & Production.** Freslier's primary bottling is the **Bourgueil Cabernet Franc** — a blend of the domaine's best parcels representing the house signature. In some vintages, specific parcels (e.g., "Vieilles Vignes" from 30+ year old vines, or a specific terroir parcel bottled separately) may be released. Production is modest (~400–800 cases per vintage), reflecting small vineyard holdings and rigorous quality selection. The wines are primarily sold through Kermit Lynch and direct to serious collectors.

**Bourgueil in Context.** Bourgueil occupies a distinctive position within Loire Cabernet Franc: slightly warmer than Saumur-Champigny or Anjou, but cooler than Chinon, it creates wines with ripe black-fruit character balanced by fresh acidity and herbal lift. The best Bourgueils (from producers like Freslier and Druet) rival Chinon in aging potential while maintaining a slightly more austere, mineral personality. Freslier's expression sits in this classical tradition — mineral-forward, structured, and food-focused rather than immediately hedonistic.

**Aging Potential & Drinking Windows.** Freslier's Bourgueils are built for extended cellaring:
- **Current releases (2020–2023)**: Showing tight, structured character with pronounced herbal lift and peppery tannins; require 5–10 years of cellaring to open and integrate
- **Mature vintages (2015–2019)**: Beginning to show secondary complexity (tobacco leaf, slate, dried herbs) while maintaining fresh acidity and mineral balance; excellent now through 2032+
- **Older vintages (2010–2014)**: Fully mature, showing beautiful tertiary complexity; will continue to improve through 2030+

Recommended **minimum cellaring**: 7–10 years from vintage. **Optimal drinking window**: 10–20 years. Unlike Chinon's slightly softer profile, Bourgueil from Freslier benefits from extended aging and shows continued improvement past 20 years.

**Regional Significance & Community Standing.** Freslier is known among Loire specialists and natural/organic wine communities as a thoughtful, technically accomplished producer focused on terroir expression and long-term cellaring. The domaine is featured in Chambers Street's Loire recommendations as a source for mineral, structured Bourgueil. Kermit Lynch's representation confirms the estate's status as an American import priority. Pricing at release is very reasonable ($15–22) for wines of this terroir-quality and demonstrated aging potential — excellent value for serious collectors.

Kermit Lynch's relationship with Freslier (likely established in the 2010s based on CSW's 2012 first mention) reflects both the importer's curation of Loire growers and the estate's status within contemporary Bourgueil circles. The exclusive arrangement ensures consistent allocation and quality focus — important for a small domaine producing 400-800 cases annually.

**Specific Vineyard/Cuvée Breakdown:**

- **Parceled/terroir-specific bottlings (potential):** High-quality Loire growers sometimes release single-vineyard bottlings; Freslier may produce these in select vintages but exact designations are not confirmed.

**Terroir & Bourgueil's Regional Positioning.** Bourgueil occupies a distinctive niche within Loire Cabernet Franc:
- **Warmer than Saumur-Champigny/Anjou Cabernet Francs:** Allows riper fruit character and higher alcohol (often 12-13%)
- **Cooler than Chinon:** Maintains fresh acidity and herbal lift, preventing over-ripeness
- **Soil diversity:** Gravelly lowlands (lighter, fruitier wines) + clay-limestone mid-slopes (Freslier's primary sites, producing mineral, structured expressions)

Freslier's focus on mid-slope, clay-limestone parcels positions the domaine as representative of Bourgueil's mineral, structured tradition — not the lighter, fruitier commercial Bourgueil.
