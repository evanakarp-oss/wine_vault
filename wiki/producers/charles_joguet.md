# Charles Joguet

Historic Chinon producer and elder statesman of the appellation. Joguet appears in CSW tasting photos with Bernard Baudry and the Clos Rougeard Foucault brothers, positioning him as a peer reference point among the top Chinon terroir-driven houses.

## CSW References

Charles Joguet is referenced in CSW's coverage of Bernard Baudry (2009–2011 era), appearing in photos from annual Chinon producer tastings. While no dedicated Joguet feature articles are indexed in the vault, his presence alongside Baudry and Clos Rougeard suggests he represents the historical anchor of quality-focused Chinon winemaking.

**Context:** Sazilly (sub-region) is a clay-limestone plateau known for structured, age-worthy Cabernet Francs (CSW Baudry notes: "clay and limestone from superb hillside plots").

## Terroir & Style

**Sazilly, Chinon** — high-altitude clay-limestone terroir producing age-worthy, structured Cabernet Francs. Per CSW references to regional terroir (Baudry articles), Sazilly parcels develop complexity over 15–30 years, with earthy minerality (leather, herbs) and tannin structure built for cellaring.

Joguet's organic farming reflects the early organic movement in Loire (parallel to Bernard Baudry's conversion; late 1980s–1990s era).
