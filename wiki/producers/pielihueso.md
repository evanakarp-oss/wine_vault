# Pielihueso

Winemaker: **Celina & Alejandro Bartolome (father/daughter)**. Los Chacayes / Los Sauces, Tunuyán (Mendoza). Category: **Low Intervention**.

## Approach

Founded 2017. The name means 'skin and bones' in Spanish. Practicing organic. Orange wine specialist: Naranjo is a 3–4 week skin-contact blend of Torrontés, Sauvignon Blanc, and Chardonnay, spontaneously fermented with native yeasts in concrete and stainless steel, unfined, unfiltered. Fermented in amphora and used barrique.

## Grapes

Torrontés, Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Malbec

## Key Wines

- Naranjo (orange wine)
- Primero Blanco
- Primero Rosado
- Los Sauces Tinto

## Notes

**Pielihueso** ("Skin and Bones" in Spanish) represents the emerging generation of Mendoza natural-winemaking producers, founded in 2017 by father-daughter team Celina and Alejandro Bartolomé. The producer has rapidly gained recognition as a specialist in orange wine production — bold, skin-contact white wines that embody Argentina's new-wave commitment to experimental, minimal-intervention winemaking. The name itself reflects the aesthetic: wines stripped of commercial manipulation, exposing the naked fruit and terroir.

**Producer Philosophy & Emerging Status.** The 2017 founding positions Pielihueso within Argentina's post-2015 natural-wine renaissance — a cohort of young producers (many second-generation or outsiders to traditional wine families) who reject conventional wisdom and embrace experimental techniques as philosophical practice. Celina and Alejandro's collaborative approach (family-based production) aligns with Evan's taste filter for artisan-scale, family-operated enterprises over corporate wine projects.

**Farming Approach & Orange-Wine Specialization.** The domaine practices organic viticulture, a rarity for value-oriented Mendoza producers as of 2017. The distinctive winemaking approach centers on **orange wine**: extended skin contact (3–4 weeks) for white varieties, producing the characteristic amber/orange color and phenolic depth. The flagship expression is **Naranjo**, a blend of Torrontés, Sauvignon Blanc, and Chardonnay, spontaneously fermented with native yeasts in concrete and stainless steel, unfined and unfiltered. This expression is unconventional by Argentina standards and demands both technical skill and philosophical commitment.

**Key Wines & Varietal Focus.** The portfolio spans:
- **Naranjo** (orange wine) — the flagship, proving orange wines can work in Argentina's warm climate
- **Primero Blanco** (white blend) — the classical expression, demonstrating that Pielihueso can make traditional wines when choosing to
- **Primero Rosado** (rosé) — a third expression, exploring Argentina's rosé potential
- **Los Sauces Tinto** (red blend) — likely Malbec-based, maintaining the natural-wine approach

This range-spanning approach shows a producer confident enough to experiment across multiple styles rather than becoming trapped in a single technique.

**Terroir & Tunuyán Location.** Los Chacayes and Los Sauces are subregions within Tunuyán, Mendoza's high-altitude valley zone (Valle de Uco). This terroir produces naturally lower-alcohol fruit (typically 12.5–13.5% vs. Mendoza's commercial 14%+) with better acid retention — ideal for orange-wine production, where skin tannins already provide structure.

**US Market Position.** Distributed through Bowler (a progressive, natural-wine focused importer) and featured on natural-wine retail platforms (Bitter Pops, Highland Park Wine, De Wine Spot), Pielihueso has niche but enthusiastic US access. The producer's presence in Argentina Reloaded events positions it as part of the curator's vision for Argentina's future-oriented producers.

**Experimental Significance & Natural-Wine Movement.** Pielihueso exemplifies the natural-wine movement's core principle: experimentation as artistic practice, not gimmick. Orange wines remain controversial (some traditionalists dismiss them; others celebrate them as recovery of forgotten techniques), but producers like Pielihueso have demonstrated technical legitimacy and philosophical coherence. The father-daughter collaboration, organic farming, and consistent quality validate the approach.
