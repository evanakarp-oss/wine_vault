# Aganum

Aganum represents one of the most challenging cases of heroic viticulture in Italy. Founded by Gennaro Moccia in 1960 and now operated by his son Raffaele, the winery occupies four hectares on the volcanic hills of the Parco Naturale degli Astroni near Naples. The vineyard sits on black sandy volcanic soil on steep, narrow terraces—conditions that demand extraordinary labor but reward with distinctive minerality. Half the vines are Piedirosso and half are Falaghina, two ancient indigenous Campanian varieties. The winery maintains exceptionally low yields and late harvests with a low-intervention winemaking approach, proving that extreme terroir challenges can yield distinctive wines worth the effort.

## Notes

**Heroic viticulture defined.** Aganum is textbook heroic viticulture—steep terraces, narrow workable spaces, volcanic black sand, extreme climate. Yet the winery has persisted since 1960, proving that some terroirs demand the extra effort precisely because they deliver unique character impossible to replicate elsewhere.

**Dual varietal focus.** The 50-50 split between Piedirosso and Falaghina represents a balanced bet on Campania's two signature grapes—a red and white that capture the region's volcanic mineral character.

**Sustainable via necessity.** Low yields and late harvests are not trendy choices but survival strategy on these steep slopes. The result is concentration and minerality born from environmental constraint, not philosophical dogma.

**Terroir purity.** The black volcanic sand, proximity to the Astroni crater, and intense sun exposure create wines that taste of place in an uncompromising way. This is raw, unpolished Campanian terroir.

**Cellar fit.** Aganum fits the vault's taste for terroir-driven, low-intervention producers who farm extreme sites. The heroic viticulture angle and Campi Flegrei location (a volcanic AVA few collectors explore) make this a compelling discovery. Worth seeking out despite likely minimal US distribution.
