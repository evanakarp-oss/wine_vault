# Monteraponi

Organic Chianti Classico specialist in medieval Radda (Chianti Classico DOCG), established early 1970s and revived in early 2000s by current owner Michele Braganti. Twelve hectares certified organic by ICEA Italia at 570m elevation on limestone soils. Traditional, minimal-intervention winemaking in cement tanks with extended skin contact (25+ days), large-oak aging only, unfiltered/unfined. Produces Chianti Classico, Chianti Vinsanto, olive oil, grappa from 200-hectare medieval village estate.

## History & Philosophy

Monteraponi represents not just a winery but an **ancient medieval village** set in a natural amphitheater just outside Radda, built around three towers dating to the **10th century**. The entire property extends over 200 hectares, set on south-facing slopes sheltered from northern winds.

**Ownership Timeline**: The land has been in Michele Braganti's family since the early 1970s. His father did not commercialize wines, but Michele **revived and formalized the winery in the early 2000s** after falling deeply in love with Radda's ability to create world-class Sangiovese.

**Philosophy**: Monteraponi is dedicated to **traditional, low-tech, and gentle winemaking**. The estate refuses industrial shortcuts: no added yeasts, nutrients, or malolactic bacteria are used. Fermentation occurs in **cement tanks**, followed by **long maceration** (even the Chianti Classico spends at least **25 days on the skins**). Wines age in **large oak only**—no barriques or new wood. The finished wines are **neither fined nor filtered**, preserving maximum phenolic expression and terroir authenticity.

## Vineyard & Terroir

Of the 200-hectare property, only 12 hectares are planted to vineyards—a low-density approach reflecting Chianti Classico tradition. The vineyards occupy some of the **highest elevations in Chianti Classico**, reaching up to **570 meters above sea level**. This elevation confers cool-climate characteristics, extended hang time, and pronounced minerality.

**Soils**: The terroir is composed of a mixture of:
- **Hard Albarese limestone** (calcium-rich, imparting structure)
- **Softer Galestro limestone** (clay-rich, providing body and texture)

This limestone-based terroir is foundational to Chianti Classico's mineral character and aging potential.

## Farming & Certification

**ICEA Italia Organic Certification**: All Monteraponi products are **certified organic by ICEA Italia**, the Italian organic certification body. The estate practices organic viticulture without synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or fertilizers.

## Key Wines

- **Chianti Classico** (flagship; 25+ days skin contact, large oak, unfiltered/unfined)
- **Chianti Classico Il Campitello Riserva** (reserve bottling from premium vineyard sites)
- **Chianti Vinsanto** (Tuscan dessert wine)
- **Olive oil**
- **Grappa** (from fermentation residues)
