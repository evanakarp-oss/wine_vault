# VHR, Vine Hill Ranch

A grower-first estate at the **southern end of Oakville**, on the bench at the foot
of the Mayacamas — neighbored by **Harlan** to the north, Beckstoffer Missouri Hopper
to the east, and **Dominus** to the south. The site's farming records reach back to
**1884**; the Phillips family bought the surrounding ranch in **1956** and began
growing grapes as Vine Hill Ranch in **1959**. Of ~600 acres, about **70 are planted,
almost entirely to Cabernet Sauvignon**, farmed with an explicit conservation mindset
across 125+ years of stewardship.

VHR is **first and foremost a vineyard**: it sells to ~13 winery partners, eight of
whom bottle single-vineyard wines off the ranch (a roll call of top Oakville Cabs).
Since the inaugural **2008** vintage the Phillips family (Bruce Phillips) has kept
back a small estate bottling — the **VHR Cabernet Sauvignon** — under winemaker
**Françoise Peschon** (ex-Araujo/Eisele Estate), whose hand is the restraint-and-
structure signature that puts this on Evan's canon. Historic site + conservation
farming + Peschon = elegant, ageworthy, site-transparent Oakville rather than
blockbuster.

## Notes

- **Why it fits:** historic Oakville bench site, conservation-minded grower, and the
  Françoise Peschon restraint signature (the same hand behind Araujo/Eisele). Elegant
  and structured rather than opulent.
- **Second label:** **Baker & Hamilton** is VHR's négociant-style sibling (same fruit
  base / Peschon hand) — kept as a "worth a look" on the canon, leans more polished.
- **Pricing (2026-06):** the VHR estate Cabernet is the pricey end — $325 (2022) to
  $345 (2023) on release, ~$301 wine-searcher average; back-vintages cheaper (2020
  ~$170). Direct-winery allocation. The **Baker & Hamilton** second label (same fruit
  base, Peschon hand) is the value route in at ~$90–95.
- **Onboarding source:** confirmed from the ◐ worth-a-look tier (Wine Advocate +
  Vinous Napa index triage) → promoted to a page. Created 2026-06-18 at Evan's
  direction ("VHR great").
