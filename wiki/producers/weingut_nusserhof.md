# Weingut Nusserhof

Rare urban winery (2.5 ha) within Bolzano city center, operated by Heinrich Mayr and daughter Gloria since 1994 organic certification (documented since 1788). Cultivates only indigenous varieties—Lagrein, Teroldego, and rare Blatterle (white) nearly extinct varietal revived by Mayr family. Soils rich in alluvial sediment, porphyry, sand shaped by Isarco River. Natural fermentation (no temperature control, no inoculated yeasts), long maceration on reds, minimum 2.5 years large French oak aging plus 2 additional years bottle-age before release.

## Notes

**Varietal rescue:** Blatterle revival showcases commitment to heritage preservation. Urban site paradox: as Bolzano expanded postwar, vineyard remained despite development. Alluvial terroir (river-deposited soils) offers minerality uncommon for city-center sites. Long aging protocols reflect quality-over-volume philosophy rare in crowded urban settings.
