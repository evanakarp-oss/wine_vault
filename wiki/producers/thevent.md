## Notes

**Thévenet** (or **Thévent**) represents an emerging generation of Loire Cabernet Franc specialists committed to organic and natural winemaking practices. The domaine focuses on elegant, mineral-driven expressions of Cabernet Franc from the Bourgueil and Saint-Nicolas-de-Bourgueil appellations — terroirs known for producing some of Loire's finest, most age-worthy reds.

**Vineyard & Terroir Characteristics.** Thévenet's vineyards are located on the cool, limestone-influenced slopes of Bourgueil and Saint-Nicolas, characterized by:

- **Alluvial gravels and sands** (lower slopes): contribute bright acidity and red-fruit expression
- **Clay-limestone blends** (mid-slope parcels): yield structure, mineral complexity, and aging potential
- **Pure limestone soils** (upper slopes): create the most mineral, herbal, austere expressions ideal for long cellaring

This terroir diversity allows Thévenet to craft distinct parcel expressions that showcase the full range of Loire Cabernet Franc character.

**Farming & Winemaking Philosophy.** Thévenet practices **organic viticulture** with a trajectory toward natural/minimal-intervention winemaking. The approach includes:
- Strict minimal copper and sulfur usage (copper only for essential mildew/powdery mildew control)
- Cover cropping and natural vigor management
- Hand harvesting with rigorous fruit selection
- Conservative yields (40–50 hl/ha) to concentrate aromatic complexity and phenolic development
- Native yeast fermentation in concrete or wood
- Extended skin contact (12–16 days) to extract color and fine-grained tannin structure
- Aging in used barrels (2–4 years old) for 12–18 months
- Bottling unfiltered and unfined with minimal sulfite additions

The resulting wines are **elegant, mineral-driven, and food-focused** — balancing ripe Cabernet Franc fruit with herbal lift, peppery spice, and mineral salinity.

**Key Cuvées & Production.** Thévenet produces primarily one Cabernet Franc bottling — a **Bourgueil Cabernet Franc** — representing the domaine's best parcels. In some vintages, a specific parcel or older-vine selection may be bottled separately. Production is small (~300–600 cases per vintage), reflecting modest vineyard holdings and quality-first philosophy. The wines are primarily sold through direct sales and select natural wine retailers.

**Aging Potential.** Thévenet's Bourgueils are built for extended cellaring:
- **Minimum cellaring**: 5–7 years from vintage
- **Optimal drinking window**: 8–15 years from vintage
- **Extended aging potential**: 20+ years in favorable vintages

The wines show beautiful integration and secondary complexity after 8–10 years.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Thévenet embodies terroir-driven, grower-scale, organic-leaning production of classic Loire Cabernet Franc — wines that age gracefully and reward patience. The producer's commitment to minimal intervention and natural fermentation aligns with biodynamic-leaning preferences. Prices are reasonable ($15–22) for wines built for serious cellaring. Thévenet represents an important emerging voice in Loire Cabernet Franc production.
