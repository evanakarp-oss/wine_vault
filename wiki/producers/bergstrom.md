## Notes

**Bergstrom** is one of the Pacific Northwest's most important and consistently acclaimed Pinot Noir and Chardonnay producers, known for biodynamically-farmed, terroir-driven wines from the Willamette Valley (Oregon) that exemplify modern American cool-climate viticulture at its finest. The estate manages five distinct vineyard parcels across premium Willamette terroirs, each producing distinctive single-vineyard expressions while also contributing to blended flagship bottlings — a portfolio strategy that reflects serious, layered quality thinking. Recent critical elevation (Wine Advocate 96 for multiple cuvées, Wine Spectator Top 100 rankings) and consistent top-tier Raeders presence establish Bergstrom as a reference-level producer deserving serious collector attention.

**Producer Philosophy & Biodynamic Farming.** Bergstrom practices certified biodynamic agriculture across its five estate vineyard parcels, implementing a comprehensive sustainability philosophy that directly aligns with Evan's taste filters (biodynamic farming, terroir-driven, artisan-scale). Biodynamic practices emphasize soil health, biodiversity, natural pest and disease management, and minimal input philosophy — resulting in wines that express specific vineyard terroir rather than over-manipulated, high-extraction profiles. This farming commitment distinguishes Bergstrom from the broader Willamette Valley producer community and positions the estate as a philosophical peer to European growers like Stéphane Tissot (Jura) and Domaine de Beaurenard (Rhône).

**Terroir & Vineyard Portfolio.** Bergstrom's five estate vineyards are strategically positioned across the Willamette Valley's most prestigious terroir zones: each parcel is named and distinct, reflecting specific soil character, microclimate, and aspect. The estate produces single-vineyard Pinot Noirs (Le Pré du Col, Silice, Gregory Ranch) alongside blended "Cumberland Reserve" flagship expressions. Chardonnay portfolio includes "Sigrid" and "Old Stones" designations. Each vineyard parcel contributes distinct aromatic character: Le Pré du Col (WA 96) shows greater floral complexity; Silice (WA 94) emphasizes mineral precision and structure; Gregory Ranch (WA 93) balances fruit accessibility with age-worthiness. This parcel-specific approach allows exploration of Willamette Valley terroir nuance.

**Winemaking Approach & Signature Expressions.** Bergstrom emphasizes bright acidity, mineral precision, and elegant tannin structure rather than extraction-driven, new-oak dominated profiles. Pinot Noirs show compelling aromatic profiles: berries, forest floor, earthy undertones, often with herbal/floral complexity. Cumberland Reserve (multiple vintages: 2023 WA 93/JS 94, 2022 WA 92/JS 94) represents the core quality statement — a blend from the estate's five vineyards plus selective purchased fruit, aged 10 months, showing focused acidity and spicy mineral finish. Chardonnay similarly emphasizes freshness and mineral definition: Sigrid 2019 (WA 96) shows Meyer lemon, citrus, grapefruit, lime with sweet baking spices and herbal complexity. These profiles position Bergstrom squarely within the "cool-climate, terroir-focused" school rather than ripe, opulent California Pinot traditions.

**Critical Recognition & Peak Performance.** Bergstrom has attracted exceptional recent critical attention: Le Pré du Col 2019 (WA 96, WE 93) ranks among the highest-scored Oregon Pinots; Chardonnay Sigrid 2019 (WA 96, WE 94) represents elite Willamette white performance; Pinot Noir Silice 2021 (WA 94, JS 94, ranked #32 Wine Spectator Top 100 of 2023) confirms sustained peak-form performance across vintages. Cumberland Reserve consistently earns high scores across multiple vintages (2023: WA 93, JS 94; 2022: WA 92, JS 94), establishing reliable excellence. This body of critical recognition positions Bergstrom as a reference-level producer — comparable to the highest-quality Burgundy or Rhône expressions in cool-climate elegance and aging potential.

**US Market Presence & Pricing Strategy.** Imported by Wilson Daniels (a major fine wine distributor), Bergstrom has consistent availability through specialty retailers. Raeders tracks 11 cuvée-vintage expressions ranging $29.99 (Cumberland Reserve 375ml) to $149.99 (Cumberland Reserve 1.5L magnum). Notably, peak expressions — Le Pré du Col 2019 (WA 96) and Chardonnay Sigrid 2019 (WA 96) — appear to be allocated or out-of-stock at Raeders, reflecting strong market demand for top-tier bottlings. More recent vintages (Silice 2021 at $119.99, Cumberland Reserve 2023 at $49.99) offer strong value for quality — WA 94 and WA 93 wines at these price points represent competitive positioning against California Pinot alternatives.

**Aging Potential & Cellar Strategy.** Bergstrom's elegant, mineral-focused Pinot Noirs show age-worthiness typical of cool-climate expressions: minimum 5-8 years cellaring before peak drinking; optimal window 10-18 years from vintage; extended aging potential to 20+ years for premium vineyard-specific bottlings. Chardonnay, particularly Sigrid, shows Burgundy-like aging potential: 8-12 years cellaring for evolution; peak drinking 12-20 years from vintage. Recent vintages (2019-2023 Pinots) are well-positioned for extended aging projects — collectors can acquire current releases and expect significant flavor evolution and complexity development over 10-15 years.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Bergstrom exemplifies multiple core taste filters: (1) **biodynamic farming** — certified, documented commitment across five estate vineyards; (2) **cool-climate terroir expression** — Willamette Valley Pinot Noir and Chardonnay emphasizing mineral precision, bright acidity, and elegance rather than ripeness; (3) **artisan-scale, vineyard-focused production** — family-owned estate managing distinct parcels with specific expressions; (4) **aged reference track record** — 2019-2023 vintages available for aging confirmation; (5) **US boutique positioning** — aligned with Evan's California/Pacific Northwest interest. Note: Bergstrom is Oregon-based (Willamette Valley), not California proper, but represents one of the finest terroir-focused Pinot Noir producers available in the US market. The estate should be a regular acquisition target for Evan's cellar — particularly for aged-parcel bottlings (Le Pré du Col, Silice) seeking 10-15 year cellaring, and for Chardonnay exploration (Sigrid 2019 is an exceptional reference).
