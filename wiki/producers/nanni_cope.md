# Nanni Copè

Giovanni Ascione (childhood nickname Nanni Copè) followed an unexpected path to winemaking. A journalist who traveled throughout Italy telling wine stories for years, he decided in 2007 to produce a wine that reflects terroir rather than writing about others' work. Located in Castel Campagnano in the upper region of Caserta, northern Campania, Nanni Copè produces just one red wine, Sabbie di Sopra il Bosco ("Sands Above the Forest"), in extremely limited quantities averaging 7,500 bottles annually—a commitment to quality over production. The wine is made primarily from Palagrello Nero, an indigenous Campanian variety, with a small amount of Casavecchia, another local rare grape. The result drinks well for 15–20 years and has conquered fans and earned highest marks in Italian wine guides since its second release in 2009.

## Notes

**Journalist to winemaker.** Ascione's career pivot—from chronicling wine to making it—brings unusual perspective. Years spent traveling and writing about Italian terroir informed his aesthetic: minimal intervention, indigenous varieties, terroir purity.

**Pallagrello Nero focus.** Sabbie di Sopra il Bosco showcases Palagrello Nero, a nearly extinct indigenous Campanian red. Ascione's championing of this varietal—blended with Casavecchia, another local rarity—represents exactly the kind of varietal rescue work worth celebrating. Each bottle is a statement about regional heritage.

**Extreme production discipline.** Averaging 7,500 bottles annually from a single wine reflects absolute commitment to quality. No diversification, no portfolio expansion, no marketing gimmicks—just one wine, made perfectly, released in limited quantities. This is the opposite of modern winery growth logic.

**Aging potential and recognition.** The 15–20 year aging window and highest marks in Italian guides since 2009 suggest these are serious, structured reds with real complexity. The late start of quality recognition (second release onward) suggests Ascione was patient with his vineyards, letting them mature before demanding excellence.

**Cellar fit.** Nanni Copè fits the vault's taste for single-variety obsessives who rescue endangered grapes. The Palagrello Nero specialty and extreme production discipline make this a distinctive addition. Limited US distribution will be a sourcing challenge, but the rarity and story justify pursuit. Campania deep-dive essential.
