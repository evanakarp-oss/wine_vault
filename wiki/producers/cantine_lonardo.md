# Cantine Lonardo

The Lonardo family has lived in Taurasi since the late 18th century, a multi-generational commitment to the region's vineyards. Though the family owned the land for many years, the estate-bottled brand "Contrade di Taurasi" began only in 1998, replacing the earlier practice of selling grapes to the cooperative. Today, brothers Alessandro and his sister Antonella (now joined by daughter Antonella, a professor) operate the five-hectare vineyard with what they call "enlightened traditionalism"—long macerations, aging in 600-liter tonneaux with minimal new wood, and organic farming certified since 2016. Cantine Lonardo has become a keeper of endangered grapes, notably Rovello (a local white), one of only three remaining producers of this vanishing variety.

## Notes

**Enlightened traditionalism.** Lonardo's philosophy balances reverence for Taurasi's traditional methods—long macerations, large-format oak aging—with organic farming that respects the vineyards themselves. This is not modernism or reactionism, but thoughtful continuity.

**Rovello guardianship.** Perhaps Lonardo's most important contribution is saving Rovello, an indigenous white grape nearly extinct. As one of only three wineries still cultivating it, Lonardo preserves not just a wine but a piece of regional viticultural heritage. The Grecomusc' bottling showcases this rare varietal.

**Organic since 2016.** The family farmed organically for generations before formal certification in 2016—a testament to long-held ecological values predating trendy certification.

**Taurasi quality.** The Lonardos produce Taurasi with the structure and aging potential the DOCG demands. These are age-worthy reds designed to develop over decades, reflecting Irpinia's calcareous volcanic soils and the region's cool microclimate.
