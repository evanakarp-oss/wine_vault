# Jamet

The reference point for classically styled, whole-cluster **Côte-Rôtie**. Run by
Jean-Paul Jamet (and historically with brother Jean-Luc, until the 2013 split of the
family holdings) and now with the next generation, the domaine builds its flagship
Côte-Rôtie from a mosaic of parcels rather than isolating single sites — a deliberate
counterpoint to the New-World single-vineyard reflex. Imported to the US by Kermit
Lynch. Surfaced for the vault via the Berserkers [Northern Rhône Tour](https://www.wineberserkers.com/t/northern-rhone-tour-clusel-roch-jamet-perret-gonon-sorrel-cave-de-tain-clape-allemand/120248)
deep dive ([[../_views/wb_northern_rhone_tour_2026_06|view]]).

## Notes

**Vineyards.** ~8.5 ha of Côte-Rôtie spread across ~25 parcels in up to 17 *lieux-dits*.
The majority sits on the **Côte Brune** — fractured, well-drained schist that gives
structure and savoury depth — with roughly a quarter on the warmer, sandier **Côte
Blonde** (more perfume and finesse). Also small amounts of Condrieu and a Côtes du
Rhône.

**Winemaking.** Universally read as *the* classicist of the appellation:
**whole-cluster** fermentation, resisting the destemming trend across Jean-Paul's
whole career. The signature is **assemblage** — complexity is built from the interplay
of many parcels blended into one Côte-Rôtie, not from single-parcel bottlings. Aging
favours older wood over new oak. The wines are built for the long haul.

**Berserkers Northern Rhône Tour (#120248, 2016).** Visited by the touring California
winemakers as the Côte-Rôtie benchmark. In the follow-up **CA-vs-Rhône blind tasting**
(sequel thread #132977), the **2012 Jamet Côte-Rôtie ranked 3rd of 16** — the
top-finishing French wine in a flight that the young (release-plush) California Syrahs
otherwise led. Reads as: structure + perfume that rewards patience.

**Cellar fit.** Squarely in Evan's N. Rhône lane (Côte-Rôtie / terroir-driven,
traditional). Double community consensus — Jamet also carries a deep dedicated *fil* on
LPV (see [[../_views/lpv_signal_map_french_regions_2026_06|LPV signal map]]). A
long-haul cellar bottle rather than a drink-now value. *(Caveat: vineyard/winemaking
