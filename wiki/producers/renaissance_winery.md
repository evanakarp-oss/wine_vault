# Renaissance Vineyard & Winery

The deep cut — a terraced hillside estate in the **North Yuba AVA** (Sierra
Foothills, Oregon House), founded **1975 by the Fellowship of Friends**. At its
height ~**365 acres** of vines on ~200 miles of terraces carved nearly 360° around a
**decomposing red-granite dome**, at **1,700–2,300'** above the Central Valley fog
line, with >40°F diurnal swings preserving acidity. Decomposed granite + metamorphic
schist soils, dry-farmed. The Cabernet is the flagship red: austere, leathery, stony,
deeply structured and built for the cellar — a genuinely off-the-radar California Cab
terroir with no real analog elsewhere in the vault.

## Wines

- **Estate / Reserve Cabernet Sauvignon (North Yuba)** — flagship across the
  1990s–2000s; single-block and Reserve bottlings, structured tannins, dark fruit,
  stony minerality.
- **Late-harvest Riesling** — the estate's other historic calling card.
- **"Taken from Granite"** — small library re-releases of Beinstock-era wines
  (somm/cult following).

## Notes

**Gideon Beinstock** became winemaker in **1993** and developed a minimal-intervention
style that predated the 2010s natural-wine movement; the late-'90s/early-2000s
Beinstock vintages are the collector sweet spot (Beinstock later founded Clos Saron).
Because the estate is so far off the radar, **older library vintages remain findable
and often underpriced for the age and quality** — the best entry point. Traditional,
European-leaning, the opposite of polished modern Napa.

No current US importer (domestic estate); chase library bottles direct and via
specialist retailers (e.g. SLAKE SF for "Taken from Granite"). Sources: see
`raw/web_research/renaissance_winery.md`.
