# Emidio Pepe

Benchmark Abruzzo producer since 1964, renowned for age-worthy Trebbiano and Montepulciano d'Abruzzo from biodynamically farmed vineyards in the Teramo hills. The Pepe family has produced wine in Torano Nuovo since 1899; Emidio set out to prove that both whites and reds from this region could age for decades, revolutionary at the time.

## Vineyard & Farming

17 hectares of vines situated between the Adriatic Sea and the Apennines, on clay over limestone soils. Certified biodynamic since 2006, with cover crops between rows and all work done by hand. Traditional biodynamic calendar practices inform the vintage cycle.

## Winemaking Philosophy

Ultra-traditional, minimal intervention approach perfected over decades. Trebbiano foot-trodden in wooden tubs; reds destemmed by hand. Both whites and reds ferment on native yeasts in cement tanks, aging there 18 to 24 months. Wines bottle unfined and unfiltered, a statement of philosophy as much as practice. This commitment to non-invasive winemaking renders wines with remarkable transparency and aging potential.

## Key Wines

- **Trebbiano d'Abruzzo** — the estate's legendary white, intensely mineral and age-worthy
- **Montepulciano d'Abruzzo** — elegant red with structure for 20+ year cellaring
- **Montepulciano d'Abruzzo Branella** — specific vineyard designation
- **Montepulciano d'Abruzzo Selezione Vecchie Vigne** — old-vine cuvée

## Legacy & Reputation

Five generations of the Pepe family have worked the estate, from founder Emidio to sisters Daniela and Sofia, with Chiara de Iulis Pepe now leading in the cellar. The winery maintains a historic library of over 350,000 bottles, from which aged vintages are released in small quantities. Emidio Pepe stands as one of Abruzzo's greatest producers and a pioneer in demonstrating the region's capacity for ageworthy wine.

## Notes

Emidio Pepe represents the gold standard of traditional Abruzzo production. The combination of biodynamic farming, ultra-traditional winemaking (foot-trodden whites, cement tank aging), and a centuries-old family commitment to the terroir make this estate essential for understanding the region's finest expressions. The estate's massive library cellar and practice of releasing aged vintages give Pepe an unusual role as both current producer and custodian of historical Abruzzo wine.
