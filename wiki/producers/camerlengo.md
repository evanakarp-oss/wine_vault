# Azienda Agricola Camerlengo

A family estate in Rapolla (Potenza province) devoted to expressing the full potential of Aglianico del Vulture through natural-winemaking principles and organic viticulture. Antonio Cascarano, an architect from Rome, acquired and restored the historic cellar and vineyard holdings inherited from his grandfather, who began producing wine and oil in 1900. The name Camerlengo—taken from the title of the Cardinal who administers the Apostolic Chamber of the Holy See—reflects the estate's respect for the land's heritage and spiritual sense of place. Camerlengo's philosophy centers on returning to natural agriculture and cellar practices that honor the right rhythms of winemaking: spontaneous fermentations, indigenous yeasts only, and refinements in wooden barrels that allow the wine's minerality to develop fully.

## Notes

The estate encompasses five hectares of organically farmed Aglianico vines planted on the potash- and silicon-rich volcanic soils of Monte Vulture, interspersed with chestnut and olive trees that contribute to the microclimate and biodiversity of the vineyard. The defining wine is Aglianico del Vulture, with the Antelio bottling representing the estate's flagship offering—a wine of structure, minerality, and aging potential. Camerlengo's commitment to low-intervention winemaking and the preservation of grandfather's pre-industrial farming methods aligns the estate with the emerging movement of quality-focused, terroir-driven producers reclaiming Basilicata's reputation for world-class volcanic Aglianico. The pyroclastic tuff and mineral-rich soils of the Vulture slopes impart a distinctive grip and complexity that expresses itself fully only through patient, respectful winemaking.
