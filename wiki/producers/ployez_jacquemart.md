## Notes

**Ployez-Jacquemart** is a historic grower-Champagne producer based in Ludes (Montagne de Reims), known for producing aged vintage and non-vintage Champagnes with a focus on complexity and extended cellaring potential. The house exemplifies the grower-Champagne aesthetic — modest production, quality-over-volume philosophy, and wines that express terroir and aging potential rather than immediate pleasure.

**Portfolio & Key Wines.** Down to Earth Wine tracks six Ployez-Jacquemart bottlings ranging from $25-$109, positioning the house across multiple price tiers and formats. Key offerings include **Extra Quality Brut** (non-vintage, $39 standard, $25 half-bottle, $85 magnum) — the house's primary expression, built for food pairing and extended aging — and **Extra Brut Rosé** (non-vintage, $55 standard, $29 half-bottle, $109 magnum), a drier, more complex expression likely crafted for serious collectors rather than celebratory service. The availability across multiple bottle sizes (375ml half-bottles, 750ml, magnums) signals distribution through fine-dining establishments and serious retail channels.

**Production Scale & House Style.** Grower-Champagnes typically produce 30,000-150,000 bottles annually, far smaller than major houses (Veuve Clicquot ~10M bottles). Ployez-Jacquemart sits within this grower tier, maintaining personal oversight of vineyard work and cellar operations. The focus on Extra Brut (minimal residual sugar, typically 0-6 g/L) rather than Brut or Demi-Sec reflects a contemporary grower philosophy: wines that showcase dosage restraint, acidity structure, and terroir rather than sweetness.

**Terroir & Regional Significance.** Ludes sits on the Montagne de Reims, a premier Champagne chalk sub-region known for Pinot Noir-driven, structured, mineral-forward wines. The Extra Brut positioning — particularly the rosé bottling — highlights Ployez-Jacquemart's attention to mineral expression and food compatibility, aligning with the broader grower-Champagne movement's emphasis on terroir and complexity over dosage.

**Importer Distribution & Availability.** Represented in the USA by Bowler and La Grenouille importers, positioning the house within quality-focused, European wine networks. The presence on Down to Earth Wine's portfolio (Evan Panzer's curated selection of grower-scale, natural producers) confirms status within serious natural/low-intervention Champagne circles.

**Aging & Drinking Window.** Non-vintage Champagnes from grower producers benefit from 3-8 years post-release aging before opening. Current releases tracked at DTE are likely 2020-2021 base vintages; they will continue improving through 2028-2032, developing complex tertiary characteristics (toasted almond, petrol, dried apple) while maintaining the fine mousse and acidity that define quality Champagne aging.
