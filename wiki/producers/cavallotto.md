## Notes

**Cavallotto** is a historic Piedmont house specializing in Barolo from the prestigious village of Castiglione Falletto, known for producing some of the region's most structured, ageworthy expressions. Founded in the 19th century, the estate is now run by the Cavallotto family and represents the old-guard Barolo philosophy: traditional viticulture, long maceration, extended barrel aging, and wines built for 20-40 year aging curves. The house holds among the highest Berserkers collector rankings in the Barolo category (rank 158 of 2067 producers), with strong recent momentum (2.0× in 2023-26), reflecting sustained appreciation within serious wine communities.

**Terroir & Vineyard Holdings.** Cavallotto operates in Castiglione Falletto, one of the three Barolo cru villages that consistently produce the most structured, mineral-focused wines in the appellation. The signature site is **Bricco Boschis**, a south-facing slope yielding dark, savory Nebbiolo with pronounced tar, licorice, and iron-ore notes. The estate practices low-intervention viticulture and traditional long-maceration winemaking (often 30+ days on skins), producing wines that are initially austere but develop remarkable complexity with 10-20 years of cellaring. Acid and tannin are pronounced in youth, requiring patience.

**Winemaking Philosophy.** Cavallotto follows the classical Barolo school: extended aging in large cask (not small oak), minimal fining or filtration, and a focus on vintage and terroir expression over "house style." The result is wines that express Castiglione's characteristic mineral severity and dark-fruit intensity, with a silky mid-palate that emerges after 5-10 years in bottle. The Bricco Boschis bottlings are flagship expressions; the San Giuseppe Riserva is the top cuvée, released only in exceptional vintages and aged extensively before release.

**Critic Recognition & Collector Standing.** Cavallotto's wines consistently score in the 92-98 range across Wine Advocate, Wine Spectator, and Vinous. The 2016 Bricco Boschis San Giuseppe Riserva scored 98 points, exemplifying the house's quality floor. CSW has championed the estate across 6 dedicated articles (2013-2024), emphasizing the producer's alignment with terroir-driven, age-worthy Barolo and the beauty of Castiglione Falletto's setting. The producer is notably championed for mature wines (10-30 year old vintages) and is a reliable recommendation for collectors seeking classic Barolo structure and aging potential rather than modern opulence.

**Aging Trajectory & Positioning.** Cavallotto's wines are marathon drinkers: Bricco Boschis peaks 12-25 years out; San Giuseppe Riserva can age 25-40 years. The 2010 Bricco Boschis (at 16 years) is at its sweet spot now (2026), developing secondary aromas and silky tannins. Evan's taste alignment is high: terroir-driven, structured for aging, old-guard philosophy, and transparent of place. The house is a core recommendation for anyone seeking mature Barolo with real aging pedigree and no modern extraction or overt oak.
