# Feudi di San Gregorio

Feudi di San Gregorio represents the modern, technically-ambitious face of Campanian winemaking — a younger estate (founded 1986) that emerged as contemporary rival to traditional houses like Mastroberardino through commitment to innovation, investment in winery infrastructure, and aggressive pursuit of critical acclaim. The estate exemplifies how new-generation entrepreneurs can elevate regional wine through quality focus and modern viticultural science.

## Founding & Modern Growth

Feudi di San Gregorio was founded in **1986** by Aniello Sannino and Salvatore Lombardo, entrepreneurs who recognized Campania's potential and invested substantially in vineyard acquisition, winery construction, and recruitment of top winemaking talent. Unlike Mastroberardino (rooted in 1878 family tradition), Feudi represents a project-based, business-model approach to winemaking: acquire best vineyard sites, hire elite talent, produce wines of demonstrable technical excellence, and market them aggressively to international critics.

This model proved remarkably successful. Within 20-30 years, Feudi di San Gregorio became recognized alongside Mastroberardino as one of Campania's two leading producers.

## Terroir & Vineyard Holdings

Feudi owns approximately **200 hectares** across Campania, with significant holdings in:

**Greco di Tufo** — volcanic soils near Tufo, known for high-acidity, mineral-driven whites. Feudi's Greco plantings are among the region's largest.

**Fiano di Avellino** — limestone-volcanic blend in Avellino, producing rounder whites. Feudi's Fiano "Pietracalda" has achieved critical fame.

**Taurasi** — Aglianico-focused holdings in Avellino hinterland. Feudi's flagship Taurasi "Taurasi Riserva Patrimonium" is among Campania's most acclaimed reds.

The volcanic Campanian geology is the foundation for Feudi's terroir story — the winery invests heavily in understanding and expressing soil character through technical winemaking.

## Modern Winemaking: Technical Excellence & International Style

Feudi di San Gregorio's winemaking emphasizes **technical precision and modern infrastructure**:

**Greco di Tufo (White):**
- Controlled-temperature fermentation in stainless steel
- Selective yeast management to optimize flavor profiles
- Result: fresh, mineral-driven white with good structure; ages 15+ years

**Fiano di Avellino (White):**
- "Pietracalda" bottling is Feudi's flagship white; limestone-soil expression
- Longer barrel aging (12+ months) compared to Greco
- Result: structured, complex white with herbs, citrus, minerals; ages 20+ years

**Taurasi (Red):**
- Extended maceration and long barrel aging (20-24 months in French oak)
- "Taurasi Riserva Patrimonium" represents the estate's highest ambition for the variety
- Result: powerful, well-structured red; ages 30-40+ years

**Production Scale:** Feudi produces 800,000+ bottles annually (making it significantly larger than Mastroberardino), positioning it as a major Campanian force with international distribution reach.

## Critical Standing & Market Presence

Feudi di San Gregorio has achieved remarkable critical success:
- **Parker/Advocate ratings:** Consistent 90-95+ scores from top critics
- **International presence:** Strong distribution in US, Europe, Asia
- **Auction performance:** Regularly appears at fine-wine auctions; older Taurasi bottlings command strong prices
- **Awards & recognition:** Winner of numerous Italian wine competitions and international tasting contests

The winery's willingness to invest in winemaking talent (often recruiting from Tuscany or Piedmont) and modern equipment has resulted in technically-excellent wines that appeal to contemporary palates.

## Philosophical Positioning

Feudi di San Gregorio differs philosophically from traditional houses like Mastroberardino:
- **Technical innovation** over traditional methods (though not radical departure)
- **Market-driven ambition** — wines are intentionally styled to appeal to international critics and collectors
- **Modern infrastructure** — investment in state-of-the-art cellar equipment
- **Aggressive marketing** — strong presence at wine competitions, critic tastings, and export markets

This approach is not "natural wine" or traditionally-conservative, but represents modern fine-wine business model applied to Campanian terroir.

## US Importation & Availability

Distributed through Skurnik, Empson (Italian specialist), and other major importers, reflecting strong international commercial presence.

## Cellar Fit & Taste Alignment

**Moderate to good fit.** Feudi di San Gregorio exemplifies:
- **Terroir-driven:** Campanian volcanic terroir is expressed, though through technical methods rather than minimal intervention
- **Regional specialization:** Greco, Fiano, and Aglianico focus emphasizes Campanian distinctiveness
- **Ageability:** Wines demonstrate excellent aging potential (20-40+ years)
- **Modern excellence:** Technical quality is demonstrable and recognized internationally

However, Feudi's technical/commercial approach differs from Evan's apparent preference for biodynamic, minimal-intervention, grower-scale production. The fit is "good" because of terroir focus and regional specialization, but "not excellent" because the philosophical alignment with natural/biodynamic methods is weaker than producers like Marco Caprai or Arianna Occhipinti.
