# Diel

Diel (Weingut Schloßgut Diel) represents the modern face of Nahe winemaking: a historical estate combining traditional dry-wine production with a distinctive emphasis on white Burgundy varieties (Riesling, Weissburgunder/Pinot Blanc) and mineral-driven, food-friendly expressions of terroir that appeal to contemporary palates while maintaining regional authenticity.

## Historical Context & Estate Tradition

The Diel estate (Schloßgut = castle estate) sits in Bad Kreuznach, the largest town in the Nahe region and a historic wine center. The estate's vineyard holdings trace to the 18th century; the current proprietor, **Armin Diel**, represents a generation of Nahe winemakers who modernized quality standards while respecting regional traditions.

The Nahe region occupies a unique position: geographically between Rheinhessen (to the north) and Rheingau (to the east), it combines aspects of both — Rheinhessen's approachability and Rheingau's mineral precision. Diel's winemaking emphasizes this middle ground: wines that are serious, age-worthy, and mineral-driven, yet friendly and accessible to contemporary drinkers.

## Terroir & Vineyard Holdings

Diel works several notable Nahe vineyard sites:

- **Kreuznacher Krotenpfuhl** — the estate's flagship vineyard; diverse soils (quartz, porphyry); produces elegant, complex Rieslings
- **Kreuznacher Hinkelstein** — clay and slate soils; structured wines with good aging potential
- **Schlossböckelheim vineyards** — southeast-facing slopes with mineral-rich soils; produces some of the Nahe's most prestigious wines

These vineyard sites, while less famous internationally than Middle Mosel or Rheingau sites, possess excellent geology and traditional craft. The Nahe's relative obscurity has allowed Diel to focus on quality rather than prestige marketing.

## Winemaking Philosophy: Dry Wines & Burgundy Focus

Diel's portfolio emphasizes **dry-wine production** with particular focus on Riesling and Weissburgunder (Pinot Blanc):

**Riesling Approach:**
- **Fully fermented to dryness** — fermentation continues until residual sugar is minimal (typically < 5 g/L)
- **Mineral expression** — minimal oak aging (predominantly stainless steel or old neutral casks) preserves fresh fruit and mineral character
- **Structured acidity** — Nahe Rieslings naturally possess good acidity; Diel works to preserve and enhance this structure through cellar management
- **Food-wine focus** — wines are intentionally styled as restaurant/food wines rather than dessert expressions

**Weissburgunder (Pinot Blanc) Specialization:**
Diel has emerged as one of the Nahe's leading Weissburgunder producers, producing dry whites that rival Alsatian Pinot Blanc:
- **Old-vine selections** — some Weissburgunder parcels planted 40-50 years ago; older vines yield richer, more complex expressions
- **Restrained oak aging** — some wines see brief neutral-cask aging, adding textural weight without dominating fruit character
- **Food-wine versatility** — Nahe Weissburgunder from Diel are excellent with light seafood, poultry, and cheese courses

## Production Scale & Quality Focus

Production ranges from 200,000-300,000 bottles annually — larger than boutique estates, yet small enough for personal oversight. Diel maintains selective distribution and refuses to compete on price, instead positioning wines as quality-focused, terroir-driven expressions.

## Regional Position & Reputation

Diel is among the Nahe's most respected producers, regularly cited alongside Dönnhoff, Emrich-Schönleber, and a handful of others as anchors of the region's quality reputation. The winery's consistency and food-wine orientation have earned strong endorsement from sommeliers and serious wine retailers.

**CSW Presence:** Multiple articles dating to 2010 indicate long-standing relationship; described as a "favorite" producer with reliable quality across vintages.

## Cellar Fit & Taste Alignment

**Very good fit.** Diel exemplifies:
- **Terroir-driven:** Nahe vineyard sites are worked with respect for geology and traditional methods
- **Dry-wine discipline:** Focus on fully-fermented expressions that emphasize mineral character

- **Sustainable farming:** While not certified biodynamic, practices reflect environmental stewardship
