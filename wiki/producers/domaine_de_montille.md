## Notes

**Domaine de Montille** is one of Burgundy's most respected estates, with nearly 300 years of documented history dating to the 1730s, and modernized by **Hubert de Montille** (who took over in 1947) and continued by his son **Étienne** (who joined in 1983). The current generation, including winemaker **Brian Sieve**, maintains a philosophy emphasizing **classical structure, organic farming, and minimal new-oak influence** — an approach that yields wines of remarkable aging potential and terroir clarity. **Key expressions** include **Volnay** (the domaine's signature and most prestigious bottling, producing silky, elegant reds with 20–30+ year aging potential), **Pommard** (more structured and masculine, also age-worthy), and **Bourgogne Blanc Le Clos du Château** (described as a monumental value expression and "mon chouchou" — the house favorite). The domaine spans Côte de Beaune appellations (Volnay, Pommard, Puligny-Montrachet, Auxey-Duresses) with approximately 20 hectares of organic-farmed vineyard. Winemaking emphasizes **new oak discipline** (used sparingly, not exceeding 20–30% across the portfolio) and native yeast fermentation to preserve fruit purity. The result is wines of remarkable elegance and structure — CSW describes them as "elegant and impeccably balanced." Chambard and CSW have championed the domaine extensively (11 articles since 2012), noting consistent quality across vintages. William Kelley (Advocate) has praised Montille's commitment to reduced sulfite use and cold cellar storage, contrasting favorably with producers using warmer cellars and oxidative practices. The domaine is **Certified Organic** and practicing biodynamics, reflecting environmental commitment aligned with the estate's classical philosophy. Aging potential is exceptional: Volnay typically requires 8–15 years cellaring minimum, with peak drinking 15–30+ years from vintage. Down to Earth does not currently carry Montille, but Kysela and Polaner distribute in the US market. The estate remains one of Burgundy's finest references for long-aging, elegantly-structured reds built on terroir expression rather than winemaker manipulation.
