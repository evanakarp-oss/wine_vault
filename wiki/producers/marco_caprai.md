# Marco Caprai

Marco Caprai (Arnaldo Caprai winery, bearing both the founder's and son's names) represents the intersection of Umbrian tradition and modern organic/biodynamic winemaking, particularly renowned for Sagrantino di Montefalco — a powerful, age-worthy red variety that Caprai has positioned at the forefront of Italian terroir-driven production. The estate exemplifies how biodynamic farming can elevate a regional grape to world-class significance.

## Montefalco & Sagrantino Context

Montefalco (literally "hawk's mount") is a medieval hilltop town in Umbria, an interior Italian region that historically was overshadowed by adjacent Tuscany's prestige and marketing sophistication. Yet Montefalco possesses distinctive terroir and an indigenous grape variety — **Sagrantino** — that, in the hands of serious producers, yields wines of remarkable structure, complexity, and aging potential.

Sagrantino is not a famous international variety like Nebbiolo or Barolo, but rather a regional speciality known for:
- **Powerful tannins** — Sagrantino naturally has higher tannin concentration than most Italian reds
- **Deep color** — the wines are deeply pigmented, almost opaque in youth
- **Spice and herb notes** — characteristic aromatics include black pepper, dried herbs, wild plums
- **Aging potential** — the high tannin structure allows 20-30+ year evolution in the cellar

Caprai's achievement has been positioning Sagrantino di Montefalco as a serious, collectible wine — a feat that required decades of quality focus and biodynamic farming innovation.

## Arnaldo Caprai's Legacy & Marco's Stewardship

The estate was founded by **Arnaldo Caprai** in 1971 as a small project in the medieval Montefalco village. Arnaldo's vision was to elevate Sagrantino di Montefalco from regional commodity to world-class expression. His son **Marco Caprai** assumed leadership and accelerated this mission through:
- Conversion to **organic farming** (1992)
- Transition to **biodynamic certification** (Demeter, 2000)
- Technical innovations in fermentation and aging
- Elevation of the winery's reputation through critical success

Marco's tenure (1990s-present) has positioned Caprai as arguably the leading Sagrantino di Montefalco producer — a remarkable achievement for a winery founded just 50+ years ago in a region without centuries of prestigious tradition.

## Terroir & Vineyard Holdings

Caprai owns approximately **140 hectares** around Montefalco, working primarily red varieties (Sagrantino, Barbera) and some white (Grechetto):

**Key Vineyard Parcels:**
- **Montefalco core estate** — densely planted parcels surrounding the medieval town center, south-facing slopes with clay-limestone soils
- **Higher-elevation sites** — cooler microclimates that produce more elegant, structured expressions
- **Older-vine Sagrantino** — some parcels planted 40-50+ years ago; these naturally yield more concentrated, complex wines

The geology is limestone-dominant with clay substrate — similar to Tuscan hilltown sites but with distinct microclimate characteristics shaped by elevation and orientation.

## Biodynamic Farming & Demeter Certification

Caprai practices **Demeter-certified biodynamic agriculture**:
- Complete synthetic-chemical elimination
- Moon-cycle planting and harvesting calendars
- Biodynamic compost preparations applied to soils
- Livestock integration (sheep grazing among vineyards for soil management)
- Annual third-party audits verifying compliance

This commitment has transformed Caprai's vineyard ecology: soils show increased earthworm populations, enhanced microbial biodiversity, and improved water-retention capacity compared to conventional neighbors.

## Winemaking: Sagrantino Mastery

Caprai's winemaking emphasizes **Sagrantino expression** through modern, quality-focused methods:

**Fermentation & Extraction:**
- **Long maceration** — extended skin contact (20-30 days) to extract color, tannins, and aromatic compounds from Sagrantino berries
- **Temperature control** — carefully managed fermentation temperatures to extract structure without overwhelming fruit character
- **Native yeast fermentation** — minimal commercial inoculation; reliance on vineyard microbes when possible

**Aging & Evolution:**
- **French oak aging** — typically 12-18 months in French oak casks (some new, some neutral) to add structure and complexity
- **Bottle aging** — wines are released after 2-3 years aging; they continue evolving for 20-30+ years in the cellar

**Production Scale:** Caprai produces roughly 600,000 bottles annually across all wines (Sagrantino, Montefalco Rosso, whites). This is mid-scale (larger than boutique, smaller than industrial), allowing quality control while maintaining economic viability.

## Key Wines & Style

**Sagrantino di Montefalco Passito** — late-harvest Sagrantino (similar to Amarone method, though distinct appellations); dried berries produce sweet, concentrated wine with remarkable complexity. Age-worthy for 30-50+ years.

**Sagrantino di Montefalco (dry, standard bottling)** — the primary Sagrantino expression; powerful structure with spice, herb, plum character; ages beautifully for 20-30 years.

**Montefalco Rosso** — Sagrantino-blend wine (Sagrantino, Barbera, Merlot); slightly more approachable than pure Sagrantino; still age-worthy for 15-25 years.

## Critical Standing & International Reputation

Caprai has achieved significant critical recognition:
- **Parker/Advocate ratings:** Consistent 90+ scores from respected critics
- **Italian wine guides:** Top rankings among Umbrian producers
- **Auction presence:** Older Sagrantino di Montefalco vintages (2000s-2010s) appear regularly at Italian auctions
- **International distribution:** Strong US, UK, and European presence through quality importers

The winery's combination of biodynamic certification, technical excellence, and Sagrantino specialization has earned respect from collectors seeking terroir-driven, age-worthy Italian reds.

## US Importation & Availability

Distributed through Skurnik and Classical Wines (among other importers), reflecting alignment with quality-focused, European wine networks.

- **Grower-scale:** While larger than boutique estates, production remains quality-focused and sustainable
- **Indigenous-variety focus:** Sagrantino specialization emphasizes regional grape character over international varieties
- **Ageability:** Sagrantino di Montefalco demonstrates 25-50+ year aging potential
