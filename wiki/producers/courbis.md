## Notes

**Domaine Courbis** is a respected **small grower** in the Northern Rhône producing traditional, age-worthy expressions of Cornas (Syrah) and elegant, mineral Condrieu (Viognier). The domaine exemplifies the grower-scale, terroir-focused, organic-farming approach that aligns directly with Evan's taste filters. The family owns limited vineyard parcels on the steep, granite-based slopes of Cornas and Condrieu — terroirs known for producing some of the Northern Rhône's finest, most distinctive wines.

**Vineyard & Terroir Characteristics.** Courbis' vineyards are located on the steep slopes of Cornas and the cliffside Condrieu terroir, characterized by:

- **Granite soils** (Cornas): produce wines with mineral precision, peppery spice, and remarkable aging potential
- **Mica and schist soils** (Condrieu cliffs): contribute mineral salinity and herbal lift to Viognier
- **Steep south-facing aspect** across both appellations: concentrates sun exposure and creates stress that produces complex, phenolic-rich fruit
- **High altitude** (200–400 meters) creates cool nights essential for maintaining freshness and acidity

This terroir is demanding — steep slopes require hand harvesting and create limited yields — but the reward is wines of extraordinary terroir expression and aging potential.

**Farming & Viticulture.** Courbis practices **organic viticulture** (certified or transitioning) with traditional, sustainable methods:
- Minimal copper and sulfur inputs (copper only for essential mildew/powdery mildew control)
- Cover cropping and natural vigor management on steep terraces
- Hand harvesting mandatory (impossible to mechanize on steep slopes anyway)
- Conservative yields (25–35 hl/ha for Cornas, 30–45 hl/ha for Condrieu) to concentrate aromatic complexity and phenolic development
- Natural pest and disease management through canopy management and timing

The steep slopes themselves enforce a philosophy of **minimal intervention and maximum terroir focus** — there's simply no room for industrial viticulture on a 45-degree angle.

**Winemaking & Signature Style.**

**Cornas:** Courbis produces a **traditional Cornas** emphasizing the appellation's characteristic **whole-cluster fermentation** and extended aging:
- Native yeast fermentation in concrete vats with extended skin contact (15–20 days)
- Aging in used barrels (typically 2–3 years old, not new oak) for 18–24 months
- Bottling unfined and unfiltered to preserve aromatic complexity and natural tannin structure
- Minimal sulfite additions

The resulting wine is **tight, structured, and mineral-forward** — classic old-school Cornas requiring 10–15 years of cellaring before opening. Tannins are firm and built from whole-cluster stems; acidity is fresh and preserves aging potential.

**Condrieu:** Courbis produces an **elegant, traditionally-vinified Condrieu**:
- Hand-harvested Viognier from old vines
- Aged in a modest proportion of oak (typically 20–30% new, balance neutral/used) — oak is a supporting player, not the dominant voice
- Native yeast fermentation, extended skin contact for white (unusual for Viognier, creates complexity)
- Bottling unfiltered and unfined
- Minimal sulfite additions

The wine shows **floral aromatics (peach blossom, white flowers, honeysuckle), mineral salinity, and white pepper** — classic mineral Condrieu that evolves beautifully over 8–15 years.

**Also Produced:** Courbis produces small amounts of **Saint-Péray** (a sparkling wine from the white Rhône varietals, Marsanne and Roussanne).

**Key Cuvées & Production.** Courbis' primary bottling is the **Cornas "Domaine"** — a blend of the family's best parcels. In strong vintages, specific parcels or older-vine selections may be bottled separately. The **Condrieu** is similarly a careful selection of the family's vineyard holdings. Production is **small** (~300–600 cases per vintage), reflecting modest vineyard holdings and steep-slope harvesting limitations. The wines are primarily sold through Kermit Lynch (US), Banville (UK), and direct to serious collectors.

**Cornas in Context.** Cornas is the Northern Rhône's most serious, age-worthy Syrah appellation — a wine that demands 15–20 years of cellaring but rewards patience with complexity and elegance. Courbis' expression is a reference point for **traditional Cornas**: whole-cluster fermented, mineral-driven, structured, and profoundly food-focused rather than immediately hedonistic. The wine represents the appellation's philosophical core.

**Condrieu in Context.** Condrieu is the world's greatest Viognier appellation, producing wines that balance floral aromatics with mineral precision and genuine aging potential. Courbis' expression is a reference for **mineral, elegant Condrieu** — the antithesis of over-oaked, fruit-forward commercial Viognier.

**Aging Potential & Drinking Windows.**

**Cornas:**
- **Minimum cellaring**: 10–12 years from vintage (shorter cellaring results in harsh, drying tannins)
- **Peak drinking window**: 15–25 years from vintage
- **Extended aging potential**: 30+ years in favorable vintages; no upper limit for well-stored bottles

**Condrieu:**
- **Minimum cellaring**: 3–5 years from vintage (can be drunk earlier, but benefits from aging)
- **Peak drinking window**: 8–15 years from vintage
- **Extended aging potential**: 20+ years in favorable vintages

Mature vintages (2015, 2010, 2005) from Courbis are showing beautifully, with significant aging potential remaining.

**Critical Reception & Community Standing.** Courbis is recognized among Northern Rhône specialists and serious collectors as a **principled, quality-focused grower** producing traditional, age-worthy wines. The domaine is featured in Chambers Street recommendations as a cellar-pick for Northern Rhône reds and whites. Kermit Lynch's representation confirms the estate's status as a top import priority. Pricing reflects quality but represents genuine value for wines of this terroir-expression and aging potential: Cornas $60–90 (typical), Condrieu $75–110.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Courbis embodies multiple core taste filters:
1. **Organic farming** — certified or transitioning, with a philosophy of minimal intervention and terroir focus
2. **Grower-scale production** — small family domaine with modest vineyard holdings and hand-harvested, steep-slope constraints
3. **Terroir expression** — mineral-driven, whole-cluster-fermented Cornas and elegant, mineral Condrieu
4. **Age-worthiness** — wines built for 15–20+ year cellaring with proven track record

The domaine's commitment to traditional winemaking, organic farming, and steep-slope viticulture aligns perfectly with Evan's stated preferences. Courbis Cornas is an **essential Northern Rhône reference** for any serious collector — representing the appellation's traditional, whole-cluster soul. Courbis should be a regular acquisition for Evan's cellar as both a reference for Cornas quality and as a reliable, elegant, food-friendly Condrieu pairing.
