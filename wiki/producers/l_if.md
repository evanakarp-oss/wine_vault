# L'If

Saint-Émilion estate owned by **Jacques Thienpont** — the same hand behind
Pomerol's [[le_pin|Le Pin]]. L'If is consciously positioned as the St-Émilion
counterpart to Le Pin: small production, similar packaging and pricing
trajectory, sold largely on the Le Pin halo. It launched as a comparatively
affordable "table wine" tier but release prices have climbed steeply over the
last decade (collectors mark the 2015 as the inflection point). Jacques has
since added a lower tier, **L'Hêtre**, to occupy the entry slot L'If vacated.

## Notes

### The Thienpont family map

A recurring point of confusion: L'If and VCC are **both Thienpont, but run by
different branches of the family — cousins' estates, not one operation.**

| Estate | Appellation | Run by |
|---|---|---|
| **L'If** (+ [[le_pin|Le Pin]], + L'Hêtre) | Saint-Émilion / Pomerol | **Jacques Thienpont** |
| **Vieux Château Certan (VCC)** | Pomerol | **Alexandre Thienpont**, now with son **Guillaume** |
| Guillot-Clauzel | Pomerol | **Guillaume Thienpont** (winemaking) |

So L'If is **not** a VCC project or second wine — it sits in Jacques's stable
(with Le Pin), while VCC is the Alexandre/Guillaume branch. They cluster
together in the collector market and in critics' value framing, which is why
they're easily conflated, but on the ground it's two teams, two appellations.

Person↔estate pairings corroborated in the WK Berserkers corpus
(`raw/berserkers/William_Kelley/`): *"At Le Pin, Jacques Thienpont…"* (Bordeaux
EP 2024), *"At VCC, Alexandre Thienpont told me…"* (Bordeaux 2023), and WK
himself on Guillot-Clauzel: *"Guillaume Thienpont is now making the wines…"*
(Suggestions for Pomerol). The cousin tree itself is well-established Bordeaux
fact; the corpus confirms each pairing but never spells out the family tree in
one quote.
