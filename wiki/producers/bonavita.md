## Notes

**Bonavita** (Giovanni Scarfone) is a tiny, historically-rooted estate in Faro, Sicily — a region once celebrated for international wine trade before the catastrophic 1908 earthquake — now reviving ancient traditions through organic farming of indigenous Sicilian grapes and producing fresh, mineral-driven reds and rosés.

**Historic Context & Heritage.** The Faro region sits 3 miles from the Calabrian coast near Messina, tracing its name "Casale del Faro" to 8th-century Greek colonization (Pharis colony). In the 18th century, Faro wines achieved international renown, exported to France and across Europe — marking it as one of Sicily's premier regions. The catastrophic 1908 earthquake and tsunami devastated Messina and its agricultural infrastructure, killing ~70,000 residents and leaving the region's vineyards decimated. Recovery was slow, and Faro never fully rebounded. Today, only 20 hectares of vineyard remain in the entire Faro DOC — making producers like Bonavita crucial to regional continuation.

**Giovanni Scarfone & Family Estate.** Giovanni Scarfone is the current steward of his family's century-old estate, Bonavita, comprising just over 2 hectares of vineyard. The estate sits at 250 meters elevation, offering "unparalleled views" of the Italian mainland — a geographic isolation that paradoxically contrasts with Messina's suburban sprawl occurring on adjacent hillsides.

**Terroir & Environment.** The vineyard's distinctive character derives from:
- **Soil:** Medium-weight clay and limestone mix; Giovanni enriches it by tilling in legumes and manure (building soil health through organic matter)
- **Climate:** Strong Sirocca winds from Africa howl across the property, protected by surrounding trees — contributing to the site's distinctive microclimate and fruit quality
- **Altitude:** 250 meters provides cooling and extended ripening

**Grape Varieties.** Bonavita works with three regional varieties:
- **Nerello Mascalese** — also found at Mount Etna; thin-skinned, elegant varietal
- **Nerello Cappucio** — complementary to Mascalese, regional Sicilian heritage
- **Nocera** — indigenous Faro varietal; valued for acidity and freshness; the "mouth" of the blend

**Winemaking Philosophy.** Giovanni's approach emphasizes freshness and balance over extraction:
- **Hand-harvest** — grapes selected by Giovanni and local workers
- **Miniature cellar** — winemaking occurs in a garage-based cellar at his parents' house
- **Native yeast fermentation** — "pied de cuve" separated to initiate fermentation
- **Co-fermentation** — all three varieties fermented together for 15 days on skins in stainless steel
- **Hybrid aging** — ~60% used oak barrels + remainder stainless steel (restrained oak use)
- **Result:** Wines "full of life" with "underlying core of fresh earth and bright fruit" — balancing power with freshness, distinctly less toasty than typical Faro bottlings

**Key Wines.**
- **Faro (red)** — co-fermented blend of Nerello Mascalese, Nerello Cappucio, Nocera; full-bodied yet balanced; earth-driven, bright fruit
- **Rosato** — Nerello Mascalese and Nocera blend; walks line between "light red" and "dark rosé" (reminiscent of Grignolino or Poulsard); Giovanni philosophizes that this may resemble ancient Greek-era Faro wines (when maceration times were shorter)

**Regional & Historical Significance.** Bonavita exemplifies Faro's small-scale revival: a family estate preserving centuries of tradition despite regional devastation, working tiny holdings (2 hectares) in a DOC reduced to 20 total hectares, producing wines that echo ancient Faro's reputation while embracing contemporary natural-wine sensibilities. The estate represents cultural continuity and terroir persistence in a region most modern wine world has forgotten.
