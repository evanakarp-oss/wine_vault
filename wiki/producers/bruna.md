## Notes

**Cantina Bruna** is a small family winery focused on one of Liguria's signature white varieties: Pigato. Located in Imperia on the Riviera Ligure di Ponente, Bruna exemplifies the maritime, mineral-driven character of Liguria's coastal whites — a region that produces some of Italy's most distinctive yet underrated seafood-wine pairings.

**Producer History & Philosophy.** Francesca Bruna represents the current generation, inheriting a vision set in motion by her father Riccardo, who decided in the 1970s to focus exclusively on elevating Pigato to its potential for "absolute excellence." This focused, single-varietal commitment (vs. the generic blending common to many Ligurian producers) has positioned Bruna as a reference point for quality Pigato. The winery operates at a modest scale, emphasizing organic farming and traditional winemaking over commercial volume.

**Terroir & Vineyard Character.** The Riviera Ligure di Ponente is a steep, maritime region where Pigato vines grow in schist-rich soils, exposed to sea breezes. This coastal exposure produces wines with distinctive salinity, herb-driven aromatics, and saline minerality — a profile that separates quality Pigato from generic versions. Bruna's vineyard holdings benefit from this terroir's natural advantages: low yields (due to steep terrain), mineral concentration, and the acidity-preserving effect of cool sea influence.

**House Style & Key Wines.** Bruna's Pigato expresses the varietal's classic profile: pale golden color, herbal and almond aromatics, medium body with crisp acidity, and persistent mineral backbone. The wines are positioned as age-worthy whites (Chambers Street rates them 3/5 for cellaring potential) — a rare claim for Pigato, which is often treated as a young, aperitivo-style wine. This ability to age reflects both low-manipulation winemaking and the inherent mineral structure of the site.

**US Market Position.** Imported by Bowler and Roscioli, Bruna has limited but loyal distribution in the US, particularly among natural-wine focused retailers and serious wine professionals familiar with Liguria's under-the-radar potential. CSW has championed the producer since 2013 (Cellar Pick designation as of 2025), positioning it as both a food-wine essential and a long-term investment for Ligurian white fans.
