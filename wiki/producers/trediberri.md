## Notes

Trediberri is an emerging star in Barolo, representing a new generation of quality-focused small producers in the La Morra zone. Founded by siblings (the "Tre di Berri" — three from the hills — giving it the name), the winery farm roughly 8 hectares organically and biodynamically across three premier vineyard sites in La Morra: Berri, Rocche dell'Annuziata, and Toriglione. The house gained attention in the US in 2023–2024 (CSW championed it twice in that window), positioning it as a rising star rather than an established classic. The wine style is fruit-forward, elegant, and built for early-drinking satisfaction alongside aging potential.

**Vineyard Holdings & Philosophy.** The three vineyard blocks are small (1.5–3 hectares each) but very well-regarded: Berri is a south-facing slope with excellent sun exposure; Rocche dell'Annuziata is a higher-elevation, cooler site known for mineral complexity; Toriglione is intermediate in character. All are farmed organically, with biodynamic practices (cover crops, minimal inputs, natural fermentation). The terroir of La Morra is known for producing softer, more perfumed Barolos relative to the harsher Serralunga or Monforte zones — this is by design and character, not inferiority. Trediberri's fruit reflects that: high-toned, aromatic, and elegant.

**Winemaking Style.** Trediberri employs whole-cluster fermentation (60–70% depending on vintage) with indigenous yeasts and moderate macerations (12–14 days). Oak élevage is 18 months in used and older Slavonian barrels, with minimal new oak. The result is wines that balance Nebbiolo's structure with La Morra's perfume: silky tannins, moderate alcohol (13–13.3%), bright acidity, and aromatic purity. The house does not filter or fine, preserving natural texture and aromatics.

**Key Cuvées.**
- **Barolo del Comune di La Morra:** The house's main bottling, a blend of the three vineyard sites. This captures the "house style" — elegant Barolo with red cherry, dried rose, and mineral notes; approachable at 6–8 years, ages beautifully to 15–20 years.

**Reputation & Allocation.** Trediberri is very new to the US market (entering via Polaner, then CSW, then expanding to fine-wine retailers). The Berserkers thread shows recent momentum (3 mentions in 2023–2026, all new entrant signals), suggesting early-adopter interest. CSW coverage (3 articles, 2 dedicated) positions Trediberri as a "discovery" producer — not yet household name, but recognized by insiders as serious.

**Aging Potential.** Trediberri's fruit-forward style suggests approachable drinking at 5–7 years, but the wines have structured tannins to age 15–20 years in top vintages. The 2018 and 2019 vintages are drinking beautifully now (2026); the 2020 and 2021 will peak 2027–2032. The house is young (commercially), so long-term aging data is limited, but early indicators are positive.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Trediberri hits multiple checkpoints: organic farming, biodynamic practices, small-producer scale, terroir-transparent winemaking, and wines built for the cellar without demanding 25-year patience. La Morra's softer, perfumed style is somewhat orthogonal to Evan's stated preference for "WK-style undercovered/value with great farming," but Trediberri's quality-to-allocation ratio and emerging reputation position it as a smart "rising star" buy. If available at allocation, Trediberri is worth tracking as a long-term cellaring prospect.
