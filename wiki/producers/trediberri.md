## Notes

**Trediberri** is an emerging star in Barolo's La Morra zone, representing a new generation of quality-focused small producers and the "three from the hills" (tre di berri). Founded by siblings, the winery farms approximately 8 hectares organically and biodynamically across three premier vineyard sites: **Berri** (south-facing slope, excellent sun exposure), **Rocche dell'Annuziata** (higher elevation, cooler, mineral-complex), and **Toriglione** (intermediate character). The house gained significant attention in the US market in 2023–2024, entering via Polaner and quickly being championed by Chambers Street Wines (3 dedicated articles, highlighting it as an emerging favorite), positioning it as an insider discovery rather than an established classic. **Key expression** is the **Barolo del Comune di La Morra** — a blend from the three vineyard sites capturing the "house style" of elegant Barolo with red cherry, dried rose, and mineral notes. 

**Winemaking Style.** Trediberri employs **whole-cluster fermentation** (60–70% depending on vintage) with indigenous yeasts and moderate macerations (12–14 days), followed by 18 months in used and older Slavonian barrels (minimal new oak), and no filtration or fining. The result balances Nebbiolo's structure with La Morra's distinctive perfume: silky tannins, moderate alcohol (13–13.3%), bright acidity, and aromatic purity. The La Morra terroir is known for producing softer, more perfumed Barolos relative to the harsher, more structured Serralunga or Monforte zones — Trediberri's fruit exemplifies this character: high-toned, aromatic, and elegant without heaviness. The wines are approachable at 6–8 years yet structured to age 15–20 years, with the 2018 and 2019 vintages currently drinking beautifully and the 2020–2021 expected to peak 2027–2032. Berserkers mention (3 citations in 2023–2026, all recent) indicates early-adopter momentum. The house represents organic and biodynamic farming discipline, small-producer scale (~8 hectares), and terroir-transparent winemaking — all aligned with Evan's taste filters. Trediberri's quality-to-allocation ratio and rising reputation position it as a smart "discovery" buy and strong cellaring prospect within emerging Piedmont producers.
