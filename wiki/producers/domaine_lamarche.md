# Domaine François Lamarche

Vosne-Romanée domaine, ~11 ha, run by **Nicole Lamarche** (with cousin
Nathalie until 2014, solo since) from 2007 onward. The pre-Nicole era was
inconsistent; the post-2007 trajectory is a textbook "domaine reborn"
case — the wines from c. 2010 onward are dramatically tighter, cleaner
and more terroir-revealing.

## La Grande Rue — *the* holding

Lamarche is the **monopole owner of La Grande Rue Grand Cru** — 1.65 ha
slotted between **La Tâche** (south) and **La Romanée-Conti** (north),
mid-slope. The Atlas spread photographed for the study view actually
calls out this site's promotion:

> *"It was only in 1992 that La Grande Rue (4 acres / 1.65 ha) was
> officially accorded Grand Cru status. Land swaps with Domaine de la
> Romanée-Conti involving the Premier Cru Gaudichots mean that La Tâche
> currently has 15 acres (6ha)."*

So La Grande Rue is **the only Grand Cru in the world that touches both
Romanée-Conti and La Tâche on the slope** — and it was a 1er Cru until
1992. That promotion is a function of both site and the Lamarche family's
historical lobbying. Under Nicole the wine is finally **drinking the
part** — perfumed, deep, and ageworthy.

## Other holdings

- **Grand Cru:** Grands Échézeaux, Échézeaux, Clos de Vougeot
- **Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru:** Les Suchots, Les Malconsorts, La Croix
  Rameau (a tiny parcel)
- **Vosne-Romanée Villages**

## Why this domaine matters

Two reasons for the study view:

1. **La Grande Rue is the most Vosne-of-Vosne site you can actually
   buy** (Romanée-Conti and La Tâche are not for sale at release; La
   Grande Rue is, just barely). It is *literally* adjacent to both on
   both sides.
2. **Les Malconsorts** — Lamarche has a serious parcel of this 1er Cru,
   which is the climat the Atlas singles out: *"The Premier Cru
   Malconsorts just south of La Tâche deserves special mention."*
   Malconsorts sits at the southern Vosne border, the last 1er Cru
   before the slope rolls into NSG-Aux Boudots.
