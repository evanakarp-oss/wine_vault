# Domaine de l'Arlot

Prémeaux-Prissey domaine owned by **AXA Millésimes** (the insurance-group
fine-wine portfolio that also owns Pichon-Longueville Baron, Suduiraut,
Pibran, Disznókő, Quinta do Noval). ~15 ha. Biodynamic. Géraldine Godot has
been technical director / winemaker since 2014, succeeding Jacques Devauges
and (before that) Olivier Leriche and Jean-Pierre de Smet.

## Holdings — the unusual Prémeaux + Vosne footprint

The domaine's interest for this study view is precisely that it spans **both
extremes** of the Côte de Nuits axis Vosne is centered on:

- **Prémeaux monopoles (south of NSG town):** Clos de l'Arlot (1er Cru, both
  red *and* a serious white from Chardonnay), Clos des Forêts Saint-Georges
  (1er Cru, the structured one), plus Clos du Chapeau village
- **Vosne shoulder:** Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru Les Suchots (the long Suchots
  strip that adjoins Romanée-Saint-Vivant)
- **Grand Cru:** Romanée-Saint-Vivant (small parcel)

## Style

Heavy use of **whole-cluster fermentation** — historically near-100%, more
calibrated in recent vintages under Godot. Wines are perfumed, lifted, and
sometimes lighter than the appellation norm; an "elegant Prémeaux" rather
than a brawny one. The whites from Clos de l'Arlot are one of the very few
serious white Nuits-Saint-Georges bottlings (alongside Gouges).

## Why this domaine matters

L'Arlot is one of the few producers from which you can taste the **full
southern-to-northern arc** in one cellar: a structured Prémeaux monopole
(Clos des Forêts), an aromatic Prémeaux monopole (Clos de l'Arlot), a
Vosne-shoulder 1er Cru (Suchots), and a Romanée-Saint-Vivant — all under
one stylistic signature. That's a near-perfect lineup for the geography
exercise on the Atlas page.
