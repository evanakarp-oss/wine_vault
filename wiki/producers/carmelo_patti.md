# Carmelo Patti

Winemaker: **Carmelo Patti**. Perdriel, Luján de Cuyo (Mendoza). Category: **Low Intervention**.

## Approach

One of Mendoza's most legendary cult producers — working alone with a single employee out of a garage-scale winery in Drummond since 1986. Organic grapes from Perdriel, native yeasts only, zero chemical additives, no consultants, no manipulation. Defines his style as 'a naked wine, without anything that can cover it up.' Holds all wines a minimum of 4–5 years before release, with at least 3 years in bottle. Fermentation in concrete, aged in older oak. ~20,000 litres/year total production. Devotee following in Argentina and internationally. Cult status earned entirely through word of mouth.

## Notes

**Carmelo Patti** stands as one of Argentina's most legendary and uncompromising low-intervention producers — a cult figure who operates entirely outside mainstream distribution channels, relying entirely on word-of-mouth reputation and direct relationships with collectors who understand his philosophy.

**Winemaking Philosophy & Scale.** Working alone (plus one employee) out of a garage-scale winery in Drummond since 1986, Patti has built a production model that prioritizes absolute integrity over commerce. He sources organic grapes from Perdriel (Luján de Cuyo), ferments exclusively with native yeasts, adds zero chemical additives, and refuses consultants or any external manipulation. Patti's self-described style as "a naked wine, without anything that can cover it up" reflects a purity-first approach that attracts serious collectors but limits conventional distribution. Annual production of ~20,000 liters reflects careful fruit selection and extremely low yields.

**Aging Philosophy & Release Timing.** A defining characteristic of Patti's practice is extended pre-release aging. All wines are held a minimum of 4–5 years before release, with at least 3 years spent in bottle — a practice virtually unheard in commercial Mendoza. Fermentation occurs in concrete, with aging in older (not new) oak. This aging-forward approach means Patti's releases represent mature, settled wines by the time they reach collectors, eliminating the need for further cellaring (though they certainly reward it).

**Key Wines & Varietal Focus.** The core portfolio centers on Mendoza's signature varietals: **Malbec** (the flagship expression), **Cabernet Sauvignon**, and **Cabernet Franc**, with occasional **Gran Assemblage** blends. The Malbec is particularly renowned for its combination of Mendoza's natural fruit richness with Patti's minimalist winemaking — the result is depth without overripeness, structure without harshness.

**Cult Status & Distribution.** Patti's reputation is entirely earned through word-of-mouth within serious collector circles in Argentina and internationally. His wines appear occasionally at high-level auctions and through specialized importers (Bowler), but consistent allocation is nearly impossible to secure. The cult status is reinforced by the producer's absolute indifference to commercial pressures — he will not alter his practices to increase yield or adjust release timing to meet market demand.

**Regional Significance.** In the context of Evan's Argentina taste filter (biodynamic/terroir-driven/artisan-scale), Patti represents the most extreme manifestation: not merely low-intervention, but anti-commercial. His influence on contemporary Mendoza winemaking is substantial, validating the idea that the world's best Malbecs emerge from minimal manipulation, not from consultant-driven adjustments.
