# Azienda Agricolavinica

Contemporary Molise producer founded in 2008 by Rodolfo Gianserra (known as "Dr. Tintilia") and Giuseppe Tudino. Located in Ripalimosani, a small town in the rugged Molise region near the Adriatic Sea, Agricolavinica champions Tintilia del Molise, the indigenous red grape variety that represents Molise's most distinctive varietal contribution to Italian wine.

## Vision & Mission

Rodolfo Gianserra returned to his native Molise with a commitment to making Tintilia known and loved internationally. In 2008, he and Tudino purchased 220 hectares of green hills destined for vineyards, olive groves, gardens, and arable land. The estate's raison d'être is to establish Tintilia as a serious fine-wine expression, positioned alongside Italy's better-known regional grapes.

## Vineyard & Farming

Approximately 22 hectares of organically farmed vineyards (out of 220-hectare total property) cultivated at altitude ranging from 500 to 750 meters. The high altitude imparts characteristic crispness and acidity to the wines while maintaining full-bodied structure. All farming conducted organically, reflecting contemporary Italian commitment to sustainable viticulture.

## Grape Varieties & Production

While Tintilia del Molise is the signature focus, the estate produces a diverse range reflecting Molise's emerging diversity:
- **Tintilia del Molise** — indigenous red, the estate's flagship
- **Sangiovese** — introduced after root-stock shipment included cuttings
- **Merlot** — complementary red
- **Riesling** — white variety
- **Sauvignon Blanc** — white variety
- **Moscato** — aromatic white
- **Pinot Nero** — lighter red
- **Aglianico** — southern Italian dark grape
- **Trebbiano** — Molise white

## Wine Character

Tintilia is characterized as fresh yet full-bodied, acidic without sacrificing flavor. The high-altitude terroir imparts distinctive mineral and crisp characteristics while maintaining the grape's natural structure. Wines reflect both the emerging-region context and Molise's positioning as a more rustic, less internationally known territory with significant untapped potential.

## Regional Context

Molise is one of Italy's smallest and least internationally recognized wine regions. It has historically lacked the infrastructure and marketing presence of more famous regions (Piedmont, Tuscany, Burgundy). Tintilia is Molise's only indigenous varietal, and Azienda Agricolavinica's mission is to establish it as a distinctive expression worthy of serious wine collectors' attention.

## Notes

Azienda Agricolavinica represents the emerging-region phenomenon: a producer using contemporary organic farming and natural-wine philosophy to establish a previously overlooked region and its indigenous varietal on the international stage. Rodolfo Gianserra's dedication to Tintilia mirrors similar efforts by producers in Abruzzo, Umbria, and other historically less-known Italian regions to claim parity with established classics. The high-altitude vineyard positioning and organic farming practices place Agricolavinica in the contemporary natural-wine movement while honoring Molise's rustic, terroir-driven tradition.
