# Rebholz

Rebholz (Weingut Rebholz) represents the union of Pfalz regional winemaking with biodynamic farming and natural-wine principles. Proprietor **Hansjörg Rebholz** is among the oldest advocates for biodynamic certification in Germany, having pursued organic methods since the 1980s and earning Demeter certification by the 1990s — a radical commitment in an era when most Pfalz producers focused on high-volume, value-oriented production.

## The Pfalz Context & Rebholz's Countercultural Stance

The Pfalz (Palatinate) is Germany's second-largest wine region by volume and has historically emphasized value, quantity, and approachability — characteristics that, while commercially successful, created a reputation for mediocrity. High-quality Pfalz winemakers existed but were outliers; the dominant narrative positioned Pfalz as "affordable everyday wine" rather than serious, age-worthy expressions.

Rebholz's commitment to biodynamic farming and dry-wine production was genuinely countercultural in the 1980s-1990s Pfalz. Unlike Mosel or Rheingau, where quality-focused winemaking had deeper historical roots, Pfalz producers pursuing biodynamics were viewed as idealistic eccentrics. Rebholz persisted despite commercial headwinds, gradually building a reputation among serious German wine collectors.

## Biodynamic Farming & Demeter Certification

Rebholz practices **certified-Demeter biodynamic agriculture**, one of the most rigorous organic/biodynamic standards. This involves:
- Complete elimination of synthetic pesticides, herbicides, and fungicides
- Moon-cycle planting and harvesting calendars
- Biodynamic compost preparations (fermented horn, silica, herbal mixtures) applied to vineyard soils
- Livestock integration (the estate maintains cattle and sheep to support soil regeneration)
- Annual third-party audits to verify compliance

This commitment has manifested in improved vineyard health: soils show enhanced microbial life, earthworm populations, and organic matter content compared to conventional neighbors.

## Terroir & Vineyard Holdings

Rebholz works vineyards in **Siebeldingen**, a village in the southern Pfalz. The soils are primarily sandy-limestone and clay — geology that yields lighter, more delicate wines compared to the Pfalz's heartland limestone-heavy sites. This terroir suits Rebholz's aesthetic of elegant, food-friendly dry wines.

Key vineyard sites include parcels with 30-50 year-old vine plantings, which naturally produce more concentrated expressions than younger plantations.

## Winemaking: Dry Riesling & Natural Wine Methods

Rebholz's winemaking reflects natural-wine principles applied to Pfalz Riesling:

**Fermentation & Cellar:**
- **Wild yeast fermentation** — no commercial inoculants; fermentation proceeds from ambient microbes
- **Extended lees aging** — wines spend 6-12 months on gross lees, developing autolytic complexity
- **Minimal sulfites** — only small amounts added at bottling, if at all; the estate relies on wine's natural resistance to oxidation/microbial spoilage
- **Unfined & unfiltered** — wines remain cloudy initially; clarity develops naturally over time
- **Bottle-aging focus** — wines are bottled relatively early and expected to continue evolving in bottle over years/decades

**Style:** Rebholz's Rieslings emphasize mineral purity, restraint, and food compatibility. They are typically bone-dry to off-dry, with citrus and green-fruit aromatics balanced by mineral smokiness. Early on, they can show reductive character (struck flint, wet stone); after decanting or brief aeration, this resolves to reveal floral and fruit complexity.

## Production Scale & Sustainability

Production is modest — roughly 50,000-80,000 bottles annually — allowing personal oversight. The small scale, combined with biodynamic farming, makes the estate genuinely sustainable in economic and environmental terms.

## Critical Reception & Market Position

Rebholz has achieved significant reputation among serious German wine collectors:
- Strong presence in European fine-wine auctions (particularly German specialist auctions)
- Regular coverage by respected German wine writers and critics
- Natural wine community recognition as a founding-generation biodynamic practitioner
- Consistent CSW import relationships (though limited retail presence historically)

The winery has not pursued aggressive marketing; instead, reputation has grown gradually through word-of-mouth and critic recommendation.

## US Importation & Availability

Distributed through Skurnik and Terry Theise networks, reflecting alignment with other serious German biodynamic producers.

## Cellar Fit & Taste Alignment

**Excellent fit.** Rebholz exemplifies all key taste criteria. Hansjörg Rebholz earned Winemaker of the Year (2002) and Gault-Millau five-grape highest rating. The estate has grown to 25 hectares with vineyard holdings in sweet spots: Kastanienbusch and Mandelberg (Birkweiler), Im Sonnenschein and Ganz Horn (Siebeldingen), Schäwer (Burrweiler). Estate specializes in powerfully dry Riesling—wines are neither enriched nor de-acidified, reflecting belief that both practices spoil natural character. Current US distribution through Skurnik and Theise (alongside other serious German biodynamic producers). Production ~50,000-80,000 bottles annually; modest scale enables personal oversight and sustainability. Key US wines include Pfalz Dry Riesling and site-specific bottlings from old-vine parcels (30-50 year plantings) emphasizing mineral purity, restraint, food compatibility. Biodynamic certification (via Demeter) and natural-wine methods position Rebholz as one of Germany's most philosophically-aligned producers with collectors seeking regenerative farming, terroir expression, and age-worthy dry-wine discipline.
