# Ciro Picariello

Ciro Picariello has become one of the reference points for Fiano di Avellino, the great white grape of inland Campania. His story began when his wife's family land was planted to vines, the first vines by Ciro himself in 1990; he and his wife founded the winery in 1997 but did not release a wine under their own label until 2004. The vineyards lie in the province of Avellino, split between the villages of Montefredane (circa 1,600 feet) and Summonte (circa 2,100 feet), on high-altitude volcanic soils. Picariello's approach is defined by an almost stubborn refusal to use oak—fining with indigenous yeasts and minimal intervention in stainless steel. The Taurasi (produced only in strong vintages) undergoes 20-day fermentation at 28–30°C, followed by 12 months in stainless steel and 12 months in 15hl French oak barrels only—a restraint unusual for the DOCG.

## Notes

**Fiano mastery.** Picariello has elevated Fiano di Avellino to reference-point status through meticulous vineyard work and minimal-intervention winemaking. The high-altitude volcanic vineyards produce whites with tension, minerality, and aging potential.

**No-oak philosophy.** The stubborn refusal to use oak for Fiano distinguishes Picariello from many Avellino producers. This restraint lets the grape and terroir speak without oak's masking influence—stainless steel fermentation with indigenous yeasts creates clean, mineral-driven whites.

**Elevation strategy.** The split between Montefredane (1,600 ft) and Summonte (2,100 ft) allows Picariello to produce wines at different ripeness levels and aromatic profiles. Higher elevation vineyards deliver fresher acids and herbal notes; lower elevations more ripeness and body.

**Selective Taurasi.** Unlike some producers who bottle Taurasi annually, Picariello only releases it in strong vintages—a quality-first decision. The long fermentation (20 days at high temperature) and measured oak aging (limited to 12 months) show respect for the grape while avoiding over-extraction or over-wooding.
