# Château Coutelin-Merville

Cru Bourgeois of **Saint-Estèphe**, owned by the **Estager family** (from the
Corrèze) for over a century — on the estate since 1902, with brothers Bernard
and François running it since the 1987 succession. A ~30 ha clay-limestone
vineyard in the north of the appellation, planted to a Merlot-led blend with an
unusually high Cabernet Franc share. The wines are supple, fruit-forward, and
priced as value — the approachable face of Saint-Estèphe rather than its
brooding tannic archetype. Barely distributed stateside; profile built from
French sources.

## Notes

**Terroir & encépagement**: ~30 ha, argilo-calcaire, ~8,300 pieds/ha, north of
Saint-Estèphe. Blend ~50% Merlot, 25% Cabernet Sauvignon, ~20–22% Cabernet Franc,
~5% Petit Verdot. The high Merlot + Cab Franc is the estate's signature and
explains the soft, accessible style.

**Vinification**: traditionnelle; élevage 12 months in barrique, barrel park
renewed 1/5 per year (~20% new oak). No organic/biodynamic/HVE claim in any
French source — farming treated as conventional/unstated.

**2015 (the bottle on hand)**: deep grenat-pourpre; nose of cassis, blackberry,
black cherry, violet, sweet spice, vanilla, blond tobacco; supple round tannins,
finish on sweet spice and mocha. A drink-now mid-weight — **best through
~2027–2030**, not a long-haul keeper.

**Taste-filter placement**: a value/undercovered Cru Bourgeois with a real
family story and a distinctive blend, but conventional farming and a soft
drink-now profile put it at the edge of Evan's Bordeaux filter — a pleasant
value drinker, not a cellar-defining buy. Full French sourcing in the linked
research view.
