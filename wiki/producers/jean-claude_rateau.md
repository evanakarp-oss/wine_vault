# Jean-Claude Rateau

## Notes

**Jean-Claude Rateau** is a pioneering Beaune (Burgundy) biodynamic producer who was the "4th vigneron in France to convert to biodynamics" in 1979 with just two hectares—now managing significantly expanded holdings, he represents the emerging wave of traditional Burgundy quality based on terroir focus, biodynamic farming, and "beautiful, old-fashioned" winemaking rather than modern extraction or new-oak emphasis. CSW describes Rateau as a "pioneer" in sustainable Burgundy production and emphasizes his commitment to "affordable organic Burgundies" and "beautiful, old-fashioned Burgundies"—a philosophy combining respect for Burgundy's classical style with genuine accessibility. The domaine's vineyard parcels rest on the limestone-clay soils of Beaune and the surrounding Côte de Beaune regions, including access to Beaune 1er Cru sites (Les Bressandes), Hautes-Côtes de Beaune, and neighboring Gevrey-Chambertin expressions that showcase diverse micro-terroirs. Biodynamic farming (certified) emphasizes natural pest management, lunar-cycle planting and harvesting, and soil health development through compost preparations and cover crops—yields are modest to concentrate aromatic complexity and phenolic development. Winemaking follows traditional Burgundy methods: native yeast fermentation, extended maceration for tannin development, and aging in a mix of barrels (primarily older, neutral wood rather than new oak) for 18+ months, allowing the wine's mineral and red-fruit character to emerge clearly. Signature expressions include **Beaune 1er Cru "Les Bressandes"** (CSW notes beautiful vintages 2017, 2020, 2023; estimated $40–70 for current releases), **Beaune Village** (entry-level expression; estimated $25–40), **Gevrey-Chambertin** (2022 mentioned in recent shipment; estimated $35–60), and **Hautes-Côtes de Beaune** (accessible, age-worthy expression; estimated $20–35). CSW cellar note indicates "best wines priced at $30 to $75" and emphasizes these wines "age beautifully for 10 to 30+ years," reflecting the estate's commitment to structure and terroir rather than immediate gratification. CSW championed Rateau with 13 articles and 6 dedicated stars, with consistent messaging: "biodynamic pioneer," "beautiful and affordable," "old-fashioned," and a "two pioneers" article (2024) equating his pioneering status with André Beaufort in Champagne. Polaner's representation confirms import priority among specialists in affordable, organic Burgundy. The estate's biodynamic-pioneer status (4th in France, 1979), commitment to affordability combined with age-worthy structure, and terroir-focused winemaking directly align with Evan's documented Burgundy taste filter (biodynamic-certified, terroir-driven, grower-scale, age-worthy).
