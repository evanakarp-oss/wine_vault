# Domaine Grivot

15 ha Vosne-based domaine. Étienne Grivot took over from his father Jean
in 1987 and modernized the wines (closer destem, more careful oak,
patient élevage); his daughter **Mathilde Grivot** joined and progressively
took the lead from ~2018. Whole-cluster experimentation has increased
under Mathilde. Organic farming.

## Holdings

One of the deepest Vosne lineups in private hands — *every step of the
hierarchy*, in scale:

- **Grand Cru** Richebourg (small parcel, the marquee), Clos de Vougeot,
  Échézeaux
- **Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru** Les Beaux Monts, Les Suchots, Aux Brûlées,
  Aux Reignots, Les Rouges (smaller)
- **Nuits-Saint-Georges 1er Cru** Aux Boudots, Les Pruliers, Les
  Roncières — **note the Boudots holding: this is the *Vosne-shoulder*
  NSG marquee.**
- **Vosne-Romanée Villages** Les Hautes Maizières (a respected lieu-dit
  on the Flagey hinge)
- Bourgogne Pinot Noir

## Why this domaine matters

For the study view, Grivot's **NSG Aux Boudots** is the single most
compelling cuvée: a top Vosne house's read on the climat that the Atlas
spread implicitly highlights as "NSG that drinks like Vosne." It usually
trades at a meaningful discount to the domaine's Vosne 1er Crus (Beaumonts,
Suchots) despite being from the same slope.

The Beaumonts and Suchots are the **Flagey-shoulder** 1er Crus discussed
in the view — Beaumonts bordering Échézeaux, Suchots adjoining
Romanée-Saint-Vivant. Grivot owns serious chunks of both.

## Style

Modern-Vosne reference: ripe but lifted, polished but not glossy, built
for 10–25 years. Less savoury than Mugneret-Gibourg, less precocious
than Georges Noëllat — squarely in the middle of the contemporary
"classical" Vosne idiom.
