# Arnaud Lambert

_The reference grower of Saumur/Brézé — bright, limestone-driven Chenin Blanc and Cabernet Franc from deep-limestone parcels around Brézé and Saint-Cyr. Organic-certified, single-parcel focused, and among the Loire's best values for terroir-transparent white and red. Grew out of his father's Domaine de Saint-Just._

## Notes

**Arnaud Lambert** is the leading contemporary grower of the Saumur/Brézé hill, making some of the Loire's most transparent limestone Chenin Blanc and Cabernet Franc. The estate grew out of his father **Yves Lambert's Domaine de Saint-Just** (founded 1996); Arnaud joined in **2005**, began the conversion to organic farming in **2009**, and reached certification in **2012**. Today he farms roughly **40 hectares** split between the communes of **Brézé** and **Saint-Cyr**, both within the Saumur appellation. Arnaud is also the steward of the historic **Château de Brézé** vineyards, one of the Loire's great limestone terroirs.

**Terroir & farming.** The Saumur plateau rises from the banks of the Loire on soils of **deep limestone (tuffeau)**, with cellars dug directly into the rock beneath the vines. Farming is organic, hand-harvested, with traditional vinifications. The house signature is precision and tension: whites of gleaming acidity and delicate earthiness, reds of fine-grained structure — both built to reward a few years in bottle.

**Key wines.** The Chenin bottlings are the calling card, made as single-parcel expressions just kilometers apart:
- **Clos du Midi** (Brézé) — vines planted 1985 in silt/clay over limestone; the accessible, brilliant-value cuvée (pear, yellow plum, cool stone).
- **Clos David** (Brézé, toward the Loire) — ~60-year-old vines; more density and tension, a lifted spice note.
- **Coulée de Saint-Cyr** (Saint-Cyr) — ~70-year-old vines; the most powerful and rich in solar vintages, with a savory fennel edge.
- Plus a range of **Saumur / Saumur-Champigny Cabernet Franc** (e.g. "Tue-Loup," "Montée des Roches," Clos de l'Étoile) — bright, limestone-inflected reds.

**Style & aging.** Among the brightest expressions of Chenin — high acidity, rich texture, constantly shifting flavors — that drink well young but improve over **5–10 years** (longer for the old-vine parcels and top vintages). The Cab Francs are mid-weight, structured, food-focused Loire reds.

**Critical standing & availability.** Chambers Street championed him hard in 2022 ("A Quiet Storm in Saumur" — their taster, a former restaurant wine director, calls the wines "omnipresent" on his lists and himself "a huge fan"). Raeder's currently lists three cuvées (the Saumur Rouge "Tue-Loup" 2021 at $39.99, plus the "La Rue Brézé" Chenin and "Montée des Roches" red). **US importer not yet confirmed in the vault** — verify before citing.

**Why Evan's taste.** A core-fit Loire producer: terroir-driven, organic-certified, grower-scale, limestone-transparent, ageworthy, and a genuine value — exactly the Saumur/Chenin + Cab Franc profile Evan favors, and a natural shelf-mate to [[domaine_guiberteau|Guiberteau]] and [[clos_rougeard|Clos Rougeard]].
