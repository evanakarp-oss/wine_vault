## Notes

**Château de Pibarnon** represents the high-quality end of Bandol, Provence's most prestigious and age-worthy red-wine appellation. Located in the dramatic coastal hills of Bandol (west of Toulon), Pibarnon produces elegant, structured Mourvèdre-based reds that exemplify the appellation's mineral depth and aging potential — wines that improve markedly over 15–25 years rather than declining with age.

**Terroir & Vineyard Character.** Bandol's terroir is distinctive within Provence: a steep, south-facing slope of limestone-rich clay soils overlooking the Mediterranean, with cooling sea breezes that preserve acidity and complexity. The appellation mandate requires a minimum of 50% Mourvèdre in any red wine labeled "Bandol" — a strict requirement that ensures the varietal's characteristic structure and tannin profile. Pibarnon's vineyard holdings benefit from this site's natural advantages: exposure, altitude, and soil minerality that produce concentrated, age-worthy fruit.

**Winemaking Style & Key Wines.** Pibarnon's Bandol Rouge is a classical expression: deep red color, black fruit and garrigue aromas, medium-full body with firm tannins, and considerable aging potential. The wines reflect a restrained house style — not extracted or oaked heavily, but built for structure and longevity. Unlike many Provence reds (which are designed for early, refreshing drinking), Pibarnon's Bandol is explicitly a cellar wine, with a 3–5 year minimum aging window before showing its full complexity.

**US Market Position.** Distributed through Skurnik and Down to Earth (Panzer's portfolio entry at $47), Pibarnon has modest but quality-focused distribution in the US. The producer appeals to serious collectors familiar with Bandol's reputation and to wine professionals seeking age-worthy reds at reasonable prices — a combination that attracts Evan's taste profile (terroir-driven, food-appropriate, structured for aging).

**Regional Significance.** Bandol remains one of France's most undervalued age-worthy appellations, particularly when compared to Bordeaux or Northern Rhône reds at similar price points. Pibarnon exemplifies this value proposition: high-quality, structured, age-worthy Mourvèdre-based wines at accessible pricing. The producer is a reference point for collectors building Provence cellars with cellaring potential.
