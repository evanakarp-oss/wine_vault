# Stella Crinita

Winemaker: **Joanna Foster & Ernesto Catena**. Vista Flores, Tunuyán (Mendoza). Category: **Radical Natural**.

## Notes

**Stella Crinita** is a small, radical-natural winery in Mendoza (Argentina) co-founded by **Joanna Foster** and **Ernesto Catena**, representing a bold experiment in minimal-intervention Argentine viticulture—the estate became the first non-European producer admitted to VinNatur (the international radical-natural wine collective), a distinction reflecting the winery's uncompromising commitment to natural fermentation, zero sulfite additions, and biodynamic farming. Demeter-certified biodynamic since 2002, the estate cultivates modest vineyard holdings in Vista Flores (Tunuyán) on limestone-clay soils at high altitude (1,100+ meters), terroirs naturally suited to producing wines with mineral precision and natural complexity when managed with radical-natural methods. Biodynamic viticulture emphasizes lunar-cycle planting and harvesting, natural pest management (minimal copper/sulfur inputs), and hand harvesting with selective sorting—yields are deliberately modest to concentrate aromatic complexity and phenolic development. Winemaking embraces complete natural intervention: 100% spontaneous (wild yeast) fermentation with no commercial inoculants, zero SO2 additions at any stage (including crushing and bottling), unfined and unfiltered bottling, and natural pH/acidity management without manipulation. Signature expressions include **Stella Crinita Barbera** (typical $30–50 estimated; age-worthy red with mineral character) and **Pet Nat Blanc** (a sparkling natural wine from white varieties, $25–40 estimated), both representing the estate's commitment to radical-natural philosophy in an Argentine context where most producers rely on sulfite additions and temperature control. Production is extremely small (tiny vineyard holdings, low yields), making Stella Crinita wines difficult to source but highly sought by radical-natural wine enthusiasts and collectors aligned with natural-wine philosophy. Skurnik's representation (alongside VellaTerra and Brazos Wine) confirms import priority status among natural-wine specialists. Stella Crinita's participation in Argentina Reloaded events (Rio 2024, Buenos Aires 2025) signals recognition among curators of Argentine artisan and natural-wine producers. The domaine's Demeter-biodynamic certification, zero-sulfite philosophy, and commitment to natural fermentation represent the apex of natural-wine production and align directly with Evan's documented Argentina taste filter (biodynamic-certified, terroir-driven, artisan-scale producers).

## Approach

Demeter-certified biodynamic since 2002. 100% spontaneous fermentation, zero SO2 at any stage, unfined and unfiltered. First non-European producers admitted to VinNatur. Production extremely small.
