## Notes

Agathe Bursin is a young, emerging biodynamic grower in Alsace known for precise, energetic white wines from the Lutzeltal terroir near Pfaffenheim. Launched in 2010, Bursin represents a new generation of Alsace growers emphasizing low-intervention winemaking, organic and biodynamic farming, and terroir-focused production over the broader, oak-influenced "international Alsace" style. Her portfolio is small but focused: Sylvaner is her signature (a humble grape elevated to nuance in Bursin's hands), plus Riesling and other aromatic varieties. The philosophy is transparency, minerality, and the expression of limestone-rich soils through minimal winery intervention.

**Vineyard Holdings & Philosophy.** Bursin works roughly 5–6 hectares in the Lutzeltal (a small valley near Pfaffenheim in south-central Alsace) on limestone-rich soils. The terroir is known for producing high-toned, mineral whites with good acidity. Her farming is biodynamic (Demeter-certified or in-process), with cover crops, minimal inputs, and hand harvesting. The philosophy is radical transparency: native yeasts, minimal sulfur additions, no temperature control, and wines bottled unfined and unfiltered. The result is wines that taste like pure terroir expression, not winery signature.

**Winemaking Style.** Bursin employs a natural-wine methodology while stopping short of the extreme interventions common in the "natural wine" category. Fermentation is spontaneous with ambient yeasts; maceration time is minimal for whites (0–5 days for Sylvaner). Oak is not used (stainless steel or clay vessels). The wines typically show low alcohol (11.5–12.5%), preserving acidity and aromatic purity. Sulfur is added minimally, only at bottling if necessary. The result is wines of remarkable clarity: the Sylvaner shows white stone fruit, citrus, green apple, and pronounced minerality; the Rieslings are dry and energetic, not sweet.

**Key Cuvées.**
- **Sylvaner Lutzeltal:** The flagship, from the local terroir. This wine elevates Sylvaner from "table wine" to complexity: mineral-driven, crisp, with citrus and herbal notes, aged in neutral containers (stainless or clay). Drink young (1–3 years) for freshness, but ages beautifully to 5–8 years. The 2017 vintage ($18 per DTE) is a benchmark modern Sylvaner.
- **Riesling (various parcels):** Bursin also produces small quantities of dry Riesling, equally precise and mineral-focused. Typically 11.5–12% alcohol, bone-dry, ages 5–10 years.

**Reputation & Allocation.** Agathe Bursin is an emerging discovery within natural/biodynamic wine circles and among Alsace connoisseurs. The house has not received major Chambers Street coverage (0 mentions) but is tracked by DTE (1 cuvée), positioning it as a Panzer discovery. The wine is affordable ($15–40), indicating quality without prestige-pricing. Secondary-market presence is minimal (no auction activity), suggesting the house is direct-to-consumer or small-network focused.

**Aging Potential.** Bursin's wines are primarily "drink-young-fresh" whites, but the best vintages develop complexity with 3–8 years of bottle age. The Sylvaner is beautiful at 1–2 years but develops herbal and mineral complexity at 5+ years. The Rieslings are similarly built for early-drinking pleasure but show improved mineral expression after 3–5 years. The 2017 Sylvaner is at its sweet spot now (2026) and will evolve pleasantly through 2028–2030.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Agathe Bursin represents the intersection of Evan's white-wine and biodynamic-farming interests: certified biodynamic, grower-scale (micro-production), terroir-transparent winemaking, and refreshingly affordable pricing. The Lutzeltal Sylvaner is not a heavy, oaky wine but a mineral, energetic expression — classic Alsace terroir done right. If seeking quality, affordable, biodynamic Alsace whites for early drinking with modest aging potential, Bursin is a canonical recommendation. The DTE inclusion suggests Panzer views this as a smart discovery bottling.
