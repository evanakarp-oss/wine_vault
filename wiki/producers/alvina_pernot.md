## Notes

Alvina Pernot is a young, dynamic white-wine producer launched by Alvina Pernot (member of the historic Paul Pernot family, known for Chardonnay in Puligny-Montrachet) in partnership with Philippe Abadie. The domaine is relatively new (established in the 2010s but gaining prominence 2018–2026) and represents a modern expression of Côte de Beaune white-wine excellence, emphasizing terroir transparency and energetic winemaking. Alvina Pernot controls small parcels across the Côte de Beaune white zones, from humble Saint-Romain village up to grand cru Bienvenues-Bâtard-Montrachet, and has become a discovery source for serious Chardonnay enthusiasts seeking quality with less prestige-pricing than the mega-houses (Leflaive, Etienne Sauzet).

**Vineyard Holdings & Philosophy.** Alvina Pernot's parcels are primarily white-grape terroirs: Saint-Romain (a secondary Côte de Beaune zone known for crisp, mineral whites), Puligny-Montrachet villages and premier crus, and notably a small holding in Bienvenues-Bâtard-Montrachet (a grand cru white). The farming is meticulous (hand harvesting, fruit selection) and aims for organic practices, though not formally certified. The philosophy is to showcase the limestone-rich soils and cool-climate precision of the Côte de Beaune; alcohol is moderate (12.5–13%) and acidity is preserved through careful harvest timing.

**Winemaking Style.** Alvina Pernot employs traditional Burgundy white winemaking: whole-bunch pressing, indigenous fermentation in barrel (40–50% new oak depending on cuvée), malolactic fermentation (variable), and 10–12 month élevage with minimal intervention. The result is wines of remarkable precision: citrus, green apple, and white-flower notes with pronounced minerality and saline finish. The use of oak is evident but integrated, adding complexity without overwhelming the terroir expression. The house also produces a small red (Pinot Noir from nearby parcels), but whites are the focus and strength.

**Key Cuvées.**
- **Saint-Romain Villages Blanc:** The entry point, from a secondary zone known for value and precision. Crisp, mineral-driven, with citrus and white flowers; ages 5–8 years but is beautiful young (2–3 years).
- **Puligny-Montrachet (various 1er crus):** The mid-tier offering, showing more complexity and depth than Saint-Romain; classic Puligny opulence with mineral restraint; ages 8–12 years.
- **Bienvenues-Bâtard-Montrachet Grand Cru:** The flagship, from one of white Burgundy's most prized vineyards (neighboring the famous Bâtard-Montrachet monopole). This is serious grand cru Chardonnay: layered, complex, with citrus, white stone fruit, and pronounced minerality; ages 12–20 years. The 2022 vintage ($899) is a statement wine — expensive but world-class quality.

**Reputation & Availability.** Alvina Pernot has gained rapid recognition since 2018–2020, particularly among white-wine connoisseurs and Burgundy specialists. The domaine is tracked by DTE (2 cuvées), suggesting Panzer recognizes it as a quality discovery. However, critical coverage is limited (no Chambers Street mentions, no major auction presence yet), indicating the house is still building reputation. Allocation is modest but available through fine-wine retailers and direct channels. Secondary-market prices are fair relative to quality — the Saint-Romain is $40–60, Puligny 1ers are $70–120, and the grand cru is $800–1000+.

**Aging Potential.** Alvina Pernot's whites are built for the cellar. The Saint-Romain is elegant young (2–3 years) but develops complexity at 5–8 years. The Puligny 1ers peak at 8–15 years, developing hazelnut, toasted almond, and complex minerality. The grand cru Bienvenues-Bâtard is a 12–20 year wine, reaching full complexity at 10+ years. The 2022 vintage (the one tracked by DTE) will be beautiful from 2026 onward and will peak 2030–2042.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Alvina Pernot aligns with Evan's Burgundy/white-wine filter: terroir-driven, grower-scale, precise winemaking, and wines built for the cellar. The domaine represents the new generation of quality-focused producers emerging from famous families (the Paul Pernot connection) with fresh perspective and international standards. If seeking high-quality, non-commodity white Burgundy with emerging-producer pricing and serious aging potential, Alvina Pernot is a canonical recommendation.
