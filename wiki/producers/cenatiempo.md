# Cenatiempo

Founded in 1945 by Francesco Cenatiempo, a pioneer of modern viticulture in Ischia, the winery is directed today by his son Pasquale. Cenatiempo produces compelling, focused, mineral-driven wines from the island's indigenous varieties on steep slopes of Ischia, a volcanic island off Naples's coast that last erupted in 1302. Viticulture here remains mountain viticulture at its most extreme, ranging from sea level to over 600 meters elevation. The volcanic soils—the literal foundation of the island—create distinctive terroir. Cenatiempo farms organically in smaller parcels and has begun adapting biodynamic principles and treatments in larger vineyards. Production focuses primarily on whites—Ischia Bianco blends plus monovarietal Biancolella and Forastera—with small quantities of reds, mainly Piedirosso, Aglianico, and Sciascinoso.

## Notes

**Ischia volcanism.** The island's foundation is volcanic—Ischia was formed by a volcano that last erupted in 1302. This geology creates wines with unmistakable minerality and tension, impossible to replicate on mainland Campania. Every bottle carries the island's geological story.

**Extreme mountain viticulture.** Elevation ranging from sea level to 600+ meters creates microclimates and harvesting logistics that rival other European heroic-viticulture sites. Viticulturists must literally climb the island's slopes to reach their vineyards.

**White-wine emphasis.** Cenatiempo's focus on Biancolella and Forastera—indigenous Ischia varieties—is a statement about regional identity. These grapes, planted nowhere else, produce wines impossible to find elsewhere. This is terroir specificity at its most extreme.

**Organic and biodynamic evolution.** The progression from conventional (1945–present) to organic (current smaller parcels) to biodynamic principles (larger parcels) suggests thoughtful evolution rather than dogmatic pivot. Phased adoption shows pragmatism alongside ecological commitment.

**Island terroir narrative.** Ischia offers a distinct story: island viticulture, volcanic geology, indigenous varieties, extreme elevation changes, organic/biodynamic farming, and limited production. This is discovery territory for serious collectors.

**Cellar fit.** Cenatiempo is worth pursuing for Evan's Campania exploration, particularly as a Ischia specialist and white-wine-focused producer. Current US portfolio via Polaner Selections includes Ischia Bianco blends (traditional white cuvées), monovarietal Biancolella and Forastera (Ischia's indigenous white varieties), and small-lot reds from Piedirosso, Aglianico, and Sciacinosso. All wines ferment with indigenous yeasts in steel or cement vats, then age in the same vessels—a commitment to minimal intervention that highlights the volcanic terroir. Founded in 1945 by Francesco Cenatiempo and currently run by Pasquale Cenatiempo and his wife Federica, the estate represents Ischia's extreme mountain viticulture and the island's singular volcanic geology. The combination of organic/biodynamic farming, indigenous Ischia varieties, limited production, and distinctive volcanic terroir makes this a compelling addition. Island wine story = automatic appeal to serious collectors.
