# Anne Gros

Vosne-Romanée domaine, ~6.5 ha, run by **Anne Gros** (daughter of François
Gros), who took over the estate in 1988 and quickly reset its reputation.
One of the four "Gros" domaines in Vosne (along with cousin Michel Gros,
brother Bernard Gros = Gros Frère et Sœur, and aunt Anne-Françoise Gros);
the genealogy is famously tangled but Anne's wines are the consensus
quality reference within the family.

## Holdings

- **Grand Cru** Richebourg (small parcel, the marquee), Clos de Vougeot
  *"Le Grand Maupertui"* (an upper-slope parcel — the favourable side of
  the Clos), Échézeaux
- **Chambolle-Musigny 1er Cru** **La Combe d'Orveau** — small upper-slope
  monopole that abuts Musigny Grand Cru. Anne Gros's parcel is one of two
  Combe d'Orveau plots (Bruno Clavelier holds the other); both are
  effectively "Chambolle next to Musigny" at non-Musigny prices.
- **Vosne-Romanée** **Les Barreaux** (monopole, village-level lieu-dit
  upslope of the village; a textbook "village-from-monopole" play)
- Bourgogne and Bourgogne Blanc

## Style

Polished, gourmand, **modern Vosne** — riper than Mugneret-Gibourg, more
filigree than Grivot, with a recognizable signature of perfume and
gentle extraction. The Clos de Vougeot Grand Maupertui is particularly
useful for the study view: it is **upper-slope Clos Vougeot** — the
"good third" of the 50ha grand cru — and gives a clean read on what the
upper Clos can actually do under a careful winemaker.

## Why this domaine matters

For the study view, Anne Gros covers the **north-side adjacency** in
depth: upper-slope Clos Vougeot (the "Musigny edge" of the Clos), a
Chambolle 1er monopole that abuts Musigny, and an Échézeaux. The Vosne
*village* monopole Les Barreaux is also worth flagging — a village
bottling from monopole-level parcel control is the kind of off-the-radar
buy that gives you "serious Vosne house" pedigree without 1er Cru pricing.

## Side note — Anne et Jean-François Gros

Anne and her husband Jean-François Gros also run a separate domaine in
**Minervois (Cazelles)**, producing Carignan and Grenache from old
bush-vines. Different project, outside scope for this view, but useful
context if a Cazelles bottle turns up on a list.
