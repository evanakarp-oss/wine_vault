# Zorzal Wines

Winemaker: **Michelini Brothers (JP, Matías, Gerardo)**. Tupungato (Mendoza). Category: **Biodynamic**.

## Approach

Founded 2008. Concrete eggs, no oak in terroir line, native yeasts, respectful enology. One of the most influential projects in the Uco Valley. Biodynamic practices throughout.

Malbec, Pinot Noir, Cab Franc, Torrontés

## Notes

**Zorzal Wines** is a flagship biodynamic producer in Tupungato (Mendoza's Uco Valley), founded in 2008 by the Michelini Brothers (JP, Matías, Gerardo) and representing one of Argentina's most influential natural-wine projects. Operating across a range of international (Pinot Noir, Cabernet Franc, Malbec) and indigenous (Torrontés) varieties, Zorzal exemplifies the modern biodynamic-agriculture movement in South America, emphasizing minimal-intervention winemaking (concrete-egg fermentation, native yeasts, no oak in terroir-focused bottlings) and site-specific terroir expression aligned with Evan's Argentina interest filters.

**Farming & Terroir Philosophy.** Zorzal practices certified biodynamic viticulture across their Tupungato holdings, leveraging the Uco Valley's high-altitude cool climate, volcanic soils, and pronounced day-night temperature swings to produce wines with balance, acidity, and mineral precision. The biodynamic approach—incorporating cosmic-calendar practices and closed-loop farm philosophy—reflects the Michelini Brothers' commitment to natural, ecosystem-based agriculture rather than conventional or merely organic certification.

**Winemaking Approach.** The signature Zorzal style emphasizes minimal intervention: native yeast fermentation, spontaneous malolactic conversion, concrete-egg or neutral-vessel aging, and avoidance of new oak (at least in the flagship terroir-driven line). This approach prioritizes grape quality and site expression over winemaker intervention or obvious oak influence, a philosophy aligned with contemporary natural-wine movements while maintaining serious structure and aging potential.

**Key Wines & Portfolio.** The core range includes **Terroir Único** (the flagship, produced from dry-farmed, high-altitude parcels, emphasizing mineral precision and site expression); **Eggo Franco** (produced in concrete eggs, showcasing the vessel's textural influence); and **Lecciones de Vuelo** ("Lessons of Flight"), reflecting the poetic, culturally-engaged branding typical of contemporary Argentine projects. Grape varieties span Malbec, Pinot Noir, Cabernet Franc, and Torrontés, allowing stylistic diversity across a single philosophical framework.

**Regional Significance & Collector Standing.** Zorzal has become one of Argentina's most respected and influential contemporary producers, positioned alongside Altos Las Hormigas and other Uco Valley pioneers as driving force for biodynamic, quality-focused wine production. Wine Enthusiast recognition and Skurnik distribution ensure consistent US availability. The producer appeals to collectors seeking Argentina's emerging terroir-driven aesthetic, particularly those interested in biodynamic farming and minimal-intervention winemaking as philosophical commitments rather than marketing tools.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Zorzal represents contemporary Argentina aligned with Evan's evolving taste filters: biodynamic farming, minimal-intervention winemaking, site-transparency, and commitment to expressing terroir over fruit ripeness. The Tupungato positioning (cool, high-altitude, lesser-known relative to mainstream Mendoza zones) and Michelini Brothers' reputation for thoughtfulness and quality make Zorzal an essential discovery for collectors exploring Argentina's natural-wine frontier.
