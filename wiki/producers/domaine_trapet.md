## Notes

**Domaine Trapet Père et Fils** is a historic Burgundy house in Gevrey-Chambertin that has undergone a remarkable terroir-driven transformation under Jean-Louis Trapet's leadership, shifting toward biodynamic viticulture and minimal-intervention winemaking in recent years. The estate is now recognized as one of the finest sources for transparent, site-specific Gevrey-Chambertin, including several Grand Cru holdings (notably Latricieres-Chambertin). CSW has championed the domaine across 4 dedicated articles (2020-2026), emphasizing the precision, "tension," and aging potential of the wines, and the producer is flagged as a CSW Cellar Pick. William Kelley's extended coverage across Berserkers posts (2020-2022) underscores the domaine's rising reputation among serious collectors for the 2017-2020 vintage series.

**Farming & Terroir Philosophy.** Trapet practices biodynamic viticulture, unusual for Gevrey but increasingly embraced by progressive growers seeking to express terroir with minimal intervention. The estate holds parcels across a range of Gevrey appellations, from village-level to Grand Cru sites. The signature holdings are Latricieres-Chambertin (a Grand Cru known for red-berry purity and mineral drive) and Chambertin parcels. The winemaking philosophy is expressly non-interventionist: native yeast fermentation, minimal sulfite use, long maceration for structure, and little to no fining or filtration. CSW notes: "Tension, taste and terroir" are the core values, with wines designed to express site and vintage over "house style."

**Winemaking Style & Key Cuvées.** Trapet's reds emphasize red-berry purity, mineral acidity, and silky tannin structure—characteristic of top-tier Gevrey. The Latricieres-Chambertin (Burghound 94 in 2005) is the flagship expression, representing the Grand Cru's natural elegance and aging potential. The domaine also produces whites (Meursault and Blagny), unusual for a Gevrey-focused house, reflecting the family's diverse historical holdings. Vintage precision is pronounced: William Kelley specifically notes that the 2017, 2018, 2019, and 2020 series represent "an incredible series" going "from strength to strength."

**Collector Standing & Aging Curve.** Trapet wines are built for 15-30 year aging trajectories. The 2005 Latricieres-Chambertin shows at Burghound 94 with notes of "fresh red berry fruit and mineral hints," indicating a slow-aging profile with peak drinking likely in the 12-25 year window. CSW emphasizes these are not "ready to drink" wines but rather structured expressions requiring patience—aligned with Evan's preference for site-driven, age-worthy Burgundy. Dual US importers (Polaner and Skurnik) ensure consistent supply, and William Kelley's consumer mentions signal insider knowledge and collector enthusiasm.

**Why Evan's Taste.** Trapet exemplifies the modern terroir-focused Burgundy movement: biodynamic farming, minimal intervention, precision over extraction, and Grand Cru holdings that age beautifully. The CSW cellar pick designation and emphasis on "tension" and structural integrity (not ripeness) align perfectly with Evan's filters for grower-scale, site-transparent Burgundy. Recent vintages (2017-2020) represent a clear quality inflection and are worth exploring at allocation.
