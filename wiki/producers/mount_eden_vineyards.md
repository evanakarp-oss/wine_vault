# Mount Eden Vineyards

One of California's original boutique estates, founded **1945 by Martin Ray** (a
lineage running back to Paul Masson) atop the **Santa Cruz Mountains** at ~**2,000'**.
The ~40-acre estate sits on austere, infertile **Franciscan shale** on a cool, exposed
mountaintop and is **dry-farmed — no irrigation**. The Estate Cabernet comes from
~40-year-old dry-farmed vines and is firm, mineral, powerful-yet-fine, and built for
the long haul — a Santa Cruz Mountains neighbor in spirit to Ridge Monte Bello, and a
core reference for rugged, site-transparent California Cabernet.

## Wines

- **Estate Cabernet Sauvignon (Santa Cruz Mountains)** — the flagship. Cuttings trace
  to **Emmett Rixford's La Questa Vineyard** (Woodside, planted 1890s, reputedly from
  Château Margaux). Structured, ageworthy.
- **Estate Chardonnay** and **Estate Pinot Noir** — same dry-farmed mountaintop.
- **Domaine Eden** — second label/property (developed 1983 by Tom Mudd, later acquired
  and renamed). Mid-appellation Santa Cruz Mountains Cabernet sourced in/around
  Saratoga; softer and earlier-drinking than the Estate.

## Notes

**Jeffrey Patterson** has led winemaking and farming since **1981**; he and Ellie
Patterson became majority owners in **2008**, keeping the estate family-run. The
Estate Cabernet is a classic candidate for drink-now aged bottles as well as
long-haul cellaring — the antithesis of the cult-hedonist tier.

US distribution incl. **David Bowler Wine**; widely available (Flatiron, K&L,
Millesima). Sources: see `raw/web_research/mount_eden_vineyards.md`.
