# Arnoux Lachaux

## Notes

**Arnoux-Lachaux** is a Vosne-Romanée Pinot Noir producer led by **Charles Arnoux**, known among serious Burgundy enthusiasts as an emerging reference for terroir-driven, traditionally-vinified red Burgundy that emphasizes mineral expression and aging potential over modern fruit-forward styles. The domaine's parcels are scattered across multiple Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru and village-level sites, with particular focus on **Chambolle-Musigny** (especially the parcel "Les Fouchères" with old-vine Pinot from 1950s massale selections, now farmed organically), **Echezeaux**, and **Nuits-Saint-Georges** expressions that showcase diverse terroir characteristics. Farming practices have moved toward organic and biodynamic methods, with William Kelley specifically noting the estate's commitment to "no-till viticulture" (minimal soil disturbance to preserve microflora health and natural complexity)—an innovative approach still rare in Burgundy that builds long-term soil health. Winemaking emphasizes native yeast fermentation, extended skin contact for complexity, and restrained new oak usage to let the wine's mineral, red-fruit core emerge cleanly—William Kelley specifically praised the 2018 Chambolle-Musigny Les Fouchères as "very open," suggesting an estate committed to expressing terroir rather than extracting maximum color and concentration. Down to Earth portfolio shows 16 cuvée/vintage entries ranging $299–$6,999, confirming the house's serious Grand Cru and high-end 1er Cru focus (particularly Romanée-Saint-Vivant at $6,999 and Latricieres Chambertin at $3,999 for 2018/2019 vintages). Berserkers mentions (8 posts by William Kelley post-2020, with momentum rising to rank 1457) indicate growing collector interest and recognition of Charles Arnoux's emerging reputation. William Kelley has noted the wines are "unfashionable a decade ago" but "things move fast"—the estate represents a rising benchmark for contemporary Burgundy quality focused on farming transparency, terroir expression, and traditional winemaking discipline.
