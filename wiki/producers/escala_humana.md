## Notes

**Escala Humana** ("Human Scale"), operated by winemaker Germán Masera, represents the emerging Argentine new wave of terroir-driven, minimally manipulated winemaking. Based in Valle de Uco (Mendoza's high-altitude subregion), Masera has gained rapid international recognition for expressive, balanced Chardonnays and Semillons that combine Mendoza's natural concentration with European-style restraint — wines that prioritize freshness and clarity over alcohol and extraction.

**Winemaking Philosophy & "Human Scale" Positioning.** The producer's name reflects a deliberate philosophical stance: "Escala Humana" emphasizes wines made at human scale (small production, careful hand-work), sold directly or through selective importers, without the industrialization or globalized marketing that characterizes mainstream Argentine wine. This positioning aligns with Evan's stated taste filter for Argentina: biodynamic/terroir-driven/artisan-scale producers who treat winemaking as a philosophical practice rather than a commercial exercise.

**Terroir & Valle de Uco Altitude.** Valle de Uco (Tunuyán subregion) sits at higher elevations (800–1,200 meters) than Mendoza's typical vineyard zones, resulting in longer growing seasons, lower sugar accumulation, and better acid retention. This terroir naturally produces lower-alcohol wines (often 12.5–13.5% vs. Mendoza's typical 14%+) with crisp acidity and mineral precision — the inverse of typical Argentina Malbec stereotypes.

**Key Wines & Varietal Focus.** The flagship expression is **Buscado Vivo o Muerto (Sought Dead or Alive) Chardonnay from El Cerro Gualtallary** — an allusion to the producer's commitment to honesty and uncompromising fruit selection. Masera also produces **Semillon**, Malbec, and experimental small-lot expressions. The winemaking emphasizes native yeast fermentation, minimal SO2, and extended aging in older (not new) oak — a restraint-first approach unusual for Argentine high-prestige whites.

**Critical Recognition & Market Position.** Masera's rapid rise to recognition (James Suckling #7 Argentina 2025) speaks to his wines' quality and stylistic distinctiveness. The producer is featured prominently in Argentina Reloaded curator Paz Levinson's selection, indicating alignment with Evan's Argentina taste filter. Skurnik importation provides access to quality US retail channels, though allocation remains limited due to small production.

**Argentine New-Wave Significance.** Escala Humana exemplifies a broader Argentine movement: young winemakers (often second-generation or outside traditional wine families) who are reconstructing Argentina's wine identity away from high-alcohol, consultant-driven extractions toward terroir-driven, minimal-intervention approaches. Masera's success validates the market appetite for this style and positions Mendoza's higher-altitude subregions as potentially Argentina's most distinctive future-oriented wine source.
