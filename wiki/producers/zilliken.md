# Zilliken

## Notes

**Zilliken** is a prestigious Mosel-Saar (Saarburg) Riesling producer helmed by **Hanno Zilliken**, known for producing some of Germany's most elegant, age-worthy mineral Rieslings from the Saar's finest vineyard sites, particularly the legendary **Saarburger Rausch** parcel that produces Kabinett and Spätlese expressions with remarkable structure and complexity. The domaine's vineyards sit on the steep, slate-dominated slopes of the Saar (a tributary of the Mosel), where naturally low yields and cool-climate stress produce small berries concentrated with aromatic complexity and mineral salinity—terroirs that require patient cellaring but reward with decades of evolving complexity. Zilliken maintains an extensive back-vintage cellar with exceptional aging conditions (naturally moist stone cellar, naturally cool temperatures, ideal humidity) that preserves old bottles in remarkable condition—Hanno Zilliken's reputation partly rests on his willingness to release mature bottles that demonstrate the aging potential of great Saar Riesling. Winemaking follows traditional, minimal-intervention Mosel methods: native yeast fermentation, careful residual-sugar management (Kabinett/Spätlese levels typical for Saar), no new oak, and bottling with minimal sulfite additions to preserve aromatic purity. The flagship **Saarburger Rausch Kabinett** (typically $30–50 for current releases; older vintages (1994 and older) available through auction and specialty retailers) represents the estate's core expression—a wine built for 20–30+ year cellaring with proven aging track record. CSW championed Zilliken with two dedicated articles (stars) and an aging score of 2, plus cellar-pick designation; recent coverage (December 2025) praised the 2024 Rieslings as "delightful" and "limited and lively." Berserkers rank shows Zilliken at #284 among Top 10 cellar producers with rising momentum (2.0× post-2023), indicating sustained collector interest. Skurnik and Theise's representation confirms the estate's priority status among serious German Riesling specialists. The domaine's commitment to age-worthy, mineral-driven Saar Riesling, exceptional cellaring conditions, and terroir focus align directly with Evan's documented German taste filter (grower-scale, mineral-driven, age-worthy, cool-climate Riesling).
