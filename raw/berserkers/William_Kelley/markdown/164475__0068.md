> *Replying to :*  
> Lots of great Beaune. The top 1er crus are devalued because there are too many 1er crus that probably shouldn’t be.

One hears that said, but I think it’s more a question of the producers of Beaune frequently being being houses who have historically treated them as, for the most part, “entry level” 1er crus, farming and vinifying them accordingly. Some Beaune 1ers are clearly better than others, don’t get me wrong, but I don’t see all that many that ought to be downgraded (and this is where I walk on my weekends, in addition to farming a small parcel, so I know the lay of the ground pretty well at this stage). On the other hand, I would certainly divide them up into more meaningful climats, because those that we have are simply too big in many cases.
