> *Replying to :*  
> I have some of his 2014 2014 Puligny "La Garenne"1er Cru, which I have not tried yet. I believe I have heard mixed reports, some complaining about too much or perhaps still unresolved oak? I am no oak-o-phobe (I’ll drink Sauzet with pleasure under the right circumstances) nor an oak-o-phile. Anyone…

By 2014, the touch with new oak had become quite light for a while. The really oaky era of Marc Colin was actually when a young Pierre-Yves was making them! He then swung in the opposite direction when he started his own label.

The '14 Puligny Le Trézin was drinking quite well, albeit very youthfully, when I had a glass from a magnum back in September 2017 while passing through the Caveau de Chassagne. If you do open it, decant it.
