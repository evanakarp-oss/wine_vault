2016 is the most classic, 2017 the most immediate and charming, 2015 the most concentrated, and 2018 the most overtly ripe. In 2015, Santenots got a bit too ripe, it’s better balanced in the other three vintages.

My favorite wine from Thierry, especially in these warm years, might just be his Pommard Sausilles though. You can see the vineyard here: [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.0184032,4.8015747,3a,75y,131.9h,85.08t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1sVQu14t_9OEDlvRuXwhb4IA!2e0!6s%2F%2Fgeo0.ggpht.com%2Fcbk%3Fpanoid%3DVQu14t_9OEDlvRuXwhb4IA%26output%3Dthumbnail%26cb_client%3Dmaps_sv.tactile.gps%26thumb%3D2%26w%3D203%26h%3D100%26yaw%3D287.81638%26pitch%3D0%26thumbfov%3D100!7i16384!8i8192)

As you can see, it’s situated in a “combe”, so cooling breezes help keep things fresh. And Thierry’s vines in this parcel are very old.
