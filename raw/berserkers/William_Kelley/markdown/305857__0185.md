I don’t see that it’s all that obtuse. If the ambient microflora is the expression of the vineyard, why take such pains to eliminate so much of it? (“Some” in your last sentence is an understatement). And by what basis do we determine which yeasts are representative of the terroir, and which are to be suppressed by our winemaking choices?

For me, the only compelling argument in favor of native ferments is that they make more complex, textural wines. But that is a pragmatic argument.

This is one of the most misunderstood subjects in winemaking and the lay public has such a strong view on it that very few winemakers ever discuss it candidly. The reality is that far fewer wineries operate with 100% native microflora than you might imagine.
