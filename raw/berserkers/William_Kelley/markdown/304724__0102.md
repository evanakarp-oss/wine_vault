All the more so because, as I get into in the article, Chablis isn’t actually more acid than the wines from further south. One négociant I spoke to recently who has made wine from all over Burgundy told me that his Chablis are typically among the highest pH wines in the cellar. The paradox of Chablis is that while the regional climate is obviously a bit more marginal and later-ripening than the regions to the south, the 1er and grand cru terroirs themselves are very sunny, precocious, warm sites within that cooler context. That, I contend in the article, is one reason why Chablis is showing warmer vintages more obviously than some ostensibly warmer parts of Burgundy.
