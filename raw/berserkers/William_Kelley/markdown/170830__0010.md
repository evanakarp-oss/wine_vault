> Pithy analysis William. Given her structured style, I tend to like her “lesser” vineyards in “lesser” years for near-term consumption. Her Bourgogne is also just the ticket whilst waiting for her 1er crus to thaw. I think her Bon Batons is one of my three favorite Bourgognes made regionwide.

It’s definitely in the conversation! And I should have said, really, that the regional and communal bottlings are superb without even getting into the premiers crus.

I remember that, as a student, my first encounter with a Barthod wine was a 2001, at the time very tight and unfriendly; and it took me a while, and the generosity of a few friends with mature bottles, to really understand what the estate is all about. Today, I’d rank this among my favorite estates in the region.
