A strong case could be made that 1999 was Engel’s strongest vintage. For me the other contenders would be 1993 and 2002, which are also really successful, but I think just a bit more marked by the vintages as opposed to the sites. Whereas the 1999s are lovely glasses of Pinot *and* very clearly differentiated as Clos Vougeot, Grands Echezeaux etc.

The 1996s by contrast I have had very bad luck with. A long-time Engel importer in the UK told me the domaine suffered with very poor corks that year. I have had corked bottles, volatile bottles and bottles that are just not very compelling, with the ‘rusty pipe’ aspect that some commentators have identified as a characteristic of the vintage’s failures. Of course, I wouldn’t turn down the chance to try one given the domaine is no longer extant, but the 1996 Engels are not the bottles I would grab in the cellar if I wanted to convince someone of the pleasures Burgundy can offer!
