You really consider 87/100 to be equivalent to 4/5? That leaves me perplexed.

Note that I didn’t write this part of the vintage chart, nor the older ratings, so I am not particularly invested in it; but in the current world of score inflation/compression, I am pretty sure that people understand that 87/100 is not intended to convey glowing enthusiasm but rather “a weak vintage as a whole in which a few interesting wines were produced”. Equally, I have a real objection to writing off whole vintages, which simply doesn’t reflect the reality of what modern agronomy and meticulous selection can deliver (like vintage charts themselves, this is a mentality from the 1980s, when everything was picked at once and put in the same tank). There are some good 2011 Champagnes, from the Aube and a few top producers in the Côte des Blancs, as well as the likes of Vilmart, and it makes no sense to ignore them.
