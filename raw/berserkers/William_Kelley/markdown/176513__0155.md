> *Replying to :*  
> Interesting to note about the point on extraction because of higher temperatures - I believe Jadot is one of the few that still sticks to this, just wondering if any one else still sticks to this practice?

There is a big difference between fermenting hot, which happens naturally (a destemmed two- or three-ton ferment in a wooden tank will generally naturally get up to over 30 degrees centigrade unless you cool it down), and a heated post fermentation maceration whereby you use temperature control to maintain a high temperature. You naturally get a bit of that if you have a tank with good thermal inertia and a decent mass of ferment, but when you start playing around with warming things up at the end, I have frequently seen people go too far. At that stage you have >12% alcohol at play which combined with high temperature and fully broken up berries is really a very extractive environment.

Jadot I think just ferment quite hot but AFAIK they are not heating at the end.
