I don’t think it’s quite as dangerous as all that. Some estates in Bordeaux take criticism very graciously; others less so. A few may try to impose a ban, but with a few exceptions these are not such expensive or rare wines that it’s hard to find a bottle to review if one deems it worthwhile. And while people may huff and puff, the Bordelais are very adaptable: already in two years I have heard countless times that “x is finished, they will never be invited back”, only to see x tasting with y three months later. My attitude has come to be to operate more or less as if I have already tendered my resignation, and to write what I think and damn the consequences.

It’s a very different world from Burgundy, however, where no one has ever written to me to complain about a tasting note.
