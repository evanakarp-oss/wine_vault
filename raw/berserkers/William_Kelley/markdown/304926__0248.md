Well, as you can see, Terrace 4 has tended to make up the vast majority of the blend in recent years… and the Terrace 3 parcels were mostly acquired only in 2010. So the DNA of the estate has always been terrace 4, the geology shared with e.g. Latour and Las Cases. I think the decision to make the grand vin exclusively from terrace 4 is more about affirming the *identity* of Montrose than quality per say, as the terrace 3 bottling is a very compelling wine (not as deep or muscular, but super length and precision) in its own terms.
