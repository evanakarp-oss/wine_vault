This year I did a cuvée where we crushed (and I mean crushed) and I experimented with a 1.5 hours maceration before basket pressing without breaking the cake. Post-malo but before precipitation we are at pH 3.21, in what was is a rather high pH white vintage in Burgundy. So I don’t think you should be afraid of crushing. On the contrary, it really brings “shoulders” to a wine, along with the dry extract that helps balance the sweetness of fruit of ripe grapes.
