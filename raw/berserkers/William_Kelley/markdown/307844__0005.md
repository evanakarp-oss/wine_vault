Have always loved this wine! 2010 was the first vintage JMG used Diam, after having lots of cork problems in 08 and 09. He used natural cork on some of his 2010 Pouilly Fuissés and they are actually often more evolved, unless you land on a pristine bottle, than the Tri de Chavigne. He also understood from the beginning that if you go to Diam you can go down to 20-25 ppm of free sulfites at bottling. Often in their first few vintages, people stick with the higher levels they were using under natural cork, with the result that the wines are very closed for a decade (e.g. 2013 Lafon, which are only now opening).
