I suspect that the stereotype of Charmes as ‘fat’ (which it certainly can be) has a lot to do with its comparative unpopularity—especially vis a vis Perrieres. I think the same is true of Batard Montrachet. It is also the largest 1er cru in Meursault and quite variable (divided into two lieux-dits as is well known). I know several seasoned tasters, including one of some prominence in the UK wine trade, who think that Lafon’s Charmes, rather than his Perrieres, is the best Meursault in his cellar (perhaps because it has tended to be less worked than the Perrieres and the Montrachet?) and I am personally tentatively inclined to agree. The handful of old Lafon Charmes that I’ve encountered are my personal benchmarks for the vineyard—most recently 1979 earlier this year was just lovely. I’d love to try more.

The Buisson-Charles rendition does indeed seem to be very serious and I’m looking forward to seeing how the wines develop.
