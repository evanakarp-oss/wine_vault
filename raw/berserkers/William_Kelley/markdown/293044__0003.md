When you look at the Masnaghetti map of the Médoc estates, one of the striking things is just how much top terroir Beychevelle has. Quite a lot of the vineyards one might easily assume belong to Ducru Beaucaillou actually belong to Beychevelle. Just in terms of terroir, I would venture that they have clear super-second potential. Today, the style is quite chunky and extracted, again somewhat in contrast to the historical reputation for supple, giving wines.
