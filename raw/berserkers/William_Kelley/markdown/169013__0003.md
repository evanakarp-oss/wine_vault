> *Replying to :*  
> A 2016 of this just arrived in a shipment on Friday. They are very different and interesting.

Amusingly, the 2016-base contains more 2015 (60%) than the 2015-base (which is 60% 2014).

Some of my favorite wines and Olivier continues to push every year. Maillons and Pierrières see more and more reserve wines that are now almost all foudre- (rather than barrel-) aged for extra freshness and precision; and along with Roises and Les Enfers (where production is too small to constitute large volumes of reserve wines), he’s moving to longer and longer sur lattes. He’s also now disgorging with a little bit of SO2 (16 releases on) which will make the wines a touch tighter out of the gates but carry them further through time.

If anyone sees either Roises or Enfers in the special 60-month sur lattes version (2014 base), don’t hesitate, buy whatever you can!
