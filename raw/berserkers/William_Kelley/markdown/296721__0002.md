I do think it’s a little fanciful, especially since Narvaux is such a large climat and between the north and the south and the bottom and the top one should expect rather different styles of wine! I actually made a bit of Narvaux in 2022 so I have seen this first hand.

However it isn’t at all fanciful to distinguish between the profiles of hillside sites along the lines of Tillets, Narvaux, Clous, Vireuils etc and lower-lying sites such as Limouzin, Pelles, Cromin etc.

To me, there is more family resemblance between sites such as Chassagne-Montrachet Pot Bois, Puligny-Montrachet Le Trezin and Meursault Tillets, for example, than there is between those sites and their lower-lying cousins at the bottom of the slope in their three respective communes. If you talk with geologists you will rapidly realize that the communal boundaries seldom correspond with distinctive geologies. Indeed, tended to grow up on alluvial fans at the foot of ravines that in fact mark shifts in the geology of the Côte d’Or’s escarpment, so this is hardly surprising.
