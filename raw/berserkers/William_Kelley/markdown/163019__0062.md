The 2009 is actually 14%, even though it is labelled at 13%. This is what my predecessor, David Shildknecht, wrote about it in 2011:
> Foillard’s 2009 Morgon Cote du Py – bottled last September – displays game and cherry with kirsch distillate and smoky black tea overtones. Not in the least heavy or confitured, it evinces unusual – indeed, uncanny brightness and lift for its vintage, finishing long and lusciously, with alluring spice and saliva-stimulating salinity. “Maybe too rich,” says Foillard – knitting his brow – about this 14% alcohol wine. But that thought would not have crossed my mind. This beauty ought to perform well for at least half a dozen years.
The fact that the 2018 is honestly labelled might have something to do with tariffs…
