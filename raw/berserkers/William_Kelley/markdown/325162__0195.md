Hi Mark,

Perhaps I’ve communicated poorly, but I don’t think your remarks accurately reflect the reality of working as a team. I have been on “tasting pannels” with disparate people who don’t know each other, and that indeed can result in what you might characterize as a “mealy mouthed mess”. Regularly tasting together (sometimes blind) with colleagues you respect, and discussing the wines and the broader world of wine in which they exist, is very different—and much more fruitful. Matthew’s reviews are signed by Matthew for a reason (with not even any grammatical corrections to make), just as I sign my own, but exchanging with colleagues about wines from producers we’re mutually familiar with is, I would contend, a strength, not a weakness. I hope it’s more liberating than it is constraining. And all the more so when it entails the publication taking a departure from its historic position.

If you were to read the report and find any signs of mealy mouthed messes, I venture that I would be surprised.

Best,

William
