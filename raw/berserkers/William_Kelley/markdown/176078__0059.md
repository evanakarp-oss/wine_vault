> *Replying to :*  
> And, even while we can take as a granted that reds are less prone to oxidation, it seems hard to believe that massive cork quality issues related to oxygen ingress wouldn’t show some effects on red burgundies under the same cork as the whites. But the reds seem fine. Open a dozen bottles of 1996…

Not sure which point I’ve missed. Reds are much more resistant to oxidation than whites, so variation in corks show up as bottle variation rather than outright oxidation. The poorer quality cork, the more the variation. While this is getting very anecdotal here, and it’s hard to control for the impact of additional age, I think any argument that red wines were not affected by the abysmal quality of corks in the 1990s is going to be hard to sustain.
