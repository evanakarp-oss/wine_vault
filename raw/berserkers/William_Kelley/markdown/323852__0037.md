My point is that describing aromas, at least as a professional critic, shouldn’t just be free association. A very easy example would be a wine containing a high level of methoxypyrazines. Firstly, you have to be able to perceive that—which will depend on your threshold and your training. And then, the description that follows is grassy/herbaceous/bell pepper. If you say it smells like cumin, you’re wrong. And there are quite a lot of key aromas like this, with a chemical basis, that professionals can train to recognize and differentiate.
