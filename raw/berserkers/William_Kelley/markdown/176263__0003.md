> *Replying to :*  
> I opened a wine the other day with an extra long DIAM 30 and it got me wondering about whether and how cork length can affect a wine, whether it’s the ageing curve or something else. Anyone have any experience or insights into this?

If you listen to Levi’s interview with the DIAM CEO, he says DIAM 30 is longer mainly because of consumer perceptions. But DIAM is obviously a homogenous closure, whereas I suppose a longer cork in theory increases your chances of a tight seal.
