I think the big problem the region has is that, from the producer perspective, the wines are really quite reasonably priced and good value. You can get excellent terroir, great pedigree (if that counts for you), and high-quality winemaking for less, in greater volume, in Bordeaux than most other places. I think the Bordelais are right to point this out and be proud of it.

But from the consumer perspective, one looks at the en primeur price and then at the market price of other vintages and the value proposition looks much less appealing (you can see people doing this very explicitly in this thread). By virtue of the volumes produced and the commercial system that distributes the wines, Bordeaux seems to have been “commoditized” to a much greater degree than many other wines, and so people think about the price of new vintages in a way they don’t seem to with other wines. People buy e.g. their Gonon allocation and would be happy to pay the same price every year, irrespective of vintage quality; but that is not how many consumers who follow the wines think about Bordeaux. It is a problem that’s easier to diagnose than to solve for the region I suspect…
