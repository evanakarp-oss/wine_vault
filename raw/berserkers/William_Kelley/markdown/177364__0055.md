Reviewing Bordeaux for TWA definitely helps in this regard

From a private consumer’s perspective, Pomerol estates aren’t as hard to visit as you might imagine, however. Obviously it’s by appointment, and you’ll have a better chance if you write to the producer explaining your interest etc in addition to simply requesting a visit. As a student I managed to visit VCC, l’Evangile, Clinet, Gazin, some of the Mouiex estates, Eglise-Clinet, and even Lafleur, Le Pin and Pétrus with some help from a trade connection. One impediment to visiting some estates, however, is that proprietors don’t always live on site and vineyard work is contracted out, with winemaking left to a consultant; so there are some “labels” where you will be met with protracted radio silence if you ask to visit.
