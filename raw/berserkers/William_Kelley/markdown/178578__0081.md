> The questions are literally in Vince’s post that you quoted, Jeff. We’re both curious about whether producers chaptalized in 2014, if some did – which ones?, and why? Vince asked some specific “Why?” questions. Like many on this board, I generally love the 2014 vintage; if producers chaptalized, it…

You can do reverse osmosis on must before fermentation. Typically you take off 5% or so of the must, concentrate it, and return it. It’s the modern version of a saignée, or tank bleed. Of course, some people do them both. These are not subjects winemakers are generally happy to talk about, which is understandable but also a pity. The real things people should be worrying about are picking overripe fruit, saignée in very concentrated vintages, extraction enzymes, aggressively heated post-fermentation macerations, etc. Sugar, like sulfur, tends to get a bad rep because it’s a technique writers have actually heard of and somewhat understand, as opposed to the others.
