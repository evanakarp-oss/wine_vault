> *Replying to :*  
> I’m two bottles into a case of the 2009. It’s quite a structured Bojo. I can’t honestly tell yet if it really repays aging, but it’s not aging badly. Had an 09 last year as well and wondered the same (still 5 left). How did 02 compare to more recent vintages?

2002 was a challenging, rainy vintage in the Beaujolais. There were some good wines but they are generally fully mature.

The style at Château des Jacques has evolved subtly over the last decade towards more structural refinement and somewhat less new wood, so the 2017s, for example, are not as firm as the 2009s. That said, the style is still more “Burgundian”, which is to say destemmed with comparatively long macerations, so it isn’t going to be mistaken for carbonic or even the large part of semi-carbonic Beaujolais.
