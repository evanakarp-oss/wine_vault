The secret is indeed out! You used to be able to find multiple vintages in stock in the US and UK markets at least; now, each vintage sells through before the next arrives. Prices are creeping up as well, though they’re still very modest by contemporary Côte d’Or standards. Vincent has been making great wines for almost thirty years, and I think it’s amply deserved. And I hope it’s symptomatic of a wine culture that’s becoming a bit less fixated on appellation. Anyone who tasted Dureuil’s wines looking for value in the context of rising prices in the Côte de Beaune will have found that his wines are also better than a lot of wines being made in the Côte de Beaune, too.

Anyone interested in Dureuil’s methods might enjoy my article of earlier this year (sorry, it’s behind the paywall!) [Robert Parker Wine Advocate](https://www.robertparker.com/articles/R6ubXzaftHA552e6o/france-burgundy-cote-chalonnaise-domaine-dureuil-janthial-s-meix-cadot-vieilles-vignes-1994-2019)

And if you like Dureuil’s wines, check out his protégé Maxime Cottenceau.

While we’re on the subject of the Côte Chalonnaise, Gautier Desvignes in Givry is showing a lot of promise, too, since he took over the family domaine a couple of years ago.
