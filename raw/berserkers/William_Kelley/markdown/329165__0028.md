This doesn’t particularly surprise me. When you take an oxidative grape variety, subject it to what are/were frequently oxidative élevage and bottling practices, and then bottle it under a variable closure, that variation is going to be expressed as heterogenous evolution within a lot, with some examples more evolved than others, potentially dramatically so. The more resistant to oxidation the underlying wine, the less noticeable the variation will be.
