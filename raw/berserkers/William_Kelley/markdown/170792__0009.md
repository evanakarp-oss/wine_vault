> Nice timing! Often your praise is detrimental to people’s bank accounts but I find Thivin’s wines for the most part very cheap, both relatively and absolutely. That’s for sure! Other very nice 2019s recently tasted in a variety of style include Pauline Passsot (Grosse Pierre), Grégoire Hoppenot,…

Passot took over in 2018, which was also Hoppenot’s first vintage! So this is new stuff. There is a lot going on in the Beaujolais, including at the farming level: just among those I cited, Passot is going for organic certification; Hoppenot has some certified vines and is planning on being fully certified in 5 years despite having some very steeply sloping sites (his tractor flipped last week); Diochon stopped using herbicides and is planning organic conversion in a few years; and Métras has been working that way for years. It is far from easy to cultivate the soils of sites that are often steep and with goblet pruning that doesn’t lend itself to mechanization, and when you factor in the fact that e.g. a hectoliter of Chiroubles sells in bulk for around 210 EUR (Chambolle-Musigny, for context, is more like 2,222 EUR) it requires some dedication to do.
