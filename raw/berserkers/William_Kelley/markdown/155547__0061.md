> This is Dom Perignon in the Moet caves. As far as the eye can see.

For those that don’t know, it might be worth adding that these are undisgorged bottled aging on the lees “sur pointes”. If the chef des caves feels that a wine has been sufficiently enriched by lees contact while resting “sur lattes”, the bottles can be put “sur pointes” like this, much reducing the surface are of lees in contact with the wine. Most vintages of Cristal Oenothèque, for example, spend a certain number of years sur lattes before several more years sur pointes before disgorgment - with richer vintages seeing less time sur lattes, as a general rule, and more time sur pointes, and vice versa for leaner vintages.
