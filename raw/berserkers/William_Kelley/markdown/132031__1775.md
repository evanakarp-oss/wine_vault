> *Replying to :*  
> To me, the word seems vaguely positive when used with tannins – like they dance or tickle my tongue. It has bad connotations when it describes my TV image when my cable box is dying.

I think what people are trying to communicate is what I would describe as “powdery” tannins. For me, that’s something I’d link with a somewhat but not exaggeratedly extracted red with a comparatively high pH. As the pH starts to get lower, the tannins start to express themselves with a more chalky, eventually angular profile. And obviously, more aggressive extraction delivers more assertive tannic structure which, at a certain point, makes it hard to get into nuances. The first time I saw “pixelated” in a tasting note was in an article by Neal Martin, but I am not sure if he was the first to introduce the term into the wine tasting vocabulary.
