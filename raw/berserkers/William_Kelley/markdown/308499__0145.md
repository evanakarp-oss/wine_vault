The variation in OTR and cork extractibles between batches of cork and individual corks clearly translates into bottle variation in all wines bottled with natural cork closures. However, only when you have the confluence of (a) a more fragile wine that shows that variation particularly flagrantly, such as white Burgundy and unlike e.g. vintage Port; and (b) a style of wine that people have high expectations for with regards to aging, do we recognize a phenomenon such as premox.
