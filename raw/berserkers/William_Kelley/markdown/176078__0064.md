I’m perplexed at what exactly you’re trying to argue here. If I understood you correctly, you posed the question why, if corks declined in quality in the 1990s, were red wines not affected? I replied that, while red wines are much less susceptible to oxidation, and thus don’t premox per se (though I can think of examples, mainly very high pH, overripe Grenache) the red wines of the 1990s are indeed affected by the poor quality of corks and that vintages such as 1996 express that as notable bottle variation.

And yes, I do think that red wines bottled with poor quality corks with a high OTR will oxidize faster and fail to fulfill their potential—irrespective of region and vintage. Is that really such a revelation?
