I think “Napa-esque” is much easier to deploy as a comparison when there aren’t actually any Napa wines on the table… much like (positive) comparisons to Coche, Rayas etc that rarely hold up to direct comparison. 2019 Montrose is very polished, and I prefer the 2020 and 2022, but if you’re going to compare it to Napa it’s going to be something like Togni, not some sort of >30 brix big boy.
