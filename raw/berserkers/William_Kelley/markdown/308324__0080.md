Not only do I drink Bourgogne Rouge, I actually produce some

If you look at my instagram, you will see a lot of things, from old Auxey-Duresses (not from d’Auvenay, no) and Saint-Romain, to Bandol, Chinon, village wines from unheard of producers… Overall I try to focus on older wines as I think that’s an important part of our wine culture that it would be a shame to loose. And yes, there is plenty of sought after, expensive wine, too… Coche, Roumier, and so on. If people have listened when I have talked about wines from less prestigious appellations such as Guffens or Dureuil-Janthial, I suspect it is partly because I am credible when it comes to the historic benchmarks, too.

And along with the bottles, I venture to add, you will find an awful lot of entirely free content, if you want to learn about mildew, or machine harvesting, or this history of appellations and the construction of terroirs, or how wine presses work, and so on.

Anyway, further exchanges will have to wait for another time, I have some tastings to do this morning!
