Certainly one would expect a producer doing everything right not to have any…

No one has funded such a study. And where are you going to get a statistically significant set of 1992 Ramonet or whatever to compare with 1996 Ramonet? Also, corks change over time, you’d have had to measure their OTR wine they were brand new to make a meaningful comparison. The reality is that this issue has impacted small production, very expensive wines, which interests collectors, but big industry / regional bodies much less.

What has been done is that people have measured the OTR of large batches of cork. And the variation can be as much as a factor of 50, as I understand it.

As I said above, changes in cork farming and processing in the late 1980s and 1990s do seem to be significant. But I am not an expert on this subject. I’m going to spend some time in Spain this year, in fact, and see what I can find out.
