> *Replying to :*  
> Allow me to point here what Pierre Citerne (who is now journalist at La Revue du Vin de France) reported for the group after a fantastic verticale in our club in Toulouse : … Malgré cette unicité de Rayas, et la constance de son unicité au fil des millésimes, il nous a semblé qu’une césure…

Interesting reflections (and also illustrative of the greater freedom French wine writers have: if I declared that Emmanuel’s vintages of Rayas are “more Apollonian than Dionysian, but in any case Greco-Roman in character, which may to some extent excuse this tired mythological analogy”, I would find myself in John’s Bingo thread pretty rapidly I think).

I agree with quite a bit of it, though I do think Jacques Reynaud’s Rayas were still pretty unique wines, and I don’t think they would be easy to confuse with Bonneau or Pégaü blind. For me, rather than approximating more to some sort of idealized typology of what Rayas “is”, I just wonder if Emmanuel’s wines might be closer in style to the Louis Reynaud-era wines? But it’s hard to say. I reckon they have a bit more overt “sucrosité” of fruit than those wines did, but of course I never tried Louis Reynaud Rayas when it was young.
