> Pretty much all Macon Rouge is Gamay I believe. I think it is a better source than Beaujolais for both complexity and depth and perhaps shows how important terroir is. Yes, that’s right regarding the grape variety; but I think the differences in style have as much to do with vine genetics as with…

Guillot-Broux - serious wines that need a bit of age for their acid-tannin structure to soften, have had them back the the 1980s

Domaine de Vignes du Maynes - their Manganite gets my vote as the best Mâconnias red, but they are in the “natural” camp so your mileage may vary, especially overseas

Domaine de la Sarazinière

Domaine Sainte Barbe
