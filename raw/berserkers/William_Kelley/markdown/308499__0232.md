Yeah, the question I think no one wants to pose is, do copper treatments play a role in this? And if so, is organic/biodynamic farming, which is much more dependent on copper, actually exacerbating the problem. I do not know of any studies on this. When you look at vintages with high disease pressure and a late treatment cycle vs the opposite, it is also hard to see much of a correlation. Ironically, in such years in addition to copper residue you’re also more likely to have sulfur residue, which would favor reductive fermentations…
