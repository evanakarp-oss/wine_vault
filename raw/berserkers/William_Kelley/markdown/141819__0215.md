> *Replying to :*  
> Clos des Ruchottes owned by Rousseau is the more elegant, mineral, fine-boned wine from the vineyard. It’s been seemingly an under appreciated wine chez Rousseau until the last decade or so. It’s a gorgeous wine, and better farming along with climate change may have added more flesh to the wine…

Agreed, the Rousseau example is the most delicate and ethereal: generally quite red-fruited, with notes of peony and orange, without the new oak that the “big three” chez Rousseau see. It’s often one of Cyrielle’s favorites in the cellar, incidentally. Since the Mugneret parcel is right at the top of the Ruchottes du Bas, I don’t know that the geology is all that different - I think we’re still talking about very shallow soils over the bedrock. But it is definitely a bit fuller and deeper-pitched. The Roumier version is the most overtly structured out of the gates, and also aromatically deeper-pitched than the Rousseau bottling.

FWIW, in my limited experience with the Thomas Bassot version, it was quite a rich wine. But, by luck of the draw, most of my experience with that wine is with sunnier, riper vintages so that may inflect my perceptions.
