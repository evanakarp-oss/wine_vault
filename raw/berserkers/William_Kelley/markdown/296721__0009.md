I don’t really agree with regards to the lieux-dits of Meursault. Many of the hillside sites were not planted in 1943 when the premier cru classification was made, as they had been left fallow after phylloxera. Roulot’s Tesson wasn’t planted until 1959. They certainly would be strong contenders for an upgrade today if the classification were to be revised; and there really is a big stylistic difference between the wines produced by these hillside sites and those from the foot of the slope and the alluvial fans.
