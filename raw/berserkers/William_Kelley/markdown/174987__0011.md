I wouldn’t hesitate to go with 1982 Lynch-Bages. It’s in a great spot today, with an hour decant. While I prefer '89 and '85, it is a superb LB when it has been well stored. One of the problems with the 1982 vintage today is that so many of the wines have travelled so much. But I bought some bottles of this from a cold Alsatian cellar where they have been stored since release, and they have all been very youthful.
