As a general rule, I think the length/detail of the note should be proportional to the interest of the wine. A tedious, anonymous wine doesn’t need 50 words; for some wines, words aren’t really enough. As far as flavor descriptors go, they may be numerous, but, at least in a professional tasting note, should be precise. In the perfume industry, you have to memorize around 1,500 smells which are all very tightly defined, with reference to particular molecules, and while that sort of precision is generally lacking from wine writing, it should be attainable. Of course, flavor descriptors are no substitute for structure/texture descriptors…
