> Good fill into base neck. Quite a bit of color variation, from watery rim to orange to garnet to medium red. A nicely aromatic nose of cigar ash, cocoa, smoke, nuts, wet forest floor, and faint amounts of blood orange and red currants. Also a slight metallic note. One of the first impressions on…

The metallic aroma (and flavor, no?) is likely reduction. This wine really blossoms with a few hours’ decant in fact. I worked through a case of magnums over the last two years, starting with pop and pour and ending up with three hours’ double decant. The last bottle showed the best!

Pape Clement made some seriously good wines back in the day, in both strong (e.g. '61, '64) and weaker vintages (e.g. '52, '81). The 1970 is one of the most red-fruited vintages I have encountered. Typically the fruit tones are quite dark and degraded.
