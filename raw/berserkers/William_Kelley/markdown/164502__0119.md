Very good question!

On the palate, mid-palate amplitude that exceeds the underlying structure, and a sweetness that seems disproportionate to the ripeness of the flavors.

Aromatically, aromas of orange rind/marmelade that veer increasingly towards outright caramel as the proportion increases.

Many (most?) of the best wines I’ve ever tasted were chaptalized, so I am far from a dogmatic opponent of the practice. But, it’s a question of proportions…
