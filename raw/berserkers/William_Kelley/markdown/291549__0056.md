I would venture that the real issue was not so much the raw materials as the use of cork-lined crown caps, which many growers used in this period, and which ended up being a much more oxidative closure for wine sur lattes than regular corks or the crown caps of today would have been. Egly himself told me that he regretted not disgorging all his wines that had seen tirage under cork-lined crown caps, as they simply cannot age for a long time sur lattes with that closure. Since the late 1990s, you have better crown caps; and then, in the late 2000s, you have Egly’s new press—that makes sense of the two step changes you see in the wines and their capacity to age. But they have nothing to do with the winemaking style or the élevage of the vins clairs.
