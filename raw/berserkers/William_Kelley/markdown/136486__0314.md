> *Replying to :*  
> I also spent last night reading this thread from start to finish and I’m still confused on where people are shaking out! With '15s available in bottle for the last ~6 months and the Paulee '15 Grand Tasting behind us, do folks have additional or revised thoughts on the vintage?

A moderately substantial report on 2015s from bottle is forthcoming in The Wine Advocate. I’m writing up notes at the moment. I won’t get into particulars before they’re published, but suffice it to say that I think folks that have purchased 2015 reds judiciously will be very happy. There are some magical wines. But, as Jacques-Frédéric Mugnier puts it, this is a vintage made for people with cellars, and the wines will be better at age twenty than they are at age ten.

There have been a few wines—and before this comment runs away with itself, we’re really talking about a handful—which show a bit stewy and overripe from bottle, despite having been quite seductive from barrel, so working through many of them again has been instructive. But one is more likely to be surprised at how bright the acids are despite the evident ripeness of the fruit.

Happy to respond to questions in so far as I’m able.
