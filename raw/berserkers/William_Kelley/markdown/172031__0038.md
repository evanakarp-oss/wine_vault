Not sure I have too much to add to this.

Champagne is so much less structured than serious white Burgundy (despite the “Montrachet with bubbles” line that writers tend to pull out whenever they like a Champagne—they need to drink more Montrachet!) and seldom as reductive that I am not sure how much opening up decanting can really achieve. I think what often happens is that you loose a bit of gas (not a bad thing) and also warm the wine up very quickly. Since most Champagne is served too cold (I had to start specifying that I didn’t want to taste frosted wines when I visited the grandes marques for the first time), this is very beneficial. Decanting is also something of an acid test for the quality of a Champagne, as if it is bad, when it gets warmer and less gassy, the faults have nowhere to hide. But, it isn’t something I do at home, as I don’t drink very young wines, and I don’t serve them very cold.
