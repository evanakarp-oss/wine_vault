Hi Howard!

Thanks for your comment, and your subscription. I publish Champagne reports at various points throughout the year, as releases occur throughout the year at various times (the opposite of a situation such as Bordeaux En Primeur when an entire region releases its wines at once, and there is an obvious time to publish a report). My colleague Yohan is now doing the same, with the objective, in fact, of being as timely as possible for major releases. Our reports are often entitled “recent and forthcoming releases” precisely because publishing at the right time for producer x’s release means coming a bit after producer y’s, and a bit before producer z’s.

In the case of the Egly-Ouriet 2014, that wine is not yet physically available in the United States and continues to be widely available pre-arrival. I do not believe any Anglophone reviewer has yet reviewed it. So I venture that when my review publishes in about 10 days time, it might still qualify as “timely”.
