Discussion of any technical matters is so taboo that it’s almost not worth asking about acidification. I tasted a couple of samples that did seem a bit atypically tangy for their sites in such a vintage. But when acidification is done well, by definition it doesn’t stand out. I’m sure plenty of the 1982s that we love today were acidified, to say nothing of some of Burgundy’s most expensive wines. Personally, I was disappointed that an article ostensibly about winemaking vs terroir (a false opposition if ever there were one) decided to reduce winemaking to acidification. There was an exchange on twitter about it to that effect: [https://twitter.com/wgfkelley/status/1656158038892457984](https://twitter.com/wgfkelley/status/1656158038892457984)
