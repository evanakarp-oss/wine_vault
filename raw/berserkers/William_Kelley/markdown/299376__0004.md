Thanks, Chet, for this very kind write up! Was a pleasure to meet you, too.

The extremely gentle barrel-by-barrel hand bottling and an élevage that is managed with a view to keeping total sulfites low vs free sulfites (20-25 ppm free with only around 40-45 total) at bottling contribute to the wines hopefully not shutting down too hard.

The Gevrey is from Aux Etelois, from the first 15 or so meters of a parcel that’s right below the Griotte-Chambertin of Fourrier and Roty.

The Beaune 1er Cru is Les Chouacheaux, a lesser-known 1er cru below the Vignes Franches and Clos des Ursules, and next to the Clos Saint-Landry of Bouchard, with David Croix’s Pointe de Tulivans (in AOC Beaune) right below. David and I have both planted fruit trees in our parcels so the neighborhood is looking good!

Oh, and the Aligoté was actually a barrel sample! It will have a full 23-24 months on the lees before bottling.
