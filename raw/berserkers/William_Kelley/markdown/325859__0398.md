So, I did open a bottle of the Hirsch Rosé around NYE to see what the discussion was about! On balance I thought I’d leave everyone to it, given on the one hand that many of you have more Ultramarine experience than me, and on the other, that I participate in enough of the wine world’s heated debates as it is. But, you forced my hand…

What I found was quite a pronounced vegetal reduction on opening. So, I decanted half the bottle and left the rest in the bottle, and followed over 24 hours. In the decanted portion, the reduction dissipated and revealed quite an attractive red berry fruit profile, with a gently herbal/minty edge. Just the effect of opening didn’t seem to be enough, because the part that remained in bottle didn’t move all that much.

My analysis was that time will be kind to this wine, even if it’s not going to number among Ultramarine’s greatest hits; and I would strongly recommend a decant (chill your decanter in advance) if you want to check in on it in the near term.
