> *Replying to :*  
> William Kelley: Probably a good idea to look for '16s when they start hitting the market too. Really strong and quite consistent Côte Chalonnaise vintage. Did the Côte Chalonnaise have the frost issues of the Côte D’Or in 2016?

Generally speaking, the damage was worse in the north than in the south. Rully suffered badly, Mercurey somewhat, and Givry not appreciably. All of the Mercurey and Givry reds I tasted, fifty plus wines over several days, were excellent.

The mildew problems that affected the north were also an issue here, so yields were down across the board.
