It’s steep, east-facing (so quite sunny), with fine soils (colluvium / freeze-thaw fragments) over fractured limestone. My experience with the wines is that they tend to express the sunny side (quite exuberant fruit young) as well as the stony side (not much fat, good underlying cut). Rapet has a big block here and makes a very nice white.
