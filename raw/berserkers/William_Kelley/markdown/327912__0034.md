It depends on the sample… if you take 1980s François Jobard or Matrot, for example, they are pretty chiseled. More chiseled than 1980s Leflaive or Louis Carillon. One can prove pretty much any thesis by picking producers that fit the argument. What is clear is that the grands crus of Puligny make “bigger” wines than all bar one of two vineyards in Meursault.

Communal-level Puligny can often be a bit light because the water table is near the surface and the yields are almost always very generous; but these are not the wines that made Puligny’s reputation.
