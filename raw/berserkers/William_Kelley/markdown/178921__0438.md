An important missing piece of this discussion is the issue of land prices. For example, right now a 0.10 hectare parcel of Chassagne-Montrachet 1er cru is for sale for 1.4 million. From a parcel like that you might produce around 600-700 bottles per year. If this parcel sells at this price, that will be the price at which inheritance tax is assessed - and how much, per hectare, one child would have to pay to buy out his or her siblings’ share on inheriting the domaine. They gray market is a mix of supply & demand & hype, certainly, but what will ultimately drive prices up will be land values; with profound consequences, I might add, for Burgundy as we know it.
