Depends what you define as “old” presses. The Vaslin screw presses of the 70s and 80s broke the cake a lot due to turning with every turn of the screw, with chains inside to facilitate this. The basket presses that were used before that certainly operated more like a static “Champagne” press cycle in a pneumatic press, but to get a good juice yield people crushed before pressing, so got more solids than if you do a static press cycle without crushing in a pneumatic press.

From my perspective, the downside of tumbling and crumbling is more the pH jump you take each time than O2 pickup - as far as white winemaking is concerned, in any case.
