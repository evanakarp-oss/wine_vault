They’re only four & five years apart respective, but I think you see the upward trajectory of Roederer’s farming in the form of greater physiological maturity in the 2012 and 2013 vs the 2008. The 2012 and 2013 both display more plenitude, and while acidities are analytically similar, those acids are “riper” and better integrated. While I’m a huge fan of the 2008, I do think 2012 and 2013 are a shade better, even if the vintages themselves in terms of potential quality were a step behind 2008—and I have drunk them side by side several times. I tend to think JB Lécaillon agrees with me on this, too, though I have never put it to him as a yes or no proposition.

It’s interesting to see that Brad ranks the 2013 so low. To me, it’s absolutely brilliant and is clearly set to age very gracefully. I love it for its crystalline, chiseled style combined with full ripeness.

Also surprised to see people preferring the regular Cristal to Cristal Rosé. Sure, the addition of the red wine does impart some structuring phenolics, but that’s really beside the point, as Cristal Rosé is also, and much more importantly, a selection of just a few of the best plots that go into Cristal. So I think of it as more of a “super Cristal” than as a rosé (indeed, it’s so pale that after 20 years on cork it isn’t easy to tell that it was once a rosé).
