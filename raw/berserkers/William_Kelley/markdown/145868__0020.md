> *Replying to :*  
> I did quite a lot of Brouilly/CdBrouilly 17s in July - and of-course BJ Nouveau and BJV Nouveau last year - but will mainly be tasting 2017s in February so will ask them then…

Ah, well that makes more sense. I did around 650 wines during two weeks in June, with forty or so domaine visits, and there was a lot of talk of hail in many of the crus. Andrew Jefford has some good quotes on the subject here [Beaujolais 2017: Talent under the cosh - Andrew Jefford - Decanter](https://www.decanter.com/wine-news/opinion/jefford-on-monday/beaujolais-2017-vintage-outlook-379376-379376/) . Of course, hail is not destiny: Côte du Py was hit twice yet Foillard made his best Côte du Py for some time. But it is certainly a factor in making what’s otherwise an excellent (and quite 2011-like) vintage more heterogenous.
