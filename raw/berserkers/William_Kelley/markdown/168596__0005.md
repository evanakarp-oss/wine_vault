> *Replying to :*  
> I’m fascinated that we’re going to see more and more Coteaux champenois as the region’s climate shifts. When was the tipping point for increased volume of coteaux starting to be produced?

Haven’t seen any numbers but I would wager that less Coteaux is made today, as a percentage of the region’s production, than was made in the 1950s!

It is an interesting genre that I think is often held back by unsuitable vine genetics for still wine production, poor cooperage choices, and the simple fact that making a still red or white wine is just a totally different from producing vins clairs. I am in the process of writing a bit about it, as context for a vertical I did with Francis Egly back to 1975 (made by his father). In any case, we are just at the beginning. I begin to see people planting interesting massale selections from the Côte de Nuits with a view to still wine production and I’m excited to see where that goes.

If I recall correctly I tried these 2018s from Roederer when they were still undergoing élevage, back in 2019, and thought they were very promising, so I’m looking forward to seeing how these have turned out!
