> John, I remember when Parker gave huge scores to 2002 Burgs from Verget. They were stirring their lees and doing things to make the wines more accessible early. It has always seemed interesting that premox started soon after these scores came out. Are you sure producers did not start doing things…

The potential oxidative impact of battonage is that is de-gases wine that would otherwise be protected by its charge of carbon dioxide. While malolactic fermentation is still going on and the wine’s charge of CO2 is being replenished, battonage actually promotes reduction as the lees scavenge oxygen. So there is a big difference between doing battonage before and after the conclusion of malolactic fermentation.
