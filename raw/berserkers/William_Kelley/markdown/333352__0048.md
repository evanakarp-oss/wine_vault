What I missed in William’s article was any reference to the source of the wine, i.e., vineyard quality.

While the examples given focus on winemaking practices, for me, farming is part of the production process, and so the same rules apply. The differences are primarily the longer time-scale of experiments (the lifespan of a yeast being measured in days, vs decades for a vine) and the notable variability between growing seasons (if winemaking requires adaptation every year, viticulture requires a whole lot more).
