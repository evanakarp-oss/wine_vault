2018 Montagny Truffieres on the market already?? That was one 2018 from Lorenzon I didn’t taste, but his 2018s that I did taste turned out very well indeed, reviews are out in a few days. For me, Lorenzon and Dureuil-Janthial are the two greatest estates in the Chalonnaise. Lorenzon’s style for his white wines is a bit more understated, without the sort of praline / nutmeg patina from Chassin barrels that you find in Vincent Dureuil’s wines, but I admire them just as much. Les Croichots is the most open young, whereas Champs Martin and Clos des Barraults have a bit more depth and cut, but are also more tightly wound. His top cuvée in white is the Pièce 15 which is the most multidimensional and complete.

Too bad about the JM Vincent, perhaps just shut down. It was a very tightly wound, acid-driven vintage for him.

Glad you enjoyed the Guffens!
