The Jacques Carillon wines are stylistically much closer to the Louis Carillon wines of old, unsurprisingly since Jacques made the later vintages of those wines. François’ wines are notably riper, more fruit-forward and gourmand. They show well young. He also works with more sites, right up to Chevalier Montrachet. Hard to see any wine from Puligny winning a white Burgundy QPR contest when there are wines like Vincent Dureuil-Janthial’s 2014 Rullys on the market.
