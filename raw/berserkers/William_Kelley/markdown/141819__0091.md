> *Replying to :*  
> William Kelley: To really measure their quality, it’s not enough to say that they’re very nice wines: one must compare them with the benchmarks in their respective appellations. So while Rousseau Charmes may be a very tasty bottle of Pinot Noir, it would be difficult to claim it’s on a par with,…

While it’s true that the terroirs can be quite different (though parts of Mazoyeres are quite like parts of Charmes), I’m not sure they’re so very far away for the comparison to be entirely otiose at this level; and I suspect that Bachelet’s old vines, cropped at Bachelet’s yields, would nevertheless make a wine very different from Rousseau’s were they growing in Rousseau’s parcel. But, accepting your point for the sake of argument, it would also be interesting to compare Rousseau’s Charmes with Roumier’s. After that, we could compare the Clos de la Roche with Hubert Lignier’s and Dujac’s.

This is not intended to disparage Rousseau, a domaine I love as much as anyone (there are few bottles in my cellar I wouldn’t change for another bottle of even their 1964 village), but just to lobby for a touch more realism when it comes to the quality of Rousseau’s ‘lesser’ grand crus these days, which now all carry very hefty price tags and can legitimately be held to the highest standards.
