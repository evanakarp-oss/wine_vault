In addition to Chassagne reds, we should really talk about Beaune reds more. Over the last two weeks, I’ve had bottles of Bouley’s Reversées and David Croix’s Tulivans that have been utterly delicious. It is an interesting AOC, as it is separated from Pommard and Savigny by two combes, and bisected by two more (cf. e.g. Pommard which is bisected by only one, and shares another with Beaune on its northern border). This means a lot of complexity in terms of geology (alluvial fans from the combes) and nuances in terms of water flow and air flow down from the combes. It’s also simply a very big AOC with lots of variation of altitude and exposition. And then, the climats are very big indeed, to the extent of being almost useless: between the bottom of Les Aigrots and the Clos des Aigrots up slope, for example, there is a world of difference; the same can be said with the bottom and top of Grèves, Teurons, etc. There is an immensely bright future for Beaune, I tend to think!
