To me, that would be crying over spilt milk. I see in Burgundy how different the grapes are in a vintage such as 2022 from what I saw in 2013 (when I didn’t really know very much). You cannot simply make 1928s by making wines like people did in 1928. The question people are asking in Bordeaux, is what do you have to change so that the wines, if not stay the same, retain (or rediscover) a strong link with their historic identity and origin? That is at the heart of the article.

It would be really fun to present some of the contentions I develop in the piece accompanied by a tasting of some wines that exemplify what I write about, as it would make it a lot more tangible I suspect!
