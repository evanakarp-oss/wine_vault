> I had the “normal” Côte de Brouilly a couple of weeks ago, and loved it, though I felt that some of the pure “joy” of Beaujolais has been sucked away as the wines become more serious. Love Thivin. It’s almost as if they can do no wrong, even in the ripe years like 15 and 18 where I may avoid other…

I think 2011 was in the end a bit more along the lines of 2017 and 2019: that’s to say, a sunny vintage that didn’t efface the region’s classic profile.

2016 in style was more in the school of 2014, but rendered uneven by climactic challenges.

The vintages I wish I bought more of were 2010 and 2013 from good producers which are aging superbly and actually often retaining more fruit than riper 2011s. Perhaps that’s because higher acid vintages also stand up better to the region’s often rather crude bottling practices.
