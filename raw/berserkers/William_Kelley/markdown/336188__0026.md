There is a difference between “aging” and suspended animation. “Aging” is commonly used as a proxy for oxidation, so I do not really like the term: I do not mean that, if you adjust your SO2 levels correctly, you can make your wines slowly oxidize even with Diam. Rather, my contention is that with appropriate SO2 levels and Diam you can see the sort of evolution one saw pre-1995 in Burgundy, without any oxidation. If you use Diam and very high levels of SO2, you will see very little evolution for 10+ years.
