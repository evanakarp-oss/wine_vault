To me it makes more sense to distinguish several eras at Dominus:

1. the beginning through late 80s: classically proportioned wines with pretty firm, rustic tannins
1. late-'80s through 1993: more refined wines with more elegant tannins and a touch more alcohol, but still very Bordelais and classically proportioned
1. 1994-1996: an up-tick in ripeness, giving the wines a more broad-shouldered, obviously Californian feel, though they are aging nicely
1. 1997-2007: Boris Champy’s time as winemaker, definitely a continuation of phase (3), and I have been surprised to see the wines losing a bit of freshness and precision as they age, with more of a porty side than they showed when they were younger
1. 2008 to the present: Tod Mostero’s period as winemaker, I find the wines very vibrant and focused even though they are weighing in at the same or higher abvs than in era (4), some of the most exciting in the valley and along with my era (2) the most exciting run of Dominus vintages to my palate.

Would be interested to hear other opinions.
