Bonhomme is pretty typical Viré-Clessé: slightly musky aromas (there is a lot of so-called muscaté selections of Chardonnay, which are aromatic [think hints of Viognier], in this part of the Mâconnais—and elsewhere for that matter), and quite a glossy, textural, muscular profile. A few of the smaller bottlings see some oak, mostly old, but for the most part vinification is in tank here (as for the vast majority of the wines of the AOC).
