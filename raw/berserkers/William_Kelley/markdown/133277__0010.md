This sounds terrific. It was a great vintage. Rousseau’s '64 Gevrey village is still drinking well, though similarly coming towards the end of its plateau. And a Gevrey village from a négoce called Allier was excellent last month too—frankly as good as Rousseau’s. I suspect these older village bottlings often contained wine from less famous 1er crus. After all, how often has anyone seen, say, 1964 Gevrey Cherbaudes or Petite Chapelle?

As for the potential of Auxey… Maison Leroy is not established in Auxey by accident!
