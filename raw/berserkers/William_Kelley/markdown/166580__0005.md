I tend to prefer the 2005 to the 2007, Jeremy!

Raphaël explained to me that in 2005 they worked hard to extract more from the skins, so the wines would have more chalky dry extract to compensate for the year’s below average acidity. With the ripe skins of 2005, that worked very well indeed. They did the same in 2015, by the way.
