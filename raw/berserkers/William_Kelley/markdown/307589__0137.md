Quite a few!

It was a very labor intensive growing season, with lots of disease pressure at various periods, then a heat wave, and picking in warm conditions. As I have high canopies and don’t hedge, I have to treat with a backpack spay, which is obviously a bit less effective than a tractor (but no compaction of the soil); so I ended up doing 16 treatments. On one occasion, I found myself treating at 7pm in between thunder storms so it is times like that that make you appreciate how important it is keeping up the cadence of treatments when you are using organic contact treatments, even with very low doses, and equally how challenging that is to do when the weather is against you. The 2023 vintage also underlined the importance of cluster weight/berry size on yield. With three clusters of 100g in a vineyard planted at 10,000 vines per hectare, you are at 22 hectoliters per hectare; obviously with 150 gram clusters you’re already at 33 hl/ha… And three clusters per vine doesn’t look like much.

- What worked? I was very impressed by how effective talc applications were against sunburn. Due to disease pressure I did some late deleafing in my parcels; but then with the heatwave coming, I applied talc (three times). I ended up with less sunburn than my neighbor who didn’t deleaf! I was also glad to carry on treating quite late, as my vines retained a full canopy for a long time so have put down good reserves for next year.
- What did I get wrong? The timing of my last cultivation came just before a bunch of rain, and I think I liberated a bit too much nitrogen and encouraged botrytis. That in turn necessitated a lot of man hours to control, amputating parts of bunches or dropping affected bunches, with a little spray of sodium bicarbonate on any cut zones. So next year I will be a bit more cautious about that. I was prioritizing the soil management and not thinking about the impact on the fruit enough.

How about the cellar? The main takeaway this year was, as ever, the importance of having balanced fruit and bringing it in at least relatively cool (I picked everything between 6am and 11am, and am thinking of buying head lamps for next year). Being able to change picking dates at the last minute was also really helpful, but to do that you need to have done a lot of work on logistics long before. 2023 was also my first vinification without any sulfites at crush, and I hadn’t appreciated quite how much sulfites can influence extraction. I have never had such even ferments of such seamless tannins. Equally, I have never spent so much money on lab analysis.
