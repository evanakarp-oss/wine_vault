You shouldn’t have worried! I want my tasting notes to be comprehensible, and firmly anchored to actual perceptible characteristics in the wine, and any kind of criticism is both interesting and useful. While I’ve defended my use of the phrase, this thread is also making me think about how I could better communicate what I want to say when I use it.

And yes, that’s pretty much exactly what I mean, translated to the context of a red wine. This is certainly a quality I associate with age-worthy white wines; and I suppose that, much as from time to time I might enjoy drinking a very young bottle of rather tannic Clape Cornas, my real interest is in drinking such red wines at maturity, so it is with white Burgundies that carry high levels of structuring extract. Just as drinking a young Clape can be pleasure tinctured with pain, it’s the same opening a 2014 Domaine d’Auvenay Aligoté today.
