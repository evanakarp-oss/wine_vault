Another deterrent, Trevor, is that if you want to use a lieu dit name on a label, you have to put that into your harvest declaration with the authorities. Given that the regional mentality is geared towards, let’s say, “optimizing” yields, this can cause problems: instead of declaring e.g. 3.5 hectares of Chablis AOC @ 57 hl/ha + whatever extra you’re allowed that year, you have to break it down parcel by parcel in the declaration, and that makes it harder to “cover” an under-producing vineyard with an over-producing vineyard to declare as much production as legally possible.
