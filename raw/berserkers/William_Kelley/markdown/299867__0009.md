I’m talking about evolution in the sense of wines being advanced/heading towards premox.

People like 2014 for its acidity, and I myself like chiseled, low pH white Burgundy. But sometimes in 2014 that comes at the expense of ripe grapes, which means some wines lack a bit of plenitude and texture, even if they have an acid spine. The keys to maintaining low pHs are in farming techniques on the one hand, and pressing on the other, not simply by picking early.
