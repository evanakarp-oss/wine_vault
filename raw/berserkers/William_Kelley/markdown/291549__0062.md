Well, my objections to tasting vins clairs are as follows:

1. one tastes a highly curated selection of possible component of putative blends
1. the wines have only just finished their fermentation and are often not completely dry, which makes it hard to meaningfully assess their structure and balance
1. the final product is so far removed in time, process, and style from the vins clairs that tasting the latter only gives vague indications about the likely profile of the former
1. it takes a lot of time and fatigues the palate

Do you feel that tasting vins clairs has ever told you anything about a wine that you wouldn’t have been able to perceive by tasting finished wines? For me, that’s really the heart of the matter.
