> *Replying to :*  
> AAnd be on the look out for especially the Ruchottes from Bernard Moreau and pretty much anything else from the Moreau ier crus, I completely agree. Tasted at Moreau when I was in Burgundy this summer and all of their wines (down to the Bourgogne Blanc and including the reds) were outstanding, but…

To my palate, Moreau belongs in white Burgundy’s top ten today.

They’re replanting part of their parcel in Ruchottes, so it’s even scarcer than it was before, but the original planting dates back to the 1930s (not so long after Ramonet planted his vines, which was what made the vineyard’s reputation).

People could argue between La Romanée and Ruchottes as Chassagne’s finest terroir, but I would vote for the latter. Romanée is a bit more dramatic, I would say, with more amplitude and sometimes more obvious tang.
