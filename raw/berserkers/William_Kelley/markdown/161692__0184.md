> *Replying to :*  
> Many years back, there was some research suggesting that ambient yeasts accounted for some of the difference between communes in Bordeaux. Do you think there’s anything to that? I realize there’s a lot of commercial yeast everywhere, and individual cellars could have unique yeast cultures, so…

People will go back and forth on the influence of yeast, I think, but I suspect that if each commune did have some sort of distinctive microbiome (built up over centuries of dumping the pomace from successful ferments back on the vineyards, as the old Italian winemakers used to do in California), that would have been lost overnight with the introduction of synthetic fungicides.
