> *Replying to :*  
> William Kelley: Larger crops are harder to ripen: in the '70s, they often didn’t; in the '80s, they mostly didn’t. This is an accepted truism whose truth may be questionable. I spoke recently with a grower who believes dropping too much fruit causes the plant to actually put more into vegetative…

This really depends on the fertility / hydrology of the soils and the favorability of the climate where the vines are growing. On the valley floor in Napa, for example, cropping vines at the sort of yields common in 1940s Bordeaux would indeed emphasize vegetative growth, as it’s more fertile and warmer. In Oakville, vines can be in balance with 5-6 tons per acre. However, in Bordeaux’s more marginal climate it is clear that the vines were in balance before the fertilizer- and clone-driven yield increases of the 1960s and after.
