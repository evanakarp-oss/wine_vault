I tend to find the more interesting category to be what the French call “winemaker’s vintages”, where the best producers, thanks to superior agronomy and technical mastery, deliver genuinely great wines in average to below-average vintages. To me, wines such as 2002 Latour and Monfortino, 2006 whites from d’Auvenay, 2005 whites from Coche, etc etc exemplify this. Without the reassurance that the wine comes from a “great” vintage, wines such as this tend to be underrated by the press and the market, yet they are often just as, or more interesting than the banner years.
