You’re absolutely right that this is a fantastic site.

Potinet-Ampeau have designated it for a long time.

The Clos des Rougets, the walled enclave within Rougeots that abuts the Tesson of Roulot and Pierre Morey, was vinified a few times by Verget and Deux Montilles. My understanding is that it goes into the Bouchard Meursault villages now. A pity.

I believe the etymology derives from the site’s red soils, and that it was once planted with red grapes. I had always assumed that the difference between Rougeots and Chevalières was due to exposition, as Chevalières turns to the north subtly and is more exposed to cooling winds, but when I discussed this with Jean-François Coche, he argued that this was not the case, and that the differences are owing principally to geology.
