For what it’s worth, I’ve had several bottles of the 1961 Beausejour, every time hoping for a great one, and experimenting with a variety of decanting regimes. All came from a cool UK cellar. And all tasted pretty much like the wine you describe. In fact, you were either more tolerant than me, or got luckier, because mine have tended to end up in the kitchen. An older friend tells me they did better in '64 for what that’s worth.

I’m assuming this was the property that’s now Beaujour Bécot. The 1961 Beasejour Duffau Lagarose is terrific and will cruise along for another few decades!
