Ripe, concentrated, good tension, with sometimes a touch of botrytis/sur maturité for people who picked on the late side. JFC related how he picked a week earlier than everyone in Meursault and came in at 13-13.2%, whereas some who picked later hit as high as 17%.

In 1983, the reds were marred by grapevine worms and rot.

But bizarrely, the reds are going to be totally different in style to the whites this year. There was much less shatter in red and thus the clusters are very compact and plump this year. Skins are thin. For bad farmers, we could end up with something a bit like 2011 but more dilute. Whereas the good farmers will be in a very different place, as I said before, maybe a bit like a 1993-2002-2017.
