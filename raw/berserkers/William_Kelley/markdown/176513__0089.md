We will see! There are second-growths that are machine harvested, however, so best practice is less universal than you might imagine. I also think a more nuanced vision of winemaking can get us beyond some of the false oppositions between ripe fruit and hygienic winemaking on the one hand, and classical balance and aging potential on the other. Bordeaux produces about as much wine as the whole of Australia, yet it is for the most part covered by the wine press in 10-15 days per year, focussed on massive tastings in offices in the city. In this respect, a lot of the complaints that people have about the critical coverage of this region are, in my view, inextricable from the way in which the wines are tasted. But honestly, it is a big project to do things differently, so I am all in favor of encouraging only modest expectations!
