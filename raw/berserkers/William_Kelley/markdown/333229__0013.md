Interestingly, a friend who has been extremely successful in the finance/investing world just wrote me with the opposite perspective, suggesting that misdirected hard work tends to have one “bump into things” in a way that laziness doesn’t.

What both of your observations share is an emphasis on the importance of observation: whether that’s not missing the interesting development in the natural process, or not missing the significance of the facts you “bump into” in the midst of misdirected activity.
