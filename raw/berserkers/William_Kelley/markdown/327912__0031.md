I rarely find much utility in these traditional stereotypes of the wines of Burgundy, which are more often repeated than they are revealed in blind tastings; and I certainly don’t think anyone should be shamed for failing to recite them. High altitude hillside sites with shallow soils, in Meursault and Puligny, have more in common with each other than they do with bottom-of-the-slope sites in their own appellation. Very few communes have any sort of uniting geological theme, since they follow the parochial boundaries of villages that are typically situated at the bottom of valleys that mark ruptures in the geology of the Côte d’Or’s limestone escarpment. So I would argue that the *climat* , not the appellation, is the real vector of “terroir” in Burgundy.
