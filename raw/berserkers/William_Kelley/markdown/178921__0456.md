I am not virtuosic enough at math to calculate how long it would take to pay off a vineyard without knowing the selling price per bottle, so I think it must have been someone else.

For most of the 20th century, you could pay off a vineyard by selling three vintages’ production. In the 1940s, you could pay off a parcel of Bonnes-Mares with two crops, Bruno Clair told me. If you wanted to pay off buying some good village level Meursault in three years today, you would have to sell at around 200-250 per bottle. Evidently, there are actually some producers who could do this; and in a sense these are the only producers where the market for the wines is coherent with the market for the real estate, seen this way. Which makes you think.
