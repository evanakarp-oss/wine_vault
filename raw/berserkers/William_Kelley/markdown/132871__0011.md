> Great notes Blake…PYCM Saint Aubin’s are the only white burgs I bought in '15s. Glad they are showing well!

The lower appellations are showing well, but the more serious wines are shutting down hard. The Bâtard, Genevrieres and Corton-Charlemagne are almost completely inscrutable. I followed open bottles (at room temperature) for a week to review, and if anything the wines got tighter. They saw a healthy dose of SO2 but I think they’re also just inherently built for the long haul. I’ll be cellaring my own bottles for an extended period.

Pierre-Yves says the vintage reminds him of 1985, FWIW.
