Beyond the fact that alcohol is in itself a solvent, the two really are entirely unconnected. The wines of the 1920s, for example, were (generally) low in alcohol and extraordinarily extracted.

What’s more, ripe, concentrated grapes liberate tannins and anthocyanins very easily, so working such fruit with very gentle extraction techniques can still deliver wines that are analytically very high in “extract”.

Hopefully my forthcoming essay will clarify some of these things and set them in context.
