Not necessarily, Johan: hotter, sunnier, riper vintages tend to deliver musts higher in sugar and lower in nutrients that take longer to ferment to dryness (one of my barrels of 2022 took 12 months to finish its sugars!). With even rudimentary temperature control you can cool down the must after pressing very easily so ambient temperatures needn’t determine the pace of fermentation.
