> *Replying to :*  
> Domaine de Bellène (Nicolas Potel) uses DIAM. Not trying to take the blame away from producers, but reading US forums really makes me think there’s something going wrong with transportation. A least as much premier or grand crus are opened as here and no way you have the same rate of premox…

Without wishing to open a can of worms, there are a number of Domaines where you wonder if there aren’t ‘export’ cuvées. Because the same wines are oxidised in the UK and US, and sound in France—even when transportation has been impeccable (and it’s less of an issue for the UK market, where premox is nonetheless a major scourge). Perhaps the wines are processed more, maybe they’re made in a different style, or maybe they’re just inferior. And psychologically, I can imagine that, from the growers’ perspective, if one have some good barrels and some inferior barrels, one might be inclined to bottle them separately and send the good ones to Taillevent etc, and the inferior ones off to the states where in all probability they’d be drunk on the young side anyway. Now I’m speculating, of course, but it surprises me people so seldom entertain the possibility.
