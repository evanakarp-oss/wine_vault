Champagne is the only wine that is commonly described as “vinous”, perhaps because with other wines it’s taken for granted. But a “vinous” Champagne is one that isn’t just bubbles and acid, but which has mid-palate texture and depth comparable with what one finds in serious still table wines. Such qualities are more likely to be found in Champagnes produced from ripe grapes and more moderate yields.
