> *Replying to :*  
> Good to know William. The previous wines surprised me greatly as when I first tried them I thought they would be good for a couple of decades.

Yeah, me too, though I drank them too quickly all the same. At one point, I drank a case of the 2010 Butteaux, which was quite steely and incisive despite the muscular, I almost want to say vinous style. I had a bottle with Thomas in 2018 after not tasting it for three or four years, and it was interesting to see that it was fully mature—but in a honeyed, nutty way rather than displaying any overtly oxidative characteristics. In other words, it just seemed to have followed quite a rapid evolutionary trajectory.

The wine to try, just to experience something totally outside the norms of Chablis yet still quintessentially Chablisien, is the 2015 Butteaux “Mise Tardive”, which had three years on the lees (most of that time in tank). I have a few bottles and will be following its evolution with interest, but it is a very striking wine—immensely textural and intense.
