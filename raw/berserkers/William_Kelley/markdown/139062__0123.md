As I recall, the 2010 is certainly more ample and overtly ripe than anything that came before, but that could just be the vintage, and the last time I tasted with the team they didn’t express any intention of changing. I liked the 2010 and actually own some, but it is certainly a bit atypical. But Jean Gautreau is getting older and the winemaking personnel have been changing, so something to watch.

If you see the '88, try a bottle: you won’t be disappointed.
