> In Bordeaux now, they have had rain and hail over the past week. Some vineyards have lost 30-50% of their fruit. 2022 is not looking too good. Sad. This is a very incorrect reading of the situation. First, most vineyards the board is interested in have suffered little damage or loss of crops. Next,…

Thanks for being the voice of reason, Jeff!

Hail has obviously taken some producers in the northern Médoc (north of Saint-Esptèphe for the most part), southern Médoc and vicinity of Fronsac out of the equation, but overall thus far for those that have been spared we are looking at an above average yield, good sanitary conditions and now, thanks to these rains, plenty of water to carry us through the end of the season. A lot will depend on the next ten days and when/if it’s possible to get treatments done in the vines, but 2022  so far has the makings of a high quality vintage in Bordeaux, as in Burgundy and Champagne. Everything is still to play for!
