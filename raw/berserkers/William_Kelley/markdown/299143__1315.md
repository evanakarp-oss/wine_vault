I think this is really the heart of why Carmes gets people so excited: it has a very strong identity. With a terroir that combines a warm mesoclimate in the city’s outskirts with clay-limestone soils that preserve a low pH; very good farming (no herbicides here!); high percentages of Cabernet Franc; vilification with significant percentages of whole bunches; long infusions and long élevages in very well-chosen vessels - all of this combines to deliver a wine with an extremely pronounced personality that’s unlike anything else made in Bordeaux today. That such a wine can emerge as one of the most sought-after of the vintage only underlines how much the market has changed in the last fifteen years.
