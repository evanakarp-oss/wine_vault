Interesting question. I wouldn’t be inclined to macerate Rhône white varieties, but why not crush and then do a static press without crumbling, selecting press fractions by pH? When you don’t have meaningful acidity, it seems to me it’s futile to try to preserve it with whole cluster pressing and blocked malo. Better to go after refreshing bitterness from the skins to “refresh” the wines and temper their richness, and fully browned juice to avoid exaggerated varietal character. Crushing more certainly seems to work this way in ripe Burgundy vintages along the lines of 2005, 2015. Whereas the region that crushes the least is of course Champagne, where it’s about avoiding extracting vegetal characteristics from marginally ripe fruit rather than about preserving low pH.
