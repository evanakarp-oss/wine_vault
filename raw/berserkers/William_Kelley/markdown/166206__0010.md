> *Replying to :*  
> Thanks for the clarification, Alan/William. We get the Les Sept Vignes label in our market. The straight Brouilly is labeled Reverdon, which, coincidently, I’m drinking the 2018 right now. A little lighter, more red-fruited, and straight-forward than the Cote de Brouilly Les Sept Vignes. It’s nice,…

All the Brouilly is labeled Reverdon, that’s the name of the cuvée. Alan has been talking about the Côte de Brouilly, even if he referred to it as the “regular Brouilly” on one occasion. In fairness to him, The Wine Advocate’s database did not include Côte de Brouilly as an appellation when I took over out Beaujolais coverage, despite it dating back to the 1930s—contemporaneous with the Brouilly AOC.

You get the Sept Vignes because you are not in the USA where Kermit Lynch is the exclusive importer and brings in the cuvée discussed in this thread.
