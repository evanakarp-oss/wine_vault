> *Replying to :*  
> The 2000 Rayas I had earlier this year was the best Grenache I have ever tasted. So Rayas. I do have a soft spot for Outpost’s Grenache, though. This is positively interesting, because the 2000 Rayas has long and often being called weak (e.g. by RMP) which I don´t agree with; it is very bright in…

I think it went through a sort of exaggeratedly tertiary, almost oxidative, phase around age 10-15, before bouncing back. It now tastes younger than it did a decade ago. The 2001 did something similar. Not sure what was going on or why, but I have found more recent vintages to be developing in a less idiosyncratic fashion.
