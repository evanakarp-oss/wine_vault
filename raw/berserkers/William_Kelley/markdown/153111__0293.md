> *Replying to :*  
> William, can you give us an idea what will be different under the new ownership?

You’ll understand that I can’t go into too much detail, we will be looking to make the website more intuitive and useful, with better visibility for our content, as well exploring some of the synergies between Michelin’s expertise in food and our own in wine. There have been articles in Forbes and elsewhere reporting on the press conference we had in NYC the other day which might give some more insights. We just had two days of meetings getting to know Michelin, and they went very well. Clearly, the stability that comes with being part of a big company will guarantee continuing editorial independence, and there have also already been some administrative changes behind the scenes that make the reviewers’ lives easier. So as I said, we are all feeling very optimistic for the future!

Thanks to everyone for the kind words, as ever.
