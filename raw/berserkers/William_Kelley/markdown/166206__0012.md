A little lighter, more red-fruited, and straight-forward than the Cote de Brouilly Les Sept Vignes. It’s nice,…

Côte de Brouilly = higher altitude, on the slopes of an extinct volcano, lots of volcanic “blue stone” as they call it.

Brouilly = lower altitude, more heterogenous geologically, but often with quite a bit of clay.

The generalization is that the former makes more serious, age-worthy wines and the latter more fruit-driven wines, but quite a bit of that is likely due to how people choose to make them.
