> *Replying to :*  
> I enjoy both Roty and Bachelet, the few that I have opened over the years…they do seem to be at the opposite ends of the spectrum, the Bachelet more elegant and the Roty more powerful

I wouldn’t go that far! Both are suppler now than they used to be, with that most noticeable chez Roty, and that has brought them closer together structurally than was probably the case two decades ago. But both come from the same part of Charmes (they’re neighbors in one parcel), from very old vines, and the two families are friends also. (They are also neighbors in the Que de Hareng in Brochon). And they are both pretty broad-shouldered, dramatic wines, even if the Roty is a bit more muscular vs the more suave Bachelet.

But for opposite ends of the spectrum, I’d say something like Camus vs Roty.
