> *Replying to :*  
> On the Bouley Pommard Fremiers, this is the location: Weinlagen . I don’t have the bottling, only his Pommard Rugiens. Fremiers seems to be right next to Volnay 1er cru les Fremiets: Weinlagen

I think people tend to fixate on the Rugiens, because it’s such a well known site. In fact, Thomas is in Rugiens Hauts, where the soils are white and calcareous (right above one of Clerget’s two parcels at the northern extreme of the climat). And it is a rather ethereal, fine-boned wine, not at all the sort of massive, rich, muscular stereotype of Rugiens that one finds realized in e.g. old bottles from Pothier-Rieusset, M Gaunoux etc. Chez Bouley, the Fremiers is the more complete and complex wine, though they are both great bottlings. It took me a few visits to understand this myself. We’re planning a vertical of Fremiers in the Spring so it will be getting a bit more publicity in the near term…
