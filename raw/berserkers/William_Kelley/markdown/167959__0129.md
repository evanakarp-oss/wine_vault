> One more thing about that picture… I see a lot of berry variation. Many berries are lightly colored and translucent. Some are actually raisined. I bet that is another reason their Burgs are more complex than our Pinots. We may have an over-obsession in the USA with uniform ripeness in our fruit and…

There is a small percentage of Pinot Gris planted in RC, as in the other vineyards of DRC (this is quite common, especially in Vosne-Romanée and Chambolle-Musigny). I think the paler berries you are seeing are Pinot Gris. DRC’s massale selections, like most “Pinot Fin” selections, tend to go through veraison quickly and quite uniformly. Sorting is then very rigorous.
