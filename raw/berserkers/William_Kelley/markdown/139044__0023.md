Ric has really excelled this decade, and the 2011 won’t disappoint. I actually marginally prefer it to his '12. The '10, '13 and '14 are also all worthy of pursuit. I haven’t been such a fan of his bottlings from the mid-2000s, when it seems to me as if he was pushing the envelope more in the cellar and playing around with more elevated levels of ripeness, before finding (or perhaps returning to) a nice equilibrium from 2009 or so on. To me, vintages such 2008, 2007, 2005 and 2004 don’t have the harmony or the savory nuance of what came later, or what came before; 06 and 03 being partial exceptions. Of course, the vintages 1990s were very strong and have generally aged well.
