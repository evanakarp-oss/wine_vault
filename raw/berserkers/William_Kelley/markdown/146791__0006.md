> *Replying to :*  
> had 08 Butteaux last night. A disappointment in the context of the 10 and 04. A bit dilute and lacking vibrant acidity, though smelled nice and had decent fruit. Not Chablis in character, more generic.

Yeah, it’s odd that the 2008 Raveneau wines are comparatively (and I stress comparatively) weak in the context of what is a great Chablis vintage—in the sense that I would prefer the domaine’s 2009s or 2012s (though it to some extent depends on the climat). I suspect it is because the domaine’s style is dependent on achieving dry extract from mature skins, and there wasn’t much skin maturity in 2008. I’m sure analytically the pH of the 08 Butteaux is lower or at least as low as the 10 or the 04, but the wine has less liveliness and character, I agree.

The surprising thing about the 2010 vintage in Chablis in general is how much more precocious it is than anyone anticipated. I expected to be drinking 11s and 09s while waiting for 10s, but the 10s are much more open than I would have believed they might be five years ago. In some cases I think there’a a bit of botrytis at play.
