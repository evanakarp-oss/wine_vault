> That said, anyone have an idea on why Gilman’s scores for recent Haut Bailly are so mediocre? He’s not flaming them a la Pavie (or Pape Clement), but he’s not really showing the love either. His is a palate I generally trust, so his so-so scores give me pause.

Bill:

Another interesting question. Hopefully John will chime in. If I may speculate in the meantime, I’d venture that Haut Bailly’s comparatively slighter frame means that it shows its new wood a bit more obviously during its elevage than sturdier-structured wines such as Montrose that receive similar percentages of new cooperage.

From bottle the wines have not seemed unduly woody to me, even just after bottling, so those additional months in barrel after the EP tastings seem to do the trick—at least for my palate.
