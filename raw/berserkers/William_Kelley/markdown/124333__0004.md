This is just a long-standing corruption, now accepted, deriving from the correct adjectival use in “varietal bottling”. I imagine some folks mistakenly imagined it was the varietal that was being bottled, and not the bottling that was varietal, and thus began using ‘varietal’ as a noun. The OED’s earliest citation for thus usage is 1955 so this has been going on some time, and it is probably a little late in the day to get pedantic about it, though I know some English grammar martinets who do, and it’s an expression I’d personally eschew.
