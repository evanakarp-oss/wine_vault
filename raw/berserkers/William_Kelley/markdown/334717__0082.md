It doesn’t help that the French don’t use “amers” (bitters) the same way we would in English. Most of my talking about wine (as opposed to writing) occurs in French, and I have noticed myself using English words in a French sense, erroneously, on several occasions.

As we are talking about white wines, however, where seed extraction isn’t an issue, by your definition most of this thread should have been talking about astringency as opposed to bitterness.

But I do still maintain that, given that the distinction in this thread is between wines with the desired characteristics and those without them, and that those characteristics are mostly attributable to the degree of skin extraction during pressing, that, *pace* Otto, what we actually call those characteristics represents a tangent more than the crux of the matter.
