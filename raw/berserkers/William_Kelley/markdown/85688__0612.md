The problem is not unique to Bordeaux. If you have clone 777 Pinot Noir on 161-49 rootstock in Burgundy in a ripe site, for example, it was difficult to produce a wine with less than 14% alcohol in a vintage such as 2022 if you wanted to attain physiological maturity. Virus free massale selections and less precocious rootstocks are likely the solution, but that entails replanting… so you factor in replacing the trellising (materials and labor) at around let’s say 30,000 EUR per hectare, 50,000 EUR for the new plants, the cost of at least three years out of production and ideally longer… and you have quite an unattractive proposition if your existing middle aged vineyard is producing wines that people are happily buying.
