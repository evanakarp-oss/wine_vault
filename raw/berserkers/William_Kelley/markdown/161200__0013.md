> There’s a clone/selection known as “828” originally from Oregon, and now in CA, that is an upright Pinot. This is not the same clone as ENTAV 828. I don’t think of the Pommard selection as upright… but others will surely comment.

I was being a little provocative, because the clusters themselves look exactly the same in terms of fatter berries and more compact clusters, even if the shoots aren’t necessarily upright (and I think that, in practice, the term is used quite loosely in Burgundy). But that would be true of a lot of vine material that has made it out to the USA, e.g.: [Foundation Plant Services](https://fps.ucdavis.edu/fgrdetails.cfm?varietyid=1184)

It would be very interesting if producers reading this would post their own photos of clusters of clonal Pinot Noir.
