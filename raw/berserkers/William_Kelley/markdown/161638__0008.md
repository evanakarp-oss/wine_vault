> *Replying to :*  
> Interesting, the '17 was rather rich and I felt quite ripe, yet Dale mentions a slightly under ripe blackberry note in the '18.

I couldn’t speak as to this particular wine, but many 2017 CdN reds were chaptalized—which, combined with below average acidities, certainly contributes to their up-front fleshy charm and texture. In 2018, natural alcohols were almost universally quite a bit higher. Given the nature of Burgundy, there must surely be some 2017 which is an exception to the rule and attained higher natural degrees than its 2018 counterpart, but I certainly didn’t hear of it yet! White Burgundy, given the yields, is another story.
