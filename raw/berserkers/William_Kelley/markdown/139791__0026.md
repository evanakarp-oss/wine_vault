> *Replying to :*  
> Well, it´s a bit more complicated: technically it´s a regional appellation - and not a Village appellation, because one can blend wines from the Northern Villages (Brochon+Fixin) with Southern Villages (Premeaux, Comblachien, Corgoloin) - and most negociants do so. But practically a Cotes du Nuits…

Not technically: the INAO officially classify Côte de Nuits Villages as an ‘appellation communale’ even though it comes from multiple communes. See Pitiot (p. 353 of the 15th ed. of *Les Vins de Bourgogne* ). It’s an arcane point, however.
