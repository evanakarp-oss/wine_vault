> *Replying to :*  
> Had a 1982 Gruaud Larose last night. They should just try to get back to making wines that taste like that. It was spectacular.

I agree that the 1982, along with the 1986 and a number of other wines from the heyday of the Cordier era, is a terrific bottle! I have quite a few in my cellar. However, one cannot simply turn back the clock, as today’s climate is very different (flowering is getting underway in the Médoc as I type) and the conditions to make those wines are not really present. It must also be said that the Cordier wines were full of brettanomyces to an extent that can only be to some extent pardoned by virtue of their immensely characterful style, and the fact that it was a different era for which one must make allowances. In the context of Gruaud Larose back to the 1920s, the period of 1970-1990 or so actually stands out as somewhat atypical, as the wines are much more rustic than the top vintages of Gruaud in the 20s-60s. I tend to think that contemporary Gruaud will have more in common with those old reference points than it will with either the Cordier heydays, or the Cordier hangover / early post-Cordier period of rather lean, weedy wines. And I’m fine with that!
