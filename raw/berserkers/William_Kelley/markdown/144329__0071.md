Yeah, they are actually comparatively recent releases. Both showing well, 2010 a bit more mature.

In general you have to remember that Irancy is a sheltered amphitheater of hills, and actually tends to make quite ripe, powerful wines despite its latitude. It captures the sun and heat, and on top of that the local culture favors relatively brawny styles, hence the traditional presence of César in the blend. Wines for Gauls, if you will…

If you’re looking for ethereality and perfume north of Dijon better to go to Epineuil than Irancy. Or Irancy in years such as 2021…
