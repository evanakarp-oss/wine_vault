> That’s really interesting William. I didn’t taste 2013 reds from barrel but what I tasted from bottle on release wasn’t my favorite and I made only limited purchases. Your post has me inclined to revisit the wines. I’m not buying much of 2015 either, but that’s for the opposite reason - the wines…

I would say that it’s definitely worth looking at your favorite producers, and if you can try a bottle before loading up, so much the better. Unless the market balks at 2015 pricing and the wines end up getting discounted (which I don’t see happening), 2013 might conceivably be the last relatively ‘cheap’ red Burgundy vintage, in the way that 2008 was in Bordeaux.

As far as 2015s are concerned, fortunately there are plenty of lower-appellation wines which are quite exciting and not too expensive which will be hitting the market soon: Volnay and Pommard villages from Glantenay and Voillot, Beaune 1er crus from Drouhin and Jadot, Auxey reds from H & G Lafouge, Maranges 1er Crus Reds from Bachelet-Monnot, Marsannays from Audoin, to name just a few that come to mind.
