The big houses’ domaine offerings have to be priced in line with the cost of purchased fruit/must/wine. If Corton-Charlemagne must, for example, sells for 40k per barrel/300 bottles, that makes for a base cost of 133 EUR per bottle. If a négociant who is buying fruit across the board happens to make a domaine CC, it’s going to cause problems if they sell for below the value of the fruit/must on the market. They can’t sell their CC for a price that’s below what they sell their négociant Puligny Folatières purchased for 30k per barrel for, for example. This is why the big houses have all taken big steps up in the last few years. It isn’t doing wonders for their distribution, but they don’t have much alternative beyond stopping buying outright.
