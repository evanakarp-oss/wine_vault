> *Replying to :*  
> No, just the regular, undesignated [Côte de!] Brouilly Actually, it’s more or less the same cuvée: Kermit imports an unfiltered version of the wine that is sold as “Sept Vignes” in other markets, under the label “Côte de Brouilly” with no qualifiers. Good info! This is indeed the Kermit bottling,…

Simply that the Sept Vignes is filtered and for sold in the domaine’s other markets, whereas the Kermit version is unfiltered and doesn’t say “Sept Vignes”. When I visit, they tend to show the Kermit version for review. I would say that the Sept Vignes is a bit aromatically purer but a bit less rich and textural, but these are fine distinctions.
