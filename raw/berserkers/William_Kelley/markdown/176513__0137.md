> there were some notable absences from domaines who have featured in the past (Liger Belair, Roumier, Grivot, Georges Noellat) which raised the question whether it was due to logistical issues or some other reason Roumier had Covid, and Noëllat want to taste from bottle, which suits me very well…

It’s a direct question, so I will answer candidly. I was very impressed by the wines when I was a student a decade ago, and I even wanted to work the harvest with Louis-Michel one year (the birth of my first child intervened). Since then, I confess I’ve fallen out of love with the wines. The combination of Séguin Moreau/Plantagenet barrel mocha and very minimal extraction make for wines that are very flashy young, but I do wonder how well they will age. Interestingly, I actually tend to think some of Louis-Michel’s earlier vintages, e.g. 2006 (his 2006 Echézeaux is brilliant!) have aged better than some more recent years (e.g. 2012, 2013). But in short, though there is no question that the (enviable) vineyards are very well farmed and that no expense has been spared in terms of materiel, and that in terms of winemaking they are, in a sense, flawless, they are not wines that do very much for me these days.
