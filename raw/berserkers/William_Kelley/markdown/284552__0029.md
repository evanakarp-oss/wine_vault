No surprise, indeed, but I find 2015s a bit soft. Many also stand out as evolving quite rapidly in the context of verticals of recent vintages. For me, there are so many places in the world that do a very good job making rich, sweet-fruited, accessible red wines from Bordeaux grape varieties that I look for something more than that from Bordeaux itself. So years such as 2015 and 2018 are not vintages I gravitate towards, whereas 2016 and 2019 are much more to my taste.

However, to return to the topic at hand, what we can agree on, I think, is that categorizing 2008 as an “off vintage” in a list including 2007 and 2013 isn’t fair to the quality of the 2008s, which was the point I was making.
