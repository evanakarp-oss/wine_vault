The days of avoiding must oxidation at Lafon are long gone! Yes, they don’t crush; but nor has e.g. Roulot, who does, been immune to problems. And I’ve had advanced and premoxed bottles from the new “old school” producers you cite, albeit few. The hypothetical of whether today’s wines would age like yesterday’s wines were they to be bottled with yesterday’s practices and yesterday’s corks is also hard to pursue, because the way cork is farmed and selected has changed, along with the growth patterns of the forests… In short, the problem is so multifactorial that the silver bullet of a more hermetic closure is very welcome, even if it isn’t an excuse to stop thinking hard about questions regarding ripeness, crushing, pressing, lees contact and the details of élevage *for aesthetic reasons* .
