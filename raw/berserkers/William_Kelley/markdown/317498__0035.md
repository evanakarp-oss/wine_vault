Duboeuf these days is more thermovinification than carbo in the strict sense.

What’s more 1962 is before Jadot purchased the Ch. de Jaques and introduced a more “Côte d’Orien” winemaking style with destemming, long macerations and new oak. Over the last decade, Ch de Jacques has pulled back from the extremes, making more supple wines with a much more discreet élevage signature, something for which we can thank Cyril Chirouze who is now making the wines at Clos Rougeard (as of 2023 vintage).
