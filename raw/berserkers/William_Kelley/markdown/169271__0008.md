> I’m not sure there’s a world in which I would pay over $200 for current vintage Lambrays no matter the yield when back vintages can be found for not much more. Especially at the 2019 pricing YEESH.

The shift is indeed aggressive but it’s worth saying that they are making real improvements, with the aspiration to brining the quality up to the level it once routinely attained (ie 1971 and before, and especially the interwar years). In the last couple of decades, the deal was you could buy something called a grand cru, for the price of a premier cru, that tasted like a village wine.
