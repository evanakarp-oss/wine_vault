Not Alan, but rather a response from my side: you’re quite right to pay attention to the notes because there’s such stylistic diversity in contemporary Champagne! This particular wine comes from a small walled vineyard behind the Doyard estate in Vertus, planted with 60-year-old vines that are cordon-pruned (which makes for smaller berries and lower total yields too). It’s picked ripe, and vinified in oak. So I would say it is unavoidably quite a concentrated, muscular, vinous Champagne. But, it is also quite structured, sees minimal dosage (2.4 g for the disgorgement of 2014 that I tasted), is produced from just the core of the coeur du cuvée (the lowest pH juice), and was disgorged recently (2020); so it’s a wine that’s quite tightly wound out of the gates. Right now, I think it’s as the wine warms in the glass and gets a bit closer to room temperature that all its texture and concentration become most apparent.
