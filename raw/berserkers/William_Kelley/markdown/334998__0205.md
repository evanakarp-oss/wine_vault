Hi Thomas,

I think already we are trying to be consistent within Bordeaux over the last decade, without getting into comparing Bordeaux and the Canaries! The scores reflect that we think this is overall the weakest vintage since 2013; though it is quite a bit better than 2013, and of course there are some estates who have, against the odds, made wines that are likely to turn out to match other more favorable vintages. People of course tend to reduce a report to either favorable or negative (you will see above some criticism of us for being too favorable on 2021, for example), but the reality if you look wine by wine, and set the 2024 reviews in the context of the last decade of scores, is that it is quite a bit more nuanced than that.

As for bracketed scores, that has always been the practice at TWA, and the brackets may expand or contract to reflect our level of confidence. Between rather fragile samples this year that could change a lot from day to day, even at the estate, and changeable weather during some of the tastings which never helps wines show their best, there were some instances where we gave a broader bracket. But this is the case ever year, and was the practice of my predecessors back to RP, so nothing new. I do not think false precision for precision’s sake is in anyone’s interest during EP.
