In its own terms, that makes sense; but 1) surely when consumers look at a vintage chart they are looking for guidance on vintaged wines and 2) how do you assess the quality of the vintage other than by tasting finished wines from that vintage?

When I started reviewing Champagne, there were a number of people who encouraged me to taste vast amounts of vins clairs, but I almost immediately came to see that this was a pointless exercise and at this stage only do it out of politeness. The idea that you can judge the quality of a vintage by tasting a few hundred base wines at Roederer, Krug and one or two growers is a joke. As the old English saying goes, the proof of the pudding is in the eating, the 2013 vintage being a great case in point.
