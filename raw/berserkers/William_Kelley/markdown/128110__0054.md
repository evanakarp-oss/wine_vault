Greatest recent triumph was identifying a 1996 Cotat Cuvée Speciale as such, which is quite an eclectic wine. But I have drunk quite a bit of Cotat over the years.

Without naming a certain prolific Master of Wine, I will relate that I once listened to him rhapsodizing about the merits of Les Clos and the distinctiveness of its terroir in Vincent Dauvissat’s cellar. Vincent proceeded to serve a bottle blind, from 1996. The MW was sure it was Les Clos, and the rhapsodies continued—until Vincent revealed the wine as his Petit Chablis. That was a ‘teachable moment’.

Most abject recent humiliation was calling a crisp-styled Provencal rosé as Marsannay rosé. I don’t drink a lot of the former and tasting in a cellar in Burgundy that seemed the likeliest possibility, but it was an embarrassing error nonetheless.

I enjoy blind tasting the most when I know enough of the parameters (e.g. the producer but not the vineyard or vintage) to know what to look for. That removes the bias of the label without removing the framework. Without a framework of some kind one has to be very disciplined or very lucky.
