The second scenario also factors in animal traction for working the soil, and you could replace the tractor for treatments with a chenillard for about the same. And the yield went down, so price per bottle of all the more or less fixed costs (picking for example, and manual work per vine) goes up. I also took the cork cost up to 2 EUR a pop to have the best of the best of the best.

So you see why it is tempting to make a big crop…

And why people working with Grenache vines at 3,300 vines per hectare in a climate where they only need to do three treatments a year and don’t need to regularly cultivate the soils (and can do so with a normal agricultural tractor so the don’t need such expensive labor) do not have comparable production costs!
