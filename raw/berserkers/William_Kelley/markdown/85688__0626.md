Esca is a fungal disease, no a viral infection, so its prevention is more to do with grafting and above all pruning decisions. Each pruning cut creates a cone of dead tissue inside the vine corresponding to the size of the wound; so if you want to avoid having lots of dead wood inside your vine, which can in turn open the door to esca, you need to place your pruning cuts in such a way as to leave the vine with unimpeded sap flows through to its extremities. Some pruning systems are more adapted to this than others. Unfortunately, pruning like this demands a bit of reflection and isn’t really compatible with contract labor that changes every year and watches the clock.
