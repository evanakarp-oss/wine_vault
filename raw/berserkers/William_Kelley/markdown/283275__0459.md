Interesting question, and one we ponder a lot. When does a fault become a flaw, and how much should faults and flaws be penalized on a numerical scale?

Haut Brion 1990, for example, contains around 6,000 ppm of volatile phenols (the aromatic signature of brettanomyces). The threshold of perception is supposedly around 300 ppm, though in reality a lot lower if you are trained. How should one score the 1990 Haut Brion?

The reality is that the 2020 Monte Bello remains a lot more pleasurable to drink than most wines rated 84/100…
