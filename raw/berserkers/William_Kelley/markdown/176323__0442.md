Again, we are speculating, which I prefer not to do, especially in public, but equally I understand people want to see the reasoning behind the opinion, so… With amphorae, depending on the porosity, you can easily either have a rather oxidative vessel or a rather reductive vessel. If your amphorae are oxidative in their tendency, your wines will evolve rapidly. If you then try to correct that but moving to less porous amphorae, you might tend to have reduction problems—which you would need to mitigate by heavily settling the wine before putting it in the amphorae, and then with rackings with aeration; which could in turn cause other problems. So really you are reinventing the wheel and hoping you will get something that rolls.
