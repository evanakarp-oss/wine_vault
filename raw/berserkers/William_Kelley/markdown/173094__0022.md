I drank a 2006 La Mission last night and ended up buying a case of magnums. It must be the richest, most powerful left bank 2006, but not to a fault despite the rather high abv. The aromatics are really beginning to open up age 15, with lots of blackberry and blackcurrant fruit mingled with the smoke, cigar and truffle aromas that one is looking for in this estate’s wines. There’s still plenty of tannin there, and while I don’t think it will be as long-lived as the 2005, I think a lot of people would guess it as 2005 in a blind tasting. Certainly, the price difference between the 2006 and 2005 shouldn’t be as big as it is.
