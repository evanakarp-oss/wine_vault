To me, it was definitely richer, flatter and higher pH (3.9 vs 3.7 on a logarithmic scale) than the 2019. Not sure if that is just a question of having a lot of Merlot in a vintage where harvest date was critical (and Cos certainly are picking a bit earlier than they used to), or uneasiness with the stylistic swing towards fresher, more elegant, less syrupy/creamy wines.

When I write notes like these I simply try to communicate what I would if one of you on this forum asked me what I thought. It is too easy to get sucked into worrying “what will the château think?” and I tend to think that self-censorship is one of the banes of contemporary wine criticism.
