> *Replying to :*  
> The Jayer-Gilles Eschezeaux has been reliably excellent over the years. The winery and vineyards were purchased in 2017 by Andre Hoffman, and the wines are now produced by a new team under the name Hoffman-Jayer. Mr. Jayer Gilles passed away a couple of years ago, sorry to say. If anyone has…

I’ve tasted 2017s and 2018s. The style hasn’t really changed: 100% destemmed, picked ripe, 100% new oak, very long élevage. The wines are quite saturated in hue, with plenty of rich extract, but do digest the oak—even the Hautes-Côtes bottlings, which I’d recommend if you like the style—with time in barrel and bottle.
