It is not about being “an apologist” for prices, Donald, it’s about pointing out that the issues are structural and that producers are as much victims as culprits. As I point out in my introduction to my 2021/2020 report, ultimately this is at root a political problem, and that we will witness the undoing of the land ownership changes effected by the Revolution of 1789 over the coming decades. Sure, producers can make a lot of cash now if they choose to, but as soon as the vineyards change hands between generations, a much more powerful logic will assert itself. Producers who sell their wines cheaply, from this point of view, are essentially shorting their children’s inheritance.
