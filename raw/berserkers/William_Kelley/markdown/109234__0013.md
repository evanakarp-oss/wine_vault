> *Replying to :*  
> Neal.Mollen: … If a bottle looks otherwise sound, would a low shoulder fill stop you? A Bordeaux with a low shoulder fill DOESN´T LOOK SOUND! The odds that it will still deliver kind of positive drinking pleasure are very low … I´ve had a lot of mid, upper-mid shoulder levels that were definitely…

I find this a little surprising. I’ve had some great bottles from the '50s and '60s with levels like this, most spectacularly a 1959 Mouton back in 2010, but also humbler wines—Chasse Spleen '62, for example.

The rule I’ve heard it that if the ullage is uniform within a case / cellar, then there is no cause for concern. If, on the other hand, the bottle has a much more extreme ullage than other bottles in the case / cellar, then it is an indicator that something may be wrong. For bottles that have just been sitting quietly in the same place for the last five decades, I wouldn’t write it off—just take it as a sign to drink up. On the other hand, if the bottle has been traded a lot then maybe it’s more alarming.
