This is an interesting question. I’ve actually had very good luck with old Verget, including 1992, 1995 and 1996 Enseignères, 1995 Valmur and 1995 Purozots, all aging gracefully this year. But I accept that they had a lot of problems, and that many people were badly burned. I’ve spoken to Jean-Marie about it, and his contention is that the problem is caused above all by fatiguing the wine with excessive élevage in wood, especially new wood. Of course, how much is too much depends on the wine in question.

As for Guffens-Heynen, their 1997s, 1999s and 2000s are all drinking very well right now.
