Absolutely.

I think one reason this debate gets so heated at times is that we are dealing with two separate but interrelated issues as if they were one. The first is the issue of oxidation, which can be solved by screwcaps and DIAM. The second is the issue of stylistic change, both because of winemaking and climate, which surely contributed to the oxidation issue, but also really changed the profile of the wines themselves. Anyone who is nostalgic for the glories of white Burgundy of yesteryear has to be interested in the second issue, which is in many respects more complicated, as it is not just a question of problem and solution.
