> *Replying to :*  
> For anyone who has read enough of Clive’s stuff, or who has been at an event where he is there in person, he is completely hierarchical. Gamay is the lowest rung on the ladder for him. It can never be better than that.

I am afraid that is the truth of it.

Though as a more general point (which is not intended to be exculpatory), it is quite cognitively demanding to simultaneously maintain a respect for the sense and empirical evidence behind the hierarchical classification of sites in Burgundy, an awareness of the system’s flaws, and a willingness to acknowledge when wines simply transcend it.
