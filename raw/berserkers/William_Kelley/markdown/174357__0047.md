Ah, well, I think it’s difficult to generalize about wine making techniques, which vary from estate to estate, with such precision. One can talk about trends over a decade or two, but to pinpoint a shift in a single year, well, that’s tricky. And sure enough, there are some 2001s that are still very tannic today.

I think you’re right, though, that contemporary wine criticism is pretty tannin averse. That is amplified when wines are tasted in large tastings where tannin accumulation exaggerates the perception of how tannic wines are.
