> William Kelley: YacobovE: I also spent last night reading this thread from start to finish and I’m still confused on where people are shaking out! With '15s available in bottle for the last ~6 months and the Paulee '15 Grand Tasting behind us, do folks have additional or revised thoughts on the…

In short, no—adding that producer style will be just as important, as e.g. the Georges Noëllat 2015s will drink much earlier than e.g. Mugneret-Gibourg’s, irrespective of appellation.

But I should add the caveat that we haven’t yet seen the full extent of how much the 2015s will shut down. There is an argument that the '15s won’t shut down as hard as the '05s, as the quality of the phenolic material is superior. But on the other hand, it’s more abundant. There is just so much substance there that it’s hard not to imagine that it will assert itself at some point.

Happily, we have what looks to be a superabundance of supple, open-knit and succulent 2017s waiting in the wings - they’ve already mostly completed malolactic fermentation - that seem set to be delicious in their youth.
