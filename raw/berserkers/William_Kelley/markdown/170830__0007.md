For me, Beaux Bruns is the most open and youthfully demonstrative (which, for Barthod, means drink age 20 rather than age 30). Combottes is also a comparatively fleshy, giving wine, from a warm site, but not to the extent of Beaux Bruns. Baudes is a baby Bonnes-Mares, with deep fruit and in recent vintages plenty of ripe tannin. Charmes is a bit ampler and more enveloping, a very complete wine. Cras and Fuées are both superb: Cras a bit chalkier in tannic profile, Fuées more seamless young. Often I prefer Véroilles to anything, however - a special site, a few steps away from the d’Auvenay Bonnes-Mares (another of my favorite wines in contemporary Burgundy).

These wines have been great for a long time, and it seems the market is finally realizing that you don’t have to have Amoureuses, Bonnes-Mares or Musigny to make some of the best wines in Chambolle-Musigny and indeed all of Burgundy. They are, however, slow burns; and I would rather open e.g. a 2000 Beaux Bruns today than a 2010 Cras, even if the Cras is the “better” wine of the two.
