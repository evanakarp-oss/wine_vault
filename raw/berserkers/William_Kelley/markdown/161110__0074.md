> *Replying to :*  
> William, Jasper, or anyone else that has info - I noticed that Arnoux-Lachaux as well as a couple others have talked about no-till viticulture. The last few years have been all about ploughing, especially by horse, and doing so to oxygenate the soil and promote good nitrogen levels as well. Is…

Yeah, this is a very new thing for the most part in Burgundy (though Guffens has been doing only a single, shallow cultivation followed by period mowing for some time in the Mâconnais). The argument is that deep ploughing kills soil microflora and obviously eliminates any kind of year-round cover crop, impeding accumulation of organic matter in the soil. This has negative consequences for both soil nutrition and structure. Cultivating the soil also makes for more available nitrogen, which is not always a good thing. We will see how it goes for Charles Lachaux this year, it’s certainly interesting.
