Ah, well we agree entirely - so I think you missed my point (perfumers don’t talk about molecules either, unless they’re talking formulae). My point is that the important wine aromas and aroma groups do in many cases have an objective chemical basis, and thus that it is possible to talk about them with the same precision that perfumers talk about smells. That doesn’t imply any need to cite molecules; just some discipline and rigor about what smells of what.

That is not currently the case for most of the wine industry. And if your singed alder is my burnt rubber, for example, it is very difficult for us to talk about what we are experiencing.
