I hope you’ll get that soon, for Bordeaux at least, in the lengthy free-to-read essay I’m about to publish on TWA about everything that has changed in Bordeaux.

I think what we have is, in the context of a warming climate, rootstocks and clones chosen for more rapid ripening combined with aesthetic objectives that dictate picking later. That’s a knot that takes some time to untie, but how well the wine ages will also have to do with the approach taken to élevage. Choices that tend to favor precise, more reductive results will be more successful at integrating higher alcohols. Anyway, it’s a big subject.
