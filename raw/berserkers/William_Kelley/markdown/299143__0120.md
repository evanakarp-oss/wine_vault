Ah, I see!

It is true that some old timers in Bordeaux are comparing '22 en primeur with ‘82, I think largely because of the wines’ rare combination of high pH and voluptuous textures with (at their best) fresh fruit flavors. But so much has changed in the interim, and the seasons are so different, that I think it can only be considered the vaguest of correspondences.

I think the press has also tended to be trigger happy with 1982 comparisons over the years because every wine critic wishes they could repeat RP’s success with the 1982 vintage. And of course, it was a seminal vintage for the region, ushering in a new era of prosperity after some hard times, so the Bordeaux ecosystem also views it with some nostalgia. To say nothing of the consumer perspective you so rightly adumbrate.
