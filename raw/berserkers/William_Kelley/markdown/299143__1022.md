Indeed, it isn’t scientific at all and one can’t even begin to “predict” pH until you’re actually sampling fruit in the few days before harvest. One can simply say that e.g. it rained last week so the vines will have taken up more potassium so the pH will rise; or that it has been very hot at night for the last month so the pHs will be high, or whatever. And then you can be positively surprised if you’re at pH 3.7 when you feared you’d be at pH 3.9…
