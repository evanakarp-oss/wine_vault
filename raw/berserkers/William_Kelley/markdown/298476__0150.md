For me the interest of inoculation in Bordeaux derives from a variety of factors. High pH musts (with the exception of the areas with clay-limestone soils), nutrient-poor musts in drought years, comparatively large fermentation vats and comparatively large wineries which are logistically challenging to manage all play into it. I’m not familiar with the Italian wines you site, I confess, but it is clear that brett is an increasing issue in a number of French wine regions, and brett issues in barrel often have their origins during vinification.
