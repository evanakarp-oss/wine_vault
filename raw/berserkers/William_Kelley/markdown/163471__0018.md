> *Replying to :*  
> while we are drilling down. Can you comment on the red vs white soil of Bonnes Mares? In terms of what we are seeing? My understanding is Roumier is mainly White. Do we see red? and while we have a deep talk, can you comment on the profile imparted by red soil vs white?

Roumier has a pretty even mix of both these days (this has evolved over the years), and there is a photo above from each of his two most important parcels… I do think the white soils with higher percentages of active limestone seem to make a more delicate, fragrant wine (d’Auvenay being the archetype thereof), but honestly, when you see the differences in viticultural practices within what is a very small area at the end of the day, it would be naive to start casually attributing differences in wine character and quality to soil types. Not that soil types don’t matter, but one can only isolate them when all things are equal—and they manifestly aren’t!
