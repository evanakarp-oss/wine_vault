While sometimes why red wines shut down can be rather mysterious, the combination most likely to shut down in bottle is low pH, high levels of tannin, and a high redox potential. High pH red wines shut down less as higher pH makes tannin less perceptible / seem suppler.

It is true that higher pH wines are less microbially stable, so they require more vigilance during maturation and at bottling. But that is a very different subject from whether or not they shut down in bottle.

Hope that clarifies matters!
