> *Replying to :*  
> The initial reviews will be light too. It’s not a vintage that any one is going to hang their hat on and proclaim something outlandish.

I think it’s also a vintage where you’ll be able to see the difference between critics’ palates and tasting approaches quite clearly. In such a heterogenous vintage, with such a distinctive style, there is very little place for consensus and it’s also very hard to “hedge”, even with the luxury of scoring in a range. Certainly, in my reviews I tried to take clear positions and give scores that very explicitly differentiate in order of preference. So we will see how the wines turn out in bottle! I will also be taking a second look at some of them later this year to follow the evolution.
