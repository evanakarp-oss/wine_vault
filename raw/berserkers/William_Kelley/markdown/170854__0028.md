> William, Do you know how many bottles are produced in 2017? Etienne de Koning I will ask when I visit the day after tomorrow! And William, How was the visit? Did you taste the line-up from 2017? I am very curious for the 2017 vintage. Thanks, Etienne

250 bottles were produced in 2017! It’s the finest, best balanced rendition to date and I think it should age especially well.

The 2017 portfolio is superb: a bit fleshier and more outgoing than 2016, but not quite as rich and muscular as 2015—a great balance.

There is also some exciting news chez Cédric that I’ll be reporting on soon…

PS - We also did a vertical of Ursules from magnum, which tended to confirm my contention that, rather than “not evolving”, these Champagnes simply evolve like still wines: that’s to say, gaining depth, aromatic range and sapid nuance, but without picking up the roasted/toasted/caramelized aromas that come from Maillard-like reactions and the breakdown of sugar. 2006 in magnum right now is incredibly demonstrative and very hard indeed to resist (so hard to resist that I have opened one tonight which I’m sipping as I write this).
