Yeah, I think there were two hurdles to cross: getting through the mildew with a functional canopy and crop intact; and then getting the grapes ripe. Of course sorting was essential, but my perception tasting was that no amount of sorting, even if you were eliminating less ripe fruit via densimetric sorting, could bring you the mid-palate you could only get through picking ripe. And so, while I can entirely understand why some thought there was nothing more to be gained and brought in what they had on the vines, our analysis was that waiting was ultimately the better strategy.
