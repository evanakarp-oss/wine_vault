Dominated by élevage = when a wine is dominated on the nose or palate by exogenous aromas imparted during its maturation. Most obviously, that would be wines dominated by vanillin/lactones/char aromas from new oak, with coarse wood tannins on the finish; but it could also be the taste of old wood (chestnut-like aromas in red, sourdough starter-like aromas in white), or the VA you’re liable to get from excessively porous amphorae, or whatever. The goal of élevage is to clarify, stabilize, refine, and resolve a wine’s redox equilibrium before bottling, but a less successful élevage can detract, sometimes fatally, from a wine’s purity of expression.
