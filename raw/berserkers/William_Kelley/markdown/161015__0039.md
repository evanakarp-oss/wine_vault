> *Replying to :*  
> The interesting question that remains for me in all of this is how the wines age under Diam (or screw cap, for that matter). Being free of TCA or Premox is huge, but the experience we’re all seeking is the 15 year old white burg that has aged into the astounding thing that didn’t exist at 2-3 years…

The 2011 Guffens-Heynen wines were bottled under DIAM 30 and they are aging magnificently, and taking on the sort of patina we look for in maturing white Burgundy. I had the 2011 Pouilly-Fuissé Tri des Hauts des Vignes from my cellar last month and it was just stunning, dominating a 2013 Ramonet Bienvenues.
