> *Replying to :*  
> William Kelley: When you get within different vineyards, how much of the difference is less which of the vineyards generally is better and in which of the vineyards does the single producer have the better exposure and the older vines?

And their vine genetics & rootstocks, when they harvest, how they vinify. The variables are almost infinite. Clearly, one would rather own an excellent old vine Gevrey villages than a Camus Chambertin, but in this case we’re dealing with finer distinctions.

I’m not sure I have an opinion (yet), but I was interested to hear Damien’s observation—which I think is borne out, at least, by his wines.

En Montceau, in any case, is an interesting spot: the lower portion of Les Champlots, just above the hamlet of Gamay and at the same elevation as the upper portion of En Remilly, turning more towards the east. Not sure where Colin’s vines are within it.
