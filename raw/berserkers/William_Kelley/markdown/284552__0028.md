> It seems to be cheating a bit to point to Le Pin, of all wines, as a vintage marker for 2008, particularly when Parker said, “2008 is an exceptionally strong vintage in Pomerol and St.-Emilion as well as Graves,” a comment he made after he had the benefit of having tasted both the 2009s and 2010s…

I wasn’t being disingenuous, it’s just the 2008 that I’ve most recent opened and drunk. It is also not a wine that evolves very slowly, so to comment on it being youthful is a bit more meaningful than saying e.g. “the 2008 Latour is still very youthful”.

I agree with RP that right bank and graves were especially strong in 2008, but the Médoc produced some real successes too. I suspect many, e.g. Montrose, will ultimately live up to RP’s often more optimistic barrel tasting notes rather than his more conservative from bottle ratings.
