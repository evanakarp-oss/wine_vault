> *Replying to :*  
> John, I remember when Parker gave huge scores to 2002 Burgs from Verget. They were stirring their lees and doing things to make the wines more accessible early. It has always seemed interesting that premox started soon after these scores came out. Are you sure producers did not start doing things…

The 2002 scores were from Pierre Rovani, in fact - and some of the wines are aging very well. The Guffens-Heynen 2002s I’ve had are all impeccable, and the 2002 Verget Puligny Enseignères was still tangy and youthful from a bottle this year. Bob covered these wines through the early-1990s, but not after.

Guffens himself thinks that wines being fatigued by excessive time in new wood, plus poor closures, is the cause of premox - which may well be true of his wines. Certainly, now that he has backed off from new wood for Verget and uses DIAM and screwcap, I haven’t heard of or experienced, any problems.
