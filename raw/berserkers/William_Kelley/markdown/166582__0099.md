> *Replying to :*  
> So while the market isn’t going to slide backward to affordable burgundy anytime soon, it is probably safe to say that most burgs won’t increase in price 200-300 percent in the next 5 years. (Save a few outliers who are probably just catching up to their kin)

I wouldn’t count on that. In particular, I think the top reds of the Côte de Beaune are likely to close some of the gap with the Côte de Nuits. Lafarge prices on the secondary market, for example, have gone up around 30% this year and that’s just the beginning, as the Asian market takes more interest in these wines.
