> *Replying to :*  
> So a new run will start and one of the last fairly priced top Burgundy will be 3 figures soon. Fine.

I do take your point, and that’s the downside of wine criticism for informed consumers as we have discussed before. But equally, how much did you buy when they were very cheap and widely available? We all had 26 years and counting to stock up, and one could buy 2014s, 2015s direct from the importer two years ago. If you have a big stash, your foresight should soften the blow; if you didn’t, can you really complain?

The fact that the market is waking up to how good these wines are sends a great message to the next generation in the Côte Chalonnaise, and can only be a good thing for the region. Vincent Dureuil has 17 hectares: Rully has 357 hectares in village and premier cru AOCs… so there is a lot of potential for anyone who wants to do the work.
