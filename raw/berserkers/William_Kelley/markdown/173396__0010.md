> *Replying to :*  
> I rarely drink California Chenin Blanc, so a Chenin Blanc from Clarksburg really has no true reference point for me. That said, I’m also not sure I would have pegged this as a California wine blind, more along the lines of a Baudry Chenin Blanc from a riper Loire vintage, say 2018. I do not know…

Thanks for the note! Now, I know enough about marketing to know that I shouldn’t say it’s the “best to date” until we sell out of the remains of the '18 and the 2019, but we are certainly very happy with it. To me, the 2020 has the complete maturity of 2019 but with a bit more cut. It’s also a bit more expressive young. It was a pretty easygoing vintage, unlike 2019 when there was quite a bit of disease pressure, and we made 400 cases. I think we’ll release it 18 months from now so it has a bit of time on cork to open up.

The 2021 incidentally, is close to dry, three weeks after pressing (we like quite long ferments). We made a few changes to the press program, which I’m very pleased with, as in terms of chemistry we have the maturity of 2019 and 2020 but the acid of 2017, with more dry extract from the skins. We also went to barrel with more lees and will extend élevage by six more months. So I am quite excited to see what it will bring. It’s a drought vintage, but in Clarksburg that has simply brought more concentration, because we are, as the wine project’s name implies, “down by the river”…
