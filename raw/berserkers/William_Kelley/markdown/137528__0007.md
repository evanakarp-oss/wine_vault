> *Replying to :*  
> I always call Roty the Dunn of Burgundy, backwards wines. He was a heavy smoker and his wines are dense and have plenty of oak, probably reflecting his palate sensitivity. He died a few years ago of throat cancer, son now making the wines, and I have not tasted any of the newer wines.

Joseph died in 2008, and sadly his son Philippe followed him (also of cancer) just after the 2015 harvest. Philippe’s brother, Pierre-Jean, is now making the wines.

My understanding is that Philippe had already taken over the reins in the cellar by the mid-1990s, so you will likely have tasted his wines. Oak (and often quite toasty oak) is certainly part of the Domaine style, but to my palate the wines have enough substance, thanks to extremely old vines and low yields, to integrate it. With bottle age, I would rank the domaine’s three grand crus among the best wines produced in Gevrey.
