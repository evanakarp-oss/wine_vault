A few years ago, I was able to drink this quite frequently, as it was well represented in the cellar of my college. It represents the end of an era, as it was one of the last, I think the last, vintage at Léoville Barton which saw no new oak. The 1985 is cut from a rather different cloth (though I actually rather prefer it).

Since you enjoyed this, you might look to the Langoa 1982. I recollect Anthony Barton remarking that he was inclined to prefer it to its grander sibling in that vintage.
