> I believe Pierrières translates to stones. Don’t know what Perrières means in English.

“P** i **errières” = stony ground. Olivier Collin’s Pierrières, for example, comes from a place where there are a lot of flints embedded in the chalk. But you do not see this name very often.

But that’s nothing to do with Perrières, meaning quarry.
