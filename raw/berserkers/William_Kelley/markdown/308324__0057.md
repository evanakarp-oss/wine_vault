Very amusing, Donald, but the fact remains that this thread began with you deliberating whether to charge a 3x mark up or a 7x mark up on the ex-domaine price (assuming you have a direct allocation and aren’t a grey market flipper, which wouldn’t surprise me), and then lamenting the price of Burgundy.

You can criticize me as much as you like, and that may even be salutary; but I confess it does genuinely irritate me when you attack producers like Dauvissat who continue to honor their covenant with their historic clientele, in the face of rampant flipping and speculation, and frankly at the expense of their own best interests.

Small artisanal producers are the thin red line between us and a world of homogenized, soulless wine, where a handful of conglomerates produce a huge but illusory diversity of labels, much as you can see in the case of contemporary Scotch whisky. You are going after the wrong people here. However, perhaps given the economic situation in Germany we can forgive you.
