This was all explained in TWA, if you happen to subscribe!
> After experimenting with various ways of designating his various terroirs—including the use of foudre numbers—Bouland has now [from the 2018 vintage onwards] alighted upon a much clearer solution: he simply distinguishes between “Cailloux” (stones) and “Sable” (sand) on the label, as well as differentiating between “Vieilles Vignes” and regular bottlings. Readers should take a moment to ensure that the bottling they are purchasing corresponds in both particulars with the accompanying reviews.
