> Just had Charles Heidsieck brut reserve today and thought it was DP-lite.

I think as with the Cliquot Extra Old this is a case of a wine shaped by the nutty patina derived from reserve wines rather than the reduction that defines Dom Pérignon. But it is interesting how both phenomena can take a wine into a sort of nutty, toasty, smoky register even though the origins are quite distinct - indeed, not far from inverse.
