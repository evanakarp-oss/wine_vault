To get into the more general question, clearly modern agronomy combined with the economic viability of extreme selection where necessary have done much to mitigate adverse vintage variation. The ideal that an estate such as e.g. Château Lafleur or Domaine Leroy aspires to is variation in style but not in quality. Between producers who have the capacity and willingness to work this way—and the support of the market to do it (because it’s very expensive)—and the rest, there is a gap that opens up in very challenging vintages. This makes generalizing about a vintage a bit like advising someone who is about to cross a river that it is three feet deep on average. Your mileage may vary!
