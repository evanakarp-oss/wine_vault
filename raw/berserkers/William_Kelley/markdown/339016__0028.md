That is a singularly unnuanced and ahistorical vision of contemporary Bordeaux. I can understand the impulse to look back with longing to a “golden age”, and most generations of Bordeaux drinkers have done this: Saintsbury was nostalgic for prephyloxera wines, just as Clive Coates was nostalgic for pre-1945 Claret, while some on this forum pine for the wines of the 1980s. But the idea that the 1970s was a golden age really underlines the fact that tastes are ultimately, above all, acquired.
