> *Replying to :*  
> They also made a Marc de Bourgogne from the Ruchottes which I think had quite a good reputation. I hugely enjoy the vigorous internal scrub performed by a good Marc de Bourgogne but they are all pretty much the same-as Fine de Bourgogne they can spend a longer time in barrel and become mellower but…

Oh, I wouldn’t agree with that! Though I would have done, once. It just needs to be distilled carefully and age in barrel a long time. If you ever see old ones from Domaine de la Folie, they are especially good, an old-timer having taken to distilling as a therapeutic activity after the traumas of WWI, but never having sold much, the result being that the marcs saw extremely long aging in wood. And, there is indeed a miraculous Leroy version!
