The style change was firmly in evidence by the early 2010s.

Their Narvaux comes from some very nice domaine holdings that make concentrated fruit, and 2017 is quite a ripe vintage, for me something of a modern-day 1992.

The old style, you should remember, was very toasty oak and quite fat, exotic wines. Today, the fruit tones are firmly orchard/citrus, and the oak is much less toasty, a touch of vanilla pod and pastry cream; and the wines have a lot more tension.
