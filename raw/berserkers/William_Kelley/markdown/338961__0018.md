The earlier malolactic thesis was the one I advanced when I reviewed 2022s, and I think it holds true. In some vintages Christophe was bottling only a month after the end of (late) malo. So the wine was going to bottle having only just completed an important microbiological transformation, and having been freshly sulfited. I think that tended to make such vintages show especially primary and structured. Wines that have gone through an earlier malolactic have seen more “real” élevage and taste more resolved and integrated.
