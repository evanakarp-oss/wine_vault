I reported what I was told… but it must be said, the narrative isn’t always consistent. One thing you learn in Burgundy is that, “everything is the same” can mean that everything is literally the same, or that e.g. the wines are all Chardonnay and all fermented in barrel but other than that handled quite differently, or simply that the wines are made with the same general aesthetic ideal in mind.

What I can say is that while the sites are different the two ranges, always presented intermingled together, do taste like one quite consistent range. And what I do know is that, according to Bernard last year, Pierre does all the tractor work.
