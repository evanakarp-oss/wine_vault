I have never liked the 2018 vintage in Bordeaux, and the only wine I own from that year is Grand Village… so I am not about to die in defensee of that particular hill. In 2017, the new leadership took over and they only influenced the blends (before, anything under 14% had been systematically declassified into the second wine). Decompacting clay soils, refining agronomy etc take time, so I think this estate just gets better and better. It requires real vision, however, as the terroir can so easily make the monster truck of Bordeaux that producing something suave and refined takes a lot of thought and intelligence.
