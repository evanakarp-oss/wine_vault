The differences are quite dramatic, and I’m not clear what one would modify in each case to make the wines ‘better’.

That said, I think she has a point, it’s just a bad example.

I do taste quite a lot of wines from the USA and also from Burgundy where each different vineyard designate bottling feels less complete than the sum of the parts might be. And sometimes that’s borne out by impromptu blends.

Drouhin’s practice of blending their Chambolle 1ers except for Les Amoureuses and their Vosne 1ers except for Petit Monts, for example, makes sense, especially given the nature of their holdings / purchases.

And a heretical suggestion: if Fourrier blended all his 1er crus except for CSJ, how great would the ensuing blend be?
