Hi Bruce!

We had less rain than forecast today: 10mm vs the predicted 25mm. Since then, the sky has opened and a nice north wind has established itself. What’s more, the forecasted rain events on 5-6 Sept have now evaporated. All of this takes us away from a sort of 1994 scenario (lots of rain on ripe grapes) and in a more interesting direction: maybe something like 1983 or 2001 in white, and a sort of 1993-2002-2017 hybrid in red. Clearly, the heat wave we just endured will have changed the profile of the wines; I could see that happening in real time, tasting the grapes. But now we’re back to cooler weather, and if it stays dryish it could really be very exciting. The grapes are fragile, after water, heat and then water again; and PN is more fragile than Chardonnay at this stage. Seeds are already much more brown than they were at this stage last year, yet this year people will likely pick a bit later. PYCM starts on Monday I think, Olivier Lamy on Sept 2; Lalou picked d’Auvenay Criots and Chevalier yesterday, afraid of heavy rain and hail. I’m likely going to pick my barrel of old vine Meursault on 3 or 4 Sept, and am agonizing about my Beaune 1er Cru parcel. So, it’s the sort of year where tasting a lot of grapes and having a flexible picking team is going to make a big difference!
