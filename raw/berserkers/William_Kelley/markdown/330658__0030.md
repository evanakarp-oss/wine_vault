Aligoté is an interesting variety. It is very fruitful, so works best when pruned short (when it was the major white variety of the Côte de Nuits, it was goblet pruned for this reason). As others have noted, there is a wide range of genetics, from fat-berried clones than never go golden, to small-berried selections that go fully golden-pink at maturity, much like Roussanne for example. The skins are quite thick and phenolic, so it can easily be bitter/herbaceous if picked underripe (high yields generally mitigate this). It is almost impossible to pick over-ripe, as it retains acidity remarkably, and alcoholic degrees, even when it is cropped at 15-25 hl/ha and picked late, rarely exceed 12.5%. The pH values will typically be a good 0.10-0.25 lower than Chardonnay in a given vintage, e.g. if your Chardonnay is at 3.20 your Aligoté will be at 3.05 in a vintage such a 2022.
