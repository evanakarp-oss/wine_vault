Just some names that come to mind, rather than any attempt at a comprehensive list :

Leroy / d’Auvenay Dugat-Py Claude Dugat Thomas Bouley J-M Vincent Dureuil-Janthial Bruno Lorenzon Charles Lachaux Guffens-Heynen Vincent Dancer Bizot

But I am still just really beginning my study of this subject… As I think I said earlier in this thread, I’m convinced that a proper emphasis on viticulture is the missing link in contemporary wine discourse between talk about “terroir” and the details of winemaking. Looking at a cadastral map of holdings, all one would learn is that Darviot-Perrin is “next to Coche”. But evidently there is more to it than that.
