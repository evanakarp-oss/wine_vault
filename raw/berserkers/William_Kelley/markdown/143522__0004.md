A very nice appreciation of this wine.

I’m afraid that it’s too late for any return to this kind of Bordeaux. The capital invested in old vines, long élevage and late release is considerable. Plus the interested constituency is small. Without intending to sound unduly pessimistic or nostalgic, the culture that created wines such Bel Air Marquis d’Aligre no longer exists.

As far as precedence goes, I wrote about BAMdA in Noble Rot last February, and as it happens served Neal the 1970 he mentions in his article! Since I only owned two bottles and the second was corked, I’m glad I picked the good bottle, as his piece truly does justice to the estate.

But the French wine press have been writing about BAMdA for quite a while now, so it has been hiding in plain sight.
