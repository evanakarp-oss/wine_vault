To give you a more serious answer, this wine is a powerful, tight-knit (and expensive) expression of white Hermitage carrying a considerable overlay of toasty new oak. If you are looking for a classic expression of white Hermitage, this is not it, as the strong wood imprint marks it out very much a contemporary Guigal wine. But if you like the flavors and textures that barrels impart, you will love it. If you want a more traditional white Hermitage, you should seek out Chave’s (which is very textural and unctuous these days) or Marc Sorrel’s.
