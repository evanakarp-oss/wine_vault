> William- thank you for the Cristal comments. I have not had the 2012 yet. Is your comparative specific to Cristal or based on broader experience? Just curious as we get into 2012 season with the big guns. I love 2008s and 2004s, but I am not a fan of 2002s. I certainly concede their greatness, but…

I was specifically referring to Cristal, where I think you taste the progress Roederer made in the vineyards between 2008 and 2012 (and which hasn’t abated since).

To generalize about Champagne as a whole, it’s interesting to talk about what could go wrong in both the vintages we are discussing. One could say that when 2008 isn’t brilliant, it’s because the grapes were a touch underripe, resulting in wines that are rather structurally lean and which were often generously dosed: 1996 had the same potential failing to a great extent. When 2012 isn’t successful, it’s because of wines that can be a bit overbearingly rich and heavy, especially when dosage wasn’t adjusted to the quite ripe, low yielding vintage. To me, it all comes down to farming though: if your viticulture produced grapes that were a touch underripe in 2008, it likely also produced grapes that lacked a bit of freshness in 2012 (especially if over-dosed). Whereas, by contrast, good farmers and winemakers made very good wines in both 2008 and 2012. I think we will see the same phenomenon in the 2018, 2019 and 2020 vintages in due course: rather than pardoning viticultural sins by allowing even bad growers to pick ripe grapes, climate change is actually throwing the difference between the best and the rest into heightened relief.

As for the style of 2012, if you do have a very strong preference for '08 and '04 over '02, it’s conceivable that you might want to emphasize 2013 over 2012 in your purchases.
