I think it’s not quite so easy to say it’s just winemaking… old vine Loire Chenin is definitely inherently more phenolic than much Burgundy Chardonnay, so if you’re looking for wines with some structural grip then, irrespective of practices, the odds are weighted in your favor. Of course, that assumes that all things equal, and of course they very seldom are (a lot of Chenin is badly farmed, badly pressed, and left with RS). But I do think it makes sense you would like Guiberteau.
