> *Replying to :*  
> Andrew L.: Personally, I wouldn’t touch a '66 with a low shoulder fill. This. I don’t think 1966 except maybe someone said Cheval Blanc has the oomph to withstand that much oxygen. This is based mostly on a few bottles of 1966 Leoville Las Cases which needs vts to be even decent.

1966 is a strong Médoc vintage, but Las Cases were not making particularly interesting wines back then in my experience (limited to the '61, so not by any means conclusive). Recently a '66 Haut Batailley was really surprisingly good, probably the best I’ve ever had along with their 2010.

The vintage was high acid and high tannin so built to last; some of them are still a bit mean and lean, but the serious growths are certainly not going to go into precipitous decline any time soon.
