It’s a great site, and has produced some great wines over the years, especially when it was known as La Gaffelière à Naudes: I have a few magnums from the '50s and '60s that are among the more treasured bottles in my cellar. There was a period when inferior vineyards on the plain were included in the blend, but that is no longer the case, and the last few vintages have been very interesting. Derenoncourt consults, and as elsewhere in his portfolio (e.g. Domaine de Chevalier), the stylistic choices have tended towards the precise and away from the creamy/lactic/oaky of late, with great results. The style is certainly more restrained than, for instance, Le Dome, which featured on your list above.
