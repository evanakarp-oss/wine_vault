3cm is a correct level for a 30-year-old wine, and from a cold cellar the 1990 Beaucastel is fully mature and drinking well. It’s a softer, more open-knit wine than e.g. the 1989, and from a warm cellar or if it has traveled a lot, it might be on the gentle downslope. If it’s the bottle listed on the site I think you’re talking about, in so far as one can tell anything from the photos, it looks good.

From the same site, the 1990 Les Cailloux from André Brunel is drinking well - it’s a bit more muscular and alcoholic than the 1990 Beaucastel, but also a bit more vigorous at this stage. I would also consider the 1990 Guigal Hermitage, which is very good indeed.
