When do you think most Volnay’s approach their optimum drinking window and could you comment on the 2017 vintage specifically. Hope to meet at…

Thanks!

I would say it’s very hard to generalize, as this has more to do with how the wines are grown and made, and their vintage, than their zip code! As you saw last night, 2017s for example are already very approachable, with a charming, succulent, supple style that they all tend to share, but at the same time plenty of space for distinctions of site and producer to show through, too. In fifteen years, I think they’ll be just as delicious, without every shutting down in the interim. By contrast, the 2015 Clos des Ducs we had (inter alia) over dinner was rather closed. I tend to prefer to drink my Burgundy in general age 20+ than age 10, personally, and I think for good producers in recent vintages that won’t be a problem. I have Lafarge '76 Volnay village in my cellar that’s still full of fruit today!
