Really hard to put numbers to many of these things, unfortunately. Of the Côte d’Or’s vineyards taken as a whole, 31% were farmed organically as of 2022. But that is just one metric, and there are very good producers who are not certified organic (as they might use phosphonates in order to reduce doses of copper, or simply not want to pay for certification and go through the audit process), just as there are some pretty bad farmers who are nonetheless certified organic.

I would say that overall, the Burgundians ended up being pretty forward-looking with regards to vine genetics when compared with many other wine regions (and despite incentives: there is no government subsidy, for example, for replanting if you don’t use clones), and that a lot of great Pinot genetics have been preserved.
