> *Replying to :*  
> DSC04517.jpg A Pic I took ablut 15 years ago. One of the most remarkable things about about the property is how much of it is just woods, not vineyard land. It gives it quite a pastoral, removed-from-the-world feel.

I think this is indeed one of the key insights into understanding the singular style of Rayas. When you drive around CdP, there is very little woodland today, and plenty of vineyards which were clearly created by ripping up woodland/scrub and restructuring the soils mechanically (any obviously mechanically crushed, jagged limestone is an indicator of this). By removing so much woodland, an already warm AOC became warmer - plus a lot of vineyards were planted on sites that were historically deemed unsuitable.
