> Would have loved to hear William Kelley’s take but I suppose it is indeed simply a case of a small production finding a strong following.

I don’t think I have too much to add to that analysis!

But if I did, I would add that, in addition to the logic of supply and demand, Lamy-Caillat represents a very rare style of white Burgundy that’s not easy to find and thus commands a big premium. That was certainly why I gravitated so strongly to the wines when I first tasted and reviewed them, back when I was working for Decanter.

Last week I drank a few bottles of the 2019 Chassagne-Montrachet, and it showed so brilliantly, I wish every Burgundy lover could taste these wines! Sadly, that is likely not to be.
