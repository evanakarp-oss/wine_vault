This is under DIAM 30 and is the first vintage of Leflaive to reflect a number of winemaking changes designed to tackle the premox issue. Those include more attention to press fractions, browning of the must, new custom-sized racking tanks, racking under nitrogen etc etc. Report and reviews coming in the next issue of TWA. I’ve suffered my fair share from premox chez Leflaive (including an '05 Pucelles last week) but I’m guardedly optimistic that they’re making progress. They also now discuss the subject candidly, which is a great first step towards solving it.
