Would be interesting to do. But such a difficult thing to generalize about, as you say. Have been drinking a few 1985 Bordeaux lately, and would consider the 1985 Lafite fully mature and about to decline, the 1985 Haut-Bailly fully mature but holding for a decade, and the 1985 Léoville-Barton mature but with a good twenty-five years ahead of it. Predicting drinking windows might be even harder than scoring wines, if only we would devote as much attention to it.
