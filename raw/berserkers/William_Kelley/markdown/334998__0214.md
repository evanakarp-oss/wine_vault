I do think universally high scores, in a region that has de facto out-sourced its marketing to wine critics for the last thirty years, has also contributed to consumer apathy. Even I score Bordeaux on average 1 point higher than Burgundy (last time I crunched the numbers) in TWA. It is very hard for a consumer to choose between dozens of wines scored >96. And if one of those wines, purchased and tasted, disappoints, then the consumer is even more lost.
