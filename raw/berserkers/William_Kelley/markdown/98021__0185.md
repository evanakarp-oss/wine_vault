> *Replying to :*  
> Lorenzon: I just recently bought 6 bottles of the 2016 reds to try following some very positive comments of W. Kelley in an instagram video (worth a look, see below). I have access to some 2010s but haven’t bitten yet - around what period did his wines move more away from extraction? I am not…

My impression is that Bruno’s style in red has evolved gradually and incrementally rather than radically. He has known what he wanted to achieve in red wine from the beginning and has honed in on it over time. I wouldn’t hesitate to try a bottle or two. His 1999 Cuvée Carline is drinking great today, though obviously was more extracted when it was young (like so many producers two decades ago). But, I’m hoping to do a vertical with him, maybe later this year. I second what Gautier wrote about his intensity and focus, and would add that he drinks good wine—and not just from Burgundy. I think the fact that he has a cultivated palate is important. My impression is that many growers still drink mainly their own wines, or simple local wines, and if you have never tasted DRC or Leroy etc it limits your sense of the possible. Bruno knows all the reference points, and it shows.
