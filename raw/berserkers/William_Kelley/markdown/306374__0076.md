Today, the norm is to pick fruit a lot greener, visually, than in the good vintages of the 1970s (I’d co-opt 1973 from your list and leave you the rest). Overall growers today are picking earlier in the vines’ cycle, with less skin-maturity, than in the celebrated vintages of the 1970s and 1980s. And even in the less favorable vintages, green-ish fruit picked in October, at the end of the vines’ cycle, isn’t the same as green fruit picked in August in terms of phenolic development. Just as red wines from unripe (idem overripe) grapes have a short life expectancy, so do whites.
