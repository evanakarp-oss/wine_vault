> *Replying to :*  
> As for Dauvissat, I of course agree that the winemaking there, which now results in an incredibly high incidence of premature oxidation, has not changed in about a hundred years.

Hardly that. In the last 100 years, they went from basket to Vaslin to pneumatic press; from new feuillettes (selling the wine in the barrel) to domaine bottling and to old 228-liter barrels; and from bottling by hand direct from the barrel to a mechanical bottling line. At some point they also went over to vinifying in tank before barreling down, probably in the 1960s or 1970s.

Personally, if I had to speculate about the cause of premox chez Dauvissat, I would be looking at dissolved oxygen at bottling, low-ish SO2, and then the closure. Vincent does not travel and isn’t really aware he has a problem, given the wines from his cellar always seem to be just fine.
