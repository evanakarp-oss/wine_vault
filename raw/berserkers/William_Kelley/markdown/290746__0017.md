In its origins, Petit Verdot first enters the historical record in the southern Médoc, in what’s today the AOC of Margaux. It was planted in the “palus”, the inferior alluvial terroirs directly adjecent to the Gironde, and then spread inland and to the north. It produces deeply colored wines, but inferior clonal selections meant, for a long time, it was hard to find good material for new plantings. Now there are better clones, and top estates of the Médoc have propagated massale selections of their own. But not all Petit Verdot is created equal, and the genetic material is important. I wouldn’t ever imagine comparing PV with Cabernet Franc, but perhaps an inferior clone cropped at high yields and picked a touch before optimal maturity could be reminiscent of a poor quality Cabernet Franc. It clearly has an interest, but for me not much as a standalone variety.
