Sure, I hosted a tasting for a dozen people last year with some bottles of Dom Pérignon—that we bough—back to the 1960s. If you had been there you would have heard me be very candid about what Dom Pérignon is and was, in terms of vineyard sourcing, production volume, positioning and so on. Some of the wines were excellent. Beyond that, well, I don’t always write my own captions/blurbs on our website.

And yes, if I get up in the morning with the desire to write about wine, it’s because I think there are values worth fighting for in the world of wine. That these values don’t happen to include thinking that the Western European middle classes should be able to roll up in their VWs and fill up the trunk with wines that cost no more than they did 20 years ago is of course where we differ.
