> Buy a single bottle of each and decide for yourself. All of the wines are good - I started buying them 19 vintages ago. I’ve developed some preferences over time, but they are more personal than anything else… William is right to point out that they require long cellar time to really shine. Note:…

I think that the idea that Charmes is some sort of second tier premier cru ought to be resisted. It’s large, so not all sites are created equal and there are necessarily many producers, not all of them good; but great Charmes is deep, rather sumptuous, very complete, and notably age worthy. If a Chambolle Charmes isn’t good, it isn’t because of the terroir!
