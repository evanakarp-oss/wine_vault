> *Replying to :*  
> One of the points raised in Steen’s article in passing is the so-called “Roulot method.” (I’m not quite sure why Jean-Marc is given credit for it, because I think that Raveneau was the first to use it, but I’ll leave that aside.) The method is simple. The wine is only allowed to remain in barrel…

I also wondered why Jon dubbed finishing élevage in steel tank *sur lie* the “Roulot method”. Leflaive had been doing it for a long time, maybe even since the time of François Virot, I’m not sure—a function, I believe, of not having underground cellars. Jean-Marie Guffens would agree with you, Don, as he argues premox is predominantly caused by wines being left too long in new wood. All the Guffens-Heynen wines now finish their elevage in cement tanks.

Leflaive still put the wines into steel after 10-12 months, incidentally: I tasted the '15s from tank in October. My intuition (I speculate) is that the problems there stem from low sulfur and perhaps a laissez faire attitude to topping, inspired by some of the more extreme aspects of the bio-dynamic philosophy. There are, after all, several different routes to prematurely oxidized wines.
