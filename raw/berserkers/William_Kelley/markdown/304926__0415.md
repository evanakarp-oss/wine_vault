If we agree that premox = a more fragile wine throwing into relief the OTR variability of natural cork closures in the form of seemingly-random oxidation within a sample set of bottles (and we already discussed that last year), then I think random earlier-than-anticipated oxidation in red wines meets that definition. It’s a more “impressive” feat to achieve in red, however, given the more robust nature of the genre.

I have never had anything like that from Canon or Figeac, however, and I also struggle to see that happening to 2019 Troplong given everything that has changed. 2009 and 2010 Troplong, however, are another matter.
