If you don’t trust us to make the selection, why would you trust our reviews?

The job of a critic, IMO, is to offer a clearly articulated vision of which producers are interesting and why, to guide readers to the most characterful and compelling wines.

The old way of doing this was to spend a few weeks per year in a wine region, tasting huge numbers of wines in massed tastings, along with big importer tastings of samples at one’s office, and hoping that the cream would float to the top. This might also be called the spray and pray method.

The new way is to spend a lot more time in a region, getting under its skin, following who is doing what in the vineyards throughout the year, and dialing in on the best producers that way. That has the disadvantage of being expensive and time consuming, and requiring some knowledge of agronomy and winemaking. But it is in some respects more efficient; it’s harder for producers to game the system; and it renders the press less dependent on regional bodies/importers to organize tastings. And it’s the opposite of tunnel vision, since by being well-connected in a given region means one is likely the first to hear about/see new developments.
