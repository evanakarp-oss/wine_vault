As it happens, I tasted this yesterday at the winery.

I would rank it a modest step up over the also lovely 2013.

Eric has been making an effort to pick a bit earlier to capture more freshness. (Which will be easier to achieve when some heritage selections which ripen at lower brix start making more of a contribution to the blend - which, since 1987, has been mainly clone 4. We had a 1984 Chardonnay from the original vines planted by William Short in 1949, and its lithe, tensile condition makes me very excited to see what Monte Bello Chardonnay will be like when clone 4 plays a less important part in the blend). He has also fine-tuned the barrel program to great effect.

Right now, the '14 is a bit shut down compared with how it showed in barrel and even how it showed in February. I noted a restrained nose featuring notes of crisp orchard fruit, white peach and citrus zest. On the palate, the wine was pure and elegantly glossy, with superb textural refinement underpinned by bright acidity. It came in at 14.2% alcohol and spent 16-7 months on the lees.

The '13 isn’t quite so elegant, and it doesn’t have quite so much cool fruit and texture to cloak its structural elements. That said, I’m very happy to have a few bottles of the 2013 in my cellar.
