> I was thinking about this thread yesterday when I was out winehunting, and it occurred to me that Ramonet and Lafon are doing the same as Sauzet in terms of bringing in a whole new range of lower priced wines (Ramonet under their own label and Lafon in some kind of venture under a different label.)…

Hi Tom,

The Ramonet situation is a bit different to what Lafon is doing. In their case, with the retirement of Noel Ramonet, the Ramonet label has changed from SCEA Domaine Ramonet to Jean-Claude Ramonet. It has changed over the years, from Domaine Pierre Ramonet, to Domaine Ramonet-Prudhon, to Domaine André Ramonet, and then to SCEA Domaine Ramonet, some of these different labeling coexisting during various periods - so it is not unprecedented for the name of the entity to change. Jean-Claude is now purchasing the gapes from Noel’s share of the vineyards, and has also added a few new appellations: Pernand-Vergelesses Les Belles Filles, Chassagne Clos du Cailleret, Chassagne Abbaye de Morgeot, etc etc. The latter two are ficofi vineyards. But aside from that, it’s business as usual chez Ramonet.

Dominique Lafon, by contrast, has set up an entirely new domaine (he only owns a tiny stake in the Domaine des Comtes Lafon, which has many family shareholders), with the wines being made in the Château de Bligny. They are stylistically a bit different from the Comtes Lafon wines too.

And yes, Gary, Beaune Pertuisots was heavily frosted in 2016, like many climats on the Montagne de Beaune. But some people did produce wine from there in 2016, including David Croix who made a nice red.

All best,

William.
