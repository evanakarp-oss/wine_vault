Malolactic in barrel certainly gives a different profile, especially early in élevage, and it is something one tries to take into account. But I wouldn’t say it makes wines more or less fragile. As ever, it’s an ostensibly simple technique that conceals many variables: the strain of malolactic bacteria (some make more diacetyl than others), the temperature of MLF (higher = more diacetyl), etc, some of which are arguably actually more important than the vessel in which MLF takes place.
