Bulk price for 100 liters of Chiroubles is around 200 EUR; for good quality Moulin-a-Vent, maybe 450. If the crus can be had for 200 EUR per hectoliter, what do you think that implies for the pricing of Beaujolais-Villages, and in turn generic Beaujolais? By contrast, for context, 100 liters of Bourgogne Côte d’Or Pinot Noir trades for around 660 EUR.

The best vineyards of the Beaujolais are often seriously sloping, prone to erosion, and difficult to mechanize due to goblet pruning and quite high planting densities. Yet they are less well valorized than the least prestigious AOCs from the Côte d’Or (which happen to be, in many cases, much easier to cultivate).

This is a problem for the region’s producers, and by extension for anyone who wants to see the Beaujolais realize all its potential.
