That’s right. It’s a parcel planted in the 1950s with massale selections (including a bit of Pinot Gris, as is quite common in Chambolle and Vosne especially) and now farmed organically. Yields are always modest and the soil is rather reddish with lots of small shards of limestone generated be freeze thaw weathering. Other producers present in this lieu-dit include Arnoux-Lachaux, Hudelot-Noëllat and François Bertheau. Its easterly exposition means it is always inherently quite racy and fine. It was the first cuvée I produced, starting in 2018, and it has been very instructive getting to know it: with good vine genetics, great soils and very good farming, the musts are very balanced and it always ferments without any kind of issue, with perfect kinetics. But it is actually quite difficult to get the élevage right. I have since found it to be more generally the case that the finer-boned, more elegant wines are those that show oak the most, and it is not so much a question of quantity (in 2018 I didn’t use any new barrels) but of quality/character. Fouchères seems to go better with long-seasoned, very light toast barrels, and with 2021 we really began to get to a place I’m quite happy with.
