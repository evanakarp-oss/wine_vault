Try Gicours 2020.

The great Giscours of the 1970s had a lot of Cabernet in them, and were quite extracted wines (without being overripe or confected obviously). By the 1980s, the percentage of Merlot soared. Getting the vineyards back into shape took a lot of work, and resulted in parcels containing both old and young vines. As of the last couple of vintages, these have been harvested separately within each parcel. We begin to get back to the DNA of Giscours now, in my view, which wasn’t really the case before.
