I actually love aged Condrieu and have had some great examples from Perret, Gangloff and others to say nothing of Château Grillet. But I think on the one hand you have a fragile variety that’s high in pH; and on the other it is often made by red wine makers, which means falling back on white wine 101 from enology school: so very gentle pressing, clean musts without enough lees, blocked malo and short élevage. What would happen if you made Viognier the way Coche-Dury makes Meursault? It would be an interesting experiment in any case.
