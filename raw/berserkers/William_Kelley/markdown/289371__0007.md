It’s from vines in the commune of Puligny, which I believe were acquired from Chartron, but I’m not sure about that. So deep soils vs the stony, sloping Sous Châtalet for d’Auvenay, and a sunnier exposition vs the north-east exposition of the latter. Viticulture is the same, but winemaking is quite different: the Domaine Leroy version is, as I understand it, fermented and matured in tank; whereas the d’Auvenay Aligoté is in 100% new oak.
