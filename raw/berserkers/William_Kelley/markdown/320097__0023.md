So if the question is, which producers represent the aesthetic of “transparency” (in reality, in wine as it is in cuisine, simply a particularly sophisticated form of artifice), I would point to the likes of Mugnier and Duroché in red, and someone like Lamy in white.

The reality is that Burgundy is both the ne plus ultra of *terroir* , with its isolation of *climats* ; but also in a sense its reduction to the absurd, as morcellation means that one can taste different, and often very differing, expressions of the same climat made by different producers…
