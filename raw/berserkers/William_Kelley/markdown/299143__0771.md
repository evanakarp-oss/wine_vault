As Jeff says, because it was high in quality. But why?

Part of it is vintage dependent. The sweeter the tannins, the more you can use. Sometimes “ripe” vintages don’t actually produce very nice tannins: in 2018 in Burgundy, for example, I used no press wine; whereas in 2019, I used everything.

But also, as a general rule, the less extractive your winemaking, the better the quality of your press wine. So if you ferment cool, with some intact berries and don’t do aggressive pump-overs, you are more likely to find that your press wine makes an active contribution to the ensemble.

Of course, then there are variables that will differ from estate to estate and vintage to vintage - quality of vine genetics, draining and pressing techniques, what sort of press you use, etc…
