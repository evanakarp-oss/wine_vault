Scores are down a lot compared to recent vintages, but “terrible” is a simplification. As ever we try to call it how we see it, and some of the reviews certainly are very critical. We are also very clear about how we situate the vintage vis a vis other recent vintages. But if you look back at RP’s 2001 EP scores, for example, you see Mouton at 90-92, Pétrus at 91-93, Lafite at 92-94, Pichon Comtesse at 89-91 etc etc and I don’t think anyone today would claim that 2001 was a failed vintage.

Sadly the quality of the wines is likely to get lost in the discussion around EP. In Bordeaux one often hears that en primeur is some sort of amazing opportunity, as it brings all the world to Bordeaux and makes all the world talk about Bordeaux. If the result, however, is that all the world simply talks about price, I am not sure it is doing the region’s wines all that much of a service…
