The issue is more that lots of plantings in the 80s, 90s and 00s occurred in a context when ripening was challenging, so rootstocks and clones that ripen rapidly were favored. In a very different context of warmer, sunnier vintages, that translates to ripeness being attained only at high alcoholic degrees. Picking unripe is not really the solution. So while you can do a lot in the vineyard and winery to mitigate overripe flavors and create fresh, vibrant wines, the issue of high alcoholic degrees is not such an easy fix. And this is particularly a problem for Merlot, which naturally delivers higher abvs and tends to ripen earlier, anyway.
