> *Replying to :*  
> So, those of you who have been buying these since the beginning - is it worthwhile opening a bottle when they arrive to see what they are about?

I would say the opportunity cost is quite high, especially in a vintage such as 2018, which shows a lot of puppy fat young but which will tighten up in the way the 2012 did with bottle age. Better would be to go to visit the vineyard and bully Graeme into opening a 2011. It takes me two-to-three years of bullying per bottle (you might be more persuasive), but the 2011 really tells you everything you need to know.
