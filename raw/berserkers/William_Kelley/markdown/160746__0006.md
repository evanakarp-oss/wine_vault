I have tasted a few 2019s, including my own Chambolle-Musigny, and my very early and informal impressions (which I wouldn’t yet put in print - this is very provisional stuff) is that 2019 is quite a surprising vintage: weather conditions were if anything more extreme than 2018, yet the wines are livelier and more elegant. There is an energy in what I have tasted that’s quite exciting (and this would also appear to apply to the Beaujolais). So I think any notion that it’s 2018-mark two looks to be mistaken. I’m certainly looking forward to tasting the wines systematically later this year.
