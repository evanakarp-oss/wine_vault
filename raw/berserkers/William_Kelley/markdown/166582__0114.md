> *Replying to :*  
> In general it seems like Burgundy prices at the high end are driven more by trends for specific producers then by anything else including the location of the vineyard. That’s how we get villages from the hottest producers selling for more than Grand Crus from other perfectly respectable producers,…

That was what I was getting at, i.e. that the top producers of the Côte de Beaune will begin to close the gap with the top producers of the Côte de Nuits in terms of secondary market pricing. There are a number of top producers of red Côte de Beaune that have yet to explode that I think are likely to do so over the next five years. And the reason they were lagging behind is obviously due to the fact that they are in the Côte de Beaune, while many emerging markets have tended to fixate on the reds of the Côte de Nuits.
