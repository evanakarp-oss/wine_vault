I devoted a bit of space to the 2003 comparison in my report:
> Given such extreme temperatures, why isn’t the 2022 vintage anything like 2003? The critical difference is that in 2003, summer heat and hydric stress followed hard on the heels of a normal, rainy spring. That shock was compounded by viticulture that promoted superficial rooting, maximized sun exposure and aeration of the fruiting zone and left soils bare throughout the growing season. The unfamiliar conditions also caught producers unawares. Vines suffered, losing their leaves; grapes shriveled and acidities fell; and harvest dates were often too late. Readers who have followed the less-successful wines produced in the 2003 vintage will be all too familiar with the results in the glass.> In 2022, by contrast, the vines were forced to adapt to warm, sunny, dry conditions from the beginning. Their reduced vegetative vigor meant that water losses by transpiration were mitigated, and below-average sized grapes with thick skins made more modest demands on the plants. Progressive agronomy paid off, too: soils richer in organic matter, protected by cover crops, retained more water and stayed cooler, helping the vines over the finish line. Thoughtful canopy management, without de-leafing, preserved grapes from sunburn, shading the fruiting zone and vineyard soils. The fact that the vineyards didn’t exhibit yellowing leaves or defoliation at the moment of harvest is the most obvious proof that 2022 is very different from 2003. And unlike in 2003, Merlot performed superbly in 2022, delivering better yields than Cabernet Sauvignon and making an important contribution to blends, demonstrating that this variety has a bright future if farmed intelligently.> The accumulated experience of 2015, 2018, 2019 and 2020 also meant that intelligent winemakers were ready to harvest at the right time, a choice of decisive importance. Many properties are now equipped with more numerous, smaller fermentation vats, meaning that larger blocks can be subdivided, picked at different dates and fermented separately. In 2022, estates with this advantage didn’t have to compromise, picking some grapes too late and some too early in order to fill a large vat, but could instead prolong harvest for the better part of a month, picking sub-block by sub-block.
When you combine that with chilling fruit on reception, handling it gently, fermenting at cool temperatures, extracting minimally, and then follow up with a more reductive élevage (less racking, no microoxygenation, in some cases larger or less permeable vessels) you retain the vibrant flavors you managed to capture in the vineyard.

We’re very clear in the article that it’s a vintage with lows as well as highs; but the highs are quite remarkable and have nothing whatsoever to do with 2003.
