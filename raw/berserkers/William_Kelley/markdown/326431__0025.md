I have only ever heard the term  used by French winemakers to refer to aromas/flavors, never structure…

When one is obliged to explain the use of a French word by reference to simpler, easier-to-pin-down English words, it begs the question of whether it might be easier and clearer to just use those English words. Especially if the French word is being used in a sense that isn’t identical with how it is actually used by French speakers.

Wine writing has tended to over-borrow from French, whether it’s <pain grillé> for toast, <sur maturité> for the quite simple but more obviously pejorative over-ripeness, or, my personal favorite, italicized < *pigéon>* for, you guessed it, pigeon.
