A lot of costs went up lately… but you can see today’s costs for the Côte d’Or in my post. If you want to do fewer hours per hectare of manual work, or do synthetic systemic treatments instead of organic, or do élevage in tank, or use cheaper corks, you can bring down the number. You can also bring it down by doing lots of it yourself and not taking much of a paycheck. Vincent has been investing for two decades and now the wines are valorized at their proper level. Before, relative to the quality and the work, they were underpriced. That is not sustainable for producers (would you like to work without a raise for a decade?) and so consumers who appreciate wines of that quality need to be prepared to pay for it—and these remain very reasonable. If you have been following them for a long time, no doubt you have large stocks acquired for very modest prices to console you for the fact that they are now a little more expensive.
