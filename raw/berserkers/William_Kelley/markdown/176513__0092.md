One thing in particular that is fresh and new about William’s reviews is an intense focus on farming and vineyard management. It is no…

The reviewer perspective here is that a lot of wines can taste great from barrel or in the first years after bottling, but that quality of farming typically shows more and more as time passes. I can think of wines that impressed me immensely as a student a decade ago which now have one foot in the grave. Knowing the vineyards the way I do now, that doesn’t surprise me. Today, I (and readers of TWA) are somewhat protected, I hope, from over-hyped charlatans or even talented winemakers working with atrocious fruit, because I have a decent sense of what is going on in the vines.
