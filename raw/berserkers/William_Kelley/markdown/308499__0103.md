Not sure I agree with this, Jamie. An open cage pneumatic press is no less oxidative than a Vaslin screw press (of course closed cage inerted pneumatic presses are a different matter). And even settling the must from a Vaslin screw press for several days leaves you with something much more turbid than a pnematic press pressed whole cluster without crushing before (unless you enzyme heavily). To be clear, I’m not discounting the role of must oxidation, but to me the big difference between a screw press and a pneumatic press is clearly the amount of dry extract from the skins plus the amount of suspended solids.
