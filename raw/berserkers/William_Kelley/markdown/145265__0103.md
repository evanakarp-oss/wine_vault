> This dynamic of “home” vs. “tastings” has probably shaped how I seek out wine knowledge/criticism/analysis more than any other concept. I very much hope the professional world includes more of the “home” and thoughtfulness side of wine exploration. I am so much more interested in a person with a…

Yeah, I’m convinced it’s much more interesting. >90% of the wines in my upcoming article were from my cellar, bought in Burgundy with great provenance from cellars in the region, and shared between at most six and more often two or three people, served attentively without suspended sediment. Unless the bottles are ex-domaine and the tasting is seriously organized I pretty much eschew all “tastings” of older wines these days, as between questionable provenance, poor wine service and almost invariably too many wines, I simply don’t enjoy participating or have confidence in the notes that result. If a hypothetical vertical tasting of e.g. ten vintages of La Tâche includes two oxidized wines, one fake and one that has travelled a week before so every glass is cloudy, what is the point? I’d rather drink one bottle of La Tâche with my wife.
