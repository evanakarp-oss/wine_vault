> *Replying to :*  
> Thomas Keim: '76 was a terrific Burgundy vintage, if the price is right, I would jump on it - I always heard the acidity was problematic. No?

1976 was an extremely hot vintage with very thick skins, so tannin rather than acidity was the issue here. I suspect the reason it’s comparatively easy to find '76s these days is that they shut down hard and people put them in a corner of the cellar and forgot about them. I had a nice 1er cru Beaune recently that was still vibrant and firm at the core. It is not on the level 1971 or 1978, however, at least in my limited experience.
