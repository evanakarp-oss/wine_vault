> I don’t really know who all the major commentators are. I subscribe to/read Gilman and Jasper. So far the reports coming from those drinking wines from the bottles rather than in barrel are very different from those two pros (and they are very different from one another). That is really what I’m…

The 2018s being tasted in London are, for the most part, not bottled wines. They are barrel samples that the merchants pick up in Burgundy and take to London to pour for the en primeur tastings.

The samples shown there are always variable in how well they perform after the journey, but in a high pH vintage like 2018, that is likely to be a particular issue.
