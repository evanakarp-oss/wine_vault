Not disputing these particular sources, but the world of wine writing is frequently more-than-sloppy when it comes to accuracy in such matters, with little in the way of fact-checking and decades-old sources frequently being repeated as evidence of current practice. One mistaken transcription or misunderstanding will then live on indefinitely. Witness, for example, the widely repeated claim that Arnaud Ente worked for Coche-Dury, repeated even by his UK importer, when he did no such thing. Or the erroneous notion that Joseph Roty perform battonage on their red wines, a claim made by Remington Norman in the 1980s that reappeared in an article last year. If there is some debate as to whether winemaking is an art or a science, there can be no question as to which heading wine writing falls under. So someone should simply drop the Château a note to ask.
