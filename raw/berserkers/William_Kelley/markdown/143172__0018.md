It’s quite difficult to think of producers that resemble Cathiard, as the style is pretty singular. What’s more, a lot has changed there between 2018 and 2008. The wines still see a lot of new oak but I think they have more concentration to back it up. I think Sebastian takes more risks too - in 2017 he was one of the last to harvest. He spends a lot of time working in the vines.

FWIW, I’m sitting at a table blind tasting Corton-Charlemagne with a bunch of other critics and some of the biggest wine buyers in the UK trade, and no one can think of anything that resembles Cathiard.

Of course, if you want to list oaky producers in Vosne, the list is very long.

Several producers mentioned here don’t have anything in common with Cathiard, at least to my palate.
