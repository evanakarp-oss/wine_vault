I’m a big fan of Bongran with age. I drank a bottle of the 2000 during lockdown in France and it was a great way to make a virtual visit to the Mâconnais—because that wine could come from nowhere else. The aging potential in a cold cellar is as long as almost anything made in Burgundy, and the “off” vintages tend to develop especially well. The oldest I have is a few bottles of the 1989, but a friend in the UK told me he has a 1964. Now that’s a true unicorn!
