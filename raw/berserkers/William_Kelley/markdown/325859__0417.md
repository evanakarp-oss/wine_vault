I don’t decant sparkling wines in general, but I was curious to see what happened. Sure enough, it did make a big difference vs. simply leaving the bottle open.

Given that CO2 amplifies the perception of bitterness (which is why Champagne pressing is so gentle, to avoid any bitter elements in the vins clairs), and reduction also amplifies the perception of bitterness, pronounced reduction in sparkling wine can be quite disagreeable in a way it rarely is in still wine. The fragility of most vins clairs to oxidation and the oxidative shock of disgorgement, however, means that actively reduced sparkling wines are rare. So I was curious to try to understand what was going on.
