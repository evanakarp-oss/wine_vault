If my nephew gets his way, she won’t be allowed to date until she is 35, and hopefully because of the large format, it will…

I was a fan of the direction Pontet Canet was going in up to 2011, and tasted the 2010 recently which was showing very well (though a bit of a monster like many 2010s). From 2012, with the introduction of amphorae, the style changed, and my note on the 2019 reflects my unvarnished reflections on the wine. Whether one likes it or not, I think no one could disagree that if you’re buying it expecting to get something along the lines of e.g. Pontet Canet 2010 or 2005, you will be surprised.
