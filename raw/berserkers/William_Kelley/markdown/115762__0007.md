Count me as another fan of aged Sancerre, from the right producers. We had a 1994 Cotat Monts Damnés in July which was just lovely.

Over time, the wines’ youthful aromas of apple, lime and what John is wont to call ‘botanicals’ to segue into more honeyed aromas and flavors along the lines of custard apple and candied fruits, beeswax, petrol, etc. This can take a very long time, however. That 1994 Cotat was still very youthful, for example.

I’m also an admirer of Boulay’s. My experiences with his Monts Damnés have been consistently excellent.

One of the great frustrations of my cellar is that my modest stock of Vatan is strictly off-limits, as I’m trying to be disciplined about waiting 10+ years after the vintage before opening bottles. So I really only get to drink it in France off wine lists.
