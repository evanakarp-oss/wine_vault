A clonal selection takes a long time to come to fruition. But there are new clones coming onto the market from time to time. And then there are so-called “massale” selections, which are really just a mixed bag of clones, say a dozen or so individuals, that have been selected and treated for virus and propagated. Massale selections of heritage vine material, however, permit much more genetic diversity and are generally less likely to have been isolated for narrow technical parameters (e.g. maximum sugar accumulation for maximum yield). Later-ripening massale selections are likely the way forward; but we should also be sure to preserve all manner of genetic material for the future as the key to the durability of our noble varieties is their genetic diversity in my view!
