> *Replying to :*  
> Glad to hear that you think highly of Lorenzon. I took a flyer on the Piece 15 & Champs Martin a little while ago, and it was a pretty good bottle with an intense salty/mineral drive. Would you say that is a characteristic of the Chalonnaise in general, or the producer/bottling? Also would love…

I would say that, in the best sites, it’s inherently **possible** but seldom realized. Achieving that effect requires serious viticulture and winemaking; whereas the Côte Chalonnaise has long been subject to a vicious cycle whereby a modest reputation encourages producers to pursue quantity over quality, thereby reinforcing negative perceptions. You probably have to be a bit crazy to put in the sort of work that Bruno has over the last two decades—high density plantings, training the canopies higher, farming organic, long élevage, amazing quality corks, even making his own barrels. And after all of that, the most acknowledgement one is likely to get is “very good for Mercurey”. Yet in a blind tasting vs wines from the same vintage from Puligny, Lorenzon’s emerge as very good with no qualifications. And they really improve with age. For the Pièce 15, the 2010 is still young but showing very well now - to give you an idea.
