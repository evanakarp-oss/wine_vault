The Domaine Leroy version, as observed above, is matured in tank, so remains pretty discreet for the first decade or two. The d’Auvenay rendition is, for me, the reference for the variety, demonstrating that the highest calling of Aligoté (as for Chardonnay) is not to make Aligoté (or Chardonnay), but to make white Burgundy. Coche’s rendition is also pretty understated out the gates (oldest barrels, often under 12% abv), but it does age very well. JF Coche used to bottle magnums of it for Gérard Depardieu back in the '80s, and would sometimes get late night phone calls extolling its virtues with a decade in bottle So I do not think either are pedestrian, but definitely neither are likely to “win” a blind tasting of young wines (unlike the d’Auvenay version).
