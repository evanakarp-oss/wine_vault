I think when you factor in the limestone soil and the low pH of Perrières vs the gravel soils and higher pH of Durfort that is not so surprising. Low pH wines from limestone in Bordeaux, especially when the winemaking isn’t adapted to make them extra demonstrative young, often offer quite a pure but primary profile in the near term, shutting down more than wines from terroirs that deliver higher pH styles.
