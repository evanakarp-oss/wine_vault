> *Replying to :*  
> Good to know! Have you tasted at many other addresses in the region when it comes to the 2017 vintage? I have received a few offers already for Côte de Beaune and Côte Chalonnaise but haven’t heard many comments on the vintage.

I have tasted about 2,000 2017s, and am writing the vintage up for The Wine Advocate, to be published the first week of January. To grossly generalize, it’s a charming, open-knit vintage in red, along the lines of 2000 or 2007, cleaner than the former and more sun-kissed than the latter. Vines that had been frosted pushed back hard, so some wines are a bit dilute. 2017 whites are more serious, classically balanced but charming, with lower acids and more open, expressive personalities than 2014, but still fresh and crisp in expression, and with surprising amounts of structuring dry extract in some cases. It’s a lovely white vintage for which there isn’t a recent analogy. I wonder if 1982s might have been a bit like that, but I never tasted those wines when they were young.
