> *Replying to :*  
> The question I have for the wines coming into Canada - how many labels have to be changed or altered based on their strict labeling laws? Every item coming into Canada is tested, and if it falls outside the regulations, the winery is required to relabel or put a sticker over the alcohol level . . .…

This is a very pertinent question, because there is actually a paper published based on this data: **Splendide Mendax: False Label Claims About High and Rising Alcohol Content of Wine** , which appeared in *Journal of Wine Economics* , Volume 10, Number 3, 2015, Pages 275–313.

It is worth a read, but it is not in the public domaine.

For the purposes of this discussion, however, it is relevant that for 57% of the wines they looked at (52,178 wines out of 91,432), the alcohol level was under-reported by more than 0.01 per cent. For these wines, “the average actual alcohol content was 13.56% and the average reported alcohol content was 13.15% with an average discrepancy of 0.42 percentage points”. The most under-reported category was, unsurprisingly, New World reds.

If 0.42 per cent is the average discrepancy for 52,178 wines, it must be said that it is remarkable that Otto, working at the monopoly, has never come across a wine that didn’t exceed the 0.5 per cent tolerance…
