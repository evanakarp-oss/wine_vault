I am the last person you’ll find who’ll undervalue the importance of agronomy!

However, in my view its biggest contribution to wine quality is by producing balanced, concentrated fruit rich in aroma precursors. This, indeed, is the strongest link between farming and fermentation outcomes, since balanced musts with healthy pHs select for more favorable microflora.

However, no matter the health of your vineyard and quality of your farming, some sites are more likely to be infested with brett, or susceptible to rot and concomitant acetic rot, for example, than others. So the question of whether those microbes are transient, weather-dependent potential pests, or the only route to making soulful wines of place, remains, and I’m very clear about how I would answer it.
