Will wait to see what the prices are, of course, but Canon, Montrose, Conseillante and Carmes are definitely on my shopping list, almost irrespective of the price, along with some stuff like Mangot, Branaire, Langoa Barton, that’s reliably very well-priced and exceptional in 2022.

As I said in the report, there are a lot of wines that it will make sense to buy, but the key question is: when? I am not interested in essentially giving a loan with negative interest rates to the place de Bordeaux, and as in 2009 and 2010, I am sure there are some wines that will be released at prices beyond which it will be challenging further to appreciate in the next ten years, if what I am hearing is correct. So it may make sense to keep one’s powder dry. But we are talking about 20-50 wines in this category, it isn’t really representative of Bordeaux as a whole.
