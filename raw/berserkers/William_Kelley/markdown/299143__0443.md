Hi Johan,

My deference to the “form” of turning a blind eye when people publish entirely different scores from what they gave blind while still claiming to be publishing the results of a blind tasting extended to not naming names; and I doubt anyone would have joined the dots had you not chosen, inexplicably, to intervene. I confess I don’t feel especially collegiate towards you or Lisa, notwithstanding the professional opportunities she gave me for which I continue to be grateful, not only because your marketing has, intentionally or not, impugned the ethics of all other wine writers (and has been taken by many to be directed at her former colleagues, even if I venture we are not actually foremost in your sights); but also because self-censorship as exemplified in this case only makes it harder to tell hard truths in Bordeaux for anyone else who might care to attempt it.

You are correct, however, that I must have misread the spreadsheet, as the score was not 12.5 but 14.0. For that, accept my apologies. But, since I have you here, can you explain to me how 14.00 for Château Rausan Gassies translates to 88/100, for Haut-Marbuzet to 90/00, to Château Olivier to 91/100, to Pape Clément to 95/100, and to Pontet-Canet to 97/00? Or how 18.5 for Batailley and Gloria becomes 94/100, but for Lafite and Pétrus becomes 100/100?

If you’d like to continue, we can. As the French say, je n’ai strictement rien à foutre.
