Most **red** winemaking up until around the 1980s strove to eliminate lees via regular fining and racking. Why? Because of brett and other microbiological potential issues. The first vintage bottled without racking at DRC for example was 1980. There are clear advantages to maturation on clean fine lees, but there are also plenty of cases where the disadvantages outweigh the advantages. For my own wines, I do a PCR for brett cells before barreling down and if everything it clean, I take everything. If it wasn’t (so far it has been), I’d barrel down clean.
