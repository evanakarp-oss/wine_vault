Nice note.

The sisters’ Clos Vougeot might well be my favorite red Burgundy grand cru if I had to chose. John Gilman sometimes compares it to Musigny and I can definitely see where he’s coming from.

I would also venture that the way it’s reviewed is a classic instance of perceived hierarchy influencing critics’ scoring. The Ruchottes-Chambertin is always served after the Clos Vougeot in the cellar, which is perfectly logical, since it is the more structural, ‘vertical’ and (generally) powerful wine. I don’t think, however, that it reflects quality; certainly not in every vintage. Yet it would be hard to point to many instances when a visiting writer has rated the Clos Vougeot above the Ruchottes.
