There are a few interesting pages on animal aromas in Harold McGee’s *Nose Dive* …

This whole set of animal/decay/manure aromas that you describe is linked to compounds such as skatole, methanethiol, ammonia, cresols, phenols and indole.

Horses in particular produce principally cresols and phenols.

Of these compounds, the volatile phenols are the ones that we most commonly find in red wines, and they are mainly derived from the action of brettanomyces.
