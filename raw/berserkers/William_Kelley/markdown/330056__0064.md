For me, Savigny taken as an ensemble is suppler/softer than Volnay. But that is perhaps because I have drunk too much old d’Angerville and de Montille

The overall quality of agronomy is higher in Volnay, and there are better genetics. There’s much more machine harvesting in Savigny, and because it is less expensive there is an obvious incentive to make more of it… But if you drink e.g. 1959 Bouchard Lavières, or old bottles of Bize, Guillemot or Chandon de Briailles, to say nothing of Leroy Narbantons (and omitting several others that one could cite), there is no doubting that one can make exquisite wines! I personally love Savigny because the combination of fine tannins and immediate fruit makes for very versatile wines with broad drinking windows.
