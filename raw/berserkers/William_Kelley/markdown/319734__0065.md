I think over the last two decades, my profession as a whole has given the benefit of the doubt a bit too frequently to some extremely overtly oaky wines that have, in many cases, remained that way today. As RP used to write on the cover of TWA, I would rather underrate than overrate a wine, and I tend to be quite critical on this point—or should I say, I have high expectations. I don’t agree that all young Bordeaux are, or need to be, dominated by their élevage, and indeed one of the impressive things about estates such as Lafleur, Canon, Palmer and others is how much progress they have made in better integrating new oak in their wines. This, by the way, has very little to do with the percentage of new wood itself, and much more to do with the choice of barrels and how they are made, how the wines are barreled down, and a host of other details.
