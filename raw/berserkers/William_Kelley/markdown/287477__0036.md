I think it’s a little ironic that people have decided to pick on the 2019 Corton here, as that is the best young Corton I have ever tasted from Bouchard, and a wine I bought in magnum for my own cellar. And yes, they are doing things differently with it: picking later (it’s from Le Corton, which isn’t the earliest ripening terroir on the hill) and using a lot more whole bunch. I suspect that, on actually tasting it, you may be surprised.
