> *Replying to :*  
> In terms of glassware, wider bowls bring out the pyrazine aromas inherent to the Cabernet-family varieties, so I find more classically tulip-shaped Bordeaux glasses work better. If I remember correctly, you mentioned in another discussion some time ago, that you go with the Sensory for almost all…

I’m a big fan and early adopter of the Sensory glasses, but I only really use then for Burgundy reds and whites/PN & Chardonnay from elsewhere, Rhône (and not all), and Piemonte. For me, I pretty much simply replaced the Zalto Burgundy glass, always my least favorite from them, with the Sensory, and kept Zalto white wine, Universal and BDX in rotation. For old Bordeaux I might go for any of those three, depending on how old: the 1921 Margaux we drank last month we had from the white wine glasses, in fact, because it was so delicate it wasn’t clear a big glass would flatter it.
