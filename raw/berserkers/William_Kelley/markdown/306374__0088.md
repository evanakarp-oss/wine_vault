I’m not discounting anecdote - just arguing that positives are more useful than negatives!

Overall, I think you have a few different scenarios:

- producers who do pretty much everything to make long-lived wines, and bottle with very good quality natural cork and high free SO2. They typically have low incidence of premox, but are not immune
- producers who by and large have a good track record, but who have had big problems with certain cuvées and vintages, likely due to dissolved oxygen pick up, errors made in adjusting SO2 levels, or (especially) bad batches of corks
- producers who make short-lived wines that are best drunk young, due to practices and poor quality corks

In terms of the causes, however, it’s a quantitative not a qualitative difference.
