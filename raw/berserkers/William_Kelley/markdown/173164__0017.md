> Has anyone tasted a red or white Rully from Domaine de la Folie?

Domaine de la Folie was historically one of, if not the, top estate in Rully (especially for still wines: historically, Rully got rich in the 19th century making crémant). It was owned by one of the pioneers of photography and was the place to be back in the day. It’s to the north of the AOC, surrounded by woods, in a somewhat cooler mesoclimate but with fine expositions. Clémence and Baptiste Dubrulle are the owners today, and they’re making progress, step by step, in restoring its reputation. They’re very nice wines, and they also make one of the best Marc de Bourgogne that you can find (and it is not expensive). In white, the Clos St Jacques is the top wine, in red, the Cuvée Marey. The Aligoté is from great raw materials and I’m encouraging them to vinify it in used barrels because it could definitely benefit from it.
