> *Replying to :*  
> I, too, find it hard to resist a little sulfuric acid in my Yquem. 2007 Chateau D’Yquem Sauternes “Pale to medium gold colored, the 2007 d’Yquem delivers powerful scents of tropical fruits—dried mangoes and pineapple paste—accented by acacia honey, toasted almonds and woodsmoke with hints of chalk…

French oenologists give a wine’s total acidity as its equivalent in g/L sulfuric acid (the rest of the world tends to use tartaric acid), so if you read off a French lab analysis or tech sheet, you could be confused into thinking that the wine contains sulfuric acid.
