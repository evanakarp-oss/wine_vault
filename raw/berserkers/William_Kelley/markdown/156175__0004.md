Just tasted the 2017s from Ramonet at the estate last week. Can’t really relate to the idea of them lacking concentration or texture vis a vis the 2014s, though they do not display as much tension or cut as 2014 that’s for sure. I suspect your bottle was just in a grumpy place. For me, the floral and elegant Boudriotte, and the chiseled Caillerets were particularly successful this year, along with the Bienvenues and the Montrachet. Ruchottes, Vergers (which is normally quite incisive) and Bâtard were all pretty rich and powerful, showing the sunny side of the vintage. The 2017 reds are very pretty and perfumed and already approachable. 2018 Bouzéron looking good, too, the grape variety being adapted to the vintage.
