Without reference to your particular examples (I don’t know if PYCM farms the Perrières, for example), there are indeed some possible factors that can be linked to viticultural practices including:

- yield + solid to juice ratio, and resulting redox potential of the wine
- copper residue on fruit
- canopy management and the result of sun exposure on skin development
- level of physiological maturity at harvest
- and others…

However, in Burgundy, the added factor is that a lot of whites are traded in must (juice), not fruit. Again, I’m talking in general terms not describing these specific cases. But when buying must, it’s generally settled pretty clear (why would you pay for solids you have to discard when you’re paying e.g. 40,000 EUR per barrel of Charlemagne), and will have been pressed following the supplier’s practices (perhaps less extractively) and treated following the supplier’s post-pressing practices (e.g. pectinase enzymes followed by sulfites). So that obviously will impact the finished wine.
