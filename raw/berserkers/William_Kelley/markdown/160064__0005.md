> Apart from the big houses, how does Bouchard stack up against other players at similar price points? I think of Chartogne Taillet, Egly Ouriet, Ulysses Colin, and Prevost in the same ballpark. How would you describe Bouchard’s house style?

Cédric really operates entirely on his own terms. Yields are around 25 hl/ha, lower than many top domaines of the Côte de Nuits to say nothing of the average in Champagne, France’s highest yielding AOC. Vinification in in stainless steel, with neither chaptalization or dosage. The prise de mousse is slow. And the wines finish up with four rather than six bars of pressure, so the mousse is very subtle. On cork, Cédric’s champagnes develop more like still wines, as the Maillard reaction-driven interactions between amino acids from sur lattes aging and sugar from dosage are not really part of the equation. The result of all this is very vinous, concentrated and intense Champagnes that combine remarkable depth of fruit and mid-palate volume with lively acids, complemented by the lower pressure mousse I already mentioned.
