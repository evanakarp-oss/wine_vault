It was your observation that “I don’t think these less expensive white Bordeaux are trying to compete with Loire and NZ” that I was taking issue with, rather than your particular examples. Clos Floridene, however, despite a healthy percentage of Sémillon, was one of Denis Dubourdieu’s own properties and the style very much reflected/reflects his approach to white wine vinification. To me, this is the by far the dominant aesthetic in Bordeaux white wine production today.
