> Michael might have a very high standard of what an “on” vintage is. I’ve heard the term “drinker’s vintage” or similar used slightly differently, and implicitly referring to worse vintages than the ones suggested in the OP, i.e., vintages with wines not meant to be aged (long) or lacking the…

There are plenty of 2008s that are more vibrant and primary than their 2009 counterparts. We drank Le Pin last month and it remains an infant. In fact, if there is a criticism of the vintage it’s that the wines are still so young that very little complexity has emerged. Yet, while the wines remain quite tightly wound in many cases, they are holding more primary fruit than many 2005s. For me, 2008 is on a similar level to 2014, better than 2015. We will see where 2020 fits into the big picture now that the wines are in bottle.
