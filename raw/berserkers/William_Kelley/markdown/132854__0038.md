Really hard to do this, as it was an era of such flux in the vineyards and (to an extent concomitantly) in style.

There are a lot of pruney, volatile and senescent 1997s. But there are also wines such as 1997 Martha’s Vineyard (singing this morning), Dunn Howell Mountain, Togni, Shafer Hillside (their best ever? it’s been a while since I had it).

On the flip side, there are plenty of lovely 1998s, disdained on release, like the Spottswoode which wowed tonight.

With so much bandwidth within each vintage, it’s very hard to rate any given vintage. And I think that would hold true of most 1990s vintages, with the exception of the consistently fine 1991s and 1994s.
