If you can get clean, balanced, physiologically mature fruit into a vat, and you are working with a good vineyard and good vine genetics, you can make exceptional wine. To do that, you need to be very present in your vineyards, from pruning to green work to phytosanitary treatments, and then to harvest at the optimal moment. So if you have a good team, a good site, and the resources to work without compromise, then as a producer you shouldn’t have to “submit” to vintage conditions, and from year to year your wines should differ more in style than quality. An estate such as Lafleur exemplifies this. Of course, in the world of wine it remains relatively rare and, when conditions are challenging, comes at a cost.
