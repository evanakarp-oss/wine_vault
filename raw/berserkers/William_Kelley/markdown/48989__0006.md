> *Replying to :*  
> Pretty much all Macon Rouge is Gamay I believe. I think it is a better source than Beaujolais for both complexity and depth and perhaps shows how important terroir is.

Yes, that’s right regarding the grape variety; but I think the differences in style have as much to do with vine genetics as with “terroir”. The Mâconnais historically produced a lot of red wine. But when Mâcon (and especially Pouilly-Fuissé) exploded internationally post-war, the incentive to replant to Chardonnay was enormous. Just as in Chassagne-Montrachet, if people kept any red grapevines, it tended to be their oldest and best vines. So, in places such as Mâcon-Cruzille you can find really interesting small- and open-clustered massale selections of Gamay that can make great wine. Add the freshness that comes from vines growing in basic soils, and the results are exciting and age-worthy.
