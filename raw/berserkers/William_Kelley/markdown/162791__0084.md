Thanks! Picked another parcel on Wednesday: 13.6 % potential abv, pH 3.20, 6.6 grams tartaric, 1.9 grams malic… Pleased with that also: it’s a low-lying parcel, with more vegetative vigor, and higher yields (relatively - still only 20 hl/ha here). Hence lower tartaric but similar malic.

Picked Chambolle today, will be interesting to see the numbers but to me it tasted more like 13.0 max, with lively flavors but much less in the way of stem lignification than my picks in the Côte de Beaune.

All in all, I think it’s clear that 2020 isn’t really directly comparable with 2003… and the wines are actually likely to be lower alcohol than 2018s and 2019s. However, it’s probably more heterogenous than either of those years.
