> *Replying to :*  
> Robert, My understanding is that Coche kept the wine in barrel and on the lees until bottling. Lees breakdown (and essentially dissolve) in a process called autolysis. Autolysis uses oxygen so the wine can often become slightly reductive. This reductive state is usually fine (even a positive) but…

Jean-François’ formula was two winters on the lees, with one (dirty) racking to homogenize the cuvées after malo, and then bottling direct from barrel. There’s a good interview with him somewhere where he discusses the role of the lees.
