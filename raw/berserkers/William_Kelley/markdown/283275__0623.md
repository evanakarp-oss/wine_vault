What makes you think that there would be meaningful variation in levels of smoke taint markers between bottles of the same, homogenized lot? And why would fining or filtration change that?

It would indeed be interesting to have all manner of analysis on all sorts of wines, but I prefer to deploy our budget strategically. The basis of wine criticism remains *tasting* , which as Ben has underlined is the ultimate diagnostic for smoke taint, though I think that in this case an analysis provided an interesting complement.
