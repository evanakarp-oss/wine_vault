I wouldn’t say Californian per se, but rather that they show all the flesh and richness that you’d expect from a Merlot-dominated blend, cropped at low yields, in very warm vintages. Thomas Duroux embraces what nature gives him and does a great job with it, but the result is obviously rather larger-scaled, more textural wines.

Anyone who is nostalgic for a more classically-proportioned Palmer will want to look at the 2021, which is terrific.
