> *Replying to :*  
> William, Assuming wine prices are in the 2019 and 2020 range, are there any red wines you would buy as futures?

I have a list of things that I want to buy, for sure (and it corresponds with my reviews)! And the fact that they are likely to be a bit more expensive than 2019s won’t put me off. Who knows how many throwback vintages like this we’ll have in the years to come? Given that I live in Burgundy and have a cellar full of “local” wines that tripled in value in the last 12 months, Bordelais pricing doesn’t shock me much.

I do think it would be a pity to write off the less famous estates en masse, as I tasted some very good wines from lesser appellations. Of course, if you want your Bordeaux to taste of syrup, this isn’t the vintage for you, at any level of the hierarchy, but for people who like Bordeaux when it tastes like Bordeaux, there are plenty of interesting things to be found.
