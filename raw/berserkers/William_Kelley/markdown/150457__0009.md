> Surprising regarding the whites Alex. I have loved what I’ve seen from '17 thus far. The Meursault Les Clous from Bouchard is a wine I buy every year from Bouchard. It is a very cool Meursault Lieu Dit and often has a Chablis-like stoniness to me. Just wondering if your bottle showed some juvenile…

The Clous is in my glass right now. This bottle is showing aromas of pear, yellow citrus, dried white flowers and beeswax. Medium to full-bodied, ample and incisive, a bit shut down after bottling, with the SO2 surpassing a bit of the wine’s texture, concluding with an elegantly chalky, mouthwatering finish (with a bit of back-end grip from SO2). It’s under DIAM 10 and should show very well in just a couple of years.
