Of course, in Champagne you are exposed to not just one closure but two. A lot of producers have exaggerated bottle variation in the 1990s, especially those who were ambitious to push for long sur lattes aging (such as Egly), because the crown caps they used for tirage were aluminum with cork liners and didn’t hold up very well. With the advent of polymer-lined stainless steel crown caps in the early 2000s, that changed, happily. The 1996-base Egly wines which were disgorged earlier show better than the vintage for this reason.
