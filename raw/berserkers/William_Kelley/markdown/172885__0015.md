> *Replying to :*  
> William, with regard to recent vintages (say, 2017, 2018, and 2019) are there major differences in the quality/style of the vintages between the Cote d’Or and the Cote Chalonnaise for either reds or whites? Thanks.

There are definitely differences in style between the vintages. I’ll confine my remarks to Dureuil because generalizations are difficult. The 2017s are the highest in acidity and the most classically balanced: tightly wound whites with great acid structure; supple, charming reds. The 2018s are amazingly successful for the vintage, with uncommon mid-palate depth in the whites (this vintage is often a bit dilute as a whole) and no excess of maturity in either color. The 2019s are the most concentrated and also have great acid in white: I suspect they’ll shut down hard, given how much substance there is in the bottle. The 2019 reds you might think of as a somewhat richer, more textural version of the very charming, pretty 2017s.

To answer what, on re-reading, I see you were *actually* asking, there are indeed some minor differences—a bit more rain in the Chalonnaise in summer 2018, for example, which was helpful—but the big picture is much the same across the region.
