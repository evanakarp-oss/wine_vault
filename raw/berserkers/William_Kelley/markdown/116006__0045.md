Probably either the Ulysse Collin Pierrieres Blanc de Blancs or the 2006 Clos Roillette Tardive… Also worked through five magnums of the 1970 Pape Clement very contentedly, as the wine is at peak.

If a wine is complex, expressive and fulfilling an appreciable amount of its potential, I have no problem encountering it quite frequently: repeated exposure heightens one’s awareness of wine’s merits—and equally defects. I remember drinking a case of the 2004 Domaine de Chevalier rouge over the course of a year: I enjoyed the first few bottles, but drank them relatively uncritically; by the end of the case, the wine’s new oak had become obtrusively obvious—an old trick if you will. That was a teachable moment, and the experience is extensible to wines encountered less regularly.
