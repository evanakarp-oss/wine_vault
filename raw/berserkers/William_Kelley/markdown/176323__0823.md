2016 is super classic, but quite youthfully shut down and will demand some patience. 2020 for me is more typically Figeac than 2019: it’s very deep and incipiently complex, a touch less extrovert than 19 without being austere, and its oak is more completely integrated. 2016, 2019 and 2020 are the three best vintages of the Faye era so far, but 2020 is my favorite.

All the work they’re doing with cover crops and hedging to adapt to the vintage conditions; picking parcel by parcel and keeping those parcels separate in the winery to optimize each; refining cooperage choices; fine-tuning vinification etc etc all show most clearly in 2020.
