It’s an interesting question, which we discussed over lunch. 1949 was before the advent of clones in the strict sense, so we are looking at a field selection. IIRC, Short’s Cabernet is known to have come from Rixford’s La Questa plantings at Woodside, but we don’t know where he found his Chardonnay - though the team at Ridge did mention that it was a small berried selection. One likely possibility is Wente’s plantings at La Cresta Blanca in Livermore - some of which came from Meursault in 1882, followed by a second tranche in c. 1912 from the University of Montpellier. The other possible source would have been Martin Ray / Paul Masson, whose Chardonnay allegedly hailed from Louis Latour’s plantings on the hill of Corton.

If your 1984 is in as good condition as the one we had, a 30 minute decant might be in order before service.
