Had the 1971 not long ago, which confirmed my suspicion that CSJ can often age better than Clos de la Boudriotte from Ramonet. With extended bottle age, the structural rusticity melts away but the fleshier, richer fruit remains. At least in 1971, it was the more youthful and gourmand of the two.

If you have any interest in seeking out other red Chassagne, try recent vintages from Hubert Lamy, Michel Niellon (their CSJ is excellent in 2015), and Bernard Moreau.
