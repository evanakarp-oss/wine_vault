Do we actually like Suchots that much? I tend to find it structurally on the rustic side, with boisterous dark fruit. (Of course, there are some notable differences within the (quite large) vineyard as far as drainage is concerned). To me, it is priced more by zip code than by what’s in the bottle (e.g. from Hudelot-Noellat it’s the same price as the much superior Clos Vougeot), though I would love to have my mind changed.

I think the example from Berthaut-Gerbet is likely to get pretty good as Amelie brings the Gerbet vineyards into better shape.

Michel Noellat is one of the largest holders, it’s a pity they don’t do a better job.
