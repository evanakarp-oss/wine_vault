> *Replying to :*  
> D@vid Bu3ker: William Kelley: 2013 might conceivably be the last relatively ‘cheap’ red Burgundy vintage, in the way that 2008 was in Bordeaux. Except then 2014 happened in Bordeaux. I have lost track of how many times people have proclaimed the end of affordability or the end of relatively cheap…

Well, up to a point. A 12-bottle case of 2014 Calon Ségur trades for 800 GBP; I bought cases of the 2008 for 250. Looking at a few other wines which I remember from the EP campaign, the story is the same, more or less, for plenty of them, e.g. Canon, Talbot, GPL - to say nothing of the first growths.

But that’s incidental, really. The Burgundy market is very different because there is so much less wine.
