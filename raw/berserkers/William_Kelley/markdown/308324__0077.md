Did I make any of these wines unaffordable, Donald? [Robert Parker Wine Advocate - Unbiased Wine Reviews](https://www.robertparker.com/articles/pLG34wQrN9h2MsY59)

Sometimes the market follows, sometimes it doesn’t. I have some very unfashionable and inexpensive wines in my cellar that many snobs would disdain to drink, and I write about such wines, too. Meanwhile, you simultaneously claim to disdain the great wines you disparage as existing in the sphere of “luxury”, while also railing at the fact that you can’t afford them.

Economically wine today is very complicated. People are drinking less alcohol, and my generation and younger consumers still are not automatically gravitating towards wine. Producers are being paid to rip up their vineyards in Bordeaux. Is the future really mass market “beverage wines”? Does it even make sense ecologically to transport such products far from their region of production? All these and other questions seem pertinent. What is clear is that the market for wine with, let’s say, aspirations to greatness is likely to be more robust, durable, and interesting for producers.
