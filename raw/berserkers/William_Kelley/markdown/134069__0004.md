The winemaker is now Hélène Mignot, with input from the rest of the Pinault team, inc. Latour’s winemaker (or more accurately, technical director), also named Hélène (Genin).

I think she’s doing a great job. The blend is now based on which blocks have the best terroir (deep, gravel / cobble soils), as opposed to being crafted for immediate impact; we’ll see what effect that has down the line. More noticeable is superior oak integration compared with the old régime. I’ve also noted (at least perceptibly) brighter acidity.

They are also about to replant some of the Syrah parcels, which the team believe were not optimally sited.

All in all I think the two Cabernet cuvées are great, better than under the Araujos—but I haven’t tasted the Syrah, so can’t comment on that.
