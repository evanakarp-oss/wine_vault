Ideally, one doesn’t have to choose, as I think both are important parts of a wine’s identity. And in any case, the distinction is likely a little false: more ripe fruit, for example, gives a particular set of aromas, and also a particular set of textures…

But I cited the perfume industry because I find their precision both impressive and admirable. Only once we perceive the same things and use, if not the same, at least mutually comprehensible terminology to talk about those things, can we most meaningfully talk about wine.
