Once you see the vineyards, it makes sense… Le Chavigne is mid-slope, nothing but stones, very old vines for the most part. And then the farming: minimal cultivation (works better with Chardonnay than Pinot Noir), very late hedging, no leaf pulling… And that is before the winemaking. Happily also under Diam 30 so no need for a very high dose of free sulfites that makes the wine closed until it oxidizes.
