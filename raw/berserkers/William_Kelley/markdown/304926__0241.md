That is their plan.

And obviously, for much of Montrose’s history, this was where Montrose was produced. You can read an article I wrote about this here: [Robert Parker Wine Advocate - Unbiased Wine Reviews](https://www.robertparker.com/articles/boiBfBrJxhtGWSjeC)

To simplify: the original 1855 vineyards were on the Terrace 4; in the 1970s, Montrose planted some lower-lying Terrace 5 stuff (which now does into Dame); and in 2010, they bought a bunch (22ha) of Terrace 3 vineyards from Phélan which are now going into a different bottling.
