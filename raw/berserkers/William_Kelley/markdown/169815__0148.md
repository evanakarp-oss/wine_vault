Drove around quite a bit over the last week, from Beaune to the southern Mâconnais… Chardonnay is hit pretty hard everywhere, with a few exceptions: mid-slope sites around Pouilly and Fuissé; the south of Auxey-Duresses, etc. In Pinot Noir, one’s mileage varies, depending a lot on both how advanced a given site is, and when it was pruned etc. For example, driving through Beaune, the bottom of Cent Vignes is largely fine; the top of Cent Vignes and all of Fèves right above are fried; the the Clos de l’Ecu above that is less impacted than Fèves… Beaune Epenotes looks pretty good whereas Pommard Epenots appears to be hit pretty hard. But, overall, it’s clear the Pinot Noir, pruned late, has done pretty well considering how cold it got; whereas Chardonnay is going to be in short supply. The sight of mid-slope Meursault is enough to make one weep.
