The last time I saw ex-domaine professional pricing, the Brut Tradition was 36.90 EUR. If it has gone up by say a third in the interim, that still seems to me a very reasonable tariff for a terrific wine from one of the very best producers in Champagne. And given inflation, dry goods costs, and the general seemingly asymptotic ascent of fine wine prices, it doesn’t even strike me as particularly notable—it’s a similar or if anything smaller increment to that taken by Taittinger Comtes and DP in the same period. Lots of Burgundy producers close to doubled ex-domaine pricing with the 2020 vintage, but perhaps that hasn’t filtered through to the retail market yet.
