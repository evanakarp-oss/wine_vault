> *Replying to :*  
> We can all agree that balance is necessary but the point Marcus & Marcus are trying to make is that, taking balance out of the equation, large brutish monsters should not a priori be considered better than small-scale, delicate wines Exactly Yes. And dry wines that weigh in at 16% for me are not…

I can only underline that this is a choice. Charles Lachaux’s 2019 Echézeaux is around 13%; Emmanuel Rouget’s is 16.2%. The appellation and vintage are the same but the wines are very different. So, a lot of what we ascribe to vintage character is actually the result of producers’ choices.
