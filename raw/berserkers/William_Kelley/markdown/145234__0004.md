Our discussion in the recent purchases thread reminded me I promised to post my note on the 1993 Bertheau Amoureuses:

This perfectly preserved bottle of the 1993 Chambolle-Musigny 1er Cru Les Amoureuses soars from the glass with a glorious bouquet of raspberries, dried rose petals, Egyptian musk, candied peel and grilled squab, complemented by an elusive top-note of violets. On the palate, it’s medium to full-bodied, satiny and enveloping, the vintage’s crisp spine of acidity beautifully cloaked in elegantly degraded fruit, its tannins melting and its finish long and pungently floral.

This year I also picked up a 1972 Bonnes-Mares and 1976 Chambolle AOC so I’m on a bit of a Bertheau kick…
