I think that’s misreading it, honestly. 2021 vs 2020 is such a contrast as to be not at all comparable. As I wrote in the report, out of over 1,000 2021s, I didn’t find any more that were vegetal or mildew tainted than I found 2020s that were overripe, and for the 2021s that are a bit dilute, there are 2020s that are almost over-concentrated. Vintage is really a very poor optic for understanding Burgundy (and increasingly, contemporary Bordeaux, I would add!), as there is such a diversity of sites, vine genetics, viticultural approaches and winemaking styles. For my own cellar, I am interested to own the best 2020s and the best 2021s, and the onus of my report was on guiding people towards those wines. Pricing is alas another subject, and I continue to advise people to backfill the best 17s and even 19s.
