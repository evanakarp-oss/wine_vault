> *Replying to :*  
> In the entire spectrum of Chardonnay styles, Burgundian Chardonnay is principally at one end of the spectrum. They are very good that end of the spectrum, but they stay at that end.

Between things such as Domaine de la Bongran from Viré-Clessé at always >14% alcohol, with botrytis and a few grams of RS, to the raciest wines of Chablis—via the ample, enveloping grands crus of Chassagne and Pulingy and the steelier wines of Saint-Aubin or Saint-Romain—I would say that Burgundian Chardonnay spans just as big a gap as the continuum that begins with Ceritas and ends with Marcassin.
