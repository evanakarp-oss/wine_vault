# Introducing Chateau Cazebonne in Graves - Producing Superb Natural Wines and Replanting Forgotten Bordeaux Varieties!

7/12/21 -

![](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0602/3790/6145/files/Cazebonne_480x480.png?v=1711120712)
"We are replanting here, all the historically forgotten grape varieties of Bordeaux, in a biodynamic approach, respectful of the terroir"  - so says the front page of the Chateau Cazebonne website, and indeed the project began in 2016. A collaboration between winelover Jean-Baptiste Dusquesne and winemaker David Poutays, this important Graves estate in Saint-Pierre de Mons extends over 40 hectares, principally on deep beds of Gironde gravel but possessing many different terroirs permitting the production of a wide range of wines. In conversion since 2016, the wines will be certified organic and Biodynamic (Demeter) as of the 2020 vintage release.We're excited to offer a selection of the estate's wines that were still available from the excellent 2019 vintage!
**2019 "Entre Amis" Graves Blanc: 50% Sauvignon Blanc, 50% Semillon - 20% of which gets 5 days of skin contact - a complex and intriguing Graves Blanc at a great price.**

**2019 "Entre Amis" Graves Rouge: 70% Cabernet Sauvignon with Merlot, Cabernet Franc and Malbec - A real wine of terroir, with earth and mineral flavors under dark stone fruit.**

**2019 "Galets de Cazebonne" Graves Rouge: Cabernet Sauvignon on a deep bed of gravel, aged in terracotta amphora, soft and fragrant with lovely silky red fruits..**

**2019 "Feldspath de Peyron" Vin de France: Old vines of Malbec aged 9 months in amphora, beautifully supple and bursting with fruit, tiny addition of volcanic sulfur at bottling.**

**2019 "Galets de Cazebonne" Blanc de Maceration: 79% Semillon, 21% Sauvignon Gris: 50% destemmed by hand, 50% fermented whole-cluster in amphora, 9 months aging in old barriques, zero added SO2.  The palate is full, with lemon, green tea, in addition to white and yellow stone fruits, delicious!**
