For those not familiar with the estate, here is a concise history from Ilya Smolenskiy of Michael Skurnik Wines:

" *Antoine Sanzay is the seventh generation of the Sanzay family to farm vines in the village of Varrains. For most of the family’s history, they sold their grapes to the local cooperative, until Antoine came along and decided to take the domaine into a new direction. He joined the domaine full time in 1999, and slowly began ending the contracts with the co-op, only producing wine from approximately 40% of the vines he owned until 2013, when he was finally able to vinify all of his fruit. Today the estate comprises 11 ha of prime sites in the limestone haven of Saumur-Champigny, including 4 ha of the famed Les Poyeux lieu-dit. Antoine has long been known as one of the great farmers in the village and has been certified organic since 2014. The cellar work is very traditional, using only indigenous yeast for the fermentations and using cement or large foudre/barrels for the elevage. The results are Cabernet Francs that show unrivaled finesse and balance. Antoine’s reputation has grown to the point where his wines are extraordinarily allocated in France, and he is widely considered one of the finest growers in all of the Loire.* "

One of our favorite writers on all things Loire is Chris Kissack, "the Wine Doctor." This exerpt is from his excellent profile of Antoine Sanzay. (Subscriptions to "the Wine Doctor" are highly recommended.)

*"It is always a delight to discover a new domaine of an unexpected level of quality and that was exactly what happened when I first tasted the wines of Antoine Sanzay . As with other appellations it is all too easy in Saumur-Champigny for younger, up-and-coming vignerons to be overlooked as everybody chases after the usual suspects, the obvious example of the latter in this appellation being Clos Rougeard . And yet, on tasting L’Expression , better known as Les Poyeux in more recent vintages, I was convinced that here we had a young vigneron turning out wines of comparable quality. The wines have the same tense substance, and in the case of Les Poyeux the same silky elegance.*

![](https://www.chambersstwines.com/Home/Image/53091?width=534)
Huge cellar, typical of Saumur
*Antoine has proven himself to be one of the most significant new names to have spring up in Saumur-Champigny in recent years. His commitment to his vines is plain for all to see, and his interventions in the cellar are only as required. He is also a charming, humorous, warm-hearted individual, who recognises the joy that wine can bring, and that wine is about people, vines, family, history and heritage, and not just how many points a bottle can score. He strives to make the very best from his vines, and always seems to work with humility. No wonder the wines here are so widely loved, by neighbours and locals, by his fellow vignerons, and of course by me"*
