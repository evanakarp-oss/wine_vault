## Key facts
- Founded **1945 by Martin Ray** (lineage traces back to Paul Masson). One of the original boutique California estates — small lots of Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Cabernet.
- Site: ~**2,000'** atop the **Santa Cruz Mountains AVA**, above Silicon Valley. ~40-acre estate vineyard on austere, infertile **Franciscan shale**; cool, exposed mountaintop.
- **Dry-farmed — no irrigation.** Cabernet from ~40-year-old dry-farmed vines; firm structure, power + finesse, mineral, age-worthy.
- Cabernet heritage: Ray's original cuttings came from **Emmett Rixford's La Questa Vineyard** (Woodside), planted 1890s with cuttings reputed to be from Château Margaux.
- **Jeffrey Patterson** has led winemaking + grape growing since **1981**; he and Ellie Patterson became majority shareholders in **2008**, keeping it family-run.
- **Domaine Eden**: second property/label, developed 1983 by Tom Mudd, acquired and renamed by Mount Eden. Mid-appellation Santa Cruz Mountains; Domaine Eden Cabernet is an appellation (sourced in/around Saratoga) blend — softer/earlier than the Estate.
- US distribution incl. David Bowler Wine; widely retailed (Flatiron, K&L, etc.).
