## Key facts
- Founded **1975 by the Fellowship of Friends** (a spiritual community), Oregon House, Yuba County. **North Yuba AVA**, Sierra Foothills.
- At its peak: ~**365 acres** of vines on ~200 miles of **terraces** carved ~360° around a **decomposing red granite dome**. Elevation **1,700–2,300'**, above the Central Valley fog line; diurnal swings often >40°F → high natural acidity, long hang time.
- Soils: predominantly **decomposed granite** with veins of metamorphic schist; well-drained, mineral-rich.
- **Dry-farmed**, austere style. Cabernet Sauvignon = flagship red across the 1990s–2000s; single-block + Reserve bottlings; structured tannins, dark fruit, deep stony minerality, leathery — built for the cellar, "traditional European" approach.
- **Gideon Beinstock** (Israeli-born artist) became winemaker in **1993**; minimal-intervention techniques that predated the 2010s natural-wine movement. The Beinstock-era late-'90s/early-2000s vintages are the collector sweet spot. (Beinstock later went on to Clos Saron.)
- **Library releases**: small amounts re-released under the **"Taken from Granite"** label — somm/cult cachet (VinePair). Older library vintages still findable, often cheap for the age/quality.
