## Key facts
- Founded 2018 by Chilean winemaker **Rodrigo Soto** + wife **Mai Errázuriz**. Soto has decades of work across the Americas (incl. biodynamic experience).
- Premise: remote high-elevation pockets of the Sonoma-side Mayacamas (Moon Mountain District AVA) can make some of California's most exciting wines.
- **Moon Mountain District AVA**: Sonoma side of the Mayacamas, due west of Mount Veeder, shares a border with the Mount Veeder AVA (Napa). Elevations ~400'–2,600'. Red, rocky **volcanic** soils.
- **`Fission` Cabernet Sauvignon**: mostly from **Alta Vista Gold Ranch**, southern end of Moon Mountain District, ~300 m (~1,000') elevation; **~50-year-old vines, never irrigated (dry-farmed)** — both rare in CA. Name "Fission" references the textural/mineral character of the volcanic soil.
- **The Nunnery**: separate vineyard at the end of Nunn's Canyon (Sonoma-Mayacamas); designed/planted by **Phil Coturri**, **organic since inception**; old terraced blocks (1970s) ~1,600', dry-farmed. Spice, red fruit, chalky tannins. Reached via redwood forest at the mountain base.
- Also makes high-elevation Chardonnay (`Myrna`) from dry-farmed 1970s plantings.
- Farming emphasis throughout: dry-farming + organic/biodynamic.
- **US importer/distributor: Vintus.** UK: Liberty Wines.
