**The 2020 is a worthy successor. And Got 97 Points And is More Elegant**

**Nose is just gorgeous herbs.** So **complex and so detailed** . **Gorgeous plum and berry fruit.** So **intense yet refined and delicate** . Heavenly nose. 9.8. Just stunning. The **nose is pungent and aromatic** with a **raspberry coulis, complex spice** and so much **stoney minerality.** Really a wine that you can sit with over hours and just watch (smell) it evolve). Wow – what a nose. Truly epic and wild yet refined at the same time.

On the **palate** , the fruit and freshness are once in a generation type stuff. Deep, **palate staining fruit** and **astonishing balance** . It goes on for minutes and after proper aeration a perfume like you cannot believe develops. There is also **mind-boggling inner mouth perfume.** Darker fruits than more red. Palate is **juicy, complex and so, so, so stoney.** And this wine is so stoney, which I will repeat often. I can only imagine the source of the vineyard and grapes given the name of the wine. **Amazing sweetness, structure and tannins** . Long. Elegant. Wonderful. This is a CDP in a pure style with astonishing depth and layers of fruit all presented on slabs of frock.

*“Another gorgeous wine from this team is the 2020 Châteauneuf Du Pape Lieu-Dit Pignan, which is 100% Grenache from one of the top terroirs in the appellation. It doesn’t have the sheer power of the 2019, but it’s finer and more elegant, offering a thrilling nose of wild strawberries, blueberries, peppery herbs, spring flowers, and exotic spices. Just incredibly pure, medium to full-bodied, and ultra-fine, with a gorgeous finish, it can be drunk any time over the coming 10-15 years. It’s a thrilling example of finesse-driven Grenache.”* **– 97 Points, Jeb Dunnuck**

*“The finest example of this cuvée I’ve ever tasted, the 2019 Châteauneuf du Pape Lieu-Dit Pignan comes from sandy soils bordering Rayas and, I believe, was brought up in tank. It might be the closest thing to an older Rayas (which were more concentrated and opulent than today’s wines) with its deep ruby/purple hue as well as insanely good aromatics of kirsch, wild strawberries, flowery incense, Provencal spice, candied orange, and garrigue. Full-bodied, ethereal, deep, and multi-dimensional on the palate, it builds with time in the glass and has ultra-fine tannins, flawless balance, and a thrilling finish. If you get a chance, buy it.”* **– 99 Points, Jeb Dunnuck (2019)**
