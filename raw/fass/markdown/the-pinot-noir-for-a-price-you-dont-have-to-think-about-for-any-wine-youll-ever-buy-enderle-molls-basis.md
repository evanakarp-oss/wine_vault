**The Greatest Wine Values – “Hand Over Land”** We all know the phrase “hand over land.” It is generally applied to Burgundy and means that it’s better to have a wine from lesser terroir made by a great producer than a wine from an average producer from great terroir. Alas, in Burgundy, you are seeing entry level wines from great producers costing $100 and up. Such is life. But, **in Germany, you can still buy great entry level wines from great producers at very fair prices** . And that is what today’s offer is all about.

**Enderle and Moll** Enderle and Moll is the cult winery that every top German collector wants. Their wines are sold for hundreds of dollars in certain top restaurants. They are “top customer only” at some places. Production is incredibly small and it’s almost impossible to get the top bottlings. I’ve only sold the top bottlings to the initial buyers when I first opened. This winery is going to go down in history as one of the first superstar wineries that defined the breakout of German pinot noir.

**The 2022 Vintage** 2022 is almost the most miraculous vintage I’ve tasted and cements E & M as the Michael Jordan of German Winemakers in that they just killed it. They are **nimble, terrific, elegant, deep, powerful, transparent, delicate, suave, lithe and insanely fruity but in a beautiful refined way .** Like 2018 with more crunch, power, refinement and depth. Florian says it’s his best ever vintage.

In 2022 the color is darker than usual and a client who buys more Enderle & Moll than anyone on the list was just there and said the 2022 reds from Basis to Ida will be the greatest vintage ever for Enderle & Moll. No doubt these will be world class which will give any Burg a run for the money.

**The Wine** Today I am selling **2022 Enderle & Moll Pinot Noir “Basis”** for **$21.99** a bottle and **$19.99 a bottle on a case.** This is gorgeous and the single best value Pinot Noir I’ve ever sold in the world in my career from anywhere. **An absolutely incredible wine from a top, cult producer** for a price that normally nets you wretched plonk from a mammoth industrial winemaker.

The **nose** is **incredibly striking off the bat** with just **a barrage of honey, brown sugar** , a hint of **sweet herbals,** **mineral and big red cherries.** Loads of **spice, resin, pine, clove, early season cherry, sour cherry** and showing tremendous complexity. Yes a $20 Pinot can show complexity. Just amazingly pure cherries on the nose after around 24 hours of air with all the spice and resin.

**The palate** is just fantastic. The 21 has high acid, deep deep fruit (most ever for this wine!) beautiful purity and great to very great length. **Very crunchy, very powerful** and very mineral. Almost a stoney effect with deep sweet cherry interspersed. Right now showing **intensely mineral** , but also has the **sappy red fruits** this bottling is know for once it’s open for 24 hours. Elegant tannin. Wonderful. This is deeper and more structured than the 2022, but when it matures, 3-4 years, it will be the knockback king of all Pinots. Just so bright and juicy and absolutely delicious. A wine that makes you just totally thrilled to be alive. And yes, while it is delicious and bright, it has that Enderle & Moll complexity . The **spice** . The **licorice. Hauntingly lithe and beautiful pure Pinot Noir** like only E and M can be. Just so delicate and refined. A wine you can knock back on a Tuesday night, after 2-3 years, or serve at a party or even serve to wine geek friends. One can never enough of as well because of the structure. This will last 5-8 years and peak in 2-3.
